What To Eat First Week GLP-1: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: Your first week on GLP-1 medication should focus on gentle, protein-rich, easy-to-digest foods. Start with smaller portions than usual, eat slowly, prioritize lean proteins like eggs, fish, and Greek yogurt, and avoid greasy, fried, or heavy meals that can trigger nausea. Aim for 80-100g of protein across 4-5 small meals, drink at least 64 oz of water between meals, and pay attention to how your body responds to different foods. This first week sets the foundation for your entire treatment.

Why Your First Week Diet Matters So Much

The first week on any GLP-1 medication, whether semaglutide, tirzepatide, or another formulation, is when your body begins adjusting to a new metabolic reality. The medication is starting to slow gastric emptying, reduce appetite signals, and change how your digestive system processes food.

For most patients, the first week is a low dose designed to let your body adapt. Side effects at this stage are usually mild, but they are real: slight nausea, reduced appetite, occasional bloating, and changes in bowel habits. What you eat during this week directly influences how severe those side effects become.

Patients who jump into their first week eating the same foods they always have, large portions, greasy takeout, heavy dinners, tend to experience more GI distress. Patients who adjust their eating from day one report smoother transitions, fewer side effects, and better results over the first month.

Think of your first week as a calibration period. You are learning what your body can handle, what sits well, and what to avoid. FormBlends physicians guide patients through this critical first week to ensure a smooth start that builds momentum for months of successful treatment.

Your First Week GLP-1 Meal Plan

First Week Goals

  • Calories: 1,200-1,600 (do not restrict aggressively; your medication will naturally reduce intake)
  • Protein: 80-100g minimum (build toward 100g+ by week 2-3)
  • Meals: 4-5 small meals rather than 3 large ones
  • Water: 64 oz minimum, sipped between meals
  • Fiber: 20-25g (increase gradually to avoid bloating)

Day 1-2: Start Gentle

Your medication is just beginning to take effect. Keep meals simple and easy to digest.

Breakfast: 2 scrambled eggs with a small piece of whole-grain toast. Herbal tea or black coffee. (220 cal, 14g protein)

Snack: 6 oz plain Greek yogurt with a few blueberries. (120 cal, 17g protein)

Lunch: 3 oz baked chicken breast with steamed green beans and 1/3 cup rice. (320 cal, 26g protein)

Snack: 1/2 cup cottage cheese with cucumber slices. (90 cal, 14g protein)

Dinner: 4 oz baked white fish (tilapia or cod) with steamed broccoli and a small baked potato. (350 cal, 28g protein)

Day total: ~1,100 cal | 99g protein

Day 3-4: Finding Your Rhythm

By now, you may notice slightly reduced appetite. Adjust portions based on how you feel but keep eating on schedule.

Breakfast: Protein smoothie: 1 scoop protein powder, 1 cup unsweetened almond milk, 1/2 banana, 1 tbsp chia seeds. (260 cal, 28g protein)

Snack: 1 hard-boiled egg + 1 string cheese. (150 cal, 13g protein)

Lunch: Turkey and vegetable soup: 3 oz lean ground turkey, diced carrots, celery, and zucchini in chicken broth. (280 cal, 24g protein)

Snack: Greek yogurt (6 oz) with 1 tbsp ground flaxseed. (130 cal, 18g protein)

Dinner: 4 oz grilled salmon with roasted asparagus and 1/3 cup quinoa. (400 cal, 30g protein)

Day total: ~1,220 cal | 113g protein

Day 5-7: Building Confidence

You are getting a sense of what works for your body. Start introducing slightly more variety while keeping the same principles.

Breakfast: Cottage cheese toast: 1 slice whole-grain bread topped with 1/2 cup cottage cheese, sliced tomato, and everything seasoning. Side of 1/2 cup berries. (250 cal, 20g protein)

Snack: Small apple with 1 tbsp almond butter. (160 cal, 4g protein)

Lunch: Chicken lettuce wraps: 4 oz grilled chicken, butter lettuce, shredded carrots, a drizzle of peanut sauce (1 tsp peanut butter + soy sauce + lime). (300 cal, 32g protein)

Snack: Protein bar (15g+ protein, under 5g sugar). (180 cal, 15g protein)

Dinner: Lean turkey meatballs (3 meatballs) with marinara sauce over zucchini noodles. Side salad with olive oil and lemon. (380 cal, 28g protein)

Day total: ~1,270 cal | 99g protein

Best Foods for Week 1

These foods are consistently well-tolerated during the first week on GLP-1 medication:

  • Eggs: Scrambled, soft-boiled, or poached. Easy to digest, high in protein, and gentle on the stomach.
  • Greek yogurt: Smooth, cool, and protein-dense. Good for morning or as a snack when appetite is low.
  • Baked or steamed fish: Lighter than chicken or red meat. Cod, tilapia, and sole are especially mild.
  • Chicken breast: Baked or poached, not fried. Keep it moist with marinades or broth.
  • Soups and broths: Hydrating and gentle. Add protein by including chicken, lentils, or egg.
  • Bananas: Easy on the stomach and provide potassium, which helps with hydration.
  • Oatmeal: Soothing, fiber-rich, and a great vehicle for protein powder.
  • Sweet potatoes: Nutrient-dense, gentle carb source that pairs well with any protein.
  • Steamed vegetables: Broccoli, green beans, zucchini, and carrots are all well-tolerated when cooked soft.

What to Avoid During Your First Week

  • Fried foods: French fries, fried chicken, onion rings, and similar foods are the number one trigger for first-week nausea. Your stomach is adjusting to slower emptying. Greasy foods make this exponentially worse.
  • Spicy foods: Wait until you know how your stomach responds before adding heat. Spicy dishes can trigger acid reflux and nausea in the adjustment period.
  • Large portions: Even if you feel hungry, eat smaller amounts than usual. You can always eat more in an hour. You cannot undo the discomfort of overeating on a GLP-1.
  • Carbonated drinks: Soda, sparkling water, and beer cause gas and bloating, which compound the GI effects of your medication.
  • Heavy dairy: Large amounts of cheese, cream sauces, and ice cream can be hard to digest during the first week. Small amounts of yogurt and cottage cheese are fine.
  • Alcohol: Skip it entirely during week 1. Your body is adjusting to the medication, and alcohol adds variables that make it harder to identify which symptoms come from the medication versus what you consumed.
  • Sugary foods and drinks: Soda, juice, candy, and pastries spike blood sugar and offer zero nutritional value. Your medication is working to stabilize blood sugar. Do not undermine it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to feel nauseous during the first week?

Yes. Mild nausea is one of the most common first-week side effects, reported by 15-40% of patients depending on the medication. It usually improves within a few days as your body adjusts. Eating smaller meals, avoiding greasy foods, and sipping ginger tea can help manage it. If nausea is severe or prevents you from eating, contact your FormBlends provider.

What if I am not hungry at all during the first week?

Reduced appetite is expected, even at the starting dose. Do not skip meals. Eat by the clock, aiming for something every 3-4 hours. Even if you can only manage a few bites, choose protein-rich foods so those bites count. A hard-boiled egg and a few spoonfuls of yogurt are better than nothing.

Can I exercise during the first week on GLP-1?

Light to moderate exercise is fine and encouraged. Walking, light cycling, and gentle yoga are all good choices. Avoid intense workouts until you know how the medication affects your energy and appetite. Stay hydrated and eat a protein-rich snack before exercising. If you feel dizzy or unusually fatigued during activity, stop and rest.

Should I take my medication before or after eating?

For injectable GLP-1 medications (semaglutide, tirzepatide), timing relative to meals does not affect the medication's absorption because it is a weekly injection that works continuously. You can inject at any time of day. Some patients prefer morning injections so any initial nausea occurs during waking hours. Others prefer evening injections so they sleep through the adjustment. Discuss timing with your FormBlends provider.

How different will my diet be from week 1 to week 4?

Week 1 is about gentle, easy-to-digest foods and learning your body's response. By week 4, most patients have identified their trigger foods, found their preferred protein sources, and established a sustainable eating pattern. Protein targets increase slightly (from 80-100g to 100-130g), meal variety expands, and portion sizes stabilize. The core principles stay the same throughout treatment: protein first, whole foods, small portions, and consistent hydration.
