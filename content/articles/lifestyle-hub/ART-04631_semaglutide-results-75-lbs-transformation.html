Addressing Common Challenges

Metabolic Adaptation

As you lose weight, your body requires fewer calories. This metabolic adaptation is natural but can make the later stages of weight loss more challenging. Strength training, adequate protein, and periodic reassessment of your caloric needs help counteract this effect. {{CITE:Metabolic adaptation during extended weight loss}}

Medication Tolerance

Some patients notice reduced appetite suppression over time. Your physician can evaluate whether a dose adjustment or treatment modification is appropriate. In some cases, combining semaglutide with other physician-approved approaches may be recommended.

Skin Management

Loose skin is expected with a 75-pound loss. The extent depends on age, genetics, and duration the weight was carried. While strength training and adequate nutrition help, some patients ultimately consider skin removal procedures.
