The Science Behind NAD+ (What You Need to Know Before Starting)

NAD+ is a molecule your cells need for energy production, DNA repair, and activating longevity-associated proteins called sirtuins. Your NAD+ levels naturally decline as you age, roughly 50% by middle age. This decline contributes to fatigue, slower recovery, cognitive changes, and the cellular deterioration we associate with aging.

The goal of NAD+ supplementation is simple: restore cellular NAD+ levels closer to what they were when you were younger. The challenge is choosing the right method, dose, and supporting strategy. This is where physician guidance makes a real difference.

Three things happen when you successfully raise NAD+ levels:

  1. Your mitochondria produce energy more efficiently (less fatigue, better physical performance)
  2. Your DNA repair systems work faster (reduced accumulation of cellular damage)
  3. Your sirtuins become more active (better metabolic regulation, reduced inflammation, improved stress resistance)

How It Works: Your Starting Path

Option A: The Oral Starter Protocol (Simplest)

Best for: Beginners, those who prefer pills over injections, preventive longevity support.

  • NMN or NR: One daily dose, taken in the morning. Your physician will recommend a specific product and dose based on your age and health status.
  • Apigenin: One dose in the evening to inhibit CD38 and preserve the NAD+ your body produces.
  • Timeline: Start seeing noticeable energy improvements within 2 to 4 weeks. Full benefits develop over 2 to 3 months.
  • Cost: The most affordable option. Starting at $199/mo

Option B: The Injectable Protocol (Moderate)

Best for: People who want faster results than oral supplementation, those comfortable with self-injection.

  • Subcutaneous NAD+ injections: Administered 2 to 3 times per week at home. Provides higher bioavailability than oral precursors.
  • Optional oral precursor on non-injection days: Maintains consistent NAD+ levels throughout the week.
  • CD38 inhibitor: Daily apigenin or quercetin.
  • Timeline: Most patients notice energy and clarity improvements within the first week.

Option C: The Comprehensive Protocol (Most Aggressive)

Best for: Those focused on maximum longevity benefit, people with significant fatigue or age-related decline.

  • Monthly IV NAD+ infusion: Provides a large bolus of NAD+ directly into the bloodstream.
  • Daily oral precursor: NMN or NR for sustained elevation between infusions.
  • CD38 inhibition: Apigenin and/or quercetin daily.
  • Sirtuin activator: Resveratrol or fisetin to maximize longevity pathway activation.
  • Timeline: Immediate effects from the first IV session, with compounding benefits over months.

Getting Started: Practical Steps

  1. Schedule your consultation. Book a telehealth appointment with a Form Blends physician. Be ready to discuss your energy levels, any health concerns, current medications, and what you are hoping to achieve.
  2. Get baseline labs (recommended). A metabolic panel, inflammatory markers (hsCRP), and a complete blood count give us a starting point to track your progress. These are not strictly required to begin but are valuable for optimization.
  3. Receive your protocol. Your physician selects your starting option (A, B, or C) and provides specific product recommendations, dosing instructions, and timing guidance.
  4. Start simple. Even within the Comprehensive Protocol, we ramp up gradually. You might start with oral precursors alone for the first two weeks, then add CD38 inhibitors, then schedule your first IV or start injections.
  5. Track how you feel. Keep brief daily notes on energy, sleep quality, mental clarity, and exercise performance. These subjective markers are surprisingly useful for protocol optimization.

Beginner tips:

  • Take NMN/NR in the morning on an empty stomach for best absorption
  • Take resveratrol or fisetin with a fat-containing meal (they are fat-soluble)
  • Take apigenin in the evening; it has mild calming effects that can support sleep
  • Store any injectable NAD+ in the refrigerator
  • Do not expect dramatic overnight changes from oral supplementation; this is a long game

Safety Considerations

  • Start low. Higher doses are not always better with NAD+ precursors. Your physician will start you at a moderate dose and increase only if needed.
  • IV NAD+ requires supervision. If you opt for infusions, ensure they are administered by trained medical professionals who can manage infusion-related side effects (flushing, nausea, chest tightness).
  • Cancer screening. Be current on routine cancer screenings before starting NAD+ therapy. While there is no evidence that NAD+ causes cancer, the theoretical concern about supporting rapidly dividing cells warrants appropriate screening.
  • Quality sourcing. The NAD+ precursor supplement market includes products of wildly varying quality. Physician-recommended sources are tested for purity and potency.
  • Interactions. NAD+ protocols are generally compatible with most medications. Your physician will confirm there are no conflicts with your specific medications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I too young to start NAD+ supplementation?

NAD+ decline begins in your thirties. Starting in your thirties or forties is preventive. Starting later is corrective. Both are valuable. There is no "too early" for supporting cellular health, though the urgency increases with age.

Can I buy NMN at a health food store and skip the physician?

You can buy NMN over the counter, but without physician guidance you may be taking the wrong dose, a low-quality product, or missing the CD38 inhibition and sirtuin activation layers that make the protocol truly effective. Will I feel a difference, or is this all about long-term cellular health?

Most patients feel tangible differences in energy, sleep, and mental clarity within the first month. The long-term cellular benefits (DNA repair, reduced biological aging) are happening simultaneously but are not directly perceptible. Think of it as getting both short-term quality of life improvements and long-term insurance for your cells.

Can I combine NAD+ therapy with other peptide protocols?

Yes. NAD+ therapy pairs well with cognitive enhancement peptides, recovery peptides, and gut health peptides. NAD+ supports the cellular energy needed for all repair and optimization processes, making other protocols more effective.
