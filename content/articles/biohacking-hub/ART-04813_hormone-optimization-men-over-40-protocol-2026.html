Phase 1: Foundation Reset (Weeks 1 Through 4)

Phase 1 focuses on removing the biggest barriers to healthy hormone production: poor sleep, excess body fat, sedentary behavior, and chronic stress.

Exercise Protocol

  • Resistance training: 3 sessions per week focusing on compound movements. Each session should include at least one lower-body compound (squat or deadlift variation) and one upper-body push and pull movement.
  • Session structure: Warm-up (10 min), compound lifts (30 min at 70 to 85% of 1-rep max), accessory work (15 min), cool-down (5 min).
  • Zone 2 cardio: 2 sessions per week, 30 to 45 minutes each. Walking, cycling, or swimming at a pace where you can hold a conversation but feel mild effort.
  • Daily movement: Walk 7,000 to 10,000 steps daily. This baseline non-exercise activity thermogenesis (NEAT) supports insulin sensitivity and cortisol regulation.

Sleep Reset

  • Set a non-negotiable 7.5 to 8-hour sleep window.
  • Morning sunlight within 30 minutes of waking (10 to 15 minutes).
  • No caffeine after noon.
  • Bedroom at 66 to 68 degrees Fahrenheit, fully dark.
  • Magnesium glycinate 400 mg before bed sleep optimization biohacking.

Nutrition Foundation

  • Protein: 1.2 to 1.6 g per kg bodyweight daily, distributed across 3 to 4 meals. Prioritize leucine-rich sources (eggs, beef, whey, chicken).
  • Fats: 25 to 35% of total calories from healthy fats. Do not go below 20% total fat; very low-fat diets reduce testosterone .
  • Carbohydrates: Primarily from whole food sources (sweet potatoes, rice, oats, fruits). Time higher carb intake around training sessions.
  • Caloric target: If body fat is above 20%, create a moderate deficit (300 to 500 calories below maintenance). If body fat is below 20%, eat at maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this protocol work if I am over 50 or 60?

Yes. The same principles apply at any age past 40. Older men may need to adjust exercise intensity (more warm-up, longer recovery periods) and may see slower but still meaningful improvements. Physician oversight becomes more important with advancing age .

How quickly will I see results?

Subjective improvements (energy, mood, sleep quality) often appear within 2 to 4 weeks. Measurable hormonal changes typically require 8 to 12 weeks of consistent adherence. Body composition changes take 3 to 6 months.

Can I follow this protocol while on testosterone replacement therapy (TRT)?

Many elements (exercise, nutrition, sleep, stress management, most supplements) complement TRT. However, some supplements (ashwagandha, tongkat ali) may have different effects when exogenous testosterone is present. Discuss your supplement stack with your prescribing physician .
