The Science Behind the 2026 Updates

Key research developments inform this year's protocol:

Circadian NAD+ metabolism. NAD+ levels in your cells naturally fluctuate throughout the day, following circadian patterns tied to your body's master clock. Research shows that NAD+ biosynthesis enzymes like NAMPT (nicotinamide phosphoribosyltransferase) peak during specific windows. Timing precursor intake to align with these peaks enhances conversion efficiency.

Subcutaneous NAD+ viability. While IV NAD+ infusions remain the gold standard for rapid NAD+ restoration, subcutaneous injections have gained traction as a practical alternative. Recent clinical observations show that subcutaneous NAD+ achieves meaningful blood level increases without the 2-to-4-hour time commitment of IV sessions.

The CD38 problem, solved. Earlier protocols focused almost entirely on boosting NAD+ production. The 2026 protocol equally emphasizes reducing NAD+ destruction by targeting CD38, the enzyme responsible for consuming up to 95% of NAD+ in aged tissues. Combining precursor supplementation with CD38 inhibition creates a "raise the floor while raising the ceiling" effect.

Sirtuin-activating compound synergy. Adding specific sirtuin activators (like resveratrol or fisetin) alongside NAD+ precursors ensures that the restored NAD+ is actually being used by the longevity pathways it is meant to fuel. Without sirtuin activation, much of the NAD+ may be consumed by other pathways with less longevity relevance.

How It Works

Layer 1: NAD+ Precursor Foundation

  • NMN or NR: Daily oral supplementation, taken in the morning to align with peak NAMPT activity. Your physician selects the precursor based on your individual response and any relevant research developments.
  • Dosing: Individualized based on age, health status, and goals. Starting doses are conservative with room for titration upward at check-ins.

Layer 2: CD38 Inhibition

  • Apigenin: A natural flavonoid that inhibits CD38 activity. Taken daily, typically with the evening meal.
  • Quercetin: Another CD38 inhibitor with additional senolytic (senescent cell-clearing) properties. Can be cycled intermittently.
  • These agents prevent your body from breaking down the NAD+ you are working to restore.

Layer 3: Sirtuin Activation

  • Resveratrol or fisetin: Added to ensure that sirtuin pathways are primed to use the available NAD+. These compounds have been shown to enhance sirtuin activity when NAD+ is adequate.
  • Timing: typically taken with a fat-containing meal for better absorption (both are fat-soluble).

Layer 4: Direct NAD+ Boost (Optional)

  • Monthly IV NAD+ infusion: For patients who want the most aggressive approach, a monthly IV session provides a bolus of NAD+ directly into the bloodstream. This is particularly valuable during the initial "loading" phase.
  • Weekly subcutaneous NAD+ injection: An alternative for patients who want more than oral supplementation but prefer the convenience of home administration.
  • These direct-delivery methods complement the oral precursor and CD38 inhibition layers.

Protocol Timeline

Safety Considerations

  • Well-tolerated. NMN, NR, apigenin, and quercetin have strong safety profiles at recommended doses. Most patients experience no side effects.
  • IV NAD+ side effects. Flushing, nausea, and chest tightness can occur during infusions. These are managed by adjusting infusion rate and are not dangerous.
  • Cancer screening. Because NAD+ supports all rapidly dividing cells, patients should be up to date on cancer screenings before starting. Active cancer is a contraindication until cleared by an oncologist.
  • Medication interactions. Generally minimal, but always disclose all medications and supplements to your physician.
  • Pregnancy and nursing. NAD+ protocols are not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to limited safety data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need all four layers of the protocol?

No. The protocol is modular. Some patients start with Layer 1 (oral precursor) and Layer 2 (CD38 inhibition) and add other layers later. Your physician will recommend the approach that matches your goals, budget, and commitment level.

How do I know my NAD+ levels are actually increasing?

NAD+ blood testing is available but not yet standardized. In practice, we track clinical markers: energy levels, sleep quality, exercise recovery, cognitive function, and metabolic lab values. These provide meaningful, actionable feedback even without direct NAD+ measurement.

Can I combine this with a weight loss protocol?

Absolutely. NAD+ optimization supports the metabolic improvements that GLP-1 weight loss programs deliver. Many patients use both for complementary benefits. Improved mitochondrial function from NAD+ can actually enhance the metabolic effects of weight loss.

Is this just for older adults?

NAD+ decline begins in your thirties. Starting supplementation earlier can be a preventive strategy rather than a corrective one. Athletes, high-performers, and anyone focused on long-term health can benefit regardless of age.
