GLP-1 Medications: What They Are and How They Work

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications originally developed to treat type 2 diabetes. Researchers discovered that these drugs also produce significant weight loss by reducing appetite and slowing the rate at which food leaves your stomach. The FDA has since approved specific GLP-1 medications for chronic weight management in eligible patients.

The two most commonly prescribed GLP-1 medications for weight loss are:

  • Semaglutide: A once-weekly injection shown to help patients lose an average of 15% of body weight in clinical studies.
  • Tirzepatide: A dual-action GIP/GLP-1 agonist that has demonstrated even greater weight reduction in some trials, averaging over 20% body weight loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I qualify for GLP-1 medication?

GLP-1 medications are generally prescribed for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related health condition. Your Form Blends physician will determine eligibility during your consultation.

Are there side effects?

The most common side effects are mild nausea, constipation, or digestive discomfort, especially during the initial dose titration period. These typically improve as your body adjusts.

How long do I need to stay on the medication?

Treatment duration varies by individual. Some patients use GLP-1 medications for several months; others benefit from longer-term use. Your physician will work with you to determine the right timeline based on your progress and health goals.
