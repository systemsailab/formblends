What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1, a hormone your body naturally produces in the gut after meals. This hormone tells your brain you are full, slows gastric emptying, and helps regulate blood sugar. GLP-1 receptor agonist medications amplify these effects, making them powerful tools for weight management. {{CITE:GLP-1 receptor agonist pharmacology}}

The two most widely prescribed GLP-1 medications for weight loss are:

  • Semaglutide: Available as brand-name Wegovy and Ozempic, as well as compounded formulations. Clinical trials showed average weight loss of approximately 15% of body weight. {{CITE:STEP trial results}}
  • Tirzepatide: A dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist sold under the brand name Mounjaro and Zepbound. Trials demonstrated weight loss of up to 22% of body weight. {{CITE:SURMOUNT trial results}}
