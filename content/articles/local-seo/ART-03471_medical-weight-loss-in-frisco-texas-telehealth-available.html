How is medical weight loss different from what I have tried before?

Most diet programs fight against your biology. They ask you to eat less while your body screams for more food. Medical weight loss medications work with your biology by reducing the hunger signals themselves, making it genuinely easier to eat less and lose weight {{CITE:hormonal appetite regulation and GLP-1 medication impact}}. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:clinical rationale for pharmacological weight loss intervention}}

Will I gain the weight back if I stop medication?

Weight regain is possible after stopping GLP-1 medications, which is why your physician helps you develop a maintenance plan. This may include a lower maintenance dose, lifestyle strategies, or a combination approach to preserve your results.

Do I need to exercise while on medication?

Exercise is not required for the medication to work, but it is strongly recommended. Physical activity helps preserve lean muscle mass during weight loss and improves cardiovascular health, mood, and long-term weight maintenance.

Is medical weight loss safe for people with diabetes?

GLP-1 medications were originally developed for type 2 diabetes, so they have an extensive safety record in diabetic patients. In fact, they can improve blood sugar control alongside weight loss.
