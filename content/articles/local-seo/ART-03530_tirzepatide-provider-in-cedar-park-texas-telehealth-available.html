Why Tirzepatide Is Getting Attention

Tirzepatide has generated significant interest in the weight loss field because of its dual mechanism. Unlike semaglutide, which targets only GLP-1 receptors, tirzepatide activates both GLP-1 and GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptors. This dual-action approach produces stronger appetite suppression and greater metabolic effects.

In the SURMOUNT clinical trials, tirzepatide demonstrated weight loss of up to 22.5% of body weight over 72 weeks, making it the most effective injectable weight loss medication currently available.

How Tirzepatide Works

Tirzepatide acts on two hormonal pathways simultaneously:

  • GLP-1 Pathway - Reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves insulin sensitivity. This is the same pathway targeted by semaglutide.
  • GIP Pathway - Enhances fat metabolism and may improve how the body processes and stores energy. The addition of GIP receptor activation is what sets tirzepatide apart.

Together, these pathways create a powerful effect on hunger, satiety, and metabolic function that goes beyond what single-action medications achieve.

Tirzepatide Dosing and Treatment Timeline

  • Dose Increases: Every four weeks, the dose may increase (5 mg, 7.5 mg, 10 mg, 12.5 mg, up to 15 mg)

Side Effects Cedar Park Patients Should Know

The most common side effects of tirzepatide are gastrointestinal and usually temporary:

  • Nausea, particularly during dose increases
  • Decreased appetite (this is also part of how the medication works)

  • Mild stomach discomfort

These effects tend to be most pronounced during the first few weeks at each new dose level and often resolve within days. Your Form Blends physician will guide you through managing any discomfort.
