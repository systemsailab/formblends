How Weight Loss Injections Work

Modern weight loss injections are not the same as the diet shots and B12 cocktails that wellness clinics have offered for years. Today's injectable weight loss medications are GLP-1 receptor agonists, a class of drugs originally developed for type 2 diabetes that demonstrated remarkable weight loss as an additional benefit.

When you inject semaglutide or tirzepatide once per week, the medication:

  • Activates GLP-1 receptors in the brain to reduce hunger signals
  • Slows gastric emptying so you feel full longer after eating
  • Improves insulin sensitivity and blood sugar regulation
  • Reduces food noise, the constant mental chatter about eating that many patients describe

The result is that you naturally eat less, make better food choices, and lose weight steadily over the course of treatment.

Managing Side Effects

Like any medication, weight loss injections can cause side effects. The most common is nausea, which tends to be mild and most noticeable during the first few weeks of treatment or after a dose increase. Other potential side effects include:

  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Mild headache
  • Fatigue during initial adjustment
  • Injection site redness or irritation

Our physicians start you on a low dose and increase gradually, which is the standard approach to minimize side effects. If you experience persistent issues, your doctor will adjust your protocol during your scheduled telehealth follow-ups.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are weight loss injections?

Weight loss injections refer to injectable GLP-1 receptor agonist medications such as semaglutide and tirzepatide. These are administered as a weekly subcutaneous injection and work by reducing appetite and improving metabolic regulation.

Are weight loss injections painful?

Most patients report minimal discomfort. The injections use a very fine needle and are administered subcutaneously, typically in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Many patients compare the sensation to a small pinch.

How often do I need to take weight loss injections?

Both semaglutide and tirzepatide are administered once weekly. You can choose the same day each week and administer the injection yourself at home.

What side effects should I expect from weight loss injections?

Common side effects include mild nausea, particularly during the initial weeks as your body adjusts. Other possible effects include constipation, diarrhea, and injection site reactions. Your Form Blends physician will help manage any side effects.
