Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between tirzepatide and semaglutide?

Both are injectable weight loss medications, but tirzepatide activates two hormone receptors (GLP-1 and GIP) while semaglutide targets only GLP-1. Clinical trials suggest tirzepatide may produce greater weight loss in some patients . Your physician will help determine which is better suited for your situation.

What side effects should I expect with tirzepatide?

The most common side effects are gastrointestinal: nausea, diarrhea, and decreased appetite. These tend to be most noticeable when starting treatment or increasing the dose and usually improve as your body adjusts .

How long will I need to take tirzepatide?

Treatment duration varies. Many patients stay on tirzepatide for several months to a year or longer to reach and maintain their goal weight. Your physician will work with you on a long-term plan that makes sense for your health goals.

Can I switch from semaglutide to tirzepatide?

Yes. If you have been on semaglutide and want to try tirzepatide, your doctor can facilitate a safe transition with appropriate dosing adjustments.
