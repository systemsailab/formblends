How GLP-1 Treatment Works

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1, a hormone your body naturally produces to regulate appetite and blood sugar. GLP-1 medications mimic this hormone, amplifying its effects. The result is reduced hunger, fewer cravings, and a greater sense of fullness after eating.

This is not about starving yourself or relying on willpower alone. The medication addresses the biological mechanisms that make weight loss so difficult, particularly the hormonal signals that ramp up hunger when you start losing weight. It is your body's survival instinct working against your goals, and GLP-1 medications help quiet that response.

What to Expect from Treatment

Realistic expectations matter. GLP-1 medications are powerful tools, but they work best when combined with thoughtful changes to diet and activity. Many describe a shift in their relationship with food that feels almost liberating. The constant background noise of cravings and hunger quiets down, and meals become simpler decisions.
