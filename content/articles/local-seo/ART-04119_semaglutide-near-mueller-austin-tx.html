How Semaglutide Supports Weight Loss

Semaglutide belongs to a class of drugs called GLP-1 receptor agonists. It mimics a hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1, which your gut naturally releases after meals. When administered as a once-weekly injection, semaglutide:

  • Reduces hunger signals in the brain
  • Slows the speed at which your stomach empties
  • Helps stabilize blood sugar levels
  • Supports sustained calorie reduction without constant willpower

In clinical trials, semaglutide helped participants achieve an average weight loss of roughly 15% of their starting body weight over 68 weeks.
