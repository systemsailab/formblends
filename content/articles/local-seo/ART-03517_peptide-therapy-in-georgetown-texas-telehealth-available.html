What Is Peptide Therapy?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules in the body. They play essential roles in processes such as tissue repair, immune function, hormone regulation, and cellular regeneration. {{CITE:peptide biology overview}} Peptide therapy involves administering specific peptides to support or enhance these natural functions.

Unlike synthetic drugs that override your body's systems, therapeutic peptides work with your existing biology. They signal your cells to perform their natural functions more effectively, which is why peptide therapy has gained significant interest for wellness, recovery, and anti-aging applications. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:peptide therapy mechanism}}
