The Science Behind Anti-Aging Peptides

Aging is not just about wrinkles and gray hair. At the cellular level, aging involves a gradual decline in the signaling molecules that keep your body repairing, rebuilding, and functioning optimally. Peptides are among those signaling molecules. {{CITE:peptide signaling and aging research}}

Anti-aging peptide therapy introduces specific amino acid chains that target key aging pathways:

  • Growth hormone secretagogues (like CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin) stimulate natural growth hormone production, supporting muscle maintenance, fat metabolism, and tissue repair. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:growth hormone secretagogue claims}}
  • NAD+ precursors support mitochondrial function and cellular energy production, which decline significantly with age.
  • Collagen-stimulating peptides promote skin elasticity, hydration, and structural integrity.
  • Thymosin peptides support immune function, which naturally weakens over time.

These are not cosmetic treatments. They target the underlying biology of aging to help you feel and perform better as the years progress.
