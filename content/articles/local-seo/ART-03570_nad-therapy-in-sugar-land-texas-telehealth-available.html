NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme present in every cell of the body. It plays a critical role in energy metabolism, DNA repair, and cellular signaling. As we age, NAD+ levels naturally decline, which researchers believe contributes to many age-related health changes. {{CITE:NAD+ decline with aging}}

What Is NAD+ Therapy?

NAD+ therapy involves supplementing the body's supply of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide to support cellular processes that slow down with age. Therapeutic NAD+ can be delivered through injections or other physician-directed methods, depending on the protocol. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:NAD+ administration methods}}

Research into NAD+ has accelerated in recent years, with studies examining its role in:

  • Mitochondrial function and cellular energy production
  • DNA repair and genomic stability
  • Sirtuin activation (proteins linked to longevity)
  • Neuroprotection and cognitive function
  • Metabolic health and inflammation regulation
