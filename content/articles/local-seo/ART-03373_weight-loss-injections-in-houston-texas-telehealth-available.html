Understanding GLP-1 Weight Loss Injections

GLP-1 receptor agonists are injectable medications that mimic a natural gut hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1. This hormone plays a key role in appetite regulation, blood sugar control, and how quickly food moves through your digestive system. When you take a GLP-1 medication, you feel fuller after eating less, experience fewer cravings, and find it easier to maintain a calorie deficit.

The most commonly prescribed GLP-1 medications include semaglutide and tirzepatide. Clinical trials have shown that patients using semaglutide lost an average of 15% of their body weight over 68 weeks, while tirzepatide users lost up to 22.5% in similar timeframes.

Who Qualifies for GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 weight loss injections are generally prescribed for adults with:

  • A BMI of 30 or higher (obesity)
  • A BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol
