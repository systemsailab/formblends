What Results Can You Expect?

GLP-1 medications have been studied extensively, and the data is encouraging. Clinical trials have demonstrated average weight loss of 15% to 20% of body weight, depending on the specific medication and individual factors. That means someone starting at 230 pounds could realistically lose 35 to 45 pounds over the course of treatment.

But the numbers only tell part of the story. Patients consistently report changes that go beyond the scale. Better energy levels. Improved sleep. Less joint pain. A different relationship with food where meals become something you enjoy rather than something you stress about. Many patients describe feeling like a fog has lifted around food decisions.

Results do not happen overnight. Most treatment protocols involve a gradual dose increase over the first several weeks, which helps minimize side effects and lets your body adjust. The most common side effects are gastrointestinal, primarily nausea, which tends to be mild and typically improves as your body adapts to the medication.

Who Qualifies for GLP-1 Treatment?

GLP-1 medications are typically appropriate for adults with a BMI of 30 or above, or those with a BMI of 27 or above who also have a weight-related condition like type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or high cholesterol. During your telehealth consultation, your provider will evaluate whether you are a good candidate based on your complete health profile.

There are some situations where GLP-1 medications are not recommended, including a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2. Your provider will walk through all relevant considerations during your visit.
