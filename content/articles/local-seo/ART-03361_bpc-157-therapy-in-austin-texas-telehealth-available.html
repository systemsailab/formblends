What Is BPC-157?

BPC stands for Body Protection Compound. BPC-157 is a 15-amino-acid peptide that was originally isolated from human gastric juice, where it plays a natural role in protecting and repairing the stomach lining. Researchers have studied it extensively in preclinical models and found that its regenerative properties extend well beyond the gut. {{CITE:BPC-157 gastric peptide research, Journal of Physiology and Pharmacology}}

The peptide works through several mechanisms. It promotes angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels), upregulates growth factor expression, and modulates nitric oxide pathways. Together, these actions support faster healing of soft tissues including tendons, ligaments, muscles, and the GI tract. {{CITE:BPC-157 mechanisms of action, Current Pharmaceutical Design}}

Key Areas of Research

  • Tendon and Ligament Repair: Studies show BPC-157 accelerates healing of damaged tendons and ligaments in animal models. {{CITE:BPC-157 tendon healing studies, Journal of Orthopaedic Research}}
  • Gut Health: BPC-157 has demonstrated protective effects against stomach ulcers, inflammatory bowel conditions, and intestinal damage in preclinical research.
  • Muscle Recovery: The peptide has been shown to promote muscle healing and reduce recovery time after injury.
  • Neuroprotection: Emerging research suggests BPC-157 may have protective effects on the nervous system, including potential benefits for brain injuries and nerve damage.
