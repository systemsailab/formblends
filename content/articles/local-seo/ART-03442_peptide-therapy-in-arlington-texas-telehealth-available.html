Frequently Asked Questions

Who is a good candidate for peptide therapy?

Adults experiencing slow recovery from injuries, signs of aging (low energy, poor sleep, declining skin quality), gut issues, or general performance decline may benefit from peptide therapy. Your physician will screen you to make sure peptides are appropriate for your health situation.

Are peptides the same as steroids?

No. Peptides are amino acid chains that signal natural processes in the body. They are not anabolic steroids and do not carry the same risk profile. Peptides work by enhancing your body's own biological functions rather than introducing synthetic hormones .

How long before I notice results from peptide therapy?

This depends on the peptide and your goals. Some patients feel improvements in sleep and energy within one to two weeks. Recovery and skin benefits typically become noticeable after four to six weeks of consistent use.

Can I combine multiple peptides?

Yes, and many patients do. Combining peptides that target different pathways can produce complementary benefits. Your physician will design a safe, effective multi-peptide protocol if appropriate for your goals.
