What Is NAD+?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every living cell. It plays a critical role in energy metabolism, DNA repair, and cellular signaling. Without adequate NAD+, cells cannot efficiently convert nutrients into energy or maintain their repair mechanisms.

Research has shown that NAD+ levels decline significantly with age, with levels at age 60 roughly half of what they were at age 20. This decline is associated with many hallmarks of aging, including reduced cognitive function, decreased physical endurance, and impaired cellular repair.

Benefits of NAD+ Therapy

NAD+ therapy aims to restore cellular NAD+ levels, potentially supporting:

  • Cellular Energy Production - NAD+ is essential for mitochondrial function, the primary energy-producing process in cells. Restoring NAD+ may improve overall energy and reduce fatigue.
  • Cognitive Function - NAD+ supports neuronal health and may help maintain mental clarity, focus, and memory as you age.
  • DNA Repair - NAD+ activates enzymes called sirtuins and PARPs that are critical for repairing DNA damage.
  • Healthy Aging - By supporting multiple cellular processes, NAD+ therapy may slow some of the functional declines associated with aging.
  • Exercise Recovery - Some patients report faster recovery from physical activity after beginning NAD+ supplementation.

What the Research Says

NAD+ research is an active and growing field. Key findings include:

  • Animal studies have demonstrated that boosting NAD+ levels can improve metabolic function, extend lifespan in certain organisms, and protect against neurodegeneration.
  • Human clinical trials are ongoing, with early results suggesting improvements in energy metabolism and physical function.
  • The role of NAD+ in activating sirtuins, a family of proteins linked to longevity and stress resistance, is one of the most studied aspects of this therapy.

While the research is promising, NAD+ therapy is still an emerging field. Your Form Blends physician will discuss what current evidence supports and what remains under investigation.
