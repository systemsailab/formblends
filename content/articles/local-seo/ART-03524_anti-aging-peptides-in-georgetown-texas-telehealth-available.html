What Are Anti-Aging Peptides?

Anti-aging peptides are short chains of amino acids that support cellular processes involved in repair, regeneration, and protection against age-related decline. As we age, the body's natural production of certain peptides decreases, contributing to slower recovery, reduced collagen production, cognitive decline, and diminished cellular energy.

Supplementing with therapeutic peptides can help restore some of these functions, supporting the body's ability to repair tissue, produce energy at the cellular level, and maintain healthy skin and cognitive performance.

The Science Behind Peptide-Based Anti-Aging

Anti-aging peptide therapy is grounded in the biology of cellular aging. Key processes targeted by therapeutic peptides include:

  • Mitochondrial function - NAD+ supports the mitochondria that power every cell in your body. Declining NAD+ is associated with reduced cellular energy output.
  • Collagen production - GHK-Cu stimulates the synthesis of collagen and other structural proteins that decline with age, affecting skin, joints, and connective tissue.
  • Telomere maintenance - Some peptides like epithalon may influence telomerase activity, potentially supporting chromosomal health.
  • Systemic inflammation - Several peptides have anti-inflammatory properties that can counteract the chronic low-grade inflammation associated with aging.
