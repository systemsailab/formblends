NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell in your body. It plays a central role in energy production, DNA repair, and cellular communication. As you age, your NAD+ levels decline, and that decline is linked to many of the symptoms people associate with getting older.

What Is NAD+ and Why Does It Matter?

NAD+ is essential for converting the food you eat into usable energy at the cellular level. It fuels your mitochondria, which are the power plants inside each of your cells. Beyond energy, NAD+ activates sirtuins, a family of proteins that regulate aging, inflammation, and stress resistance.

Research shows that NAD+ levels can decline by as much as 50% between the ages of 40 and 60. This drop is associated with fatigue, brain fog, slower recovery, weakened immune function, and accelerated aging at the cellular level.

How NAD+ Therapy Restores Levels

NAD+ therapy works by supplementing your body with NAD+ precursors or direct NAD+ formulations that raise your intracellular levels. This supports mitochondrial function, enhances DNA repair mechanisms, and helps your cells operate more efficiently. The result is often felt as improved energy, sharper thinking, and better overall resilience.

What the Research Says About NAD+ Therapy

The science behind NAD+ is growing rapidly. Key findings include:

  • NAD+ supplementation improved mitochondrial function and energy metabolism in multiple animal studies, with human trials showing promising early results.
  • Raising NAD+ levels has been shown to activate DNA repair pathways, potentially slowing aspects of cellular aging.
  • Studies on NAD+ precursors like NMN have demonstrated improvements in insulin sensitivity, cardiovascular function, and cognitive performance in early-phase human trials.

While research is still evolving, the existing evidence supports the biological rationale for NAD+ therapy, and many patients report significant subjective improvements in energy, mental clarity, and overall vitality.
