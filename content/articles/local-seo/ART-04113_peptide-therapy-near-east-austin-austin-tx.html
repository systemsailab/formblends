Frequently Asked Questions

Is peptide therapy the same as hormone therapy?

No. While both involve biological signaling molecules, peptides and hormones are different. Peptides are shorter chains of amino acids that trigger specific responses. Hormone therapy involves replacing or supplementing hormones like testosterone or estrogen. Our physicians can help clarify which approach is best for your situation.

How soon will I notice results from peptide therapy?

This depends on the specific peptide and your goals. Some patients report changes within the first two weeks, while others see gradual improvements over one to three months. Your provider will set realistic expectations during your consultation.

Do I need lab work before starting?

In some cases, your physician may request lab work to establish a baseline. This will be discussed during your consultation, and we can help coordinate labs near East Austin if needed.
