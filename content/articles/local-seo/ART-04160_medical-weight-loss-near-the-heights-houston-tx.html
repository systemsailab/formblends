Frequently Asked Questions

How is medical weight loss different from going to a nutritionist?

Nutritionists focus on food choices and meal planning. Medical weight loss involves a physician who can prescribe FDA-recognized medications, monitor your health through lab work, and adjust your treatment based on clinical data. It addresses the biological drivers of weight gain, not just dietary habits.

Generally, patients with a BMI of 27 or above who have struggled to lose weight through diet and exercise alone are good candidates. Your physician will evaluate your full health picture to make a recommendation.

How often will I have follow-up appointments?

Most patients have check-ins every four to six weeks, though this can vary based on your treatment plan. During the early phase of medication, your provider may want to connect more frequently to monitor how your body responds.
