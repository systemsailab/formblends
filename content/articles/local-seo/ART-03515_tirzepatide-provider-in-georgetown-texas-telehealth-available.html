What Is Tirzepatide?

Tirzepatide is a dual-action injectable medication that targets both the GLP-1 and GIP receptors. This dual approach sets it apart from single-action GLP-1 medications like semaglutide. By activating two hormonal pathways simultaneously, tirzepatide delivers powerful appetite suppression and improved metabolic function.

In the SURMOUNT clinical trial program, tirzepatide demonstrated weight loss of up to 22.5% of body weight over 72 weeks. This makes it one of the most effective pharmaceutical weight loss options currently available.

Eligibility for Tirzepatide

Tirzepatide is generally prescribed for adults who meet the following criteria:

  • BMI of 30 or higher
  • BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related comorbidity
  • No history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 syndrome
  • Not currently pregnant or planning pregnancy

Your provider will conduct a full review of your health status before prescribing.

What to Expect During Treatment

Tirzepatide treatment begins at a lower dose (typically 2.5 mg weekly) and gradually increases over several months. This slow titration helps your body adjust and reduces the likelihood of digestive side effects.

Common side effects in the early weeks may include nausea, reduced appetite, and mild gastrointestinal discomfort. These tend to lessen as treatment continues.
