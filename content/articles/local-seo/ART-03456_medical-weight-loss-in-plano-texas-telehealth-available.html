Frequently Asked Questions

What makes medical weight loss different from dieting?

Dieting relies on willpower to overcome biological hunger signals. Medical weight loss uses prescription medications that change those signals at a hormonal level, making it physically easier to eat less and lose weight .

Do I need to follow a specific diet while on medication?

There is no mandatory diet plan, but healthy eating habits will improve your results. Most patients naturally eat less while on GLP-1 medications and find it easier to make better food choices because cravings are significantly reduced.

How much weight can I realistically lose?

Clinical trials show average weight loss of 15 to 22 percent of body weight over 12 to 18 months, depending on the medication used. Individual results vary based on adherence, starting weight, and metabolic factors.

Is this safe for people with other health conditions?

GLP-1 medications actually improve several obesity-related conditions, including type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and sleep apnea. Your physician evaluates your full medical history and ensures the treatment is safe for your specific situation .
