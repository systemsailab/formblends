How Weight Loss Injections Work

Modern weight loss injections use GLP-1 receptor agonist technology to address the hormonal drivers of hunger and overeating. These are not appetite suppressant stimulants. They work by mimicking a natural gut hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1, which signals your brain to reduce hunger and tells your stomach to empty more slowly.

The result is a natural reduction in caloric intake without the jittery side effects associated with older weight loss drugs. Patients typically eat smaller meals and experience fewer cravings, making it easier to maintain a calorie deficit. how GLP-1 works

Self-Injection: What Georgetown Patients Need to Know

Weight loss injections are self-administered at home, which concerns some patients initially. The process is simpler than most people expect:

  • Injections use a very small, thin needle similar to those used for insulin
  • The injection site is typically the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm
  • Each injection takes less than a minute to prepare and administer
  • You inject once per week on the same day each week

Your Form Blends physician will walk you through proper technique during your consultation. Many patients are surprised at how quick and painless the process becomes after the first couple of doses.
