What Is Retatrutide?

Retatrutide is a triple-action hormone receptor agonist that targets GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors simultaneously {{CITE:Retatrutide is a triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon receptor agonist}}. This three-pronged approach sets it apart from current medications like {{INTERNAL_LINK:semaglutide}} (which targets GLP-1 alone) and {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide}} (which targets GIP and GLP-1). By adding glucagon receptor activation, retatrutide increases energy expenditure directly, essentially telling your body to burn more calories even at rest.

In Phase 2 clinical trials, participants taking the highest dose of retatrutide lost an average of 24.2% of their body weight over 48 weeks {{CITE:Jastreboff et al., NEJM 2023, retatrutide Phase 2 results}}. Some participants lost over 30%. Those numbers surpass anything seen with currently available GLP-1 medications.
