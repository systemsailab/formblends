About Semaglutide

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that has become one of the most prescribed weight loss medications in the country. It mimics the hormone GLP-1 to reduce appetite, slow digestion, and regulate blood sugar. In the landmark STEP clinical trial program, semaglutide produced average weight loss of approximately 15% of body weight over 68 weeks.

Originally developed for type 2 diabetes (branded as Ozempic), semaglutide was later approved specifically for weight management (branded as Wegovy). Who Qualifies for Semaglutide in Cedar Park

Semaglutide is prescribed for adults meeting these criteria:

  • BMI of 30 or higher
  • BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related health condition (hypertension, type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol, sleep apnea)
  • Prior unsuccessful weight loss efforts through lifestyle changes

Patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, MEN2 syndrome, or who are pregnant or breastfeeding are not candidates.

Side Effects and What to Expect

Semaglutide treatment begins at a low dose (0.25 mg weekly) and increases every four weeks. The most common side effects during the early weeks include:

  • Nausea (usually mild and temporary)
  • Decreased appetite
  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Mild stomach discomfort

These side effects typically improve as your body adjusts. Your Form Blends physician will manage your titration to minimize discomfort.

Most Cedar Park patients begin noticing appetite changes within the first two weeks and meaningful weight loss within four to eight weeks of starting treatment.
