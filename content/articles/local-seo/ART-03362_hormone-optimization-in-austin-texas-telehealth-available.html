What Is Hormone Optimization?

Hormone optimization is a medical approach to identifying and correcting hormonal imbalances that affect your quality of life. It goes beyond simply treating a deficiency. The goal is to bring your hormone levels into the range where you function and feel your best. {{CITE:Hormone optimization vs. replacement therapy, Endocrine Society guidelines}}

This is different from traditional hormone replacement therapy (HRT), which typically focuses on replacing a single hormone that has dropped below clinical thresholds. Optimization looks at the full hormonal picture, including testosterone, estrogen, progesterone, thyroid hormones, cortisol, DHEA, and growth hormone markers, and addresses imbalances that may not trigger a diagnosis but still impact how you feel day to day.

Common Signs of Hormonal Imbalance

  • Persistent fatigue that does not improve with sleep
  • Difficulty losing weight despite consistent effort
  • Reduced muscle mass or strength
  • Low libido or sexual dysfunction
  • Brain fog, poor concentration, or memory issues
  • Mood swings, irritability, or anxiety
  • Poor sleep quality or insomnia
  • Hair thinning or skin changes

If several of these sound familiar, your hormones may be part of the equation. A thorough evaluation can clarify what is going on.
