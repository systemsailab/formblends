What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists are a class of injectable medications that have become the leading pharmaceutical option for medical weight loss. They work by mimicking a natural hormone that regulates appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves blood sugar control. {{CITE:GLP-1 receptor agonist overview}}

The result is a significant and sustained reduction in hunger, which allows patients to reduce caloric intake without the constant battle against cravings. Clinical trials have documented weight loss of 15% to 22.5% of body weight, depending on the specific medication. {{CITE:GLP-1 weight loss range}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 efficacy claims}}

What to Expect from GLP-1 Weight Loss

Treatment starts with a low dose that increases gradually over several weeks. This titration period helps your body adjust and minimizes common side effects. Here is a typical progression:

  • Weeks 1-4: Starting dose. Appetite reduction begins. Some patients experience mild nausea that typically fades.
  • Weeks 5-12: Dose increases. Weight loss becomes measurable, usually 1-2 pounds per week.
  • Months 3-6: Target dose reached. Most significant weight loss occurs during this phase.
  • Months 6+: Continued weight loss or maintenance, depending on goals.

Eligibility and Safety

GLP-1 medications are appropriate for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related health condition. Contraindications include personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, MEN2 syndrome, and pregnancy. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 eligibility and contraindications}}

Common side effects during dose titration include nausea, constipation, and mild stomach discomfort. These typically improve as treatment continues. Your physician monitors you throughout. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 side effects}}
