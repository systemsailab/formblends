How does tirzepatide compare to semaglutide?

Both are highly effective injectable weight loss medications. Tirzepatide targets two receptors (GIP and GLP-1) versus semaglutide's single receptor target. In comparative studies, tirzepatide produced modestly greater average weight loss, though individual responses vary .

What are the common side effects?

Nausea, diarrhea, decreased appetite, and occasional constipation are most frequently reported, especially during dose escalation. These effects usually subside as your body adjusts. Your physician can modify your titration if needed.

How long does treatment typically last?

Most patients stay on tirzepatide for at least six months. Many continue for a year or longer. Your doctor works with you on a long-term plan that includes reaching your goal and maintaining results.

Is tirzepatide available to anyone?

Tirzepatide is generally prescribed for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher, or 27 with at least one weight-related condition. Your physician reviews your complete health profile to determine eligibility.
