The Science Behind NAD+

NAD+ sits at the center of your cellular metabolism. It is required for your mitochondria to convert nutrients into ATP, the energy currency your cells run on. Beyond energy production, NAD+ activates a class of proteins called sirtuins that regulate inflammation, stress response, and the aging process itself. {{CITE:NAD+ and sirtuin biology, Cell Metabolism}}

By age 50, most adults have roughly half the NAD+ levels they had at 20. {{CITE:Age-related NAD+ decline, Nature Reviews Molecular Cell Biology}} This decline contributes to many symptoms that people attribute to "just getting older," including chronic fatigue, cognitive slowing, poor sleep, weakened immunity, and increased susceptibility to metabolic disorders.

How NAD+ Therapy Helps

NAD+ therapy works by restoring your intracellular NAD+ levels through direct supplementation or precursor compounds. When NAD+ levels are replenished, your cells can produce energy more efficiently, repair damaged DNA more effectively, and maintain the signaling pathways that keep your body functioning well.

Patients commonly report improvements in:

  • Sustained energy throughout the day
  • Mental clarity and focus
  • Sleep quality and consistency
  • Exercise recovery and physical endurance
  • Overall sense of vitality and wellbeing

What the Research Shows

NAD+ research has accelerated significantly over the past decade. Key findings include:

  • NAD+ supplementation restored mitochondrial function in aged mice to levels comparable to young mice. {{CITE:NAD+ and mitochondrial rejuvenation, Science}}
  • Human clinical trials on NMN showed improved insulin sensitivity and muscle function in older adults. {{CITE:NMN clinical trial results, Science Translational Medicine}}
  • NAD+ replenishment activated DNA repair pathways and improved cellular stress resistance in multiple preclinical studies. {{CITE:NAD+ DNA repair studies, Cell Reports}}
  • Sirtuin activation through NAD+ has been linked to improved cardiovascular health, neuroprotection, and metabolic function. {{CITE:Sirtuins and NAD+ in cardiovascular health, Circulation Research}}

While the field is still maturing, the biological rationale for NAD+ therapy is well supported, and patient-reported outcomes consistently align with the research.
