How GLP-1 Medications Work

GLP-1 receptor agonists mimic a hormone that your body produces naturally after meals. This hormone signals fullness to your brain and helps regulate blood sugar. The medications amplify this signal, creating a stronger and more sustained feeling of satisfaction after eating.

The effects go beyond just feeling full. Patients consistently report that their relationship with food changes in ways they did not expect. The mental chatter about what to eat next, the cravings that hit at 3 PM or after dinner, the urge to finish everything on the plate even when you are no longer hungry, all of that quiets down. It is not that food becomes uninteresting. It just stops being the thing your brain circles back to every twenty minutes.

Clinical research shows that these medications produce average weight loss of 15% or more of body weight. Many patients see results exceeding 20%. For someone weighing 260 pounds, that could mean losing 39 to 52 pounds. Combined with improvements in blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol, the health transformation can be significant.

What Treatment Looks Like

GLP-1 treatment starts with a lower dose that increases gradually over several weeks. This careful approach, called titration, gives your body time to adjust to the medication and minimizes side effects. The most common side effect is nausea, which is typically mild and tends to resolve as your body adapts.

Most patients notice changes in appetite within the first two to three weeks. Meaningful weight loss usually becomes visible within the first month. Over the full course of treatment, results continue to build. Your provider monitors everything and adjusts your plan based on how your body responds.

For the best results, GLP-1 medication should be paired with reasonable lifestyle adjustments. That does not mean cutting out everything you love. It means making slightly better choices more often. Walking the Beltline instead of driving. Choosing grilled over fried when it feels right. Small changes that add up over time, supported by a medication that makes those choices feel natural rather than forced.

Are You a Candidate?

GLP-1 medications are generally recommended for adults with a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or above with a weight-related health condition such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or high cholesterol. Your provider will review your full medical profile during your telehealth consultation to determine eligibility.

Certain medical conditions, including a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2, are contraindications for GLP-1 treatment. Your provider will discuss all relevant factors with you.
