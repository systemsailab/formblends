Why Medical Weight Loss Works

Medical weight loss addresses the biological factors that make sustained weight loss difficult for many people. Hormonal signals, metabolic adaptation, and genetic factors all play a role in how your body regulates weight. {{CITE:biology of weight regulation}} When diet and exercise alone fall short, it is often because these biological systems are working against your efforts.

GLP-1 receptor agonist medications target the hormonal pathways that drive hunger and satiety, giving patients a meaningful advantage. Clinical trials have documented weight loss ranging from 15% to 22.5% of body weight, far exceeding what most patients achieve through lifestyle changes alone. {{CITE:GLP-1 vs lifestyle-only weight loss}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:medical weight loss rationale}}

Who Qualifies for Medical Weight Loss

The Form Blends program is designed for adults who meet clinical criteria for pharmaceutical weight management:

  • BMI of 30 or above (clinical obesity)
  • BMI of 27 or above with a weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, or sleep apnea
  • History of unsuccessful weight loss through diet and exercise

Contraindications include pregnancy, breastfeeding, personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, and MEN2 syndrome. Your physician screens for all relevant factors before prescribing. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:medical weight loss eligibility}}

Clinical data shows average weight loss of 15% to 22.5% of body weight depending on the medication. Individual results depend on medication response, adherence, and lifestyle choices. {{CITE:expected weight loss ranges}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:setting weight loss expectations}}

Is medical weight loss safe?

GLP-1 medications have been studied in large clinical trials and have well-documented safety profiles. Side effects are generally mild and temporary. Physician supervision throughout treatment ensures your safety. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 safety}}

How long does the program last?

Do I need to follow a strict diet?

No mandatory diet is required. GLP-1 medications naturally reduce appetite, which helps most patients eat less without strict meal plans. Your provider may suggest general nutritional guidelines to maximize results. {{INTERNAL_LINK:nutrition tips}}
