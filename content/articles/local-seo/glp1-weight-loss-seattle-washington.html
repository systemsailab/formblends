How GLP-1 Medications Work

GLP-1 receptor agonists like semaglutide and tirzepatide work by mimicking a natural hormone involved in appetite regulation. When you eat, your body produces GLP-1 to signal fullness. These medications enhance that signal, making it stronger and longer-lasting. The practical result is less hunger, fewer cravings, and a natural reduction in how much you eat.

What sets GLP-1 medications apart from previous weight loss approaches is the quality of the evidence behind them. Clinical trials involving thousands of participants have consistently shown average weight loss of 15% to over 20% of body weight. That is not a marginal improvement. For someone weighing 230 pounds, it translates to losing 35 to 46 pounds or more.

The experience patients describe is not one of deprivation. It is more like a reset. The obsessive thoughts about food ease up. Meals become something you enjoy without the anxiety of overeating. You can sit down to dinner at a restaurant in Capitol Hill or Ballard and feel satisfied with a normal portion, without the internal battle that used to follow every bite.

What Treatment Looks Like

GLP-1 treatment follows a gradual dosing schedule. You begin at a low dose that increases over several weeks, allowing your body to adjust and reducing the risk of side effects. The most common side effect is nausea, which is typically mild and fades as your system adapts to the medication.

Changes in appetite usually become noticeable within the first two weeks. Weight loss follows within the first month and continues to build throughout the course of treatment. Your provider monitors your progress closely and adjusts the plan based on how you are responding.

The medication works best alongside reasonable adjustments to diet and activity. Seattle offers plenty of options for staying active, from the Burke-Gilman Trail to the stairs at Kerry Park to simply walking the hills that define so many neighborhoods. Even during the rainy months, indoor options abound. The key is finding what works for you and letting the medication handle the appetite management that makes everything else easier.

Is GLP-1 Treatment Right for You?

GLP-1 medications are generally recommended for adults with a BMI of 30 or above, or those with a BMI of 27 or higher who have a weight-related health condition like type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol. Your provider will assess your individual health profile during your consultation to confirm that this treatment is safe and appropriate for you.

Certain conditions, including a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2, make GLP-1 medications unsuitable. Your provider will review all relevant factors during your evaluation.
