What Is CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 is a modified version of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH), the natural signal your brain sends to the pituitary gland to produce growth hormone (GH). As you age, your body produces less GHRH, which leads to declining GH levels. By age 40, most adults have lost 50 percent or more of their youthful GH output. CJC-1295 restores that signal.

There are two forms you will encounter:

  • CJC-1295 with DAC: Has a Drug Affinity Complex that extends its half-life to 6 to 8 days. This produces a sustained elevation in GH levels.
  • CJC-1295 without DAC (Modified GRF 1-29): Has a shorter half-life and produces more natural, pulsatile GH release. This is the version more commonly used in clinical practice.

Both versions work. Your provider will recommend the right one based on your goals and health profile. cjc-1295 benefits

How CJC-1295 Is Administered

CJC-1295 is administered as a subcutaneous injection, meaning a small needle is inserted just under the skin. This is the same type of injection used for insulin. Common injection sites include the lower abdomen, outer thigh, or upper arm. cjc-1295 how to inject

The injection itself takes about 30 seconds, and most patients report minimal discomfort once they get used to the process. Your provider or pharmacy will walk you through the technique during your initial consultation.

Typical Beginner Protocol

While your provider will customize your dosing, a common starting protocol for CJC-1295 without DAC looks like this:

  • Dose: 100 to 300 mcg per injection
  • Frequency: Once daily, typically before bed
  • Duration: Initial cycles of 8 to 12 weeks, followed by reassessment
  • Combination: Often paired with ipamorelin (100 to 300 mcg) for synergistic GH release

Side Effects to Know About

CJC-1295 is generally well-tolerated. Common side effects, especially in the first few weeks, include:

  • Injection site redness or itching (usually resolves within minutes)
  • Mild water retention
  • Occasional headache
  • Tingling or numbness in hands or feet (a sign of GH elevation)
  • Increased hunger
