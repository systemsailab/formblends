NAD+ Before And After: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: People who undergo NAD+ therapy commonly report improvements in energy, mental clarity, mood, and physical endurance. Some effects are noticeable within hours of an IV infusion, while subcutaneous and oral protocols may take days to weeks. Individual responses vary, and controlled long-term human data is still accumulating. NAD+ therapy is not FDA-approved for any medical condition .

What Is NAD+?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It is essential for converting food into cellular energy, repairing damaged DNA, and activating sirtuins, a family of proteins that regulate aging and stress responses .

NAD+ levels decline significantly with age. This decline has been connected to reduced mitochondrial function, accumulated cellular damage, and many of the physical and cognitive changes associated with aging . The premise of NAD+ therapy is straightforward: restore what time has depleted.

This guide covers what people typically experience before and after starting NAD+ therapy, what the research shows in terms of measurable changes, and how to set realistic expectations.

Before NAD+ Therapy: Common Starting Points

People who seek out NAD+ therapy often share a common set of concerns. Understanding where you are starting from helps frame what to look for as therapy progresses.

Common Reasons People Start NAD+ Therapy

  • Persistent fatigue: Feeling tired despite adequate sleep, often described as a deeper cellular exhaustion rather than simple sleepiness.
  • Brain fog: Difficulty concentrating, slower mental processing, or feeling less sharp than in previous years.
  • Reduced physical performance: Decreased endurance, slower recovery from workouts, or a sense that the body does not bounce back like it used to.
  • Accelerated aging concerns: Noticing age-related changes in skin, energy, sleep quality, or overall resilience.
  • Metabolic changes: Weight gain that seems resistant to diet and exercise, or shifts in blood sugar and cholesterol markers.
  • Poor sleep quality: Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking feeling unrested.

These experiences align with what researchers observe in the context of declining NAD+ levels. Mitochondrial dysfunction, which NAD+ depletion contributes to, can manifest in exactly these ways .

After NAD+ Therapy: What People Report

Anecdotal reports and early clinical data suggest several categories of improvement after NAD+ therapy. It is important to note that individual experiences vary and these reports do not constitute guaranteed outcomes.

Energy and Vitality

Improved energy is the most consistently reported change. Many people describe it not as a stimulant-like burst but as a restoration of baseline vitality. They feel more like themselves, with energy that sustains through the day rather than peaking and crashing.

With IV infusions, some people notice this shift within hours of their first session. With subcutaneous injections, the change tends to build over the first 1 to 2 weeks of consistent use. Oral precursors may take 2 to 4 weeks for noticeable effects.

Mental Clarity and Focus

Improved cognitive function is another frequently reported benefit. People describe clearer thinking, better focus, and improved ability to sustain attention on complex tasks. Some report that the "fog" they had grown accustomed to simply lifts.

This aligns with preclinical research showing that NAD+ restoration improves neuronal energy metabolism and reduces neuroinflammation in animal models .

Sleep Quality

Many people report improvements in sleep quality after beginning NAD+ therapy. This may be related to NAD+'s role in regulating circadian rhythm through sirtuin-mediated pathways. SIRT1, activated by NAD+, plays a role in the molecular clock that governs sleep-wake cycles .

Reports include falling asleep more easily, fewer nighttime awakenings, and waking feeling more rested. These changes are typically noticed within the first 1 to 3 weeks.

Physical Performance and Recovery

Athletes and active individuals often report improved workout endurance and faster recovery after starting NAD+ therapy. This is consistent with research showing that NAD+ supports mitochondrial function in skeletal muscle, which directly impacts exercise capacity .

Some people notice reduced muscle soreness, faster return to baseline between training sessions, and improved overall physical resilience. These effects are typically observed after 2 to 4 weeks of consistent therapy.

Mood and Emotional Wellbeing

Some individuals report improvements in mood and overall sense of wellbeing. While NAD+ is not a treatment for mood disorders, its role in cellular energy production and neurotransmitter metabolism may contribute to a general sense of improved emotional resilience.

Skin and Appearance

Some people report improvements in skin quality, including better hydration, improved texture, and a healthier overall appearance. NAD+ supports DNA repair in skin cells and may influence the cellular processes that contribute to skin aging .

What the Research Measures

Beyond subjective reports, clinical studies have measured objective changes associated with NAD+ restoration:

Blood NAD+ Levels

Clinical trials have confirmed that both oral precursors and injectable NAD+ effectively raise circulating NAD+ levels in humans. A study of NMN supplementation showed significant increases in blood NAD+ metabolites within weeks of starting treatment .

Insulin Sensitivity

A 2021 clinical trial in postmenopausal women found that NMN supplementation improved skeletal muscle insulin sensitivity, a marker of metabolic health .

Physical Function

Studies in older adults have shown improvements in gait speed, grip strength, and other measures of physical function with NAD+ precursor supplementation, though results have been mixed across different trials .

Vascular Health

A clinical trial of NR demonstrated improvements in blood pressure and aortic stiffness in older adults, suggesting benefits for cardiovascular health .

Realistic Timeline of Changes

Setting appropriate expectations is important. Here is a general timeline based on clinical observations and anecdotal reports:

IV NAD+ Infusion

  • During and immediately after: Some people report feeling a noticeable energy shift within hours. Others feel fatigued on the day of infusion and energized the following day.
  • After 2 to 4 sessions: Most people report consistent improvements in energy, clarity, and overall wellbeing.
  • After loading phase: Transitioning to a maintenance protocol helps sustain benefits.

Subcutaneous NAD+ Injection

  • Week 1: Some individuals notice subtle improvements in energy. Others may not feel significant changes yet.
  • Weeks 2 to 3: Most people begin noticing consistent improvements in energy and cognitive function.
  • Week 4 and beyond: Benefits tend to stabilize and become part of a new baseline.

Oral NAD+ Precursors (NMN, NR)

  • Weeks 1 to 2: Minimal noticeable changes for most people.
  • Weeks 3 to 4: Gradual improvements in energy and wellbeing begin to emerge.
  • Months 2 to 3: Cumulative benefits become more apparent, including potential improvements in measurable biomarkers.

Factors That Affect Your Results

  • Starting NAD+ levels: Individuals with more severely depleted NAD+ may notice more dramatic improvements.
  • Age: Older individuals, who typically have lower baseline NAD+ levels, may experience more noticeable benefits.
  • Delivery method: IV infusion provides the fastest and most bioavailable delivery. Subcutaneous and oral routes are effective but work on a different timeline.
  • Lifestyle factors: Sleep quality, exercise, diet, and stress management all influence how well NAD+ therapy works. NAD+ supports cellular function, but it works best as part of a comprehensive health approach.
  • Consistency: Regular, consistent dosing produces better results than sporadic use.

Safety and Side Effects

NAD+ therapy has demonstrated a favorable safety profile in clinical studies. Side effects are generally mild and include flushing, nausea, chest tightness (with IV infusion), and injection site discomfort .

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly will I feel results from NAD+ therapy?

With IV infusion, some people notice improvements within hours. With subcutaneous injections, most people report consistent benefits within 2 to 3 weeks. Oral precursors typically take 3 to 4 weeks or longer. Individual responses vary.

What is the first thing people notice after starting NAD+?

Improved energy and mental clarity are the most commonly reported initial benefits. Many people describe it as a return to a more youthful baseline rather than a stimulant-like effect.

Will I feel worse before I feel better?

Some people experience mild fatigue or flu-like symptoms during the first day or two of therapy, particularly after IV infusions. This is generally temporary and resolves quickly. If symptoms persist, contact your physician.

How long do the benefits of NAD+ therapy last?

Benefits are maintained with ongoing therapy. If you stop NAD+ supplementation, NAD+ levels will gradually return to their pre-treatment baseline over time. Your physician can help you establish a maintenance protocol.

Can I measure my NAD+ levels before and after therapy?

Some labs offer NAD+ level testing through blood draws. While not yet standard clinical practice, measuring NAD+ levels before and during therapy can help confirm that your protocol is effectively raising your levels. Ask your physician about testing options.
