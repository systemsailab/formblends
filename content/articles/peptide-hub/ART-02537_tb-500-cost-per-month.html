What Factors Affect the Monthly Cost of TB-500?

Several variables influence what you will pay each month for TB-500 therapy:

  • Dosage: During the loading phase, weekly doses are typically higher (5 to 10 mg per week), which uses more peptide and increases cost. Once you move to a maintenance dose (2 to 4 mg per week), the monthly expense often drops
  • Provider type: Prices vary between telehealth platforms, compounding pharmacies, and in-office clinics. Telehealth providers like FormBlends often offer competitive pricing because overhead costs are lower
  • Pharmacy sourcing: TB-500 is produced by compounding pharmacies, and prices differ based on the pharmacy's location, quality standards, and supply chain
  • Included services: Some providers charge separately for consultations, follow-up visits, and supplies like syringes and bacteriostatic water. Others bundle everything into a single monthly price

When comparing prices, make sure you are comparing similar packages. A lower sticker price from one provider might not include the medical supervision that a slightly higher price from another provider does.

How Much Does TB-500 Cost During the Loading Phase?

The loading phase is the most expensive part of a TB-500 cycle because you are using more peptide per week. If your physician prescribes 5 mg per week for four weeks, you will need 20 mg total for the loading phase. At current market rates, this could run anywhere from $150 to $350 depending on your provider and pharmacy .

Some patients on higher loading protocols (up to 10 mg per week) may see costs closer to $300 to $500 for the loading phase alone. However, higher doses are not always necessary and your physician will prescribe only what your situation requires.

How Much Does TB-500 Cost During Maintenance?

Once you transition to maintenance dosing, the cost drops noticeably. At 2 to 4 mg per week, your monthly TB-500 expense typically falls between $75 and $200. This is often the phase where patients feel they are getting the best value, since benefits continue while the financial commitment decreases.

If your physician recommends cycling TB-500 with rest periods between cycles, you will have months where the peptide cost is zero, which further reduces your average monthly expense over the course of a year.

Does Insurance Cover TB-500?

In most cases, health insurance does not cover TB-500 or other compounded peptide therapies. TB-500 is not FDA-approved as a pharmaceutical drug, which means it falls outside the coverage policies of most insurance plans . This makes TB-500 an out-of-pocket expense for the vast majority of patients.

Some patients use Health Savings Accounts (HSA) or Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA) to pay for peptide therapy. Whether your HSA or FSA will cover TB-500 depends on your specific plan and how the expense is classified. We recommend checking with your plan administrator to find out if this is an option for you.

Is TB-500 Worth the Cost?

The value of TB-500 depends on your individual situation. Patients dealing with chronic tendon injuries, slow-healing wounds, or persistent joint pain often find that the cost of TB-500 is reasonable compared to alternatives like repeated cortisone injections, physical therapy sessions, or surgical intervention. When you consider that a single orthopedic consultation can cost $200 to $500, a few months of TB-500 therapy may be a cost-effective part of your recovery plan.

Athletes and active individuals who use TB-500 for recovery support often view it as an investment in their ability to train consistently without being sidelined by injuries. The cost of downtime from training or competition can far exceed the price of a TB-500 cycle.
