Sermorelin vs Tesamorelin: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: Sermorelin vs Tesamorelin compares a two GHRH analogs that stimulate growth hormone through the same receptor. Both are GHRH receptor agonists, but Tesamorelin is FDA-approved specifically for HIV-associated lipodystrophy and is particularly effective at reducing visceral abdominal fat. Sermorelin was previously FDA-approved for GH deficiency diagnosis but its brand was discontinued. Tesamorelin has stronger clinical evidence for visceral fat reduction .

Can You Use Both?

Yes. Sermorelin and Tesamorelin work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both stimulate natural gh production and gh stimulation. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.
