CJC-1295/Ipamorelin Dosage Guide: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: The standard CJC-1295/Ipamorelin dosage is 100-300 mcg of each peptide administered via subcutaneous injection, typically once daily before bed. Most physicians prescribe a combined formulation with a standard dose of 200-300 mcg CJC-1295 and 200-300 mcg Ipamorelin per injection. Treatment cycles typically run 3 to 6 months, followed by a 1 to 2 month break {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:CJC-1295/Ipamorelin standard dosing protocol}}.

Why Bedtime Dosing?

Growth hormone is naturally released in its largest pulse during the first 90 minutes of deep sleep. Injecting CJC-1295/Ipamorelin 30 to 60 minutes before bed amplifies this natural GH pulse, working with your body's circadian rhythm rather than against it {{CITE:Largest natural GH pulse occurs during first 90 minutes of deep sleep}}.

Additionally, the mild drowsiness some users experience becomes a benefit when timed with bedtime.

Empty Stomach Requirement

Insulin suppresses GH release. Inject CJC-1295/Ipamorelin at least 2 hours after your last meal and at least 30 minutes before eating anything. A post-dinner injection window (8-10 PM for most people) typically satisfies this requirement {{CITE:Insulin suppresses GH release; inject 2+ hours after meals}}.

Combined vs Separate Formulations

Many compounding pharmacies offer CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin as a pre-combined formulation in a single vial. This simplifies dosing to a single injection. Some physicians prefer separate vials for more flexible dose adjustment. Both approaches are effective {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Combined vs separate CJC-1295/Ipamorelin formulations}}.

Dose Adjustment Signs

  • Water retention or tingling: Consider reducing to 200 mcg or less until symptoms resolve, then gradually increase
  • No noticeable effects after 4 weeks: Verify injection technique, timing, and fasting status before increasing dose
  • Increased hunger: Normal with Ipamorelin; manage with protein-rich snacks if needed
  • Blood sugar changes (diabetic patients): GH antagonizes insulin; may require diabetes medication adjustment under physician supervision

Cycling Protocol

Most practitioners recommend 3 to 6 months on followed by 1 to 2 months off. This allows the pituitary to maintain its own GH production capacity and prevents potential desensitization. Some physicians allow continuous use at lower doses for anti-aging, with periodic lab monitoring of IGF-1 and metabolic markers {{CITE:CJC-1295/Ipamorelin cycling prevents pituitary desensitization}}.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to inject CJC-1295/Ipamorelin?

30 to 60 minutes before bed, at least 2 hours after your last meal. This aligns with natural GH rhythms and maximizes the deep-sleep GH pulse.

Can I take CJC-1295/Ipamorelin twice a day?

Some aggressive protocols include a morning injection (upon waking, before breakfast) in addition to the bedtime dose. This increases total GH output but also side effects. Discuss with your physician before adding a second daily dose.

How long can I use CJC-1295/Ipamorelin?

Standard cycles are 3 to 6 months. Many patients do multiple cycles per year with breaks in between. Long-term use is generally well-tolerated with appropriate cycling and monitoring.

Does body weight affect dosing?

Standard dosing is fixed (200-300 mcg) rather than weight-based. Individual response varies more by age and existing GH levels than body weight. Your physician will adjust based on your response and IGF-1 labs.
