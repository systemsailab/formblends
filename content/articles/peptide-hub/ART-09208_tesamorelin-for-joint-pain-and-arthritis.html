Tesamorelin for Joint Pain and Arthritis

Tesamorelin for joint pain and arthritis relates to how this peptide's anti-inflammatory and metabolic properties may ease joint discomfort and support cartilage health. While Tesamorelin has not been specifically studied for arthritis, its ability to reduce systemic inflammation and improve cellular energy production are relevant to joint health.

Joint Health and Cellular Energy

Cartilage cells (chondrocytes) depend on mitochondria for the energy needed to maintain the extracellular matrix. In osteoarthritis, mitochondrial dysfunction in chondrocytes is well-documented {{CITE:Mitochondrial dysfunction in chondrocytes is a documented feature of osteoarthritis}}.

Tesamorelin may help by:

  • Supporting cellular energy production in joint tissue
  • Reducing inflammatory cytokines that drive cartilage breakdown
  • Improving metabolic health, which reduces overall disease burden on joints
