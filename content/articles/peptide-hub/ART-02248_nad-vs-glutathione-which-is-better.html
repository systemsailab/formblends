NAD+ vs Glutathione: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: NAD+ vs Glutathione compares a coenzyme (not a peptide) with a tripeptide antioxidant. NAD+ is used for cellular energy, dna repair, sirtuin activation. Glutathione is used for antioxidant defense, detoxification. These compounds serve different purposes and your choice depends on your primary health goal. They can often be used together since they work through independent mechanisms {{CITE:NAD+ and Glutathione serve different therapeutic purposes}}.

Can You Use Both?

Yes. NAD+ and Glutathione work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both cellular energy and antioxidant defense. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has stronger evidence?

NAD+ has extensive biochemical; growing clinical. Glutathione has extensive biochemical and clinical. The strength of evidence depends on the specific application you are considering.

Which should I start with?

Start with whichever addresses your most pressing health concern. If you need cellular energy, start with NAD+. If you need antioxidant defense, start with Glutathione. A physician can help you prioritize.
