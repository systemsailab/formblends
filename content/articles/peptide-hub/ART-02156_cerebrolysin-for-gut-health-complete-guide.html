The Gut-Brain Connection

The gut-brain axis is a bidirectional communication system between the central nervous system and the enteric nervous system (the "second brain" in your gut). In theory, improving brain health could positively influence gut function through vagal nerve signaling and neurotransmitter regulation. However, this indirect connection does not make Cerebrolysin a gut treatment.

  • Cerebrolysin's neurotrophic factors target central nervous system neurons, not enteric neurons
  • No clinical or preclinical studies have examined Cerebrolysin for GI conditions
  • The gut-brain axis is better addressed through direct gut-targeting compounds
