Is Sermorelin Legal in the US?

Yes, sermorelin is legal in the United States when obtained through a licensed healthcare provider with a valid prescription. It is classified as a prescription medication, not a controlled substance, making it accessible for patients who meet clinical criteria for growth hormone deficiency or related conditions. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:sermorelin legal status and prescription classification in the US}}

Understanding Sermorelin's Legal Classification

Sermorelin acetate is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog that contains the first 29 amino acids of the naturally occurring GHRH molecule. Unlike synthetic human growth hormone (HGH), which carries tighter federal restrictions under the 1990 Anabolic Steroids Control Act, sermorelin occupies a different regulatory space. {{CITE:sermorelin contains first 29 amino acids of GHRH}}

We often hear from clients who confuse sermorelin with HGH. The distinction matters legally. Federal law restricts the distribution of HGH to specific FDA-approved indications. Sermorelin, by contrast, works by stimulating the pituitary gland to produce its own growth hormone rather than introducing exogenous hormone directly. This mechanism places it outside the strict distribution controls that apply to HGH. {{INTERNAL_LINK:sermorelin vs HGH}}

How Sermorelin Is Dispensed

Once prescribed, sermorelin is typically dispensed through compounding pharmacies. This is an important detail. The original branded product, Geref Diagnostic, was discontinued by the manufacturer in 2008 for commercial reasons, not safety concerns. {{CITE:Geref Diagnostic discontinued in 2008}} Today, compounding pharmacies prepare sermorelin under the oversight of state pharmacy boards and must comply with United States Pharmacopeia (USP) standards.

Licensed compounding pharmacies that prepare sermorelin must hold valid state licenses and follow Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP) or equivalent standards. We always encourage patients to verify that their pharmacy is properly accredited. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:compounding pharmacy standards for peptide preparation}}

What Is Not Legal

Purchasing sermorelin without a prescription, importing it from overseas suppliers, or buying it from unregulated online sources labeled "for research purposes only" falls outside the boundaries of legal use for human consumption. These products may not meet purity or potency standards, and using them carries both legal and health risks. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:risks of unregulated peptide sourcing}}

Research-grade peptides sold by chemical supply companies are intended for laboratory use. They are not manufactured under pharmaceutical-grade conditions and are not approved for human injection. We strongly advise against using these products.

The Difference Between Sermorelin and Controlled Substances

Sermorelin is not listed on any schedule of the Controlled Substances Act. This means it does not carry the same prescribing restrictions, record-keeping requirements, or penalties for misuse that apply to Schedule II through V drugs. Physicians can prescribe it without the DEA registration requirements associated with controlled substances. {{CITE:sermorelin is not a scheduled controlled substance}}
