Pentosan Polysulfate For Wound Healing: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: Pentosan Polysulfate for wound healing is not a primary application. While GAG molecules play a role in wound healing scaffolding, PPS has not been studied for dermal or surgical wound healing. Its mild anticoagulant effect could theoretically impair wound hemostasis. For wound healing, {{INTERNAL_LINK:Thymosin Beta-4 for wound healing}} (cell migration, angiogenesis) and {{INTERNAL_LINK:BPC-157 for wound healing}} (growth factors) are established wound healing peptides with strong evidence {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:PPS and wound healing}}.

Caution: Anticoagulant Effect

PPS has mild blood-thinning properties. In the context of wound healing, this could potentially increase bleeding at wound sites. If you are using PPS and planning surgery, inform your surgeon well in advance {{CITE:PPS mild anticoagulant effect may increase bleeding at wound sites}}.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use PPS for wound healing?

No. Use TB-4 or BPC-157 for wound healing. PPS may actually impair wound hemostasis due to its anticoagulant properties.

Should I stop PPS before surgery?

Discuss with your surgeon. Given PPS's mild anticoagulant effect, your surgeon may recommend stopping 1-2 weeks before planned procedures.
