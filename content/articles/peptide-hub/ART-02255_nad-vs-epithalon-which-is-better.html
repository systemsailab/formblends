NAD+ vs Epithalon: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: NAD+ vs Epithalon compares a coenzyme (not a peptide) with a telomerase-activating peptide. NAD+ is used for cellular energy, dna repair, sirtuin activation. Epithalon is used for telomere extension, anti-aging. These compounds serve different purposes and your choice depends on your primary health goal. They can often be used together since they work through independent mechanisms .

Can You Use Both?

Yes. NAD+ and Epithalon work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both cellular energy and telomere extension. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has stronger evidence?

NAD+ has extensive biochemical; growing clinical. Epithalon has preclinical + limited clinical (khavinson research). The strength of evidence depends on the specific application you are considering.

Which should I start with?

Start with whichever addresses your most pressing health concern. If you need cellular energy, start with NAD+. If you need telomere extension, start with Epithalon. A physician can help you prioritize.
