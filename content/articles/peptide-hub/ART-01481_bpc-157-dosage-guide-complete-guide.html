What Is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic 15-amino-acid peptide derived from a protein found in human gastric juice {{CITE:BPC-157 is a 15-amino-acid synthetic peptide from human gastric juice}}. It has been studied in hundreds of preclinical experiments for its potential to support tissue repair, gut health, and recovery. How BPC-157 Dosing Works in Research

In the scientific literature, BPC-157 doses are typically reported in micrograms per kilogram of body weight (mcg/kg). The most commonly studied dose in rodent models is 10 mcg/kg, though doses ranging from 1 mcg/kg to 50 mcg/kg have been used across different studies {{CITE:BPC-157 doses in rodent studies range from 1 mcg/kg to 50 mcg/kg, with 10 mcg/kg being most common}}.

Translating animal doses directly to human doses is not straightforward. Body surface area scaling, metabolic rate differences, and bioavailability all play a role. This is why we strongly recommend working with a physician rather than attempting to calculate your own dose from research papers.

Dosing by Administration Route

Subcutaneous Injection

This is the most common method of BPC-157 administration. Subcutaneous injection delivers the peptide just beneath the skin, where it enters the bloodstream relatively quickly. Most dosing protocols for subcutaneous use fall in the 250-500 mcg range per injection, once or twice daily.

Many practitioners recommend injecting as close to the injury site as possible for localized effects. For example, an individual addressing knee pain might inject subcutaneously near the knee. For systemic effects, the abdomen is a common injection site.

Cycling and Duration

Most physician-supervised BPC-157 protocols involve defined cycles rather than continuous, indefinite use. Common cycling patterns include:

  • 4-week cycle: Often used for mild issues or general wellness support. A 4-week course followed by a 2-week break allows assessment of results.
  • 6-8 week cycle: The most commonly discussed protocol for moderate concerns such as joint pain, gut healing, or muscle recovery.
  • 8-12 week cycle: Sometimes used for more significant injuries or chronic conditions, always under close medical supervision.

After completing a cycle, many physicians recommend a break of 2 to 4 weeks before reassessing whether another cycle is warranted. This approach allows the body to consolidate healing gains and helps your physician evaluate the ongoing need for treatment.

There is no established research on the safety or efficacy of long-term, uninterrupted BPC-157 use in humans. Cycling is considered the prudent approach until more data is available.

Timing and Frequency

The research does not clearly establish an optimal time of day for BPC-157 administration. However, common practices include:

  • Once daily dosing: Typically administered in the morning. This is the simplest protocol and may be sufficient for lower-dose applications.
  • Twice daily dosing: Morning and evening injections, splitting the total daily dose into two. This approach maintains more consistent peptide levels and is often recommended for more significant therapeutic targets.
  • Pre-workout or post-workout: Some individuals time their dose around training sessions. There is no strong research evidence favoring one timing over the other, but some practitioners suggest pre-workout administration may prime the healing response.

For oral administration, taking BPC-157 on an empty stomach (at least 30 minutes before a meal) is generally recommended to maximize absorption.

How to Measure BPC-157 Accurately

Accurate dosing is critical. BPC-157 typically comes as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder that must be reconstituted with bacteriostatic water before use. Here is the general process:

  1. Check the vial label: Know the total amount of BPC-157 in the vial (commonly 5 mg or 10 mg).
  2. Add bacteriostatic water: The amount of water you add determines the concentration. For example, adding 2 mL of bacteriostatic water to a 5 mg vial creates a concentration of 2.5 mg/mL (2,500 mcg/mL).
  3. Calculate your dose: If your target dose is 250 mcg and your concentration is 2,500 mcg/mL, you need 0.1 mL (or 10 units on a standard insulin syringe).
  4. Use an insulin syringe: These syringes allow precise measurement of small volumes.

How often should I take BPC-157?

Most protocols involve once or twice daily administration. Once daily is common for lower doses and general wellness, while twice daily (splitting the total dose) is often used for more targeted therapeutic goals. Your physician will advise on the best frequency.

Can I take too much BPC-157?

For injectable forms, food timing is not a significant concern. For oral BPC-157, taking it on an empty stomach (at least 30 minutes before eating) is generally recommended for better absorption. If you experience nausea, a small amount of food may help.

How long should a BPC-157 cycle last?

Most protocols range from 4 to 12 weeks, followed by a break. The duration depends on what you are addressing and how you respond. Your physician will recommend an appropriate cycle length and reassess at the end of each cycle.
