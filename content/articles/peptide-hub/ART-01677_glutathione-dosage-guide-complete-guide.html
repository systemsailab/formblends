What Is Glutathione?

Glutathione (GSH) is a tripeptide antioxidant composed of glutamine, cysteine, and glycine. It is produced naturally in every cell and serves as the body's primary defense against oxidative stress, while also playing critical roles in detoxification and immune function {{CITE:Glutathione is the body's primary defense against oxidative stress}}.

Because glutathione levels decline with age and environmental stressors, supplementation has become a popular approach in longevity and wellness medicine {{CITE:Glutathione levels decline with age and environmental stressors}}. However, dosing is not one-size-fits-all. The optimal dose depends on the delivery method, your baseline glutathione status, your health goals, and any concurrent medical conditions.

This guide covers the dosage ranges that have been used in clinical research and practice. All dosing should be determined by a licensed physician based on your individual needs.

Glutathione Dosage by Administration Route

IV Glutathione Dosage

Intravenous administration delivers glutathione directly into the bloodstream, bypassing digestive absorption for maximum bioavailability. IV glutathione is typically administered in a clinical setting or under physician supervision.

Clinical dosage ranges:

  • General wellness and antioxidant support: 600 mg to 1,200 mg per session, administered 1 to 2 times per week {{CITE:IV glutathione for general wellness typically ranges from 600 to 1200 mg per session}}
  • Higher-dose protocols: 1,200 mg to 2,400 mg per session have been used in research settings for specific conditions {{CITE:Higher-dose IV glutathione protocols up to 2400 mg have been used in research}}
  • Neurological research protocols: Doses of 1,400 mg IV have been studied in Parkinson's disease research, administered 2 to 3 times per week {{CITE:1400 mg IV glutathione has been studied in Parkinson's disease research}}

IV infusions typically take 15 to 30 minutes. Your physician will determine the appropriate infusion rate, as overly rapid administration can cause temporary flushing or nausea.

Subcutaneous Injection Dosage

Subcutaneous injection is the most practical option for at-home glutathione therapy. It offers better bioavailability than oral forms while avoiding the need for IV access.

Clinical dosage ranges:

  • Standard dose: 200 mg to 400 mg per injection, administered 2 to 3 times per week {{CITE:Subcutaneous glutathione standard dose is 200 to 400 mg, 2 to 3 times per week}}
  • Higher protocols: Up to 600 mg per injection have been used, depending on physician assessment
  • Concentration: Glutathione for injection is commonly available in concentrations of 200 mg/mL

Oral Glutathione Dosage

Oral glutathione has historically been limited by poor bioavailability, as the tripeptide can be broken down in the digestive tract before absorption. However, newer formulations, including liposomal and S-acetyl glutathione, have shown improved absorption {{CITE:Liposomal and S-acetyl glutathione formulations show improved oral absorption}}.

Clinical dosage ranges:

  • Standard oral dose: 250 mg to 500 mg daily {{CITE:Standard oral glutathione dose ranges from 250 to 500 mg daily}}
  • Research doses: Clinical trials have used up to 1,000 mg per day of oral glutathione for periods of up to 6 months {{CITE:Clinical trials have used up to 1000 mg daily oral glutathione for up to 6 months}}
  • Liposomal forms: Liposomal glutathione at doses of 500 mg to 1,000 mg has demonstrated measurable increases in blood glutathione levels {{CITE:Liposomal glutathione at 500 to 1000 mg demonstrated increased blood glutathione levels}}

Dosage Considerations by Goal

General Antioxidant Support

For broad antioxidant and wellness support, physicians commonly start with moderate doses: 200 to 400 mg subcutaneously 2 to 3 times per week, or 600 to 1,200 mg IV once to twice weekly. Oral supplementation at 250 to 500 mg daily may serve as a maintenance approach between injectable sessions.

Skin Health

Clinical studies examining glutathione's effects on skin have used oral doses of 250 mg to 500 mg daily for 4 to 12 weeks {{CITE:Skin health studies used 250 to 500 mg oral glutathione daily for 4 to 12 weeks}}. Injectable protocols for skin health goals may use 200 to 600 mg subcutaneously 2 to 3 times per week. Results typically begin to appear after 4 to 8 weeks of consistent use. Starting Low and Building Up

Most physicians recommend starting at the lower end of the dosage range and gradually increasing based on tolerance and response. This approach allows your body to adjust and helps identify the minimum effective dose for your goals.

A typical starting protocol might look like:

  • Week 1 to 2: 200 mg subcutaneously, 2 times per week
  • Week 3 to 4: 300 mg subcutaneously, 2 to 3 times per week
  • Week 5 onward: Adjust based on response and physician guidance

Your physician may also recommend periodic lab work to assess glutathione levels and overall oxidative stress markers, which can help guide dose optimization.

Factors That Influence Optimal Dosage

Several factors affect how much glutathione you may need:

  • Age: Older adults typically have lower baseline glutathione levels and may benefit from higher or more frequent dosing {{CITE:Older adults have lower baseline glutathione levels}}
  • Oxidative stress load: Individuals with high toxin exposure, chronic inflammation, or intense exercise regimens may require higher doses
  • Genetics: Polymorphisms in glutathione-related genes (GSTM1, GSTT1, GSTP1) can influence how efficiently your body produces and recycles glutathione {{CITE:Polymorphisms in GST genes influence glutathione production and recycling}}
  • Diet: Adequate intake of cysteine, glycine, and selenium supports endogenous glutathione production and may reduce supplementation needs
  • Concurrent medications: Certain drugs deplete glutathione, potentially increasing supplementation requirements

Safety and Side Effects

Glutathione has demonstrated a favorable safety profile across the dosage ranges described above. Clinical trials using up to 1,000 mg of oral glutathione daily for 6 months have reported no serious adverse effects {{CITE:Clinical trials using up to 1000 mg oral glutathione daily for 6 months reported no serious adverse effects}}.

Common side effects at any dose are mild and may include injection site discomfort, mild headache, or digestive upset. Higher doses, particularly via IV, may increase the likelihood of flushing or nausea, which can usually be managed by adjusting the infusion rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best dose of glutathione for beginners?

Most physicians recommend starting with 200 mg subcutaneously 2 times per week or 250 mg orally daily. This allows you to assess tolerance before increasing. Your physician will personalize this starting point based on your health history.

How often should I take glutathione?

Frequency depends on the route and your goals. Injectable glutathione is commonly used 2 to 3 times per week. Oral supplementation is typically daily. IV sessions are usually weekly or biweekly. Your physician will determine the appropriate schedule.

Can I take too much glutathione?

While glutathione is generally well tolerated even at higher doses, taking more than your body needs is unlikely to provide additional benefit and may increase the chance of mild side effects. Your physician can help you find the minimum effective dose for your goals.
