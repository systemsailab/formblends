Is Glutathione FDA Approved?

Glutathione is not FDA approved as a drug for any specific medical condition. However, it is legally available as a dietary supplement and as a physician-prescribed compounded medication. The FDA recognizes glutathione as a naturally occurring substance, and it has a well-documented safety profile in clinical research . Understanding the regulatory landscape helps you make informed decisions about using it.

Detailed Answer

The FDA approval question comes up often, and it deserves a clear explanation because the answer has important nuance.

FDA approval is a specific process where a pharmaceutical company submits clinical trial data proving a drug is safe and effective for a particular condition. The FDA then reviews that data and either approves or denies the drug for that specific use. This process typically costs hundreds of millions of dollars .

Glutathione has not gone through this process for a simple reason: it is a naturally occurring tripeptide that your body already produces. Natural substances cannot be patented in the same way synthetic drugs can, which means pharmaceutical companies have little financial incentive to fund the approval process.

How Glutathione Is Regulated

  • As a supplement: Oral glutathione is regulated under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA) of 1994. Manufacturers must follow Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) but do not need pre-market FDA approval .
  • As a compounded medication: Injectable glutathione is prepared by licensed compounding pharmacies under the oversight of state boards of pharmacy and, in some cases, FDA section 503A or 503B regulations .
  • As a food substance: Glutathione is Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) as a food ingredient by the FDA.

The lack of specific drug approval does not mean glutathione is unregulated or unsafe. It simply means no company has pursued the formal drug approval pathway for it.
