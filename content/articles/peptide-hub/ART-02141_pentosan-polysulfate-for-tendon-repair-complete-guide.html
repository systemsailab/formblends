Pentosan Polysulfate For Tendon Repair: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: Pentosan Polysulfate for tendon repair has some relevance through its effects on connective tissue extracellular matrix. PPS supports GAG production and inhibits matrix-degrading enzymes that affect not only cartilage but also tendons and ligaments. Veterinary use of PPS for equine tendon injuries shows some positive outcomes. However, for direct tendon healing, BPC-157 for tendon repair and Thymosin Beta-4 for tendon repair offer more targeted and effective tendon-specific repair mechanisms .

PPS and Connective Tissue

Tendons share extracellular matrix components with cartilage, including proteoglycans and GAGs. PPS's enzyme inhibition and GAG-supportive effects may provide modest benefits to tendon tissue, particularly in preventing further matrix degradation in chronic tendinopathy.

Veterinary protocols sometimes include PPS alongside other treatments for equine tendon injuries, though its role is primarily supportive rather than primary .
