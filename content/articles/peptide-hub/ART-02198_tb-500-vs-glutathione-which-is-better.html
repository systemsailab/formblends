What Is TB-500?

TB-500 is a synthetic peptide derived from the active region of thymosin beta-4, a protein that drives cell migration and wound healing. It upregulates actin, promotes angiogenesis, and reduces inflammation to accelerate tissue repair across muscles, tendons, ligaments, and other connective tissues. {{CITE:TB-500 overview}}

What Is Glutathione?

Glutathione is a tripeptide composed of three amino acids: glutamine, cysteine, and glycine. It is produced naturally in every cell of the body and is the most abundant intracellular antioxidant. Glutathione plays critical roles in neutralizing reactive oxygen species (ROS), recycling other antioxidants like vitamins C and E, supporting liver detoxification, and maintaining immune function. {{CITE:Glutathione biological roles}}

Glutathione levels decline with age, chronic illness, poor nutrition, and environmental toxin exposure. Supplementation is available through IV infusion, liposomal oral forms, and precursors like N-acetyl cysteine (NAC). {{CITE:Glutathione depletion causes}}

Can You Stack TB-500 and Glutathione?

TB-500 and Glutathione are complementary. TB-500 handles structural tissue repair while Glutathione provides the antioxidant protection needed to support healing and reduce oxidative damage. Combining the two can create a synergistic recovery environment. This is a common pairing in integrative medicine settings. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:TB-500 Glutathione stacking}}

Side Effects and Safety

TB-500 side effects: Generally well-tolerated. Injection site reactions, temporary fatigue, and lightheadedness are most commonly reported. {{CITE:TB-500 safety}}

Glutathione side effects: IV glutathione can cause bloating, cramping, and allergic reactions in rare cases. Oral and liposomal forms are well-tolerated. Long-term high-dose IV use has raised theoretical concerns about zinc depletion. {{CITE:Glutathione safety profile}}

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Glutathione a peptide?

Technically, yes. Glutathione is a tripeptide made of three amino acids (glutamine, cysteine, glycine). However, it functions primarily as an antioxidant and detoxification agent rather than as a signaling peptide like TB-500. It is more commonly categorized as a supplement than a therapeutic peptide.

Can Glutathione help with injury healing?

Glutathione supports healing indirectly by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation at injury sites. However, it does not directly promote cell migration or tissue remodeling the way TB-500 does. For targeted injury repair, TB-500 is the more effective option.

What is the best way to take Glutathione?

IV infusion provides the highest bioavailability. Liposomal oral glutathione is the next best option for those who prefer not to use IVs. Standard oral glutathione has poor absorption due to degradation in the digestive tract. NAC (N-acetyl cysteine) is an effective oral precursor that the body converts to glutathione. {{CITE:Glutathione administration methods}}

Does TB-500 have antioxidant properties?

TB-500 has anti-inflammatory properties but is not classified as an antioxidant. It does not neutralize free radicals or support detoxification pathways the way Glutathione does. For antioxidant protection, Glutathione is the appropriate choice.

How often should you get Glutathione IV therapy?

IV Glutathione is typically administered weekly or biweekly, though some protocols call for more frequent sessions during acute illness or detoxification. Maintenance dosing of once or twice monthly is common for general wellness. A healthcare provider should determine the appropriate frequency based on individual needs.

The Bottom Line

TB-500 and Glutathione serve very different purposes. TB-500 is the best choice for targeted tissue healing and injury recovery. Glutathione is the best choice for antioxidant defense, detoxification, and immune support. They are highly complementary and can be used together to support comprehensive recovery and cellular health. Consult a healthcare provider to determine which suits your needs. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:final recommendation}}
