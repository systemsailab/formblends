CJC-1295/Ipamorelin For Inflammation: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin for inflammation works indirectly through IGF-1's documented anti-inflammatory properties. IGF-1 suppresses NF-kB signaling, reduces pro-inflammatory cytokines, and promotes tissue repair that resolves inflammatory states. The enhanced deep sleep from GH peptides also reduces systemic inflammation, as poor sleep is a major inflammatory driver. For targeted anti-inflammatory therapy, dedicated peptides like BPC-157 have more direct evidence .

How GH/IGF-1 Affect Inflammation

  • IGF-1 anti-inflammatory signaling: IGF-1 activates the PI3K/Akt pathway, which suppresses NF-kB and reduces production of TNF-alpha, IL-6, and other inflammatory mediators
  • Tissue repair: GH accelerates healing of damaged tissues, resolving the inflammatory stimulus at its source
  • Sleep-mediated anti-inflammation: Deep sleep reduces cortisol and inflammatory markers. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin's sleep enhancement indirectly reduces systemic inflammation
  • Immune modulation: GH and IGF-1 support immune cell function without promoting excessive inflammatory responses

Clinical Context

GH-deficient adults show elevated inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6) that improve with GH replacement . This suggests that age-related GH decline may contribute to the chronic low-grade inflammation (inflammaging) that drives many age-related diseases.

CJC-1295/Ipamorelin, by restoring more youthful GH and IGF-1 levels, may help attenuate this inflammatory burden. However, no clinical trial has specifically studied CJC-1295/Ipamorelin as an anti-inflammatory intervention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CJC-1295/Ipamorelin anti-inflammatory?

Indirectly, yes. IGF-1 has anti-inflammatory properties and improved sleep reduces inflammatory markers. But it is not primarily an anti-inflammatory peptide.

Can CJC-1295/Ipamorelin help with autoimmune inflammation?

GH and IGF-1 have complex effects on the immune system. Some autoimmune conditions may benefit from GH optimization, while others could theoretically worsen. Discuss with your physician, especially if you have an autoimmune diagnosis.

Should I use CJC-1295/Ipamorelin or BPC-157 for inflammation?

For targeted anti-inflammatory effects, BPC-157 has more direct evidence. For systemic inflammation associated with aging and GH decline, CJC-1295/Ipamorelin addresses the root hormonal cause. Many practitioners use both.
