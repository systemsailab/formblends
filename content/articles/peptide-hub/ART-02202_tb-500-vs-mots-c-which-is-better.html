TB-500 vs MOTS-c: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: TB-500 vs MOTS-c compares a tissue healing peptide with a mitochondrial-derived metabolic peptide. TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4) promotes wound healing, cell migration, and tissue repair. MOTS-c is a mitochondrial peptide that improves metabolic function, insulin sensitivity, and exercise performance by activating AMPK pathways. TB-500 is for physical healing and recovery; MOTS-c is for metabolic optimization and exercise enhancement. They address different aspects of health and can be combined .

When to Choose Each

  • TB-500: Active injury recovery, wound healing, post-surgical repair, cardiac tissue support
  • MOTS-c: Metabolic optimization, improving insulin sensitivity, enhancing exercise performance, metabolic syndrome support
  • Both: Athletes who need injury recovery (TB-500) while also optimizing metabolic performance (MOTS-c)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for athletes?

It depends on the need. An injured athlete benefits more from TB-500 for tissue repair. A healthy athlete seeking performance enhancement benefits more from MOTS-c for metabolic optimization. Many athletes use both in different phases of training.

Is MOTS-c well-studied?

MOTS-c research is newer than TB-500 research. It was first described in 2015 and has promising preclinical data on metabolic regulation, but human clinical trial data is still emerging. TB-500 has a longer research history with more extensive preclinical evidence for tissue repair .
