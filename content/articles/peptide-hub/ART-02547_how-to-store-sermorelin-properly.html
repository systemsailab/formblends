How To Store Sermorelin Properly?

Store unreconstituted sermorelin in a refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius). After reconstitution with bacteriostatic water, keep it refrigerated and use within 4 to 6 weeks. Never freeze reconstituted sermorelin, and always protect vials from light and heat.

Before Reconstitution: Storing Lyophilized Sermorelin

When your sermorelin arrives from the pharmacy, it will likely be in lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder form. In this state, the peptide is relatively stable, but proper storage still matters for maintaining potency.

  • Refrigeration is preferred: Keep the vial in the refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit. This is the optimal range for peptide stability.
  • Room temperature is acceptable short-term: Lyophilized sermorelin can tolerate room temperature (up to 77 degrees Fahrenheit) for short periods during shipping or handling without significant degradation.
  • Avoid heat exposure: Do not leave vials in a car, near a window, or anywhere temperatures exceed 77 degrees Fahrenheit. Heat accelerates peptide degradation.
  • Protect from light: UV exposure can break down peptide bonds. Store vials in their original packaging or in a dark area of the refrigerator.

Reconstitution: Mixing Your Sermorelin

Before you can inject sermorelin, you need to reconstitute the lyophilized powder with bacteriostatic water. This step is straightforward but requires care:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly.
  2. Wipe the tops of both the sermorelin vial and the bacteriostatic water vial with an alcohol swab.
  3. Draw the prescribed amount of bacteriostatic water into a syringe.
  4. Insert the needle into the sermorelin vial and slowly release the water down the inside wall of the vial. Do not spray it directly onto the powder.
  5. Let the water dissolve the powder gently. You can swirl the vial slowly, but do not shake it. Shaking can damage the peptide molecules.
  6. Once fully dissolved, the solution should be clear. If it appears cloudy or contains particles, do not use it.

After Reconstitution: Refrigeration Is Non-Negotiable

Once reconstituted, sermorelin becomes significantly more fragile. The solution must be refrigerated at all times when not in use. Here are the key rules:

  • Always refrigerate: Return the vial to the refrigerator immediately after drawing your dose.
  • Use within 4 to 6 weeks: Most compounding pharmacies assign a beyond-use date of 28 to 42 days after reconstitution. Follow the date specified by your pharmacy.
  • Never freeze: Freezing reconstituted sermorelin can cause the peptide to denature and lose effectiveness. The ice crystal formation physically disrupts the molecular structure.
  • Keep the vial upright: This minimizes contact between the rubber stopper and the solution, reducing potential contamination.
  • Avoid repeated temperature cycling: Taking the vial out of the fridge and letting it warm up, then putting it back, accelerates degradation. Draw your dose efficiently and return the vial promptly.

Common Storage Mistakes

We see a few recurring errors that compromise sermorelin potency:

Leaving Vials on the Counter

It takes only a couple of hours at room temperature to begin affecting a reconstituted peptide solution. If you accidentally leave it out overnight, the potency may be significantly reduced. When in doubt, discard the vial and start fresh.

Storing Near the Freezer Compartment

Some refrigerators have cold spots near the back wall or close to the freezer section where temperatures can drop below freezing. Place your vial on a middle shelf, away from the coldest zones.

Using a Mini-Fridge Without Temperature Control

Inexpensive mini-fridges often have inconsistent temperatures and may freeze items or fail to stay cold enough. If you use a mini-fridge, place a thermometer inside and verify it maintains 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit consistently.

Signs Your Sermorelin Has Degraded

If you notice any of the following, discard the vial and contact your pharmacy:

  • The solution appears cloudy or discolored
  • Visible particles or floating matter in the solution
  • The vial has been left at room temperature for more than 4 hours
  • The beyond-use date has passed
  • The rubber stopper appears damaged or the seal is compromised

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I freeze sermorelin to make it last longer?

You can freeze lyophilized (unreconstituted) sermorelin for long-term storage, though refrigeration is usually sufficient. Never freeze reconstituted sermorelin, as ice crystals will damage the peptide structure and reduce potency.

What happens if my sermorelin gets warm during shipping?

Lyophilized sermorelin can tolerate brief temperature excursions during shipping without significant loss. If the cold packs in your package are still cool upon arrival, the product is likely fine. Contact your pharmacy if you have concerns.

How do I know if my sermorelin is still good?

A properly stored reconstituted solution should remain clear and colorless. If it is cloudy, discolored, or contains particles, it may have degraded. Also check the beyond-use date assigned by your pharmacy.

Can I store sermorelin in a bathroom medicine cabinet?

No. Bathrooms are subject to heat and humidity fluctuations from showers and baths. Always store sermorelin in the refrigerator, which provides a stable, cool, dark environment.
