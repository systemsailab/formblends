GHK-Cu vs NAD+: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: GHK-Cu vs NAD+ compares a collagen-stimulating copper peptide with a cellular energy coenzyme. GHK-Cu directly stimulates collagen, elastin, and tissue remodeling for skin and wound healing. NAD+ supports mitochondrial energy, DNA repair, and sirtuin activation for cellular health. GHK-Cu targets tissue structure; NAD+ targets cellular function. Both are anti-aging but through completely different mechanisms .

Can You Use Both?

Yes. GHK-Cu and NAD+ work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both collagen stimulation and cellular energy. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has stronger evidence?

GHK-Cu has preclinical + clinical (topical). NAD+ has extensive biochemical; growing clinical. The strength of evidence depends on the specific application you are considering.

Which should I start with?

Start with whichever addresses your most pressing health concern. If you need collagen stimulation, start with GHK-Cu. If you need cellular energy, start with NAD+. A physician can help you prioritize.
