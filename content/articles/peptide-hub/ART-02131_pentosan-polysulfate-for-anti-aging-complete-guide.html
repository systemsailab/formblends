Joint Health as Anti-Aging

Joint deterioration is one of the most impactful aspects of aging. Loss of cartilage leads to pain, reduced mobility, decreased exercise capacity, weight gain, and loss of independence. PPS addresses this by:

  • Protecting remaining cartilage from progressive destruction
  • Restoring synovial fluid quality for better joint lubrication
  • Reducing joint inflammation that accelerates cartilage loss
  • Improving subchondral bone health

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should I consider PPS for joint protection?

Adults over 40 with early signs of joint stiffness, pain, or family history of osteoarthritis may benefit from proactive PPS therapy. Earlier intervention provides better outcomes.

Is PPS better than glucosamine for anti-aging joint support?

PPS has stronger disease-modifying evidence than glucosamine. PPS actively inhibits cartilage-destroying enzymes, while glucosamine provides building blocks. They can be used together.
