Storing Unreconstituted (Powder) Sermorelin

Sermorelin arrives from compounding pharmacies as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder in a sealed vial. In this form, the peptide is relatively stable because the removal of water during the freeze-drying process slows degradation reactions. {{CITE:Lyophilization stability benefits for peptides}}

Best Practices for Powder Storage

  • Refrigerate if possible: While lyophilized Sermorelin can tolerate room temperature, refrigeration extends its shelf life and adds a margin of safety.
  • Avoid heat: Never store Sermorelin near a window, on a countertop that gets warm, or anywhere the temperature routinely exceeds 77°F (25°C).
  • Protect from light: UV light can degrade peptides over time. Store vials in their original packaging or in a drawer/box that blocks light.
  • Keep dry: Moisture can compromise the lyophilized powder. Do not store vials in areas with high humidity, like a bathroom.
  • Check the expiration date: Lyophilized peptides have manufacturer-assigned expiration dates. Do not use expired product.

Can You Freeze Unreconstituted Sermorelin?

Freezing lyophilized Sermorelin is generally acceptable and can extend shelf life. The powder form is already freeze-dried, so additional freezing does not introduce the same risks as freezing a liquid solution. If you have purchased extra vials and do not plan to use them for several months, storing them in the freezer is a reasonable option. {{CITE:Freezing stability of lyophilized peptides}}

Storing Reconstituted Sermorelin

Once you add bacteriostatic water to your Sermorelin powder, the clock starts ticking. The reconstituted solution is far more susceptible to degradation than the dry powder form. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Sermorelin how to reconstitute}}

Refrigeration Is Mandatory

Reconstituted Sermorelin must be kept between 36-46°F (2-8°C). The standard refrigerator setting works perfectly. Place the vial toward the back of the refrigerator, where the temperature is most consistent. Avoid storing it in the door, as the temperature fluctuates there every time the door opens.

How Long Does Reconstituted Sermorelin Last?

When properly refrigerated and reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, Sermorelin typically maintains potency for 28-30 days. After this period, the peptide may begin to degrade, and potency can decrease gradually. {{CITE:Reconstituted peptide stability timeline with bacteriostatic water}}

If you reconstituted with sterile water (which lacks the benzyl alcohol preservative), use the entire vial within 24 hours. Without preservative, bacterial growth can begin rapidly in a punctured vial. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Sterile water single-use requirement for reconstituted peptides}}

Do Not Freeze Reconstituted Sermorelin

Freezing a reconstituted peptide solution can damage the peptide through ice crystal formation, which disrupts the molecular structure. Additionally, the freeze-thaw cycle can cause aggregation, where peptide molecules clump together and lose their biological activity. If you need to store Sermorelin for longer than 30 days, keep it in lyophilized form until you are ready to use it. {{CITE:Freeze-thaw damage to peptide solutions}}

Signs Your Sermorelin May Have Degraded

Degraded Sermorelin will not deliver the intended therapeutic benefit and could potentially cause unexpected reactions. Here are warning signs to watch for:

  • Cloudiness: A properly reconstituted solution should be clear and colorless. Any cloudiness or haziness suggests contamination or degradation.
  • Visible particles: Floating specks, fibers, or clumps in the solution indicate a problem. Do not inject.
  • Discoloration: Yellow, brown, or any color change from the original clear appearance is a red flag.
  • Unusual odor: While Sermorelin solution is essentially odorless, a foul or unusual smell could indicate bacterial contamination.
  • Reduced efficacy: If you have been experiencing consistent benefits and suddenly notice they are fading without any change in your protocol, degraded peptide could be the cause.

When in doubt, discard the vial and use a fresh one. The cost of a replacement vial is far less than the risk of injecting a compromised product. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:When to discard peptide vials}}

Traveling With Sermorelin

Traveling does not mean you have to pause your Sermorelin therapy. With some planning, you can keep your peptide safe and effective on the road.

Short Trips (1-3 Days)

  • Use an insulated cooler bag with an ice pack. Gel-based ice packs are preferable to loose ice, which can cause temperature swings as it melts.
  • Do not let the vial freeze. Place a barrier (a small towel or paper towel) between the ice pack and the vial.
  • Bring pre-loaded syringes only if your physician approves this approach. Pre-loaded syringes should be used within a few hours.

Longer Trips (4+ Days)

  • Consider bringing an unreconstituted vial and bacteriostatic water separately. Reconstitute at your destination where you have refrigerator access.
  • A medical-grade travel cooler with temperature monitoring is ideal for trips longer than a week.
  • If flying, keep your medication in your carry-on bag. Checked luggage can be exposed to extreme temperatures in the cargo hold.

Air Travel Tips

  • Carry a copy of your prescription or a letter from your prescribing physician.
  • Syringes and injectable medications are permitted through TSA when accompanied by a prescription label.
  • Declare your medication at the security checkpoint if asked.
  • Pack supplies in a clear, labeled bag for easy inspection.

Storage Mistakes That Waste Your Money

Sermorelin is an investment in your health, and improper storage is the fastest way to lose that investment. Here are the most common storage mistakes we see:

  • Leaving reconstituted vials on the counter: Even 30 minutes at room temperature accelerates degradation. Return the vial to the refrigerator immediately after drawing your dose.
  • Storing in the refrigerator door: Temperature swings from opening and closing the door can compromise peptide stability over time.
  • Exposing to direct sunlight: A vial left near a window or on a sunny countertop, even briefly, can lose potency.
  • Reconstituting too much at once: If your dosing schedule means a vial will last longer than 30 days, consider using a smaller vial size or splitting the powder across two reconstitution sessions (if your pharmacy provides appropriate vials).
  • Not checking the expiration date: Lyophilized peptides have finite shelf lives. Using expired product means reduced or zero potency.

Storage FAQ

Can I store Sermorelin at room temperature?

Unreconstituted (powder) Sermorelin can be stored at room temperature for shorter periods, though refrigeration is preferred. Reconstituted Sermorelin must always be refrigerated.

What happens if my Sermorelin was left out of the fridge overnight?

If reconstituted Sermorelin was left at room temperature for several hours, some potency loss is likely. For a single overnight incident, the peptide may still be partially active, but we recommend discarding it and using a fresh vial to ensure proper dosing. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Reconstituted Sermorelin room temperature exposure risk}}

How should I dispose of expired or degraded Sermorelin?

Do not pour liquid medications down the drain or flush them. Check with your local pharmacy for a medication take-back program, or use an FDA-recommended disposal method such as mixing with coffee grounds or kitty litter in a sealed bag before placing in household trash. Dispose of needles in a sharps container.

My Sermorelin arrived warm during shipping. Is it still good?

If the peptide was shipped in lyophilized (powder) form, brief exposure to warm temperatures during transit is unlikely to cause significant degradation. However, if it arrived reconstituted and warm, contact your pharmacy for a replacement. Many pharmacies include temperature indicators in their shipments. {{CITE:Peptide shipping temperature stability}}

Can I store my Sermorelin vial lying on its side?

Upright storage is preferred for reconstituted vials to minimize the solution's contact with the rubber stopper. However, brief periods on the side (such as during transport in a cooler bag) are unlikely to cause problems.

Does the type of bacteriostatic water affect storage life?

As long as the bacteriostatic water contains the standard 0.9% benzyl alcohol concentration and is from a sealed, sterile source, the storage life should be consistent regardless of brand. Always use pharmaceutical-grade bacteriostatic water. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Sermorelin how to reconstitute}}
