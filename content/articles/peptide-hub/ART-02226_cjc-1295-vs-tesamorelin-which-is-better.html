CJC-1295 vs Tesamorelin: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: CJC-1295 vs Tesamorelin compares a two GHRH analogs that work through the same receptor. Both stimulate pituitary GH release, but Tesamorelin is FDA-approved specifically for HIV-associated lipodystrophy and has strong clinical evidence for visceral fat reduction. CJC-1295 (especially with DAC) has a longer half-life and is more commonly used in general wellness protocols with Ipamorelin {{CITE:CJC-1295 and Tesamorelin serve different therapeutic purposes}}.

Can You Use Both?

Yes. CJC-1295 and Tesamorelin work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both extended gh stimulation and gh stimulation. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.
