How GH Drives Fat Loss

Growth hormone is one of the body's primary fat-burning hormones. It acts through several distinct mechanisms:

1. Direct Lipolysis

GH binds to receptors on adipocytes (fat cells) and activates hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL), the enzyme that breaks stored triglycerides into free fatty acids and glycerol. This is why GH pulses during sleep and fasting are associated with fat mobilization {{CITE:GH activates hormone-sensitive lipase in adipocytes for triglyceride breakdown}}.

2. Fatty Acid Oxidation

GH increases the rate at which released fatty acids are burned for energy in muscle and liver tissue. This prevents mobilized fat from being re-stored and ensures it is actually used as fuel {{CITE:GH increases fatty acid oxidation in muscle and liver tissue}}.

3. Visceral Fat Targeting

GH preferentially reduces visceral fat (the deep abdominal fat surrounding organs) over subcutaneous fat. Visceral fat is the most metabolically dangerous type, linked to insulin resistance, cardiovascular disease, and systemic inflammation. Studies show GH therapy reduces visceral adipose tissue by 10-20% over 6 to 12 months {{CITE:GH therapy reduces visceral adipose tissue by 10-20% over 6-12 months}}.

4. Insulin Sensitivity Effects

GH has a complex relationship with insulin. Short-term, GH can temporarily reduce insulin sensitivity. Long-term, the reduction in visceral fat and improvement in body composition typically results in net improved metabolic health. Your physician should monitor fasting glucose and HbA1c during therapy.

5. Lean Mass Preservation

Unlike caloric restriction alone, which often causes muscle loss alongside fat loss, GH-supported fat loss tends to preserve or increase lean muscle mass. This creates the "recomposition" effect many patients describe {{CITE:GH-supported fat loss preserves lean muscle mass during caloric restriction}}.

Maximizing Fat Loss Results

  • Timing: Inject before bed on an empty stomach. Fasting enhances GH release and fat mobilization during sleep
  • Diet: A moderate caloric deficit amplifies results. GH makes fat loss more efficient but does not override energy balance
  • Resistance training: Exercise stimulates additional GH release and takes advantage of GH's anabolic effects on muscle
  • Sleep: Prioritize 7-9 hours. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin enhances deep sleep, which is when the bulk of GH-mediated fat burning occurs
  • Avoid eating 2-3 hours before injection: Elevated insulin from recent meals blunts GH release
