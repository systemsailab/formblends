How To Store CJC-1295 Properly

Reconstituted CJC-1295 must be stored in the refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius) and used within 4 to 6 weeks. Unreconstituted lyophilized powder is more stable and can be stored at room temperature short-term, but refrigeration extends its shelf life significantly. Proper storage protects peptide integrity and ensures you get the full therapeutic benefit.

Storage Before Reconstitution

When CJC-1295 arrives from your compounding pharmacy, it is typically in lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder form. In this state, it is relatively stable:

  • Room temperature (68 to 77 degrees F): Stable for several weeks. Acceptable for short-term storage or shipping.
  • Refrigerated (36 to 46 degrees F): Stable for months. This is the recommended storage condition before reconstitution.
  • Frozen (below 32 degrees F): Can extend shelf life further for long-term storage. Acceptable for unreconstituted powder but avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

Keep the vial in its original packaging and away from direct light. UV exposure can degrade peptides even in lyophilized form.

Storage After Reconstitution

Once you add bacteriostatic water to the lyophilized powder, the clock starts. Reconstituted CJC-1295 is much more sensitive to temperature and must be refrigerated immediately.

Key Rules for Reconstituted CJC-1295

  • Always refrigerate. Store between 36 and 46 degrees F (2 to 8 degrees C). The main body of your refrigerator is fine, but avoid the door shelves where temperature fluctuates.
  • Use within 4 to 6 weeks when mixed with bacteriostatic water. The bacteriostatic agent (benzyl alcohol) helps prevent microbial growth.
  • Use within 5 to 7 days if mixed with plain sterile water, which lacks a preservative.
  • Never freeze reconstituted CJC-1295. Freezing can damage the peptide bonds and reduce potency.
  • Protect from light. Keep the vial in its box or wrap it in foil if stored outside packaging.

Signs of Degraded CJC-1295

Knowing how to spot degradation helps you avoid injecting compromised product:

  • Cloudiness or particles: Reconstituted CJC-1295 should be clear and colorless. Any cloudiness, floating particles, or discoloration indicates contamination or degradation.
  • Changed color: A yellowish tint suggests breakdown. Discard the vial.
  • Unusual smell: While CJC-1295 is nearly odorless, any strong or unusual smell indicates a problem.
  • Reduced effectiveness: If you notice a drop-off in the benefits you were experiencing (sleep quality, energy) without any other explanation, the peptide may have lost potency due to storage issues.

When in doubt, discard the vial and use a fresh one. The cost of a replacement vial is far less than the risk of injecting degraded product.
