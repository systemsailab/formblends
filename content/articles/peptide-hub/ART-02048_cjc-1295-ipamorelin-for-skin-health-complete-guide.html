GH and Skin Biology

Your skin contains GH and IGF-1 receptors. When activated, they stimulate:

  • Type I and III collagen production: The structural proteins that provide skin firmness and volume {{CITE:GH stimulates type I and III collagen production in the dermis}}
  • Elastin synthesis: The protein responsible for skin bounce-back and flexibility
  • Hyaluronic acid production: The molecule that holds moisture in the skin
  • Skin cell turnover: Faster renewal of epidermal cells for a fresher, more even complexion

Collagen production declines approximately 1% per year after age 25 {{CITE:Collagen production declines approximately 1% per year after age 25}}. By age 50, you have lost roughly 25% of your skin's collagen. GH peptides address this decline from the inside out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will CJC-1295/Ipamorelin reduce wrinkles?

It can reduce the appearance of fine lines by increasing collagen density and skin thickness. Deep wrinkles (from decades of UV damage or muscle movement) will not be eliminated by GH peptides alone. For deep wrinkles, Botox and fillers remain more effective.

How does this compare to GHK-Cu for skin?

GHK-Cu is a copper peptide that directly stimulates collagen and has antioxidant properties. It can be used topically or injected. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin works systemically through GH elevation. They can be used together for a multi-pathway approach to skin health.

Will my skin improve everywhere or just my face?

GH elevation is systemic, so collagen production increases throughout the body. Users notice improvements in face, neck, arms, and chest skin. The face is typically where changes are most noticeable because facial skin is thinner and more visible.
