Tesamorelin for Beginners Guide

March 6, 2026 · Peptide Q&A

Tesamorelin is an FDA-approved injectable peptide that stimulates your pituitary gland to release more growth hormone. It is primarily prescribed for reducing visceral abdominal fat and is one of the most well-studied peptides available. Beginners should know that it requires daily subcutaneous injections and takes 8 to 12 weeks to show visible results.

This guide covers everything a first-time user needs to understand before starting tesamorelin therapy.

What Is Tesamorelin?

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). Unlike synthetic growth hormone itself, tesamorelin works by signaling your own pituitary gland to increase its natural GH output. This approach produces a more physiological growth hormone release pattern, including natural pulsing throughout the day.

It was approved by the FDA in 2010 under the brand name Egrifta for the treatment of excess visceral fat in HIV-positive patients. It is now widely prescribed off-label for body composition, anti-aging, and metabolic health.

How to Take Tesamorelin

Tesamorelin is administered as a daily subcutaneous injection, typically in the abdominal area. Here is the basic process:

  1. Reconstitute: If using a lyophilized (freeze-dried) vial, add the prescribed amount of bacteriostatic water. Swirl gently; do not shake.
  2. Draw the dose: Using an insulin syringe, draw the prescribed amount (typically 2 mg for the standard dose).
  3. Inject: Pinch a fold of abdominal skin, insert the needle at a 45-degree angle, and inject slowly.
  4. Rotate sites: Alternate injection locations within the abdomen to reduce irritation.

Most providers recommend injecting at bedtime on an empty stomach, as this aligns with the body's natural growth hormone secretion pattern during sleep. best time to take tesamorelin

Standard Dosing

The FDA-approved dose is 2 mg injected subcutaneously once daily. Some compounding pharmacies provide tesamorelin in different concentrations, so pay close attention to the volume you draw based on your specific vial's concentration. Your provider will walk you through the exact measurement.

What to Expect

  • Weeks 1 to 4: Improved sleep quality, mild increase in energy, possible injection-site redness
  • Weeks 4 to 8: Improved recovery from workouts, skin quality changes, IGF-1 levels rising on lab work
  • Weeks 8 to 12: Measurable reduction in visceral fat, changes in waist circumference and body composition
  • Weeks 12 to 26: Continued and progressive improvements in body composition

Common Side Effects

Most side effects are mild and temporary:

  • Injection-site redness, itching, or swelling
  • Joint pain or stiffness (related to increased GH activity)
  • Numbness or tingling in hands (carpal tunnel-like symptoms)
  • Headaches
  • Mild fluid retention

These effects often diminish as your body adjusts. Report persistent or worsening symptoms to your provider.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a prescription for tesamorelin?

Yes. Tesamorelin is a prescription medication. You need a licensed healthcare provider to evaluate your health, order labs, and write the prescription. Telehealth clinics have made this process convenient and accessible. where to buy tesamorelin online

Is tesamorelin the same as growth hormone?

No. Tesamorelin is a growth hormone-releasing hormone analog, not growth hormone itself. It stimulates your pituitary to produce GH naturally, which is considered a safer and more physiological approach than injecting synthetic GH directly.

How long should a beginner stay on tesamorelin?

Most providers recommend an initial course of 12 to 26 weeks. The pivotal clinical trials ran for 26 weeks, and this duration showed the best results. After the initial course, your provider will assess whether to continue, cycle off, or adjust the protocol.

Can I take tesamorelin if I have never injected before?

Absolutely. Subcutaneous injections with insulin syringes are simple to learn. Most clinics provide instructional videos or one-on-one training. The needles are very thin (typically 29 to 31 gauge), and most people find injections nearly painless after the first few attempts. peptide injection tips for beginners

What labs should I get before starting tesamorelin?

At minimum, your provider should order IGF-1, fasting glucose, HbA1c, a comprehensive metabolic panel, and a lipid panel. These establish your baseline and help monitor the peptide's effects on growth hormone levels and metabolic health over time.
