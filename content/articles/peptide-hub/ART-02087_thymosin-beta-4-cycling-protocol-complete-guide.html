Thymosin Beta-4 Cycling Protocol: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: Thymosin Beta-4 cycling protocol typically follows a treatment course approach: 2 to 4 weeks of loading at higher doses, followed by 4 to 8 weeks of maintenance at lower doses, then an off-cycle period of 4 to 8 weeks. Unlike hormonal peptides (which require cycling for receptor sensitivity), TB-4 cycling is based on the practical reality that tissue repair has a natural endpoint. Once the target tissue is repaired, continued dosing provides diminishing returns .

Why Cycling Works for TB-4

TB-4 cycling differs from hormonal peptide cycling. The rationale is:

  • Repair has an endpoint: Once a tendon is healed or a wound is closed, the repair stimulus is no longer needed. Continuing TB-4 after the tissue is repaired provides little additional benefit
  • Cumulative effects: TB-4's tissue repair effects are cumulative and persist after stopping. Repaired tissue remains repaired
  • Cost efficiency: Cycling reduces overall treatment cost while delivering the full benefit during the active treatment course
  • Assessment opportunity: Off-cycle periods allow you and your physician to assess how much improvement was achieved and whether additional courses are needed

Cycling by Treatment Goal

Acute Injury Protocol

  • Loading: 2.5 mg twice weekly for 3-4 weeks
  • Maintenance: 1.5 mg twice weekly for 4-6 weeks
  • Off-cycle: 6-8 weeks minimum
  • Reassess: If injury is not fully resolved, consider a second course

Chronic Tendinopathy Protocol

  • Loading: 2 mg twice weekly for 2-3 weeks
  • Maintenance: 1 mg twice weekly for 6-8 weeks
  • Off-cycle: 4-6 weeks
  • Repeat: Often requires 2-3 treatment courses for chronic conditions

Wellness/Anti-Aging Protocol

  • Standard dose: 1.5 mg twice weekly for 8 weeks
  • Off-cycle: 8 weeks
  • Repeat: 2-3 courses per year

Pre/Post-Surgery Protocol

  • Pre-surgical: 2 mg twice weekly for 2 weeks before surgery
  • Post-surgical: 2 mg twice weekly for 2-3 weeks, then 1 mg twice weekly for 4 weeks
  • Total course: 6-7 weeks around surgery

What Happens Off-Cycle

  • Week 1-2: TB-4 clears from the system. Tissue repair processes continue from the momentum established during the treatment course
  • Week 2-4: Tissue remodeling and maturation continue. Collagen crosslinking strengthens repaired tissue
  • Week 4-8: Tissue reaches final strength. Assessment of results is appropriate at this point

Repaired tissue does not deteriorate when TB-4 is stopped. The structural improvements are permanent (assuming no re-injury).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take TB-4 continuously?

While not harmful in the short term, continuous use is not recommended because TB-4's repair effects have natural endpoints. Cycling is more cost-effective and clinically appropriate. Continuous use should be discussed with your physician for specific circumstances.

How many treatment courses do most people need?

For acute injuries, one course is often sufficient. For chronic conditions (like long-standing tendinopathy), 2 to 3 courses are common. For anti-aging maintenance, some patients do 2 to 3 courses per year on an ongoing basis.

Can I overlap TB-4 with other peptide cycles?

Yes. TB-4 can be used alongside other peptides (BPC-157, CJC-1295/Ipamorelin, etc.) with different cycling schedules. There are no known interactions. Some patients use TB-4 during their "off-cycle" from GH peptides, or vice versa.
