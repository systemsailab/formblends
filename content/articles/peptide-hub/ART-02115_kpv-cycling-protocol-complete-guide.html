KPV Cycling Protocol: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: KPV cycling protocol typically follows a treatment course of 4 to 12 weeks depending on the condition, followed by an assessment period. Unlike hormonal peptides that require cycling for receptor sensitivity, KPV cycling is based on the clinical goal: once inflammation is controlled and the underlying condition is managed, continuous dosing may not be necessary. What Happens Off-Cycle

  • Anti-inflammatory effects gradually diminish over 1 to 2 weeks as KPV clears
  • Benefits may persist if the underlying inflammation source has been addressed
  • Monitor symptoms during the off period to determine if additional courses are needed
  • Maintain anti-inflammatory diet and lifestyle habits to sustain benefits

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take KPV continuously?

Long-term continuous use has not been well-studied. Given KPV's favorable safety profile, some physicians keep patients on longer courses for chronic conditions. Discuss with your physician.

How do I know when to stop?

When symptoms are well-controlled and stable for 2 to 4 weeks, you and your physician may trial an off period. If symptoms return, a new treatment course can begin.

Can I use KPV as needed rather than on a schedule?

Some patients use KPV reactively for inflammatory flares rather than on a fixed schedule. This approach may work for conditions with clear flare patterns (seasonal eczema, IBD flares).
