Is PT-141 Legal In The US?

Yes, PT-141 (bremelanotide) is legal in the United States. The FDA approved bremelanotide in 2019 under the brand name Vyleesi for treating hypoactive sexual desire disorder in premenopausal women {{CITE:FDA Vyleesi approval 2019}}. Beyond the brand-name version, PT-141 is also legally available as a compounded peptide prescribed by licensed physicians for both men and women.

Detailed Answer

PT-141 has a clear legal status in the U.S., but the details matter depending on how you plan to obtain and use it.

FDA Approval Status

In June 2019, the FDA approved bremelanotide (Vyleesi) as the first self-administered injectable treatment for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women {{CITE:FDA Vyleesi approval details}}. This made PT-141 a fully FDA-approved pharmaceutical product for that specific indication.

Compounded PT-141

In addition to the brand-name product, compounding pharmacies can legally prepare PT-141 when prescribed by a licensed physician. Compounded versions are often more affordable than brand-name Vyleesi and can be prescribed for off-label uses {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:PT-141 compounding legality}}. These pharmacies operate under FDA and state board of pharmacy regulations.

Off-Label Use

While Vyleesi was approved specifically for women with HSDD, physicians can legally prescribe PT-141 off-label for other purposes. Off-label prescribing is a well-established, legal medical practice in the United States. Doctors routinely prescribe approved medications for uses beyond their original FDA indication when they believe it will benefit the patient {{CITE:off-label prescribing legality}}.

Common off-label uses of PT-141 include:

  • Sexual dysfunction in men
  • Low libido in postmenopausal women
  • General sexual wellness support
