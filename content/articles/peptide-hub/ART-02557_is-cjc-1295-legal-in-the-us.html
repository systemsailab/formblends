Is CJC-1295 Legal in the US?

Yes, CJC-1295 is legal in the United States when obtained through a licensed healthcare provider with a valid prescription from a regulated compounding pharmacy. It is not a controlled substance, not a scheduled drug, and not banned for personal medical use. However, it is not FDA-approved as a finished pharmaceutical product, which creates some regulatory nuance worth understanding.

Understanding CJC-1295's Regulatory Status

CJC-1295 is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) with a modified structure that extends its half-life compared to native GHRH. It comes in two forms: CJC-1295 with DAC (Drug Affinity Complex), which has a longer duration of action, and CJC-1295 without DAC (also called Modified GRF 1-29), which produces more pulsatile GH release. FDA Classification

CJC-1295 has not received FDA approval as a drug for any medical indication. This means it cannot be manufactured and sold as a finished drug product with therapeutic claims. However, this does not make it illegal. Many compounds used in clinical practice are prescribed through compounding pharmacies without individual FDA approval of the compound itself. The FDA regulates the drug approval pathway, while compounding is regulated separately under Sections 503A and 503B of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act.

DEA Status

CJC-1295 is not listed on any schedule of the Controlled Substances Act. It carries none of the prescribing restrictions, storage requirements, or criminal penalties associated with controlled substances. State Regulations

Most states follow federal guidance and do not independently regulate CJC-1295.
