CJC-1295/Ipamorelin How To Inject: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is injected subcutaneously (under the skin) using a 29-31 gauge insulin syringe, typically in the abdomen or upper thigh. Inject 30 to 60 minutes before bed on an empty stomach (2+ hours after eating). The injection takes about 30 seconds and most patients describe it as a brief, mild pinch .

Supplies

  • Reconstituted CJC-1295/Ipamorelin vial (from refrigerator)
  • U-100 insulin syringes (29-31 gauge, 1/2 inch)
  • Alcohol swabs
  • Sharps disposal container

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare

Wash hands. Clean workspace. Remove vial from refrigerator. Swab the rubber stopper with alcohol and let it air dry 10 seconds.

2. Draw Your Dose

  1. Pull back plunger to draw in air equal to your dose volume
  2. Insert needle through vial stopper and push air in
  3. Invert vial and slowly draw your dose
  4. Tap out air bubbles and push them back into the vial
  5. Confirm correct volume and withdraw

Return the vial to the refrigerator immediately.

3. Prepare Injection Site

Choose your site (abdomen 2+ inches from navel, or upper thigh). Swab with alcohol in a circular motion. Let dry.

4. Inject

  1. Pinch a fold of skin
  2. Insert needle at 45-degree angle in one smooth motion
  3. Release the skin pinch
  4. Slowly depress plunger over 3 to 5 seconds
  5. Wait 5 seconds before withdrawing needle
  6. Apply light pressure with cotton ball (do not rub)

5. Dispose

Place used syringe in sharps container. Never recap or reuse needles.

Timing Tips

  • Best time: 30-60 minutes before bed
  • Empty stomach: At least 2 hours after last meal. Insulin from food blunts GH release
  • Do not eat after injection: Wait until morning
  • Consistency: Inject at the same time each night for best results

Reconstitution Quick Reference

If your pharmacy shipped lyophilized (powder) vials, reconstitute with bacteriostatic water before first use. Add water to the glass wall (never directly onto powder), gently swirl, and refrigerate. See your pharmacy's specific reconstitution instructions for the exact water volume based on your prescribed concentration.

Common Issues

  • Bruising: Avoid visible veins; apply gentle pressure after injection
  • Stinging: Ensure alcohol has dried completely; let solution warm slightly in hand
  • Leaking: Wait 5 full seconds before withdrawing needle; inject slowly
  • Anxiety about needles: Ice the site for 30 seconds before injection; the needle is extremely small

Frequently Asked Questions

Does injecting CJC-1295/Ipamorelin hurt?

Most patients describe it as a brief, mild pinch or nothing at all. The insulin syringes used are extremely thin (29-31 gauge). Most people become fully comfortable within 3 to 5 injections.

Can I inject in the morning instead of bedtime?

Bedtime is preferred to align with natural GH release. Morning injection is possible on an empty stomach but will not amplify the natural sleep-related GH pulse. Some aggressive protocols use both morning and evening injections.

Where is the best injection site?

The abdomen (2+ inches from navel) provides consistent absorption and easy access. Rotate between left and right sides. Upper thigh is a good alternative. Avoid the same spot on consecutive nights.

What if I forget a dose?

Skip it and resume the next night. Do not double up. Missing an occasional dose will not significantly impact results over a multi-month protocol.

Do I need someone to inject me?

No. Self-injection of subcutaneous peptides is straightforward. Most patients are fully independent within the first few attempts.
