Is Tesamorelin Legal in the US?

March 6, 2026 · Peptide Q&A

Yes, tesamorelin is legal in the United States. It is an FDA-approved prescription medication sold under the brand name Egrifta and Egrifta SV. It was approved in 2010 for the reduction of excess abdominal fat in HIV-positive adults with lipodystrophy. A valid prescription from a licensed healthcare provider is required.

Tesamorelin is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog that stimulates the pituitary gland to produce and release growth hormone. Its legal status is straightforward compared to many other peptides because it holds full FDA approval for a specific indication.

FDA-Approved Use

Tesamorelin's FDA approval is limited to one indication: the reduction of excess visceral adipose tissue in HIV-infected patients with lipodystrophy. This means the drug has been evaluated through rigorous clinical trials for safety and effectiveness within this population.

When a provider prescribes tesamorelin for this approved use, insurance may cover part or all of the cost, depending on your plan. The branded product (Egrifta SV) is manufactured by Theratechnologies Inc.

Off-Label Prescribing

Many providers prescribe tesamorelin off-label for purposes beyond its FDA-approved indication. Common off-label uses include:

  • General body fat reduction and body composition improvement
  • Anti-aging and growth hormone optimization
  • Cognitive health support (based on early research)
  • Metabolic health improvement in non-HIV patients

Off-label prescribing is legal and common across medicine. Providers can prescribe any FDA-approved drug for uses beyond its labeled indication if they believe it is medically appropriate for the patient.

Compounded Tesamorelin

In addition to the branded product, tesamorelin has been available through compounding pharmacies. Compounded versions are typically less expensive than brand-name Egrifta. However, the regulatory landscape for compounded peptides has been evolving.

The FDA has been reviewing which peptides compounding pharmacies can produce. Tesamorelin's status on the FDA's bulk drug substance list determines whether compounders can legally prepare it. Check with your provider or pharmacist for the most current compounding availability.

Tesamorelin Is Not a Controlled Substance

Tesamorelin is not classified as a controlled substance under the Controlled Substances Act. It does not carry the same restrictions as anabolic steroids or narcotics. It is a prescription-only medication, meaning you cannot legally purchase it without a provider's authorization, but possession with a valid prescription is fully lawful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an HIV diagnosis to get tesamorelin?

No. While the FDA-approved indication is for HIV-related lipodystrophy, providers can prescribe tesamorelin off-label for other conditions. Many anti-aging and wellness clinics prescribe it for general body composition and growth hormone optimization in non-HIV patients.

Is tesamorelin banned in sports?

Yes. Tesamorelin falls under WADA's prohibited list as a growth hormone-releasing factor. It is banned both in competition and out of competition for athletes subject to anti-doping testing. Using it with a prescription does not exempt you from anti-doping rules.

Can I buy tesamorelin online legally?

You can legally purchase tesamorelin online through a licensed pharmacy with a valid prescription. Many telehealth clinics offer consultations and can send prescriptions to compounding pharmacies that ship directly to your door. Avoid unregulated websites that sell peptides without requiring a prescription.

Is tesamorelin legal in other countries?

Legal status varies by country. Tesamorelin is approved in the US and Canada. Other countries may not have approved it, may classify it differently, or may restrict its import. Check your destination country's pharmaceutical regulations before traveling with tesamorelin.

What happens if I buy tesamorelin without a prescription?

Purchasing prescription medications without a prescription violates federal and state pharmacy laws. Beyond the legal risk, unregulated products may be counterfeit, contaminated, or incorrectly dosed. Working with a licensed provider protects both your legal standing and your health.
