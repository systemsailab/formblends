What Is CJC-1295/Ipamorelin?

CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is a combination of two synthetic peptides prescribed together:

  • CJC-1295: A growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH) analog. It mimics the natural signal your brain sends to your pituitary gland to produce growth hormone. The modified version (with DAC) has an extended half-life of 6-8 days, meaning it keeps working between doses
  • Ipamorelin: A growth hormone secretagogue that stimulates specific GH release without significantly affecting cortisol or prolactin levels. It produces a clean, targeted GH pulse

Together, they create a synergistic effect: CJC-1295 amplifies and extends the GH release that Ipamorelin triggers, resulting in higher and more sustained GH levels than either peptide alone.

Who Is It For?

CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is typically prescribed for adults over 30 experiencing symptoms of age-related GH decline:

  • Declining sleep quality despite good sleep habits
  • Gradual increase in body fat, especially around the midsection
  • Reduced energy and recovery from exercise
  • Loss of skin elasticity and slower wound healing
  • General sense of aging faster than expected

It is not appropriate for everyone. Your physician will evaluate your health history, current medications, and lab work before prescribing.

How to Get Started

Step 1: Consultation

Schedule a consultation with a physician experienced in peptide therapy. They will review your symptoms, health history, and order baseline lab work (typically IGF-1, comprehensive metabolic panel, fasting glucose, and HbA1c).

Step 2: Prescription

If appropriate, your physician will prescribe CJC-1295/Ipamorelin from a licensed compounding pharmacy. The medication arrives as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder that you reconstitute with bacteriostatic water.

Step 3: Learn to Inject

Injections use tiny insulin syringes (29-31 gauge). The needle is extremely small and most patients describe minimal discomfort. Your provider will walk you through the process. See our CJC-1295/Ipamorelin how to inject guide for detailed instructions.

Step 4: Establish Your Routine

Most protocols call for a single injection before bed on an empty stomach (at least 2 hours after eating). Consistency matters more than perfection. Try to inject at the same time each night.

Step 5: Follow-Up Labs

Your physician will recheck IGF-1 and metabolic markers at 4 to 6 weeks to confirm your dose is producing the desired response and adjust if needed.

Common Beginner Concerns

Is it safe?

CJC-1295/Ipamorelin has a favorable safety profile compared to exogenous GH. Because it works through your own pituitary gland, GH release is regulated by natural feedback mechanisms. Common side effects are mild: temporary water retention, occasional tingling in hands, and injection site redness. See our CJC-1295/Ipamorelin side effects guide for complete details.

Is it legal?

Yes. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is legally prescribed by licensed physicians and dispensed by compounding pharmacies in the United States. It requires a valid prescription. See our CJC-1295/Ipamorelin legal status guide.

How is it different from HGH?

HGH (human growth hormone) is the actual hormone injected directly. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin stimulates your body to produce its own GH. This means more natural pulsatile release, lower risk of side effects, and your pituitary gland remains active rather than being suppressed.
