How Inflammation Drains Energy

Chronic inflammation is one of the most underrecognized causes of fatigue. When the immune system is chronically activated, it consumes significant metabolic resources:

  • Metabolic cost: The immune system uses approximately 20% of basal metabolic energy when chronically activated, diverting resources from daily activities {{CITE:Chronic immune activation consumes approximately 20% of basal metabolic energy}}
  • Cytokine-induced fatigue: Pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-1 beta) directly act on the brain to produce fatigue, a phenomenon known as "sickness behavior." This is the same mechanism that makes you tired when fighting an infection
  • Sleep disruption: Inflammation disrupts sleep architecture, reducing deep sleep and causing nighttime awakenings. Poor sleep compounds daytime fatigue
  • Mitochondrial stress: Chronic inflammation damages mitochondria, the cellular energy producers, reducing ATP output

By reducing chronic inflammation, TB-4 can free up metabolic resources and remove the cytokine-driven fatigue signal, resulting in improved energy without any direct stimulant effect.

When TB-4 Helps Energy Most

TB-4 is most likely to improve energy in patients experiencing:

  • Post-infectious fatigue: Persistent fatigue after viral illness (including long COVID) driven by ongoing inflammation
  • Chronic pain fatigue: Pain from injuries, arthritis, or tendinopathy that disrupts sleep and drains energy
  • Inflammatory conditions: Autoimmune or chronic inflammatory states where the immune system is constantly active
  • Recovery periods: Post-surgical or post-injury fatigue where the body is directing energy toward healing

For fatigue caused by hormonal decline, thyroid dysfunction, or nutritional deficiencies, other interventions are more appropriate as first-line treatment.
