Ipamorelin How To Reconstitute: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: To reconstitute ipamorelin, slowly inject bacteriostatic water into the vial containing the lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder, aiming the stream against the glass wall rather than directly onto the powder. A common ratio is 2 mL of bacteriostatic water per 5 mg vial, which yields a concentration of 250 mcg per 0.1 mL (10 units on an insulin syringe) {{CITE:Standard ipamorelin reconstitution uses bacteriostatic water at a ratio that allows precise dosing}}.

What Is Ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a selective growth hormone-releasing peptide (GHRP) that stimulates the pituitary gland to produce growth hormone without significantly affecting cortisol, prolactin, or appetite {{CITE:Ipamorelin selectively stimulates GH release without raising cortisol or prolactin}}. It arrives from compounding pharmacies as a lyophilized powder that must be mixed with a sterile diluent before injection.

What You Need Before You Start

Gather all of the following items before beginning the reconstitution process:

  • Ipamorelin vial: Typically comes in 2 mg or 5 mg vials of lyophilized powder.
  • Bacteriostatic water (BAC water): This is sterile water that contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative, which allows the reconstituted solution to remain stable for multiple uses over several weeks {{CITE:Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol to preserve multi-dose reconstituted peptide vials}}.
  • Insulin syringes: Use 1 mL (100 unit) insulin syringes with 29 to 31 gauge needles for both reconstitution and injection.
  • Alcohol swabs: For sterilizing vial tops before drawing or injecting fluid.

Important: Do not use normal saline or plain sterile water for reconstitution of multi-dose vials. Normal saline can degrade peptides faster, and sterile water without a preservative does not protect against bacterial contamination in multi-dose vials {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:sterile water without preservative is not recommended for multi-dose peptide vials}}.

Step-by-Step Reconstitution Process

Step 1: Wash Your Hands

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water. Dry them with a clean towel. This is a basic but critical step to reduce contamination risk.

Step 2: Swab the Vial Tops

Use a fresh alcohol swab to clean the rubber stopper on both the ipamorelin vial and the bacteriostatic water vial. Allow the alcohol to air dry for about 10 seconds before proceeding.

Step 3: Draw Bacteriostatic Water

Using a clean insulin syringe, draw the desired amount of bacteriostatic water. Common volumes based on vial size:

Step 4: Add Water to the Ipamorelin Vial

Insert the needle through the rubber stopper of the ipamorelin vial. Angle the needle so the tip touches the inside wall of the glass vial. Slowly press the plunger to let the water trickle down the side of the vial.

Do not squirt the water directly onto the powder. Direct force can damage the delicate peptide structure. The goal is a gentle, controlled stream that dissolves the powder gradually.

Step 5: Let It Dissolve

After adding the water, set the vial on a flat surface and allow the powder to dissolve on its own. This usually takes 2 to 5 minutes. You can gently swirl the vial with a slow rotating motion if some powder remains undissolved after a few minutes.

Do not shake the vial. Vigorous shaking can create foam and denature the peptide, reducing its potency {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:shaking can denature reconstituted peptides through mechanical stress}}.

Step 6: Inspect the Solution

Once fully dissolved, the solution should be clear and colorless. If you notice cloudiness, visible particles, or discoloration, do not use the vial. These are signs of contamination or degradation.

Step 7: Store Properly

Place the reconstituted vial in your refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius). Do not freeze reconstituted ipamorelin. For detailed storage guidance, see our {{INTERNAL_LINK:Ipamorelin storage instructions}} guide.

How to Calculate Your Dose

Once reconstituted, you need to know how many units to draw for your prescribed dose. The formula is straightforward:

Dose in units = (desired dose in mcg / concentration per 0.1 mL) x 10

For example, if you reconstituted a 5 mg vial with 2 mL of BAC water (250 mcg per 0.1 mL) and your prescribed dose is 250 mcg:

250 mcg / 250 mcg per 0.1 mL = 1 x 10 = 10 units on an insulin syringe

If your prescribed dose is 300 mcg with the same concentration:

300 mcg / 250 mcg per 0.1 mL = 1.2 x 10 = 12 units on an insulin syringe

Your physician or pharmacy should provide specific dosing instructions. If you are uncertain about the math, ask your provider before injecting.

Common Reconstitution Mistakes to Avoid

  • Squirting water directly onto the powder: This can damage the peptide. Always aim along the vial wall.
  • Shaking the vial: Gentle swirling is fine. Shaking denatures the protein.
  • Using the wrong diluent: Always use bacteriostatic water for multi-dose vials. Do not use normal saline or plain sterile water.
  • Touching the needle: Keep the needle sterile. If you accidentally touch it, discard it and use a new one.
  • Skipping the alcohol swab: Always clean the rubber stoppers before inserting a needle.
  • Leaving reconstituted peptide at room temperature: Refrigerate immediately after mixing. Reconstituted ipamorelin degrades rapidly at room temperature.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does reconstituted ipamorelin last?

When stored properly in the refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit, reconstituted ipamorelin with bacteriostatic water typically remains stable for 3 to 4 weeks {{CITE:Reconstituted ipamorelin with bacteriostatic water remains stable for approximately 3-4 weeks when refrigerated}}. Always inspect the solution before each use.

Can I use sterile water instead of bacteriostatic water?

Sterile water can be used for single-dose vials, but it lacks a preservative. For multi-dose vials, bacteriostatic water is required to prevent bacterial growth between uses.

What if I accidentally shake the vial?

A brief accidental shake is unlikely to completely destroy the peptide, but it may reduce potency slightly. If foam forms, let it settle completely before drawing a dose. For future reconstitutions, stick to gentle swirling.

Can I pre-load syringes with reconstituted ipamorelin?

Pre-loading syringes is generally not recommended because the peptide may adhere to syringe walls over time, and the sterility of pre-loaded syringes is harder to maintain. Draw each dose fresh from the vial.

What if the powder does not dissolve completely?

If small particles remain after 10 minutes of gentle swirling, the vial may have been compromised. Do not use a solution that is not completely clear. Contact your pharmacy for a replacement.
