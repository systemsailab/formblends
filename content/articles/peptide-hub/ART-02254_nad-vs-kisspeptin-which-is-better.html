NAD+ vs Kisspeptin: Which Is Better?

Quick Answer: NAD+ vs Kisspeptin compares a coenzyme (not a peptide) with a reproductive hormone peptide. NAD+ is used for cellular energy, dna repair, sirtuin activation. Kisspeptin is used for gnrh stimulation, reproductive hormone regulation. These compounds serve different purposes and your choice depends on your primary health goal. They can often be used together since they work through independent mechanisms .

Can You Use Both?

Yes. NAD+ and Kisspeptin work through independent mechanisms and do not interfere with each other. They can be combined when your health goals span both cellular energy and gnrh stimulation. Your physician can design a protocol that incorporates both compounds with appropriate timing and dosing.
