Cerebrolysin Legal Status: Complete Guide

Quick Answer: Cerebrolysin legal status varies by country. It is approved as a prescription medication in over 50 countries (including EU member states, Russia, China, and many Asian and South American nations) but is not FDA-approved in the United States. In the US, Cerebrolysin can be legally obtained through compounding pharmacies with a physician's prescription or through personal importation for personal use. It is not a controlled substance and is not banned by any sports organization {{CITE:Cerebrolysin approved in 50+ countries, available in US through compounding pharmacies}}.

How to Obtain Cerebrolysin Legally in the US

Option 1: Compounding Pharmacy

  1. A licensed US physician evaluates you and determines Cerebrolysin is appropriate
  2. The physician writes a prescription for compounded Cerebrolysin
  3. A 503A or 503B compounding pharmacy prepares the product
  4. The medication is shipped to you or the physician's office

This is the most common and straightforward legal pathway in the US.

Option 2: Personal Importation

The FDA's personal importation policy allows individuals to import medications approved in other countries for personal use, typically a 90-day supply. This requires the medication to be for a serious condition and not available domestically in the same form. This pathway is more complex and less commonly used than compounding.

Why Cerebrolysin Is Not FDA-Approved

  • The manufacturer (Ever Pharma, Austria) has not pursued US FDA approval, which requires extensive and expensive US-based clinical trials
  • As a biological mixture rather than a single-molecule drug, Cerebrolysin faces a more complex regulatory pathway
  • The existing global approval and market presence may not justify the FDA approval investment
  • Lack of FDA approval does not indicate safety or efficacy concerns; it reflects a business and regulatory decision

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it legal to use Cerebrolysin in the US?

Yes. Using a compounded medication with a physician's prescription is legal. Compounding pharmacies operate under state pharmacy boards and FDA oversight (503A/503B regulations). You need a valid prescription from a licensed physician.

Is Cerebrolysin a controlled substance?

No. Cerebrolysin is not scheduled by the DEA and is not a controlled substance. It does not have abuse potential and is not subject to controlled substance regulations.

Can athletes use Cerebrolysin?

Cerebrolysin is not on the WADA or USADA prohibited substance list. Athletes can use it without doping concerns. However, always verify with the most current prohibited list for your specific sport and competition level.
