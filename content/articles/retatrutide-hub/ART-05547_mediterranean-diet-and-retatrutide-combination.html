How Retatrutide Changes Your Relationship with Food

Retatrutide reduces appetite and slows gastric emptying {{CITE:retatrutide effects on appetite and gastric motility}}. When total caloric intake drops, nutritional quality of every meal becomes more important.

Combining Mediterranean with Retatrutide

Potential Benefits

  • May support steady weight loss alongside retatrutide's effects
  • Can help manage blood sugar
  • Provides structured eating framework

Considerations

  • Protein intake must stay adequate (0.7-1.0g per pound of goal body weight) {{CITE:protein requirements during GLP-1 therapy}}
  • Hydration is critical
  • Some restrictions may make it harder to meet nutritional needs
  • GI tolerance may differ with specific food patterns

Meal Planning Tips

  • Prioritize protein at every meal. Aim for 25-30 grams minimum.
  • Eat slowly. Retatrutide narrows the gap between comfortable and overfull.
  • Space meals evenly. Four to five smaller meals work better.
  • Supplement strategically. A multivitamin can fill gaps {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:supplement recommendations during retatrutide therapy}}.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a specific diet on retatrutide?

No specific diet is required, but nutrient-dense foods improve outcomes {{INTERNAL_LINK:best foods on GLP-1 medications}}.

What if I cannot eat enough?
