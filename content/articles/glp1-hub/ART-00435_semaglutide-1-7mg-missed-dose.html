Semaglutide 1.7mg: What To Do If You Miss a Dose

If you miss a dose of semaglutide 1.7mg, take it as soon as you remember if it has been 5 days or fewer since the scheduled injection. If more than 5 days have passed, skip the missed dose and take your next injection on your regular day. Never double up on doses to make up for a missed one.

The 5-Day Rule Explained

Semaglutide has an elimination half-life of approximately 7 days, which means the medication stays active in your system for an extended period after each injection. The prescribing information uses a 5-day window as the cutoff for taking a late dose.

Here is how to apply this in practice:

Scenario 1: You Remember Within 5 Days

Take your 1.7mg injection as soon as you remember. Then resume your regular weekly schedule. You have two options for getting back on track:

  • Option A: Shift your injection day to the day you took the late dose. Going forward, this becomes your new weekly injection day.
  • Option B: Take the late dose and return to your original injection day the following week, as long as at least 2 days separate the late dose from the next scheduled dose.

Scenario 2: More Than 5 Days Have Passed

Skip the missed dose entirely. Wait until your next regularly scheduled injection day and take your normal 1.7mg dose. Do not take a double dose or inject earlier than your next scheduled day to compensate.

Why the 5-Day Cutoff Matters

Because semaglutide has a long half-life, taking a dose too close to your next scheduled injection would result in higher-than-intended drug levels. This increases the risk of nausea, vomiting, and other GI side effects without providing additional therapeutic benefit. The 5-day rule ensures adequate spacing between injections.

How a Missed Dose at 1.7mg Affects You

Missing a single dose of semaglutide 1.7mg is unlikely to derail your progress. Here is what you may notice:

  • Appetite return: Because semaglutide suppresses appetite through GLP-1 receptor activation in the brain, you may notice increased hunger 3 to 5 days after missing a dose. Food cravings and "food noise" may temporarily return.
  • Blood sugar fluctuations: Patients using semaglutide for glycemic control may see a modest rise in blood glucose levels. If you have type 2 diabetes, monitor your blood sugar more frequently after a missed dose.
  • Temporary weight fluctuation: A single missed dose may cause a small uptick on the scale, primarily from increased food intake and water retention. This typically resolves once you resume your regular dosing.
  • No loss of tolerance: Missing one dose at 1.7mg does not reset your GI tolerance. You can resume at 1.7mg without needing to re-titrate from a lower dose.

What If You Miss Two or More Doses in a Row

Missing two consecutive weeks is more significant. After 14 or more days without semaglutide, your GLP-1 receptor sensitivity may partially reset, and side effects could return when you restart.

If you have missed two or more consecutive doses, contact your prescriber before resuming. They may recommend:

  • Restarting at 1.7mg if the gap was 2 weeks
  • Stepping back to 1.0mg for a week or two if the gap was 3 weeks or longer
  • A full re-titration from 0.25mg if the gap was a month or more

The decision depends on how long you were off the medication and how your body originally handled the titration.

Tips to Avoid Missing Doses

  • Set a weekly phone alarm: Pick the same day and time each week. Many patients inject on the same day as another weekly routine (trash day, a recurring meeting) to build a habit.
  • Use a medication tracking app: Apps like Medisafe or the built-in Health app on iPhone can send reminders and log your injections.
  • Keep your pen visible: Store your semaglutide pen in the refrigerator door where you will see it daily. This visual cue helps prevent forgotten doses.
  • Travel preparation: If traveling, pack your pen with ice packs in a medication cooler. Set a reminder adjusted for time zone changes.
  • Pair with an existing habit: Inject right before or after something you already do every week, like a Sunday meal prep or a weekly workout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take my missed 1.7mg dose the next day?
Yes. If it has been 1 day since your scheduled injection, take it right away. One day late is well within the 5-day safe window and will have minimal impact on your treatment.
Will missing one dose cause me to gain weight?
One missed dose is unlikely to cause meaningful weight gain. You may see a small scale increase from water retention or slightly higher food intake, but this typically corrects within a week of resuming your schedule.
Should I take a half dose if I missed my injection and it is close to my next dose day?
No. There is no guidance supporting partial doses of semaglutide. Either take the full 1.7mg dose (if within 5 days) or skip it entirely and wait for your next scheduled day.
Do I need to restart from a lower dose after one missed injection?
No. Missing a single dose at 1.7mg does not require re-titration. Simply resume at 1.7mg on your next injection day. Re-titration is only necessary after extended gaps of 3 weeks or more. semaglutide dose titration guide
I keep forgetting my doses. Should I switch to a daily medication instead?
Semaglutide is also available in a daily oral form (Rybelsus) for type 2 diabetes, though the oral version has not been approved at a weight management dose. If adherence to weekly injections is a persistent problem, discuss alternatives with your prescriber.
