What Happens If I Miss A Wegovy Dose?

Missing a Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg) dose temporarily reduces the medication's appetite-suppressing effects but will not cause serious medical harm. If fewer than 5 days have passed since your scheduled dose, take it as soon as you remember. If 5 or more days have passed, skip it and wait for your next regularly scheduled injection.

Detailed Explanation

Wegovy is an FDA-approved once-weekly injectable semaglutide prescribed specifically for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight with at least one weight-related condition. Like all semaglutide-based medications, it has a half-life of about 7 days, meaning drug levels taper gradually rather than dropping off suddenly when a dose is missed.

What You May Notice After a Missed Dose

The most common experience after missing a Wegovy injection is a gradual return of appetite. Many patients report feeling hungrier than usual, having stronger food cravings, and feeling less full after meals. These changes typically begin 2 to 4 days after the missed dose as semaglutide levels fall below the therapeutic threshold. You may also notice slight changes in digestion, as the medication's effect on gastric emptying diminishes.

The Missed Dose Protocol

Novo Nordisk recommends a straightforward approach. If your missed dose is within 5 days of the scheduled date, inject as soon as you remember and then return to your normal weekly schedule. If more than 5 days have elapsed, do not take the missed dose. Instead, wait and take the next dose on your regularly scheduled day. This prevents overlapping doses that could increase the risk of gastrointestinal side effects.

Effect on Weight Loss Progress

One missed dose is unlikely to derail your weight loss trajectory. Clinical trials for Wegovy showed that the medication's benefits accumulate over weeks and months of consistent use. A single skipped injection creates a small and temporary dip in drug levels, but the overall trend of your treatment remains intact. The key is getting back on schedule as quickly as possible rather than worrying about one missed injection.

When Missing Doses Becomes a Pattern

Repeatedly missing Wegovy doses is a different situation. If you miss two or more consecutive weekly injections, your body begins to lose tolerance to the medication. Resuming at your previous dose level may cause more intense nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea than you experienced during your initial titration. In this case, your physician may recommend stepping back to a lower dose and titrating up again over several weeks. This is especially relevant for patients on the full 2.4 mg maintenance dose.

What to Consider

  • Use a weekly phone alarm or calendar reminder set for the same day and time each week.
  • Keep your Wegovy pen accessible and properly stored so it is ready when your injection day arrives.
  • Do not take two injections at once or within a few days of each other to compensate.
  • If you miss more than one dose in a row, contact your prescribing physician before resuming treatment.
  • Track your weight and appetite patterns to help identify if missed doses are affecting your progress.
  • Travel and schedule disruptions are common reasons for missed doses. Plan ahead by packing your pen and keeping it temperature-controlled.
