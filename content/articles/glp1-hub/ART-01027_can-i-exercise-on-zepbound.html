What You Need to Know: Exercise Guidelines for Zepbound

Build a Foundation First

If you are new to exercise, start with daily walks and two sessions per week of basic strength exercises. The first month on Zepbound involves dose titration, so your body is already adjusting to the medication. Let yourself build fitness gradually rather than doing too much too fast.

Strength Training Is Non-Negotiable

Given how aggressively Zepbound can reduce calorie intake, resistance training becomes your most valuable tool. Aim for two to three sessions per week that cover all major muscle groups. Compound movements like squats, presses, rows, and deadlifts give you the most return on your time investment.

Work Around Side Effects

Zepbound's most common side effects include nausea, diarrhea, and reduced appetite, particularly in the first week or two after each dose increase. Plan your harder workouts for days when you feel your best. Lighter activity like walking or gentle yoga is still beneficial on days when GI symptoms are more present.

Keep Calories and Protein Adequate

Zepbound can suppress appetite to the point where patients unintentionally eat far too little. Severely low calorie intake accelerates muscle loss and can leave you feeling too weak to exercise. Work with your provider to establish a calorie floor and prioritize protein at 0.7 to 1 gram per pound of body weight daily.

Track Body Composition, Not Just Weight

The scale will move quickly on Zepbound. But weight alone does not tell you whether you are losing fat or muscle. Consider periodic body composition assessments, waist measurements, and strength benchmarks to get a fuller picture of your progress.

Related Questions

Does Zepbound make it harder to exercise?

It can during the adjustment periods after dose increases. Most patients find that within one to two weeks at a new dose, their energy stabilizes and they can exercise normally. Eating adequately before workouts helps significantly.

How often should I work out while on Zepbound?

Three to five structured workout days per week is a good target. Include at least two to three resistance training sessions. Daily walking on top of that provides additional metabolic benefits without taxing your recovery.

Will Zepbound affect my muscle gains?

Building significant new muscle while in a calorie deficit is challenging regardless of medication. However, you can maintain your existing muscle and even make modest strength gains with consistent training and adequate protein. The priority is preservation, not necessarily growth, during active weight loss.

Is Zepbound safe for athletes?

Zepbound is generally safe for active individuals and athletes. However, athletic performance may be affected during the initial adjustment period. Athletes should work closely with their physician to manage dose timing and nutritional needs around training schedules {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Zepbound use in athletically active patients}}.
