Wegovy results after 3 weeks confirm that your body has accepted semaglutide. You have taken three injections at 0.25 mg, reached near-steady-state blood levels, and established a baseline for how this medication affects your appetite and digestion. The scale may show a modest drop of 1 to 5 pounds. Most importantly, you are one week away from your first dose increase to 0.5 mg, where the weight loss journey truly begins.

The 0.5 mg Transition: What to Know

Next week brings your first dose increase. The jump from 0.25 mg to 0.5 mg is a 100% dose increase, the largest proportional increase in the Wegovy escalation schedule. Here is what to expect:

  • Appetite: Noticeably stronger suppression within 2-3 days of the first 0.5 mg injection
  • Nausea: May return briefly in about 20-30% of patients. Usually milder and shorter than the initial 0.25 mg nausea
  • Weight loss: Should pick up to 0.5-1 lb per week or more
  • Meal size: Many patients find their meal portions drop by 30-50% compared to pre-treatment

{{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Patients who experienced significant GI issues at 0.25 mg should discuss extending the introductory period with their provider before escalating.}}

Frequently Asked Questions

I have only lost 1 pound in 3 weeks. Is Wegovy working?

Yes. At 0.25 mg, 1 pound of weight loss is within the expected range. The medication is active in your body even if the scale is barely moving. Significant weight loss starts at 0.5 mg and accelerates through the higher doses.

Can I request a faster escalation schedule?

Some providers are flexible about shortening the 0.25 mg phase to 2-3 weeks if tolerability is excellent. However, the standard 4-week protocol provides the smoothest transition. Discuss your preferences with your provider. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy dose schedule}}

Should I be worried about hair loss?

Hair thinning can occur with significant weight loss (not specifically from semaglutide). It is usually temporary and related to caloric deficit and nutritional changes. Ensuring adequate protein (80-100g daily), iron, zinc, and biotin can minimize this risk. Hair loss typically occurs months into treatment, not at week 3.

Is week 3 a good time to get blood work done?

If you did not get baseline labs before starting, get them now. Otherwise, plan for your first follow-up labs at week 12 (after 8 weeks at the 0.5 mg dose). This timing captures meaningful metabolic changes.
