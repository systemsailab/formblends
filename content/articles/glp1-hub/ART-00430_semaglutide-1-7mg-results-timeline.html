Before You Start: What You Should Have Achieved at Lower Doses

Most patients arrive at 1.7mg after 12 weeks of titration through 0.25mg, 0.5mg, and 1mg. By this point, you should have experienced:

  • Reduced appetite and portion sizes
  • Initial weight loss of 8 to 12 percent of starting body weight
  • Adaptation to common GI side effects
  • Established healthy eating patterns that support further weight loss

Week 1 at 1.7mg (Overall Week 13)

The first week at 1.7mg is an adjustment period. Key changes include:

  • Appetite: Noticeably stronger suppression than at 1mg. Many patients describe feeling genuinely uninterested in food for extended periods.
  • Side effects: Mild nausea may return temporarily. This is normal during dose escalation and usually resolves within 3 to 5 days.
  • Weight: Initial weight change may include water fluctuations. Expect 1 to 2 pounds of loss.
  • Energy: Some patients feel slightly fatigued as caloric intake drops further. Prioritize protein and hydration.

Week 2 at 1.7mg (Overall Week 14)

  • Appetite: Suppression stabilizes. You develop a new baseline for hunger and fullness signals.
  • Side effects: Nausea and GI symptoms typically resolve by mid-week for most patients. {{CITE:GI side effect resolution timeline at semaglutide 1.7mg}}
  • Weight: Steady loss of 1.5 to 2 pounds. Cumulative loss from treatment start often reaches 10 to 13 percent of starting weight.
  • Mental clarity: Patients frequently report reduced "food noise" and an improved relationship with eating. Decision fatigue around meals decreases.

Week 3 at 1.7mg (Overall Week 15)

  • Appetite: Consistently controlled. Eating becomes more routine and less emotionally driven.
  • Weight: Continued steady loss. Clothes may fit noticeably looser. Patients who have been exercising regularly often see accelerated body composition changes.
  • Lab markers: If checked at this point, many patients see improvement in fasting glucose, HbA1c, and triglyceride levels. {{CITE:metabolic improvements during semaglutide treatment}}
  • Social eating: Many patients become more comfortable navigating restaurants and social meals because appetite control feels natural rather than forced.

Week 4 at 1.7mg (Overall Week 16)

  • Appetite: Fully adjusted. This is your new normal at this dose.
  • Weight: Total loss at 1.7mg typically reaches 5 to 8 additional pounds beyond what was achieved at 1mg. Cumulative loss from treatment start may reach 12 to 15 percent of starting body weight.

Factors That Influence Your Results at 1.7mg

Diet Quality

Patients who prioritize lean protein (at least 100 grams daily), vegetables, and whole grains see better results than those who eat less but still consume calorie-dense processed foods. The medication reduces appetite, but food quality still drives outcomes.

Physical Activity

Regular exercise, especially resistance training, accelerates fat loss and preserves muscle mass. Patients who combine semaglutide with 150 or more minutes of weekly activity consistently outperform those who rely on medication alone. {{CITE:exercise and semaglutide outcomes}}

Sleep and Stress

Poor sleep and chronic stress elevate cortisol, which can slow weight loss and increase cravings even with GLP-1 medication. Aim for 7 to 9 hours of sleep per night.

Hydration

Dehydration is common at higher doses because reduced food intake means less water from food sources. Adequate water intake (64 to 80 ounces daily) supports metabolism, reduces constipation, and can help manage nausea.

When to Expect a Plateau

Weight loss plateaus are normal and can occur at any dose level. At 1.7mg, a plateau typically happens when:

  • Your body reaches a new metabolic set point and temporarily resists further loss
  • Caloric intake has unconsciously increased as you became comfortable with the appetite suppression
  • Muscle gain from exercise offsets fat loss on the scale (body composition is changing even if weight is not)

If you plateau for more than 3 weeks at 1.7mg, your physician may recommend advancing to 2.4mg. {{INTERNAL_LINK:semaglutide 2.4mg what to expect}}

Frequently Asked Questions

How much total weight can I lose on semaglutide 1.7mg?

Most patients lose 5 to 8 pounds during their 4 weeks at 1.7mg, on top of the 15 to 25 pounds already lost during earlier titration steps. Total cumulative loss by the end of the 1.7mg phase averages 12 to 15 percent of starting body weight. {{CITE:total weight loss at semaglutide 1.7mg}}

What if I am not losing weight at 1.7mg?

First, review your diet and activity levels. If those are on track, consider tracking calories for one week to identify hidden intake. If weight loss remains stalled despite good adherence, your physician may recommend increasing to 2.4mg. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Form Blends telehealth consultation}}

Do results at 1.7mg last after stopping the medication?

Weight regain is common after stopping GLP-1 medications without lifestyle modifications in place. Clinical data shows that patients who maintain diet and exercise habits retain more of their weight loss compared to those who do not. {{CITE:weight regain after stopping semaglutide}}

Is it normal to lose more weight in the first week at 1.7mg?

Yes. The first week at a new dose often produces slightly higher weight loss due to the combined effect of stronger appetite suppression and mild GI side effects that reduce food intake. This typically normalizes by week 2. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:weight loss pattern at new dose levels}}

When will I notice physical changes beyond the scale?

Most patients notice looser clothing and visible body changes by weeks 14 to 16. Others may receive comments from friends and family around this time. Waist circumference often decreases faster than scale weight, especially if you are exercising. {{CITE:waist circumference changes during semaglutide treatment}}
