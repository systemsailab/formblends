What Happens If I Miss A Tirzepatide Dose?

Missing a tirzepatide dose may lead to temporary increases in appetite, reduced blood sugar control, and a brief pause in weight loss progress. If fewer than four days (96 hours) have passed since your missed dose, take it as soon as possible. If four or more days have passed, skip the missed dose and take your next dose on the regularly scheduled day.

The Four-Day Rule for Tirzepatide

Tirzepatide (sold as Mounjaro and Zepbound) is administered once weekly via subcutaneous injection. The prescribing information specifies a four-day cutoff for missed doses. If you are within the four-day window, take the dose immediately and then return to your regular schedule. If you are beyond four days, wait for your next scheduled dose to avoid taking two injections too close together.

This rule differs slightly from semaglutide's five-day window because tirzepatide has a shorter half-life (approximately five days versus seven days for semaglutide). The shorter window helps prevent overlapping doses that could intensify side effects.

Symptoms You May Experience After Missing a Dose

Tirzepatide works on both GIP and GLP-1 receptors to suppress appetite, slow digestion, and regulate blood sugar. When you miss a dose, these effects begin to diminish. Common experiences include a noticeable return of appetite and food cravings, faster gastric emptying leading to feeling hungry sooner after meals, elevated blood sugar readings if you are managing type 2 diabetes, and reduced feelings of fullness that the medication normally provides.

These symptoms are typically mild after missing a single dose because tirzepatide's five-day half-life means the drug is still partially active in your system for several days beyond your scheduled injection date.

Effect on Weight Loss and Blood Sugar

One missed dose will not erase weeks or months of progress. Tirzepatide accumulates in the body over time, so a single gap in dosing does not cause the drug to vanish from your system entirely. That said, your appetite suppression will be weaker during the gap, which could lead to eating more than usual if you are not mindful. For diabetes patients, monitor your blood sugar more frequently around a missed dose and follow your provider's guidance for managing any spikes.

Missing Multiple Consecutive Doses

If you miss two or more doses in a row, do not simply resume at your current dose level. Contact your prescribing physician. They may recommend stepping back to a lower dose and re-titrating over several weeks. This is especially important for patients on higher doses (10 mg or 15 mg), where restarting at full strength after a prolonged gap frequently causes severe nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

The re-titration process typically starts at 2.5 mg and increases in 2.5 mg increments every four weeks, following the same schedule used when you first started the medication.

What to Consider

Never take a double dose of tirzepatide to compensate for a missed injection. Taking two doses close together significantly increases the risk of serious gastrointestinal side effects and does not provide better results. If you travel frequently or have a schedule that makes weekly injections difficult to remember, set a consistent alarm, use a medication tracking app, or coordinate your injection day with a routine activity to build the habit.

Related Questions

Can I switch my tirzepatide injection day?

Yes. You can change your injection day as long as your last dose was administered at least three days (72 hours) prior. After the change, continue with the new day each week going forward.

Does missing tirzepatide cause rebound hunger?

You may notice increased hunger as the medication's effects wear off, but this is not a rebound effect. It is simply the return of your baseline appetite without the drug's suppressive action. Normal appetite regulation returns once you resume dosing.

How many doses can I miss before tirzepatide stops working?

There is no specific number that renders the medication permanently ineffective. However, after missing two or more consecutive doses, your provider may need to restart you at a lower dose. Consistent weekly dosing is necessary for tirzepatide to deliver optimal weight loss and blood sugar results.

What should I do if my tirzepatide pen malfunctioned and I am not sure I got the full dose?

If the injection device did not click or fully depress, you may not have received the complete dose. Do not attempt a second injection with a new pen. Contact your prescribing physician or pharmacist for guidance on whether to take a partial or replacement dose.

Is it better to take a late dose or skip it entirely?

If you are within the four-day window, taking a late dose is better than skipping it. Beyond four days, skipping is the safer choice to maintain proper spacing between injections and avoid compounding side effects.
