Mounjaro Dosage Guide: Complete Guide 2026

The Mounjaro dosage guide starts with a simple principle: go slow. Tirzepatide uses a six-step dose escalation schedule that begins at 2.5 mg and can go up to 15 mg weekly. This gradual approach is designed to give your gastrointestinal system time to adjust, minimize side effects, and help your physician find the dose that delivers the best balance of weight loss and tolerability for your body.

Overview: The Six Dose Levels

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is available in six dose strengths, each delivered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection:

The 2.5 mg dose exists purely for tolerability. It was not designed to produce significant weight loss. Think of it as the onboarding phase where your body gets used to the medication's effects on gastric motility and appetite signaling.

The Standard Titration Schedule

Weeks Dose Expected Changes
Weeks 1-4 2.5 mg Mild appetite changes, minimal weight loss (1-3 lbs), body adjusting
Weeks 5-8 5 mg Appetite noticeably reduced, weight loss begins (1-2 lbs/week), possible GI effects
Weeks 9-12 7.5 mg Stronger appetite suppression, steady weight loss, food noise quieting
Weeks 13-16 10 mg Significant appetite reduction, consistent 1.5-2.5 lbs/week loss typical
Weeks 17-20 12.5 mg Strong therapeutic effect, approaching maximum benefit
Week 21 onward 15 mg Maximum dose, peak weight loss effect

Each dose increase happens at minimum 4-week intervals. This is the fastest recommended pace. Your physician may extend any step to 6, 8, or even 12 weeks based on your response and side effect profile.

What Happens at Each Dose Level

2.5 mg: The Starter Phase

This is your body's introduction to tirzepatide. Some patients feel almost nothing at this dose. Others notice a subtle reduction in hunger, slight changes in digestion, and perhaps mild nausea. Weight loss is minimal, typically 1 to 3 pounds over the first month. Do not be discouraged. This dose is accomplishing exactly what it is supposed to: preparing your system for higher, more effective doses.

5 mg: The First Real Dose

Most patients notice a meaningful change at 5 mg. Appetite reduction becomes more pronounced. Portion sizes naturally shrink. Food cravings, particularly for high-calorie and processed foods, often start to diminish. This is typically where patients first report that "food noise" has quieted. Weight loss of 4 to 8 pounds over this 4-week period is common. GI side effects may flare for the first week after the increase.

7.5 mg: Building Momentum

Weight loss accelerates for many patients at 7.5 mg. Appetite suppression is consistent and noticeable. Patients often report needing conscious effort to eat enough protein and nutrients because hunger signals are so much quieter. By the end of 12 weeks on the medication, many patients have lost 8 to 15% of their starting weight.

10 mg: The Sweet Spot for Many Patients

In clinical trials, the 10 mg dose produced an average weight loss of 19.5% over 72 weeks. For many patients, this dose offers an excellent balance of efficacy and tolerability. Some patients find that increasing beyond 10 mg provides diminishing returns in terms of additional weight loss relative to the increase in side effects. Your physician will help you evaluate whether moving higher makes sense for your situation.

12.5 mg: Approaching Maximum

Patients who continue to titrate past 10 mg are typically those who have tolerated lower doses well and still have significant weight to lose. Appetite suppression is very strong at this level. Careful attention to protein intake and nutrition becomes especially important to preserve lean muscle mass.

15 mg: The Maximum Dose

The 15 mg dose produced the headline results from SURMOUNT-1: 22.5% average weight loss at 72 weeks, with 57% of participants losing 20% or more of their body weight. Not every patient needs to reach this dose, and not every patient tolerates it well. But for those who do, the weight loss results are the strongest available from any non-surgical intervention .

Customizing Your Dose: When the Standard Schedule Does Not Fit

Slowing Down Titration

If you experience significant side effects at any dose level, your physician may recommend:

  • Staying at the current dose for an additional 4 weeks before increasing
  • Stepping back to a lower dose temporarily
  • Increasing by smaller increments if using compounded tirzepatide (which allows more granular dose adjustments)

Stopping Below Maximum Dose

There is no requirement to reach 15 mg. If you are achieving good weight loss and tolerating a lower dose well, your physician may recommend maintaining that dose. Some patients achieve their goals at 7.5 mg or 10 mg and never need to go higher.

Dose Adjustments with Compounded Tirzepatide

One advantage of compounded tirzepatide (available through programs like Form Blends) is the ability to customize doses more precisely. Instead of jumping from 5 mg directly to 7.5 mg, a compounding pharmacy can prepare intermediate doses like 6 mg or 8 mg. This can help patients who are sensitive to dose changes titrate more gradually.

Timing and Administration

When to Inject

  • Inject once weekly, on the same day each week
  • You can choose any day that fits your schedule
  • If you need to change your injection day, ensure at least 72 hours (3 days) have passed since your last dose
  • Morning, afternoon, or evening injections are all acceptable. Some patients prefer evening injections so that any nausea peaks while they sleep

Where to Inject

  • Abdomen: At least 2 inches from the navel. This is the most common site.
  • Front of the thigh: Mid-thigh area
  • Upper arm: Back of the upper arm (may need assistance)

Rotate injection sites each week. Avoid injecting into the same exact spot consecutively to prevent skin irritation or lipodystrophy (changes in fat tissue at the injection site).

Missed Doses

If you miss a dose and fewer than 4 days (96 hours) have passed since the scheduled injection, take it as soon as you remember. If more than 4 days have passed, skip the missed dose and take the next one on your regular schedule. Do not double up on doses Mounjaro injection guide.

Dosing for Specific Situations

Patients with Type 2 Diabetes

The dosing schedule is the same regardless of whether you are using tirzepatide for diabetes or weight management. However, patients on insulin or sulfonylureas may need dose reductions of those medications as tirzepatide improves blood sugar control. Monitor blood glucose closely, especially during the first weeks at each new tirzepatide dose .

Patients with Kidney or Liver Issues

No dose adjustment is required for mild to moderate kidney or liver impairment. Tirzepatide has not been extensively studied in patients with severe renal or hepatic impairment, so extra caution and monitoring are warranted for these patients .

Older Adults

No dose adjustment is needed based on age alone. However, older adults may be more susceptible to dehydration from GI side effects. Extra attention to hydration and a potentially slower titration schedule may be appropriate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I feel fine on a lower dose? Do I still need to increase?

Not necessarily. If you are losing weight at a satisfactory pace and tolerating your current dose well, your physician may recommend staying there. The goal is to find the lowest effective dose for your needs, not to automatically push to the maximum.

Can I skip a dose level?

Eli Lilly's prescribing information recommends following the sequential titration from 2.5 mg upward. Skipping dose levels is generally not recommended, as it increases the risk of GI side effects. However, your physician can make clinical decisions based on your individual response.

How long does the full titration take?

Following the standard schedule (4 weeks at each level), reaching 15 mg takes approximately 20 weeks (about 5 months). If your physician slows the titration due to side effects, it may take longer.

What if I have side effects every time I increase?

Some GI effects during dose increases are normal and expected. If they are mild and resolve within 1 to 2 weeks, that is a typical response. If they are severe or persistent, your physician may slow the titration, hold your current dose longer, or consider whether a different medication might be more appropriate Mounjaro side effects.

Is there a difference in dosing between brand Mounjaro and compounded tirzepatide?

The active ingredient is the same. The dosing schedule follows the same principles. One advantage of compounded formulations is the ability to create custom intermediate doses (like 3.5 mg or 6 mg) that are not available in the brand pre-filled pens, which can help with more gradual titration.

What dose do most patients end up on?

This varies. In our clinical experience, a significant number of patients achieve their goals at 10 mg. Some need 12.5 mg or 15 mg. A smaller number do well at 5 mg or 7.5 mg. The right dose is the one that produces meaningful weight loss with acceptable side effects for you specifically.
