Appetite and Hunger Changes at 1.7mg

The 1.7mg dose delivers a significant step up in GLP-1 receptor activation compared to 1mg. Most patients notice:

  • Reduced food noise: The constant background chatter about food diminishes substantially. Many patients describe forgetting to eat or feeling indifferent toward meals they previously craved.
  • Earlier satiety: You may feel full after eating half or less of what you previously consumed. Portion sizes naturally decrease without conscious effort.
  • Fewer cravings: High-calorie, high-sugar foods become less appealing. Patients often report choosing lighter meals without willpower struggles.
  • Longer gaps between meals: Going 5 to 7 hours between eating without discomfort becomes common.

Weight Loss at 1.7mg: What the Data Shows

Clinical trial data shows that patients reaching the 1.7mg dose level typically achieve 10 to 12 percent total body weight loss by this point in treatment (around weeks 13 to 16). Individual results vary based on starting weight, diet, activity level, and metabolic factors.

At this dose, the rate of weight loss often accelerates compared to the earlier titration steps. Patients who had plateaued at 1mg frequently see renewed progress after increasing to 1.7mg.

Common Side Effects at 1.7mg

The dose increase from 1mg to 1.7mg can trigger a temporary return of side effects that you may have experienced during earlier titration steps. These typically resolve within 1 to 2 weeks as your body adjusts.

  • Nausea: The most common side effect at this dose level. Usually mild to moderate and worst during the first week.
  • Decreased appetite: While desired, some patients find the appetite suppression stronger than expected. Eating small, frequent meals can help.
  • Constipation or diarrhea: GI changes are common during dose adjustments. Staying hydrated and consuming adequate fiber can help manage these symptoms.
  • Fatigue: Some patients report tiredness during the first week at a new dose. This usually passes quickly.
  • Mild headache: Occasionally reported, often related to reduced caloric intake or dehydration rather than the medication itself.

What to Eat at the 1.7mg Dose

With stronger appetite suppression, nutrition quality becomes more important than ever. When you are eating less volume, every meal needs to count. Focus on:

  • Protein first: Aim for 25 to 30 grams of protein per meal. This preserves lean muscle mass during weight loss and supports satiety. Chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, and legumes are strong choices.
  • Hydration: Many patients underdrink at higher doses because reduced eating also reduces water intake from food. Aim for at least 64 ounces of water daily.
  • Small, frequent meals: Three smaller meals plus one to two snacks often works better than two large meals, which may trigger nausea.
  • Avoid greasy or heavy foods: These are more likely to cause nausea and GI discomfort at this dose level.

Energy and Exercise at 1.7mg

Most patients at the 1.7mg dose report stable or improved energy levels once they adjust to the new dose (usually after week one). Because you are eating less, fueling around workouts becomes important:

  • Eat a protein-rich snack 1 to 2 hours before exercise
  • Stay well hydrated before, during, and after activity
  • Focus on strength training 2 to 3 times per week to preserve muscle mass
  • Moderate cardio (walking, cycling, swimming) supports cardiovascular health and accelerates results

How Long to Stay at 1.7mg

The standard protocol is to remain at 1.7mg for at least 4 weeks before considering an increase to 2.4mg. However, some patients achieve their goals at 1.7mg and stay at this dose as their maintenance level. Your physician will evaluate:

  • Whether you are tolerating the dose well
  • Your rate of weight loss
  • Whether you have reached your target weight
  • Your overall health markers (blood sugar, blood pressure, cholesterol)

There is no requirement to advance to 2.4mg. Many patients do well long-term at 1.7mg.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1.7mg of semaglutide a high dose?

It is an intermediate-to-high dose. The maximum approved dose for weight management is 2.4mg weekly. At 1.7mg, you are receiving about 71 percent of the maximum dose, and most patients experience strong therapeutic effects at this level.

Will I feel more nauseous at 1.7mg than I did at 1mg?

Some patients do experience a temporary increase in nausea during the first week after stepping up to 1.7mg. This is typically milder than the initial nausea experienced when first starting semaglutide, because your body has already partially adapted to the medication.

How much weight should I have lost by the time I reach 1.7mg?

By week 13 (when most patients reach 1.7mg), average weight loss is approximately 8 to 12 percent of starting body weight. For a person starting at 220 pounds, that translates to roughly 18 to 26 pounds. Individual results vary widely.

Can I stay on 1.7mg permanently?

Yes. If you are achieving your goals and tolerating the medication well, your physician may recommend 1.7mg as your long-term maintenance dose. Not all patients need to reach the maximum 2.4mg dose.
