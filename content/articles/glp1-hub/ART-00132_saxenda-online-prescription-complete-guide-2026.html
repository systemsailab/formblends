Choosing a Telehealth Provider

Not all online prescription services are created equal. The quality difference between a thorough physician-supervised program and a quick prescription mill can significantly affect your outcomes, safety, and overall experience. Here is what to evaluate:

Must-Have Features

  • Licensed physicians. Your prescriber should be a board-certified physician (MD or DO) or a nurse practitioner with prescriptive authority in your state. Verify credentials through your state medical board if unsure.
  • Ongoing monitoring. Any program that writes a prescription and disappears is not providing adequate care. Look for regular check-ins (at least monthly) with a provider who can adjust your treatment.
  • Side effect management. The program should have a clear protocol for managing nausea, GI issues, and other side effects, including the ability to contact a provider between scheduled appointments.

  • Transparent pricing. Understand exactly what you are paying for: consultation fees, medication costs, shipping, and any monthly subscription charges.

Red Flags to Avoid

  • No medical evaluation. Any service that offers Saxenda without a proper health assessment is operating irresponsibly and potentially illegally.
  • Guaranteed approval. Legitimate providers sometimes determine that Saxenda is not appropriate for a patient. A platform that guarantees every applicant will receive a prescription is prioritizing revenue over safety.

  • Unlicensed providers. Ensure that the prescribing clinician is licensed in your state. Telehealth regulations generally require the provider to hold an active license in the state where the patient is located.
  • Pressure to buy additional products. Some platforms upsell unnecessary supplements, coaching packages, or "VIP" tiers. Your medication and physician oversight should be the core offering.

Insurance and Prior Authorization

Many commercial insurance plans cover Saxenda, but almost all require prior authorization. This means your provider must submit documentation to your insurer proving medical necessity. The typical requirements include:

  • Documented BMI meeting FDA criteria (30+ or 27+ with comorbidity)
  • Evidence of failed lifestyle intervention (usually 3 to 6 months of documented diet and exercise attempts)
  • Current lab work showing relevant health markers
  • A letter of medical necessity from your prescribing physician

The prior authorization process takes 3 to 14 days depending on the insurer. Some are approved on the first attempt; others require an appeal. A good telehealth program handles this administrative burden for you.

Important coverage notes for 2026:

  • Medicare Part D: Does not cover weight loss medications, including Saxenda. Legislative efforts to change this are ongoing but have not passed as of early 2026.
  • Medicaid: Coverage varies by state. Some state Medicaid programs cover Saxenda; many do not.
  • Tricare: Covers Saxenda with prior authorization for eligible beneficiaries.
  • Employer plans: Increasingly covering GLP-1 medications, though some have implemented quantity limits or step therapy requirements (requiring you to try cheaper medications first).

State-by-State Considerations

Telehealth prescribing regulations differ by state. While all 50 states allow some form of telehealth prescribing, specific rules about initial consultation requirements, prescribing authority, and controlled substance management vary:

  • Most states allow Saxenda to be prescribed via telehealth with a synchronous (live video) or asynchronous (messaging-based) consultation.
  • Some states require a synchronous video visit for the initial prescription, with asynchronous follow-ups permitted afterward.
  • A few states have specific requirements about establishing a patient-provider relationship before prescribing, which may require an in-person visit or additional documentation.

Saxenda is not a controlled substance (it is not a Schedule II-V drug), which simplifies telehealth prescribing compared to medications like phentermine. Reputable telehealth platforms ensure compliance with the specific regulations in your state.
