Tirzepatide 5mg: What To Do If You Miss a Dose

If you miss a dose of tirzepatide 5mg, take it as soon as you remember if fewer than 4 days (96 hours) have passed since your scheduled injection. If more than 4 days have elapsed, skip the missed dose and take your next dose on the regularly scheduled day. Do not take two doses within 3 days of each other. {{CITE:tirzepatide prescribing information missed dose}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:tirzepatide 5mg missed dose guidance}}

The 96-Hour Rule at 5mg

The missed dose protocol for tirzepatide is the same at all dose levels, including 5mg. The critical threshold is 96 hours (4 days) from your scheduled injection time. {{CITE:tirzepatide missed dose 96-hour cutoff}}

Here is a practical decision guide:

  • Missed by 1 day: Take it immediately. Resume your normal schedule next week.
  • Missed by 2 days: Take it. You can either shift your weekly injection day or return to your original day (maintain at least 3 days between doses).
  • Missed by 3 days: Take it. Shift your injection day forward or wait the minimum 3 days before your next regular dose.
  • Missed by 4 days: This is the cutoff. If exactly 96 hours have passed, you are at the boundary. Most prescribers recommend taking it if you are within the window, even if barely.
  • Missed by 5 or more days: Skip the missed dose. Resume on your next scheduled injection day at your normal 5mg dose.

How Missing a 5mg Dose Affects You

At 5mg, missing a dose has slightly more noticeable effects than missing a dose at 2.5mg because your body has adapted to a higher therapeutic level. Here is what to expect:

  • Return of appetite: Most patients notice increased hunger beginning 3 to 5 days after their last injection. This is the clearest signal that the medication is clearing your system.
  • Food noise: The mental preoccupation with food that tirzepatide suppresses may return. Patients often describe this as "the food noise coming back." {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide and food noise}}
  • Blood sugar changes: For patients with type 2 diabetes, blood sugar may rise after a missed dose. Monitor more frequently and contact your prescriber if readings are consistently elevated. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:blood sugar impact of missed tirzepatide dose}}
  • Digestive changes: Some patients notice that their digestion speeds up after missing a dose as the gastroparesis effect wears off.

A single missed dose will not undo your progress. Weight regain from one missed injection is minimal to nonexistent.

Resuming After a Missed Dose

When you take your next 5mg dose after a gap, you may experience a mild return of side effects similar to when you first started the dose. This is normal and typically less intense than your initial adjustment period.

To minimize discomfort when resuming:

  • Eat smaller meals on injection day and the following day
  • Avoid fatty and greasy foods for the first 48 hours
  • Stay well hydrated
  • Take your injection in the evening if nausea is a concern (sleeping through the peak nausea window)

Missing Multiple Consecutive Doses

If you have missed two or more consecutive 5mg doses, the situation requires more careful handling:

  • Two missed doses: Contact your prescriber. Most will advise restarting at 5mg if you were previously tolerating it well. Some may recommend a brief return to 2.5mg for one to two weeks.
  • Three or more missed doses: The medication has fully cleared your system. Your prescriber will likely restart you at 2.5mg for at least two to four weeks before stepping back up to 5mg. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:tirzepatide restart protocol after extended gap}}

The reason for restarting at a lower dose after an extended gap is safety. Your GI tolerance resets when the drug clears your system. Jumping back to 5mg after several weeks off increases the risk of severe nausea and vomiting. {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide titration schedule}}

Preventing Missed Doses

Consistency matters at 5mg because this is a therapeutic dose where the medication is producing its core effects. Strategies to maintain adherence:

  • Weekly alarm: Set a non-dismissable alarm on your phone for the same day and time each week.
  • Medication tracker: Use an app like Medisafe or MyTherapy that sends reminders and tracks adherence.
  • Visible storage: Keep your pen on a designated refrigerator shelf where you see it daily.
  • Travel preparation: When traveling, pack your pen in an insulated case and set reminders adjusted for time zone changes. {{INTERNAL_LINK:traveling with tirzepatide}}
  • Backup plan: Know the 96-hour rule by heart so you can act quickly if you realize you missed a dose while away from home.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take my tirzepatide 5mg dose 2 days early?
Taking a dose early is generally not recommended. The minimum interval between doses is 3 days (72 hours). If you need to shift your injection day, discuss the change with your prescriber first. {{CITE:tirzepatide minimum dosing interval}}
Should I double my dose after missing one?
Absolutely not. Never take 10mg to compensate for a missed 5mg dose. This significantly increases the risk of severe side effects including vomiting, diarrhea, and dehydration. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:danger of doubling tirzepatide dose}}
Will I gain weight from one missed dose?
One missed dose is unlikely to cause measurable weight gain. You may see a temporary increase on the scale from water retention or increased food intake, but this typically resolves within days of resuming your medication.
I missed my dose and my next one is in 2 days. Should I still take the missed one?
No. You must maintain at least 3 days (72 hours) between doses. If fewer than 3 days remain until your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and resume on schedule. {{CITE:tirzepatide 72-hour minimum between doses}}
Does missing a dose delay my ability to titrate to 7.5mg?
One missed dose usually does not affect the titration timeline. However, if you have missed multiple doses, your prescriber may want you to spend additional time at 5mg before considering an increase. {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide 7.5mg switching to}}
