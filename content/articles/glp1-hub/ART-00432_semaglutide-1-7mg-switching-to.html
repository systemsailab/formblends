Switching to semaglutide 1.7mg is straightforward when you follow the proper titration schedule. If you are moving up from semaglutide 1mg, simply increase your next weekly injection to 1.7mg after completing at least 4 weeks at 1mg. If you are switching from another GLP-1 medication like liraglutide or tirzepatide, your physician will determine the appropriate starting dose and transition timing.

Switching from Semaglutide 1mg to 1.7mg

This is the most common transition path. After spending at least 4 weeks at 1mg with acceptable tolerability, you can step up to 1.7mg on your next scheduled injection day. No washout period is needed.

Checklist Before Stepping Up

  • Tolerability: You should be managing side effects well at 1mg. If you still have significant nausea or GI issues at 1mg, your physician may recommend staying at that dose longer before increasing.
  • Weight loss trajectory: If you are still losing weight steadily at 1mg, there may not be an immediate need to increase. Your provider will weigh the benefit of faster results against the risk of more side effects.
  • Minimum time at 1mg: At least 4 weeks. Some protocols recommend staying longer if side effects were notable at 1mg.

What to Expect in the First Week

The transition from 1mg to 1.7mg represents a 70 percent dose increase, which is the largest single step in the standard titration. Expect:

  • Temporary return of mild nausea (usually resolves in 3 to 5 days)
  • Stronger appetite suppression than you experienced at 1mg
  • Possible mild fatigue during the first few days
  • Some patients report feeling slightly "off" for 24 to 48 hours after the first injection at this dose

Switching from Another GLP-1 Medication to Semaglutide 1.7mg

From Liraglutide (Saxenda)

Liraglutide is a daily GLP-1 agonist, while semaglutide is weekly. The switch requires careful physician supervision. A common approach:

  • Stop liraglutide on the day before starting semaglutide
  • Start semaglutide at 0.25mg and follow the full titration schedule
  • Your physician may recommend an accelerated titration (shorter time at each step) since your body is already accustomed to GLP-1 receptor activation
  • Do not start directly at 1.7mg without completing at least an abbreviated titration

From Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound)

Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist, so switching to semaglutide (GLP-1 only) is a different pharmacological profile.

  • Allow at least one week after your last tirzepatide dose before starting semaglutide
  • Your physician will determine the starting dose based on your previous tirzepatide dose level
  • Patients switching from tirzepatide 5mg or higher may start semaglutide at a higher initial dose, but this requires physician approval
  • Expect a transition period where appetite suppression may temporarily decrease, since the GIP receptor component is no longer active

From Compounded Semaglutide to Brand-Name

If you are switching from a compounded semaglutide formulation to FDA-approved semaglutide (or vice versa), the transition is simpler since the active ingredient is the same. Key considerations:

  • Confirm the exact dose you were taking in the compounded version (compounding pharmacies may use different concentration formats)
  • Continue at the equivalent dose on your regular schedule
  • Report any differences in efficacy or side effects to your provider, as absorption can vary between formulations

Switching from a Different Medication Class

From Phentermine

Phentermine is a stimulant-based appetite suppressant with a completely different mechanism. Switching to semaglutide involves:

  • Typically discontinue phentermine and start semaglutide at 0.25mg with full titration
  • Some physicians allow a brief overlap period, but this is off-label and requires close monitoring
  • Expect a different appetite suppression profile: semaglutide works on satiety and food reward pathways rather than stimulant-driven appetite reduction

From Metformin

Metformin is often used alongside GLP-1 medications rather than replaced. Your physician will determine whether to continue metformin when starting semaglutide, as the combination can be beneficial for patients with insulin resistance.

Timing Your Switch

Plan your transition thoughtfully:

  • Avoid switching during travel or major events: The first week at a new dose can involve GI adjustment. Schedule your first 1.7mg injection during a normal week.
  • Keep your injection day consistent: Switch doses on your regular injection day for simplicity.
  • Have supplies ready: Make sure you have received your 1.7mg pen or vial before your injection day so you do not miss a week waiting for delivery.

Managing the Transition Period

The first 1 to 2 weeks after switching to 1.7mg are the adjustment window. Here is how to make it smoother:

  • Eat before injecting: Having a light meal before your injection can reduce nausea
  • Stay hydrated: Increase water intake to at least 64 ounces daily
  • Reduce portion sizes proactively: Do not wait until nausea forces you to eat less. Smaller meals from day one help prevent discomfort.
  • Track symptoms: Keep a simple log of any side effects, their severity, and how long they last. This information is valuable for your next provider check-in.
  • Be patient: Give your body the full 2 weeks to adjust before deciding the dose is not right for you.

When Not to Switch to 1.7mg

Your physician may recommend staying at your current dose or exploring other options if:

  • You are still experiencing significant side effects at 1mg after 4 or more weeks
  • You are losing weight steadily at your current dose and there is no clinical reason to increase
  • You have a history of pancreatitis or gallbladder disease that could be worsened by higher doses
  • You are pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding
  • Your kidney or liver function requires dose adjustment

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch directly from 0.5mg to 1.7mg, skipping 1mg?

This is not recommended. Skipping dose levels increases the risk of severe nausea and other GI side effects. The gradual titration exists to help your body adapt safely.

How long does it take to feel the full effect of 1.7mg after switching?

Most patients reach steady-state drug levels at the new dose within 4 to 5 weeks of consistent weekly dosing. However, appetite and weight loss effects are typically noticeable within the first 1 to 2 weeks.

Will I gain weight between stopping my old medication and starting semaglutide?

Can I switch from tirzepatide 2.5mg directly to semaglutide 1.7mg?

This requires physician evaluation. The two medications have different mechanisms and potencies. Most providers recommend starting semaglutide at a lower dose (0.5mg or 1mg) and titrating up, even if you were on a moderate tirzepatide dose.
