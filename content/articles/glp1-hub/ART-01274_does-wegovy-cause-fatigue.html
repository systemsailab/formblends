Does Wegovy Cause Fatigue?

Wegovy can cause fatigue in some patients. In clinical trials, approximately 11 percent of participants taking Wegovy 2.4 mg reported fatigue compared to about 5 percent on placebo. It is one of the more commonly reported non-GI side effects and tends to be most noticeable during early treatment and dose escalation.

Detailed Explanation

Wegovy is the brand name for semaglutide 2.4 mg, approved specifically for chronic weight management in adults with obesity (BMI of 30 or greater) or overweight (BMI of 27 or greater) with at least one weight-related comorbidity. Because Wegovy uses a higher dose of semaglutide than Ozempic (which maxes out at 2 mg), fatigue rates tend to be somewhat higher.

Clinical Trial Data on Fatigue

The STEP clinical trial program provides the most robust data on Wegovy's side effect profile. Across STEP 1 through STEP 5, fatigue was consistently reported at higher rates in the semaglutide group than in the placebo group. In STEP 1, which enrolled over 1,900 adults without diabetes, fatigue occurred in approximately 11 percent of the treatment group. This makes it one of the top non-gastrointestinal adverse events associated with the medication.

Why Wegovy Causes More Fatigue Than Lower-Dose Semaglutide

Wegovy's 2.4 mg dose produces a more pronounced appetite suppression effect than lower doses used for diabetes. Patients on Wegovy typically experience a greater caloric deficit, which directly contributes to feelings of tiredness. The average weight loss in STEP trials was approximately 15 percent of body weight, representing a significant metabolic shift that the body must adapt to over time.

The dose-escalation schedule for Wegovy spans 16 weeks, starting at 0.25 mg and increasing monthly to 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.7 mg, and finally 2.4 mg. Each dose increase can temporarily worsen fatigue as the body adjusts to stronger appetite suppression and metabolic changes. Patients often report that fatigue peaks during the transition to the 1.7 mg and 2.4 mg doses.

Contributing Factors

Beyond the direct pharmacological effects, several lifestyle factors can amplify fatigue on Wegovy. Rapid reduction in carbohydrate intake can cause temporary energy dips as the body shifts toward fat metabolism. Inadequate protein consumption accelerates muscle loss during weight loss, which reduces overall stamina. Poor hydration, disrupted sleep from nausea, and the psychological adjustment to a new eating pattern all play supporting roles.

When Fatigue Typically Resolves

Most patients report that fatigue is worst during the first one to two months and during the final dose-escalation steps. Once patients have been on the full 2.4 mg dose for four to eight weeks, energy levels generally stabilize. Some patients report feeling more energetic than before treatment once they have lost a significant amount of weight and their fitness improves.

What to Consider

  • Prioritize protein at every meal to preserve lean muscle mass, which is critical for maintaining energy during weight loss.
  • Avoid crash-diet behavior. Even though appetite is reduced, consuming at least 1,200 calories daily (or as directed by your physician) helps prevent severe fatigue.
  • Stay hydrated with at least 64 ounces of water per day.
  • Light physical activity such as walking or stretching can help improve energy levels and counteract fatigue.
  • Discuss with your prescriber if fatigue is severe or does not improve, as they may slow the dose-escalation timeline.
  • Consider a multivitamin or targeted supplementation if blood work reveals deficiencies.
