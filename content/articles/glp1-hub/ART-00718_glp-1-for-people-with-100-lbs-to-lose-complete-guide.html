The Non-Negotiable Habits

Protein at Every Meal

You cannot lose 100 pounds without losing some muscle. You can minimize the loss with adequate protein. This is the single most important nutritional habit for your journey. Every meal, every snack, protein first. If you eat nothing else right, eat your protein.

Strength Training

Cardio burns calories. Strength training preserves the muscle that keeps your metabolism running. As you lose weight, your caloric needs decrease. Maintaining muscle through resistance training slows this metabolic decline and produces a better-looking, more functional body at your goal weight.

Water

80 to 100+ ounces daily. GLP-1 medication side effects (nausea, constipation) worsen with dehydration. Your kidneys need water to process the metabolic byproducts of fat breakdown. Carry a bottle everywhere. Drink before you feel thirsty.

Sleep

Seven to nine hours per night. Sleep deprivation elevates hunger hormones and reduces insulin sensitivity, directly opposing what your GLP-1 medication is trying to do. Good sleep is not a luxury on a 100-pound journey. It is a performance enhancer.

What if my weight loss stalls at 50 lbs?

Plateaus during major weight loss are expected. Your provider may increase your dose, switch medications, review your nutrition, or add strategies to break through. Do not interpret a plateau as failure. It is a data point that guides the next adjustment.

Will people treat me differently after losing 100 lbs?

Yes, and it can be complicated. Many people experience mixed emotions about the social changes that accompany major weight loss. The world is kinder to smaller bodies, and recognizing that reality after years at a higher weight can bring up anger, relief, and sadness simultaneously. Professional support helps you process these changes healthily.

Should I tell people I am on medication?

That is entirely your choice. You owe no one an explanation for your medical decisions. Some people find that sharing creates accountability and support. Others prefer privacy. Both approaches are valid.
