State-by-State Considerations

While telehealth prescribing is legal nationwide for non-controlled substances like semaglutide, some states have additional requirements:

  • States requiring synchronous consultation: A few states require a live video or phone consultation before the first prescription. Your provider will handle scheduling if applicable.
  • Prescriber licensing: The physician must hold an active medical license in your state of residence. Reputable telehealth programs verify this automatically.
  • Pharmacy regulations: Some states have specific rules about which pharmacies can ship medications across state lines. This primarily affects compounded medications.

Form Blends operates in compliance with each state's telehealth regulations and will inform you of any state-specific requirements during your intake process.

Red Flags: How to Spot Illegitimate Providers

Not every online weight loss program operates responsibly. Watch for these warning signs:

  • No physician involvement. If a website offers to ship you semaglutide without any medical evaluation, that is illegal and dangerous.
  • AI-only evaluation. An algorithm cannot replace physician clinical judgment. Your intake should be reviewed by a licensed MD or DO.
  • No follow-up care. A prescription without ongoing monitoring is irresponsible. Your provider should check in regularly and be available for questions.
  • Unusually low prices. If the price seems too good to be true, the medication may be sourced from unregulated suppliers, counterfeit, or improperly compounded.
  • Guaranteed approval. Any program that guarantees you will be approved before evaluating your medical history is not practicing medicine. Some patients genuinely should not take semaglutide.
  • No pharmacy information. Legitimate providers will tell you which pharmacy fills their prescriptions. If they will not disclose this, that is a red flag.
