Does Compounded Semaglutide Affect Your Period?

Compounded semaglutide can affect your period in the same ways that brand-name semaglutide does, primarily through the hormonal shifts that accompany weight loss. The active molecule is identical to what is in Ozempic and Wegovy, so the metabolic effects on your body, including changes to estrogen levels and reproductive hormone balance, are the same. These menstrual changes are typically temporary.

Many patients choose compounded semaglutide for its accessibility and cost advantages. If you are one of them and have noticed your period changing since starting treatment, the explanations and guidance here apply to your situation just as they would for any other semaglutide formulation.

Why Your Period May Change on Compounded Semaglutide

The mechanism is straightforward. As compounded semaglutide helps you lose weight, your body fat decreases. Fat tissue produces estrogen through a process called aromatization. Less body fat means less peripheral estrogen production, which changes the hormonal signals governing your menstrual cycle.

Additionally, semaglutide improves insulin sensitivity. For women with insulin resistance (common in PCOS and obesity), lower insulin levels allow reproductive hormones like FSH and LH to function more normally. This can restore regular ovulation in patients who previously had absent or irregular cycles.

Typical Changes Patients Notice

  • Lighter periods: The most common change, resulting from reduced estrogen and thinner endometrial lining
  • More regular cycles: Improved hormonal balance often leads to more predictable timing
  • Period resumption: Women who had lost their period due to obesity or PCOS may start menstruating again
  • Spotting between periods: Hormonal fluctuations during active weight loss can cause breakthrough bleeding
  • Temporary irregularity: The first few months of treatment often show the most cycle variability

These changes mirror what patients on brand-name Ozempic and Wegovy experience because the active drug is the same.

Contraception While Using Compounded Semaglutide

Two contraception factors deserve attention. First, if weight loss restores your ovulatory function, you could become pregnant when you previously could not. This is a real possibility for women with PCOS or obesity-related anovulation. compounded semaglutide and fertility

Second, semaglutide slows gastric emptying, which could theoretically reduce absorption of oral birth control pills. While this effect has not been conclusively proven for semaglutide specifically, many providers recommend a backup non-oral contraceptive method as a precaution. IUDs, implants, and injectable contraceptives are unaffected by stomach motility changes.

When to Contact Your Provider

Most period changes on compounded semaglutide are benign. However, reach out to your healthcare provider if you experience:

  • Absence of periods for three or more consecutive months
  • Very heavy bleeding (soaking through a pad or tampon every hour for several hours)
  • New or severe pelvic pain that was not present before treatment
  • Persistent spotting that does not resolve within the first few months
