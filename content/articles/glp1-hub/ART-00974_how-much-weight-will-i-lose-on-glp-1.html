What Are GLP-1 Medications and How Do They Cause Weight Loss?

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1, a hormone your body naturally produces after eating. GLP-1 medications (also called GLP-1 receptor agonists) mimic this hormone at higher, more sustained levels. The result is a significant reduction in appetite, slower stomach emptying, and improved blood sugar regulation. {{CITE:claim}}

Several GLP-1 medications are available today, including:

  • Semaglutide (found in {{INTERNAL_LINK:Ozempic}} and {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy}})
  • Tirzepatide (found in {{INTERNAL_LINK:Mounjaro}} and {{INTERNAL_LINK:Zepbound}}), which targets both GLP-1 and GIP receptors
  • Compounded semaglutide, a more affordable option prepared by licensed pharmacies

All of these medications share the same core mechanism: they help your brain recognize fullness sooner and reduce cravings between meals.

What Does a Realistic Weight Loss Timeline Look Like?

GLP-1 weight loss happens gradually, which is actually a good thing for long-term success. Rapid weight loss can lead to muscle loss and nutritional deficiencies. Here is what a typical timeline looks like:

  • Month 1: You begin at a low dose. Appetite starts to decrease. Weight loss of 3 to 6 pounds is typical.
  • Months 2 to 3: Your dose increases to a therapeutic level. You may lose 1 to 3 pounds per week.
  • Months 4 to 6: Many patients have lost 8% to 12% of their starting weight by this point.
  • Months 6 to 12: Continued weight loss or transition to maintenance, depending on your goals.

Plateaus are a normal part of the process. They do not mean the medication has failed. Our team monitors your progress and adjusts your plan as needed.

What Can I Do to Maximize My Weight Loss on GLP-1?

GLP-1 medications are most effective when combined with lifestyle changes. Here are the strategies that make the biggest difference:

  1. Prioritize protein. Eating enough protein (0.7 to 1 gram per pound of goal body weight) helps preserve muscle mass during weight loss.
  2. Move your body regularly. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week. Strength training is especially helpful.
  3. Stay hydrated. Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Dehydration can worsen side effects and stall progress.
  4. Follow your dosing schedule. Consistency matters. Take your medication at the same time each week.
  5. Limit processed foods and sugar. These can trigger cravings and undermine the appetite-reducing effects of your medication.
  6. Get adequate sleep. Poor sleep disrupts hunger hormones and can slow weight loss. {{CITE:claim}}

Do GLP-1 Medications Work for Everyone?

While GLP-1 medications are effective for a large majority of patients, results vary. In clinical trials, a small percentage of participants (roughly 5% to 10%) did not achieve significant weight loss. {{CITE:claim}} Factors that may limit effectiveness include certain medical conditions, other medications that promote weight gain, and difficulty tolerating therapeutic doses.

Will I Keep the Weight Off After Stopping GLP-1 Medication?

Studies show that patients who stop GLP-1 medications without maintaining lifestyle changes tend to regain a portion of the weight within a year. {{CITE:claim}} This is because the hormonal signals that the medication was providing return to their previous state.

Long-term success requires building habits that last beyond the medication. Some patients stay on a lower maintenance dose to help sustain their results. Others transition off medication entirely after reaching their goal weight, supported by a solid nutrition and exercise foundation.
