Managing Side Effects at Three Months

The step to 1.0 mg is the largest relative dose increase in the semaglutide titration. Here is what to watch for :

  • Nausea: Brief and usually milder than the initial episode. Eating bland, small meals on injection day helps.
  • Constipation: May intensify at 1.0 mg. A daily routine of psyllium husk, magnesium citrate, and 80+ ounces of water is effective for most patients.
  • Hair shedding: Some patients notice increased hair loss around month three. This is related to rapid weight loss (telogen effluvium) rather than the medication itself and is usually temporary .
  • Acid reflux: Slowed gastric emptying can worsen heartburn. Eating smaller meals and avoiding lying down after eating helps.
