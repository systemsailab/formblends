Side Effects at Six Months

At the maintenance dose, most patients have adapted well :

  • GI effects: Minimal for most patients at stable doses. Eating too much or too fast may cause temporary discomfort.
  • Constipation: Managed with ongoing fiber and hydration routine.
  • Nutritional concerns: Reduced food intake requires intentional nutrient-dense eating to prevent deficiencies.
  • Hair shedding: If present, typically begins to resolve around month 6 as weight loss rate slows.
  • Skin changes: Patients with 30+ pounds of loss may notice skin laxity in areas like the abdomen and upper arms.
