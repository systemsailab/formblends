Semaglutide Results After 4 Months: What to Expect

Four months on semaglutide is a milestone worth recognizing. If you are looking for semaglutide results after 4 months, you will find that most patients have lost 14 to 22 pounds by this point, representing about 6% to 9% of their starting body weight . You are now at or approaching the higher dose range where the medication's full weight loss potential begins to emerge.

Dosing at Four Months

By week 16, most semaglutide patients are at 1.7 mg weekly (Wegovy dosing) or approaching the 2.0 mg maximum (Ozempic dosing) . This is a critical inflection point because:

  • 1.7 mg is the second-to-last step on the Wegovy ladder
  • Appetite suppression at this dose is considerably stronger than at 1.0 mg
  • Metabolic effects on blood sugar and lipids are near their peak
  • Weight loss rate is accelerating compared to earlier months

Side Effects at Four Months

Most patients are well-adapted to semaglutide by month four, but the 1.7 mg dose can bring some new considerations :

  • Nausea: Brief recurrence with dose increase, typically resolving in 2 to 3 days.
  • Constipation: May require ongoing management. Consider a fiber supplement plus 80+ ounces of water daily.
  • Fatigue: Caloric deficit from reduced appetite can cause low energy. Ensure you are eating enough protein and healthy fats.
  • Hair thinning: If present, it usually peaks around months 3 to 5 and resolves on its own. Biotin and a balanced diet support hair health.
  • Muscle loss: Without adequate protein and resistance training, weight loss can include lean muscle mass. This is preventable with proper nutrition and exercise.

Strategies for Month Four Success

  • Push protein to 100 to 120 grams daily. As your appetite decreases further, each meal needs to prioritize protein.
  • Strength train consistently. At least 3 sessions per week focused on major muscle groups.
  • Schedule a body composition scan. DEXA or InBody scans reveal whether you are losing fat vs. muscle.
  • Review your supplement stack. At minimum: multivitamin, vitamin D, omega-3, and potentially collagen peptides.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 to 22 pounds of weight loss at 4 months on track?

Yes. This represents 6% to 9% of body weight, which is consistent with clinical trial data. You still have room for significant additional loss as you approach or reach the 2.4 mg maintenance dose semaglutide results after 6 months.

When does semaglutide weight loss peak?

Most patients experience peak weight loss rate between months 4 and 8, with total weight loss continuing through months 12 to 15. After that, weight stabilizes at a new baseline .

Should I worry about losing muscle mass?

Muscle loss is a real concern during any significant weight loss. The best prevention is consuming adequate protein (at least 0.7 grams per pound of body weight daily) and performing resistance exercises 2 to 3 times per week .

Can I stay at 1.7 mg instead of going to 2.4 mg?

Some patients achieve their goals at 1.7 mg and stay there. Others benefit from the full 2.4 mg dose. Your provider will help determine the right dose based on your results, tolerability, and goals .

What happens if I hit a plateau at month four?

Plateaus are normal. Your body may be adjusting to a new setpoint. Solutions include increasing exercise, adjusting calorie intake, ensuring adequate sleep and stress management, and potentially advancing to a higher dose.
