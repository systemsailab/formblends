Does Wegovy Affect Your Period?

Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg) can affect your period through the hormonal shifts that accompany significant weight loss. The medication itself does not directly target reproductive hormones, but losing 15% or more of your body weight changes your hormonal landscape in ways that commonly alter menstrual cycle patterns. Most of these changes are temporary and resolve as your body adjusts.

Wegovy is prescribed at a higher dose than Ozempic and is specifically indicated for weight management, which means its users often experience more pronounced weight loss and, consequently, more noticeable hormonal shifts. This makes period changes a particularly relevant topic for Wegovy patients.

Why Significant Weight Loss Affects Menstruation

Adipose tissue is not just storage. It functions as an endocrine organ, actively producing estrogen and other hormones that regulate your reproductive cycle. When Wegovy helps you shed a significant amount of body fat, estrogen production from fat tissue drops proportionally.

This hormonal recalibration affects the entire chain of events that governs menstruation. The hypothalamus and pituitary gland, which send signals to the ovaries, adjust to the new estrogen levels over weeks to months. During this transition, cycle length, flow volume, and timing can all fluctuate.

For patients whose periods were previously heavy or irregular due to excess estrogen from obesity, the shift often brings welcome improvement. Lighter, more predictable cycles are a common positive outcome of reaching a healthier weight.

What Changes Are Most Common

  • Lighter flow: Reduced body fat means less estrogen-driven endometrial buildup
  • More regular cycles: Hormonal balance improves for many patients who previously had irregular periods
  • Temporary irregularity: During the first 3 to 6 months, cycles may be unpredictable as hormones adjust
  • Return of ovulation: Patients with PCOS or obesity-related anovulation may start ovulating again
  • Occasional spotting: Breakthrough bleeding can occur during the hormonal transition phase

Contraception Matters More on Wegovy

Two factors make contraception especially important for Wegovy users. First, weight loss can restore fertility in women who had obesity-related anovulation, meaning pregnancy becomes possible where it previously was not. Second, Wegovy slows gastric emptying, which could theoretically reduce the absorption of oral birth control pills.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are period changes on Wegovy permanent?

Usually not. Most menstrual changes stabilize once your weight and hormonal levels settle into a new baseline. The transition period typically lasts 3 to 6 months. If changes persist beyond that, your healthcare provider can investigate further.

Can Wegovy cause heavier periods?

Temporarily heavier periods are possible during the early hormonal adjustment phase, though this is less common than periods becoming lighter. If heavy bleeding persists or is accompanied by severe cramping, see your provider to rule out other causes.

Should I be concerned if my period stops on Wegovy?

An occasional missed period during rapid weight loss is not unusual. However, if you miss three or more consecutive periods, see your doctor. They will check for pregnancy and may evaluate your hormone levels and nutritional status to make sure you are not losing weight too quickly.

Can I get pregnant more easily while on Wegovy?

Yes, particularly if you had obesity-related infertility or PCOS. Weight loss can restore ovulation in women who were not ovulating regularly. If pregnancy is not your goal, consistent contraception is essential throughout treatment. Wegovy and fertility

Does Wegovy make PMS symptoms worse?

Some patients report that Wegovy's GI side effects (nausea, bloating) overlap with and worsen PMS symptoms during the days leading up to and during their period. Eating smaller, more frequent meals and staying well hydrated may help manage the overlap.
