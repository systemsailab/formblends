Liraglutide 1.8mg Weight Loss: What the Data Actually Shows

The average weight loss on liraglutide 1.8mg ranges from about 4% to 6% of body weight based on clinical trial data, though individual results vary widely. If your physician has prescribed liraglutide at this dose, understanding realistic expectations can help you stay on track and make informed decisions about your treatment.

Clinical Trial Results at 1.8mg

Keep in mind that the LEAD participants were managing type 2 diabetes, not specifically seeking weight loss. Their baseline BMI, concomitant medications, and study goals were different from dedicated weight-management trials.

How 1.8mg Compares to Other Liraglutide Doses

Weight loss with liraglutide is dose-dependent. In the SCALE Obesity and Prediabetes trial, the 3.0mg daily dose produced an average weight loss of 8.0% of body weight over 56 weeks, compared to 2.6% for placebo. {{CITE:SCALE Obesity and Prediabetes: liraglutide 3.0mg produced 8.0% mean weight loss vs 2.6% placebo over 56 weeks.}}

A dose-ranging study published in The Lancet showed a clear gradient:

  • 1.2mg daily: approximately 4.8 kg lost over 20 weeks
  • 1.8mg daily: approximately 5.5 kg lost over 20 weeks
  • 2.4mg daily: approximately 6.3 kg lost over 20 weeks
  • 3.0mg daily: approximately 7.2 kg lost over 20 weeks

{{CITE:Astrup et al., Lancet 2009: liraglutide dose-ranging study showing dose-dependent weight loss over 20 weeks.}}

This means liraglutide 1.8mg delivers roughly 70 to 75% of the weight loss seen at the full 3.0mg dose. For patients who tolerate 1.8mg well but struggle with side effects at higher doses, this can be a practical long-term option.

What Affects Your Individual Results

Averages from clinical trials are useful benchmarks, but your personal experience may differ. Several factors play a role:

  • Starting weight: Patients with higher baseline BMI often see larger absolute weight loss in kilograms, though the percentage may be similar.
  • Diet quality: Liraglutide reduces appetite, but it works best when paired with a balanced, calorie-appropriate eating plan. Patients who make deliberate dietary changes tend to lose more. {{INTERNAL_LINK:diet tips while on GLP-1 medications}}
  • Physical activity: Regular exercise amplifies weight loss and helps preserve lean muscle mass during treatment.
  • Metabolic health: Insulin resistance, thyroid function, and other metabolic factors influence the pace and extent of weight loss.
  • Medication adherence: Liraglutide requires daily injections. Missing doses reduces drug exposure and can slow progress.
  • Concomitant medications: Some drugs (beta-blockers, certain antidepressants, insulin) can promote weight gain, partially offsetting the effects of liraglutide.

When to Discuss Moving to a Higher Dose

The Saxenda prescribing information recommends evaluating weight loss at 12 weeks on the maintenance dose (3.0mg). If a patient has not lost at least 4% of baseline body weight by that point, discontinuation should be considered. {{CITE:Saxenda prescribing information: evaluate response at 12 weeks; consider discontinuation if less than 4% weight loss.}}

A similar principle can guide decisions at 1.8mg. If you have been at this dose for 8 to 12 weeks and have not seen meaningful progress, your physician may recommend advancing to 2.4mg and eventually 3.0mg, or exploring alternative medications. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide titration schedule}}

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 5 to 6% body weight loss meaningful?

Yes. A 5% reduction in body weight has been shown to improve blood pressure, cholesterol levels, blood sugar control, and reduce the risk of developing type 2 diabetes. Clinical guidelines consider 5% a clinically significant threshold. {{CITE:A 5% weight loss is associated with clinically meaningful improvements in cardiometabolic risk factors.}}

Will I lose more weight if I increase to 3.0mg?

On average, yes. The dose-response relationship with liraglutide is well established. The jump from 1.8mg to 3.0mg typically adds another 2 to 3 percentage points of body weight loss. However, side effects also tend to increase, so the decision involves balancing efficacy and tolerability. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide 3.0mg weight loss average}}

How does liraglutide 1.8mg compare to semaglutide for weight loss?

Semaglutide at its approved weight loss dose (2.4mg weekly) produces significantly greater weight loss than liraglutide 1.8mg daily. The STEP 1 trial reported approximately 14.9% body weight loss with semaglutide 2.4mg, compared to the 4 to 6% range typically seen with liraglutide 1.8mg. {{CITE:STEP 1: semaglutide 2.4mg weekly achieved ~14.9% weight loss vs ~2.4% with placebo over 68 weeks.}} Your physician will help determine which medication best fits your health profile.

Can I keep losing weight at 1.8mg long-term?

Most patients reach a weight plateau within 6 to 12 months at any given dose. Continued weight loss after that typically requires dose adjustment, dietary changes, or increased physical activity. Maintaining the weight you have already lost is also a valid and important goal.
