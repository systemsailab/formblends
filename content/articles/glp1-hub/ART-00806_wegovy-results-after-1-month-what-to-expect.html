Week-by-Week Guide to Your First Month on Wegovy

Week 1: Starting at 0.25 mg

Your first Wegovy injection is a 0.25 mg prefilled pen . This dose is approximately one-tenth of the full therapeutic dose, so do not expect dramatic changes right away.

  • You may feel a slight decrease in appetite by day two or three
  • Mild nausea is common, affecting about 20% of patients
  • The injection itself is nearly painless using the prefilled autoinjector
  • Scale weight may not change at all

Week 2: Building Medication Levels

With your second injection, semaglutide begins to accumulate in your system. The medication has a half-life of about one week, meaning it takes several doses to reach steady blood levels.

  • Appetite suppression becomes slightly more noticeable
  • First-week nausea typically begins to subside

  • You may observe that you feel satisfied sooner at meals

Week 3: Appetite Control Emerges

This is typically when patients start to feel genuine appetite changes. The medication is reaching more consistent levels, and the GLP-1 receptor activation becomes more apparent:

  • Interest in high-calorie comfort foods may decline
  • Cumulative weight loss of 1 to 3 pounds is common
  • Some patients report better sleep and more stable energy

Week 4: Preparing for the 0.5 mg Step

By the end of month one, your body has adapted to the 0.25 mg dose. Your provider will move you to 0.5 mg for weeks five through eight.

  • Total weight loss typically ranges from 2 to 5 pounds
  • Appetite suppression is established but not yet at full strength
  • GI side effects have usually resolved
  • You have developed new awareness of portion sizes and hunger cues

Side Effects to Watch For

Wegovy's side effect profile during the first month is similar to other semaglutide formulations :

  • Nausea: Most common, reported by about 44% of patients across the trial period, though it is milder at the 0.25 mg starting dose (roughly 15 to 20%).
  • Diarrhea: Affects about 10% of patients at the starting dose.
  • Constipation: Occurs in approximately 10% of patients.
  • Abdominal pain: Mild cramping reported by some patients in the first two weeks.
  • Headache: May occur as eating patterns change.

Managing these side effects involves eating smaller meals, staying well hydrated, and avoiding foods that are greasy or overly rich. Most symptoms improve within two to three weeks as your body adjusts .

Tips for Your First Month on Wegovy

  • Inject at the same time each week. Consistency maintains steady medication levels.
  • Rotate injection sites. Alternate between abdomen, thigh, and upper arm to prevent irritation.

  • Stay active. Walking 20 to 30 minutes daily accelerates results and improves mood.
