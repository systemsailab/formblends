Does Tirzepatide Cause Fatigue?

Tirzepatide can cause fatigue in some patients. Clinical trials report fatigue or tiredness in roughly 5 to 9 percent of participants, with higher rates at larger doses. It is most common during the initial weeks of treatment and during dose titration, and it typically improves as the body adjusts.

Detailed Explanation

Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist available as Mounjaro (for type 2 diabetes) and Zepbound (for weight management). As a newer medication in the incretin class, it works through two hormonal pathways rather than one, which contributes to its strong efficacy for both blood sugar control and weight loss. Fatigue is a recognized side effect, though it is less commonly reported than GI symptoms like nausea and diarrhea.

How Common Is Fatigue on Tirzepatide?

In the SURMOUNT-1 trial for weight management, fatigue was reported by approximately 7 percent of participants at the 10 mg dose and 9 percent at the 15 mg dose, compared to roughly 4 percent on placebo. In the SURPASS trials for type 2 diabetes, similar rates were observed. The dose-dependent pattern suggests the medication plays a direct role in causing tiredness in susceptible patients.

Why Tirzepatide May Cause Fatigue

The primary driver of fatigue on tirzepatide is the substantial reduction in caloric intake. Tirzepatide reduces appetite more aggressively than many other weight loss medications, and patients often eat significantly fewer calories than their baseline. This caloric deficit, while necessary for weight loss, can produce feelings of low energy, especially before the body fully adapts to burning stored fat.

Tirzepatide's dual mechanism of action means it affects both GIP and GLP-1 pathways, which influence insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, and gastric emptying. The combined metabolic shifts can temporarily affect energy balance. Dehydration from GI side effects and blood sugar changes in diabetic patients are additional contributing factors.

How Long Does Fatigue Last?

Most patients experience the worst fatigue during the first four to six weeks and during each dose increase. Because tirzepatide is titrated gradually over several months (starting at 2.5 mg and increasing every four weeks), patients may notice brief episodes of fatigue at each new dose level. By the time patients reach their maintenance dose and sustain it for a few weeks, energy levels generally return to normal or near-normal.

What to Consider

  • Maintain adequate protein intake (at least 60 to 80 grams daily) to preserve muscle mass and support energy production during weight loss.
  • Drink plenty of fluids, aiming for at least 64 ounces of water daily, and more if experiencing GI side effects.
  • Eat regular meals rather than skipping them, even if appetite is significantly reduced.
  • Light to moderate exercise may help counteract fatigue by improving circulation and energy metabolism.
  • Contact your physician if fatigue is severe, persistent, or accompanied by dizziness, confusion, or heart palpitations.
  • Your prescriber may slow the dose-titration schedule if fatigue is significantly affecting your quality of life.
