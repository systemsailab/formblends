Eligibility Requirements for Online Ozempic Prescriptions

Not everyone who wants Ozempic qualifies for a prescription. Understanding the eligibility criteria before you start the process saves time and frustration.

BMI Requirements

For off-label weight loss prescribing, most providers require a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related comorbidity (type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, obstructive sleep apnea, or PCOS). If Ozempic is being prescribed for its FDA-approved indication of type 2 diabetes, BMI requirements may not apply. Your online provider will calculate your BMI based on the height and weight you provide, and some platforms verify these during a video consultation.

Medical Exclusions

Certain conditions disqualify patients from Ozempic use: personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC), Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2), active or recent pancreatitis, pregnancy or breastfeeding, and known hypersensitivity to semaglutide. Patients with severe kidney disease, a history of diabetic retinopathy, or active gallbladder disease may still qualify but require closer monitoring. A responsible online provider will screen for all of these conditions before prescribing.

State Licensing and Telehealth Regulations

Telehealth prescribing is regulated at the state level, and understanding these regulations ensures you're working with a legitimate provider.

Prescriber Licensing Requirements

Telehealth providers must be licensed in the state where the patient is located at the time of the consultation, not where the provider is physically located. This means a doctor in California cannot prescribe to a patient in Texas unless they also hold a Texas license. Reputable platforms ensure their providers are properly licensed in every state they serve. If you move to a new state during treatment, you may need to be seen by a differently licensed provider within the same platform.

Ozempic is not classified as a controlled substance (unlike phentermine, which is Schedule IV), so it can be prescribed via telehealth without the additional restrictions that apply to controlled substances.
