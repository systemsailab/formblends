What Happens If I Miss A Compounded Semaglutide Dose?

Missing a compounded semaglutide dose temporarily reduces its appetite-suppressing and metabolic effects but is not dangerous. The same general guidelines apply as with brand-name semaglutide: take the missed dose within 5 days, or skip it and resume your regular schedule. Always follow the specific instructions provided by your prescribing physician and compounding pharmacy.

Detailed Explanation

Compounded semaglutide is prepared by licensed compounding pharmacies and prescribed by physicians as an alternative to brand-name products like Ozempic and Wegovy. It contains the same active ingredient (semaglutide) and works through the same GLP-1 receptor agonist mechanism. Because the active molecule is identical, the pharmacokinetics are similar, with a half-life of approximately 7 days and a gradual decline in drug levels after a missed dose.

What to Expect After Missing a Dose

After skipping a compounded semaglutide injection, you will likely notice a return of appetite within a few days. Hunger signals may increase, cravings for calorie-dense foods may return, and the feeling of fullness after meals may diminish. If you were experiencing side effects like mild nausea or reduced appetite, those will also fade temporarily. These changes are normal and reverse once you resume your dosing schedule.

Missed Dose Guidelines

Because compounded semaglutide uses the same active molecule as brand-name versions, the general 5-day rule is a reasonable framework. If fewer than 5 days have passed since your scheduled injection, take the dose as soon as you remember and continue your normal weekly schedule. If 5 or more days have passed, skip the missed dose and take your next injection on the regularly scheduled day. However, compounded formulations can vary in concentration and dosing frequency depending on your prescriber's protocol. Always defer to the specific instructions given by your physician and pharmacy.

Important Differences with Compounded Semaglutide

One key difference between compounded and brand-name semaglutide is that dosing protocols may vary more widely. Some compounding pharmacies provide multi-dose vials rather than pre-filled pens, and your physician may prescribe a different concentration or injection frequency than what is standard for Ozempic or Wegovy. This means the missed-dose rules from brand-name labeling may not apply exactly. If you are unsure about what to do after a missed dose, contact your prescribing provider directly rather than relying on brand-name medication guidelines.

Resuming After an Extended Break

If you have missed multiple doses of compounded semaglutide, do not resume at your previous dose without consulting your physician. Re-titration may be necessary to avoid intense gastrointestinal side effects. Your provider can assess how long you have been off the medication and determine the safest dose to restart at. This is especially important for patients who were on higher doses before the break, as the body loses tolerance relatively quickly.

What to Consider

  • Follow the dosing instructions specific to your compounding pharmacy and prescribing physician, which may differ from brand-name guidelines.
  • Do not double your dose to make up for a missed injection.
  • If your compounded semaglutide comes in a multi-dose vial, check the beyond-use date to make sure it is still within its use window.
  • Store your compounded semaglutide according to the pharmacy's instructions, typically refrigerated.
  • Contact your prescribing physician before resuming if you have missed more than one consecutive dose.
  • Keep a log of your injection dates to track adherence and spot patterns in missed doses.
