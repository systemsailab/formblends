Semaglutide Results After 1 Year: What to Expect

One year on semaglutide is the benchmark that clinical trials use to measure long-term success, and the results at this milestone are striking. Patients researching semaglutide results after 1 year can expect a total weight loss of 28 to 45 pounds, representing roughly 12% to 17% of starting body weight {{CITE:semaglutide 52-week weight loss data}}. At this point, most patients have reached or are very close to their maximum weight loss on the medication.

The STEP 1 trial reported 14.9% body weight loss at 68 weeks (approximately 15.5 months) on semaglutide 2.4 mg {{CITE:STEP 1 primary endpoint data}}. At the one-year mark (52 weeks), most patients had achieved approximately 13% to 14% loss, with modest additional loss continuing through the end of the study.

Health Transformation at One Year

Twelve months of semaglutide therapy produces improvements that extend far beyond the scale:

  • Cardiovascular: The SELECT trial proved semaglutide reduces heart attacks, strokes, and cardiovascular death by 20%. Your improved blood pressure and lipids contribute to this protection {{CITE:SELECT trial one-year cardiovascular outcomes}}.
  • Diabetes prevention: The STEP 1 trial showed that 84% of patients with prediabetes at baseline had normal blood sugar at one year. Semaglutide effectively prevents progression to type 2 diabetes {{CITE:semaglutide prediabetes regression data}}.
  • Liver health: Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease improves substantially, with many patients showing normal liver enzyme levels.
  • Joint and mobility: Reduced load on joints means less pain, better function, and more active living.
  • Mental health: Most patients report improved quality of life scores, better body image, and reduced food-related anxiety.

The Maintenance Question

At one year, the central question becomes: what happens next? {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide one-year treatment decisions}}

  • Continue at 2.4 mg: For patients still losing weight or who want maximum weight maintenance support.
  • Reduce to a lower maintenance dose: Some patients maintain at 1.0 or 1.7 mg, saving money and reducing side effects.
  • Discontinue with monitoring: Possible but associated with significant weight regain. The STEP 1 extension study showed patients regained about two-thirds of lost weight within a year of stopping {{CITE:STEP 1 extension discontinuation data}}.
  • Switch to compounded semaglutide: Cost-effective for long-term maintenance {{INTERNAL_LINK:Form Blends pricing}}.

Side Effects and Monitoring at One Year

At one year, semaglutide is well-tolerated by the vast majority of patients who made it this far {{CITE:semaglutide 52-week safety profile}}:

  • GI effects: Essentially resolved at stable doses.
  • Nutritional monitoring: Critical. A full year of reduced intake requires regular testing for vitamin and mineral deficiencies.
  • Bone density: Consider a DEXA scan, especially for postmenopausal women.
  • Muscle mass: Body composition testing reveals whether lean mass preservation strategies have been effective.
  • Psychological wellbeing: The emotional journey of a 30+ pound loss over a year deserves attention and support.

Strategies for Year Two and Beyond

  • Comprehensive annual health assessment. Full metabolic panel, lipids, CBC, vitamins, minerals, thyroid, and body composition.
  • Exercise evolution. Focus on strength, endurance, and functional fitness. Your body is capable of much more than when you started.
  • Nutritional optimization. Work with a dietitian to build sustainable eating patterns for your new body weight.
  • Maintenance dose discussion. Explore whether a lower dose can maintain your results.
  • Community and support. Stay connected with your care team and peer support networks {{INTERNAL_LINK:Form Blends telehealth consultation}}.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 28 to 45 pounds at one year the maximum I can lose on semaglutide?

Most patients reach near-maximum weight loss between months 12 and 15. Some continue to lose small amounts through month 18. The total depends on your dose, starting weight, and lifestyle factors {{CITE:semaglutide maximum weight loss timeline}}.

What percentage of patients reach 10% or more weight loss at one year?

In the STEP 1 trial, approximately 70% of patients on semaglutide 2.4 mg achieved at least 10% body weight loss, and about 50% achieved 15% or more {{CITE:STEP 1 weight loss responder analysis}}.

Do I need semaglutide forever?

Not necessarily, but evidence strongly suggests that weight regain is common after stopping. Many experts view obesity as a chronic condition requiring ongoing treatment, similar to blood pressure medication {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:obesity as a chronic disease treatment model}}.

Can I switch to tirzepatide after a year on semaglutide?

Yes. If you want additional weight loss or have plateaued, tirzepatide may provide further results. Your provider can manage the transition {{INTERNAL_LINK:semaglutide vs tirzepatide comparison}}.

How do one-year semaglutide results compare to bariatric surgery?

Semaglutide at 2.4 mg produces about 15% total body weight loss on average, compared to 25% to 35% for surgical options like gastric sleeve or bypass. However, semaglutide avoids surgical risks, is reversible, and has proven cardiovascular benefits {{CITE:semaglutide vs bariatric surgery one-year comparison}}.
