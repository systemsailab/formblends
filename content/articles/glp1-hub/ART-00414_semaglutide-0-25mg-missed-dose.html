Semaglutide 0.25mg Missed Dose: What to Do and How to Get Back on Track

Life happens. Travel, a busy week, or simply forgetting your injection day can all lead to a missed dose of semaglutide. If you are at the 0.25mg introductory dose and you have missed a scheduled injection, you are not alone, and there are clear steps you can follow.

We put together this guide based on the prescribing information and clinical guidance to help you understand what to do when you miss a dose, how it affects your treatment, and how to prevent it from happening again. Your physician is always the best resource for personalized advice, but this information will help you respond quickly and confidently.

The General Guidance for a Missed Dose

According to the prescribing information for semaglutide, the approach to a missed dose depends on how much time has passed since your scheduled injection: {{CITE:Semaglutide prescribing information provides guidance on missed doses based on the number of days elapsed since the scheduled injection.}}

If It Has Been 5 Days or Fewer Since Your Missed Dose

Take the missed dose as soon as possible. Then resume your regular weekly schedule. For example, if your normal injection day is Monday and you remember on Thursday, take the injection on Thursday and continue with Monday the following week.

If It Has Been More Than 5 Days Since Your Missed Dose

Skip the missed dose entirely. Wait until your next regularly scheduled injection day and take your normal dose at that time. Do not double up by taking two doses to make up for the missed one. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Patients should never take a double dose of semaglutide to compensate for a missed injection.}}

This five-day threshold is important because semaglutide has a half-life of approximately one week. {{CITE:Semaglutide has an elimination half-life of approximately 1 week.}} Taking a dose too close to your next scheduled injection could result in overlapping drug levels that increase the risk of side effects, particularly GI symptoms.

How a Missed Dose Affects Your Body

Understanding what happens pharmacologically when you miss a dose can help put your mind at ease:

Drug Levels Decline Gradually

Because semaglutide has a long half-life, a single missed dose does not mean the drug disappears from your system immediately. After your last injection, levels decline gradually over the following days. You may still have some medication activity for several days after your scheduled dose was due.

Appetite May Return Partially

As drug levels drop, you may notice a temporary increase in appetite or a return of cravings that had diminished. This is normal and typically resolves once you take your next dose. At 0.25mg, these changes may be subtle since the appetite effects at this introductory dose are already mild for many patients.

GI Symptoms Are Unlikely to Worsen

Missing a dose generally does not cause GI side effects. In fact, patients who were experiencing mild nausea or digestive changes may notice temporary relief during the gap. When you resume, there is a small chance of GI symptoms returning briefly, similar to what you experienced with your first injection.

One Missed Dose Is Not a Setback

A single missed dose at the 0.25mg level is unlikely to significantly affect your overall treatment trajectory. The introductory phase is about building tolerance, and one missed week does not undo the adaptation your body has already made. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:A single missed dose during the titration phase does not typically require restarting the titration schedule.}}

What If You Miss Multiple Doses?

If you miss two or more consecutive doses, the situation changes. Extended gaps in treatment mean that semaglutide levels in your body have dropped significantly or cleared entirely. In this case:

  • Contact your physician. They will advise whether you should resume at 0.25mg or whether any adjustments to your plan are needed.
  • Your physician may recommend restarting the titration. If you have been off the medication for several weeks, your body may have lost the GI adaptation it gained during your initial weeks of treatment. Restarting at 0.25mg helps prevent a surge of side effects.
  • Do not jump to a higher dose. Even if your physician had planned to escalate you, resuming after an extended gap should start at the introductory dose unless your physician specifically directs otherwise.

{{MEDICAL_REVIEW:After an extended treatment interruption, patients may need to restart semaglutide at the introductory titration dose to reestablish tolerability.}}

Clinical Trial Context: Dosing Consistency

The STEP clinical trials emphasized consistent weekly dosing throughout the treatment period. The dose-escalation schedule used in the trials was:

  • Weeks 1 through 4: 0.25mg once weekly
  • Weeks 5 through 8: 0.5mg once weekly
  • Weeks 9 through 12: 1.0mg once weekly
  • Weeks 13 through 16: 1.7mg once weekly
  • Week 17 onward: 2.4mg once weekly

{{CITE:STEP trials used consistent weekly dosing with a 16-week escalation schedule.}}

Adherence to the weekly schedule was important for achieving the trial outcomes. While a single missed dose is manageable, consistent adherence produces the best results.

How Titration Is Affected by Missed Doses

If you miss a dose during the 0.25mg phase, it may extend the time before your physician recommends moving to 0.5mg. This is because:

  • Semaglutide takes approximately four to five weeks of consistent dosing to reach steady-state levels at any given dose. A missed dose delays reaching steady state. {{CITE:Semaglutide reaches steady-state concentration after approximately 4-5 weeks of consistent weekly dosing.}}
  • Your physician wants to evaluate your tolerance based on consistent exposure. If you missed a week, the data from that period is less reliable for making dosing decisions.
  • Extending the 0.25mg phase by a week or two to account for a missed dose is a conservative but reasonable approach.

Discuss any missed doses with your physician so they can factor this into your escalation timeline.

Preventing Missed Doses

Consistency is one of the most important factors in successful GLP-1 therapy. Here are practical strategies to help you stay on schedule:

  • Set a recurring alarm. Choose a specific day and time each week and set a phone reminder.
  • Link it to a routine. Pair your injection with a weekly habit you already have, such as a specific meal or activity.
  • Keep your medication visible. Store your pen (per the storage requirements) in a place where you will see it on injection day.
  • Use a tracking app or calendar. Mark each injection on a calendar or use a medication tracking app to maintain a visual record.
  • Travel planning. If you are traveling, pack your medication in your carry-on and adjust your injection time for time zone changes. Consult your physician about travel considerations.
  • Have a backup plan. If your regular injection day falls on a busy or unusual day, decide in advance when you will take it instead.

Safety Reminders

  • Never take a double dose. Taking two doses in one week to "catch up" increases the risk of severe nausea, vomiting, and other side effects. {{CITE:The semaglutide prescribing information explicitly warns against doubling doses to compensate for missed injections.}}
  • Do not change your dose without physician approval. Even if you feel fine, dose changes should be managed by your provider.
  • Monitor for unusual symptoms when resuming. After a missed dose, pay attention to how you feel when you resume. While most patients tolerate the restart well, report any concerns to your physician.
  • Stay hydrated. As always, adequate hydration supports GI tolerance and overall health during treatment.
  • Remember the contraindications. Semaglutide is not appropriate for patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2. {{CITE:Semaglutide contraindicated in patients with MTC or MEN 2.}}

Frequently Asked Questions

I missed my dose by two days. Should I take it now or wait?

If it has been five days or fewer since your scheduled injection, take the missed dose as soon as you remember. Then continue with your regular weekly schedule. If more than five days have passed, skip the dose and take your next one on schedule.

Will missing one dose undo my progress?

No. A single missed dose at 0.25mg will not significantly impact your overall treatment. Semaglutide's long half-life means some drug activity continues for days after a missed injection. Resume your schedule and continue as planned.

Should I restart from week one if I missed a dose?

For a single missed dose, restarting the titration is generally not necessary. If you have missed two or more consecutive doses, contact your physician. They may recommend restarting at 0.25mg depending on how long the gap was.

Can I change my injection day?

Yes, you can change your injection day as long as your last dose was at least two days (48 hours) prior. {{CITE:Semaglutide injection day may be changed as long as at least 2 days (48 hours) have elapsed since the last dose.}} After selecting your new day, maintain that schedule going forward. Discuss any changes with your physician.

What if I keep forgetting my doses?

If missed doses are becoming a pattern, talk with your physician. Adherence is critical for treatment success. Your care team can help you troubleshoot barriers and find strategies that work for your lifestyle.
