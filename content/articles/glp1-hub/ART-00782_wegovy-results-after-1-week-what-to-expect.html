Wegovy Results After 1 Week: What to Expect

Wegovy results after 1 week are going to be underwhelming if you are expecting a dramatic transformation, and that is exactly how it should be. The 0.25 mg starting dose is one-tenth of the full maintenance dose. It exists to help your body adjust with minimal side effects. Most patients notice mild appetite changes and possibly light nausea, but the scale barely moves. Here is a detailed look at what happens and what to do about it.

Weight Loss Reality Check

Here is what the data tells us about weight loss during the first week of Wegovy.

Clinical Trial Data

In the STEP 1 trial, participants on semaglutide 2.4 mg (Wegovy) lost an average of 14.9% of their body weight over 68 weeks. But almost none of that loss happened during the first week at 0.25 mg. At the 4-week mark (still on 0.25 mg), the average weight change was approximately 0.5 to 1% of body weight. That puts the week 1 contribution at roughly 0.1 to 0.3% of body weight. {{CITE:Wilding JPH et al. STEP 1: semaglutide 2.4 mg for overweight and obesity. NEJM 2021}}

Why This Is Actually Good News

Slow starts produce better long-term outcomes. Patients who experience rapid early weight loss often face worse side effects, higher dropout rates, and more muscle loss. The gradual escalation from 0.25 mg to 2.4 mg over 16 weeks is designed to produce sustainable, primarily fat-based weight loss. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy weight loss timeline}}

Common Side Effects at 0.25 mg

The 0.25 mg dose is the gentlest in the Wegovy escalation. Side effects at this level are milder than at any subsequent dose.

  • Nausea: Affects about 15% of patients. Typically mild, lasts 1-3 days, and improves before the second injection
  • Reduced appetite: The intended effect. If you feel slightly less hungry between meals, the medication is working
  • Constipation: Reported by 5-8% of patients. Increase water and fiber intake. A magnesium supplement or stool softener can help if needed
  • Headache: Occurs in some patients during the first few days. Usually mild and resolves with hydration and over-the-counter pain relief
  • Fatigue: A small percentage of patients feel slightly more tired than usual for the first 2-3 days

If you experience none of these, that is also completely normal. Many patients at 0.25 mg have no noticeable side effects at all. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy side effects guide}}

How to Optimize Your First Week

Injection Tips

  • Inject at room temperature (take the pen out of the fridge 30 minutes before)
  • Rotate injection sites: abdomen, thigh, or upper arm
  • Choose a consistent day each week that fits your schedule
  • Some patients prefer injecting in the evening so any nausea occurs during sleep

Eating Strategy

  • Protein at every meal: eggs, Greek yogurt, chicken, fish, legumes (25-30g per meal)
  • Eat when hungry but do not force large meals
  • Avoid foods that commonly trigger nausea: very greasy, very spicy, or very rich dishes
  • Small, frequent meals are easier to tolerate than 3 large ones during the adjustment period

Hydration

Drink at least 64 ounces of water daily. This helps with both constipation (common) and nausea (sometimes triggered by dehydration). Herbal tea and broth count toward your daily intake.

What Not to Do

  • Do not weigh yourself daily. Weekly weigh-ins are more meaningful
  • Do not dramatically cut calories. Your body needs adequate nutrition during the adjustment phase
  • Do not compare your week 1 to anyone else's. Responses are highly individual
  • Do not judge whether Wegovy "works" based on 7 days at a sub-therapeutic dose

Frequently Asked Questions

When will Wegovy start really working?

Most patients feel meaningful appetite suppression at the 0.5 mg dose (weeks 5-8) and see consistent weight loss from month 2-3 onward. The peak rate of weight loss typically occurs between months 4 and 8 at the 1.7 mg and 2.4 mg doses. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy results timeline}}

Is it normal to gain weight during week 1?

Yes. Weight fluctuates daily due to water, food volume, and hormonal factors. A 1-2 pound increase on any given day does not mean the medication is not working. Focus on the trend over weeks and months, not day-to-day numbers.

Can I skip the 0.25 mg dose and start higher?

We strongly advise against this. Skipping the introductory dose significantly increases the risk of nausea and vomiting, which can make you miserable and may cause you to abandon treatment. The 4-week introductory phase exists because clinical trials showed it improves tolerability and long-term adherence. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy dosage schedule}}

Should I exercise more during week 1?

Maintain your current exercise routine but do not dramatically increase intensity. If you are new to exercise, start with daily walking. The best time to ramp up exercise is once you are tolerating the medication well and have established a protein-rich eating pattern.
