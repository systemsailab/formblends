What Zepbound's Dual Mechanism Is Doing

At week 2, both the GIP and GLP-1 pathways are engaged. Even at the introductory dose, the dual mechanism is producing subtle but measurable changes:

  • Appetite center (hypothalamus): Receiving dual satiety signals, reducing both hunger and food reward
  • Pancreas: Improved insulin response to meals; glucagon suppression reducing fasting glucose
  • Stomach: Slowed gastric emptying creating prolonged fullness after eating
  • Fat tissue: Early metabolic changes in adipocytes (fat cells) that will become more pronounced at higher doses

Frequently Asked Questions

I lost weight in week 1 but plateaued in week 2. Is this normal?

Yes. Early weight loss often includes a water component that stabilizes. A flat week 2 after a good week 1 is a common and meaningless pattern. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Short-term weight fluctuations at the introductory dose are not clinically significant.}}

When does the 5 mg dose start?

Week 5. You will stay at 2.5 mg for a full 4 weeks before your first increase. This timeline is based on clinical trial protocols that showed the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Zepbound dosing guide}}

Can Zepbound help me at just 2.5 mg long-term?

The 2.5 mg dose is not designed for long-term use. It produces minimal weight loss compared to therapeutic doses. The target maintenance range is 5-15 mg depending on your individual response and goals.

Is Zepbound safe to take with birth control?

Zepbound may reduce the absorption of oral medications (including oral contraceptives) due to slowed gastric emptying. If you take oral birth control, discuss this with your provider. You may need a backup contraceptive method during dose changes. {{INTERNAL_LINK:Zepbound and birth control}}
