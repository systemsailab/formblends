How To Store Zepbound?

Zepbound (tirzepatide) pens should be stored in the refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius). Unopened pens may also be kept at room temperature below 86 degrees Fahrenheit for up to 21 cumulative days. Do not freeze Zepbound, and throw it away if it has ever been frozen.

Proper Refrigerator Storage

Keep Zepbound pens in the original carton to block light exposure. Store them in the main compartment of the refrigerator rather than the door shelves, which experience the most temperature variation. Avoid placing pens near the back wall or next to the freezer section, where temperatures can drop low enough to freeze the medication accidentally.

When stored correctly in the refrigerator, Zepbound pens remain good until the expiration date on the packaging. If you pick up a 30-day supply, keep the pens you are not using yet in the fridge and only remove one when it is time for your next injection.

Room Temperature Storage

If you cannot refrigerate Zepbound, the pen can stay at room temperature (up to 86 degrees Fahrenheit or 30 degrees Celsius) for a maximum of 21 cumulative days. This means every hour outside the fridge counts toward that 21-day total, even if you alternate between refrigerated and room temperature storage. After 21 cumulative days at room temperature, discard the pen whether or not it has been used.

Zepbound pens are single-dose, so each pen is used once and thrown away. There is no "after first use" storage concern for an individual pen. The 21-day room temperature rule applies to unopened pens you have not yet injected.

Avoiding Freezing and Overheating

Freezing destroys tirzepatide by damaging the protein structure of the molecule. If a pen has been frozen, even briefly, discard it. Do not attempt to thaw and use it. Heat above 86 degrees Fahrenheit also degrades the medication. Common scenarios to avoid include leaving pens in a hot vehicle, storing them near a heat vent or oven, or placing them in direct sunlight on a counter or windowsill.

Traveling With Zepbound

When traveling, use an insulated medication pouch with gel ice packs to keep Zepbound cool. Wrap the pen in a cloth or place it in a separate compartment so it does not touch the ice pack directly. Direct contact with ice can freeze the pen. If you are flying, carry the medication in your carry-on bag. Temperatures in airplane cargo holds are not regulated and can reach extremes in either direction.

Pack your prescription label or a pharmacy printout with the medication in case airport security has questions about injectable devices. For road trips, keep the travel case inside the car cabin where air conditioning maintains a comfortable temperature, not in the trunk.

Checking Zepbound Before Injection

Look at the medication through the pen inspection window before every injection. Zepbound should be clear and colorless to slightly yellow. If you see cloudiness, discoloration, or floating particles, do not use the pen. These changes may mean the medication was exposed to improper temperatures or has otherwise degraded. Contact your pharmacy for a replacement.

Related Questions

Is Zepbound stored the same way as Mounjaro?

Yes. Zepbound and Mounjaro both contain tirzepatide and share identical storage requirements. Both need refrigeration at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit, both allow up to 21 cumulative days at room temperature (below 86 degrees), and neither should be frozen. The only difference between the two products is their approved indication, not their formulation or storage needs.

What happens if I leave Zepbound out for more than 21 days?

Discard the pen. After 21 cumulative days at room temperature, the manufacturer cannot guarantee the medication's stability or effectiveness. Using a pen past this window may result in a dose that does not work as expected. Contact your prescriber or pharmacy for a replacement.

Can I store Zepbound in a cooler bag for several days?

Yes, as long as the temperature inside the cooler stays between 36 and 86 degrees Fahrenheit. Use gel packs rather than loose ice, and check the temperature periodically with a small thermometer. Prevent the pen from contacting ice directly to avoid freezing. Refresh the gel packs as needed to maintain cooling.

How do I safely throw away a used Zepbound pen?

Place each used pen in an FDA-cleared sharps container. Do not bend or break the needle, and do not recap the pen before disposal. When the sharps container is three-quarters full, seal it and dispose of it through a pharmacy take-back program or local household hazardous waste collection.
