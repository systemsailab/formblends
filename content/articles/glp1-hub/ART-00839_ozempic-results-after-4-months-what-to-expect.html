Dosing Context at Four Months

Ozempic's titration is simpler than Wegovy's, with fewer dose steps :

  • Month 1: 0.25 mg
  • Month 2: 0.5 mg
  • Months 3 to 4: 1.0 mg
  • Optional: 2.0 mg (if additional efficacy is needed)

By month four, most patients have been at 1.0 mg for about four weeks. Some providers may have already advanced to 2.0 mg for patients seeking additional weight loss or better blood sugar control. The decision depends on your individual response and goals.

Side Effects and Ongoing Management

Most patients are well-adapted to Ozempic by month four. Persistent side effects are usually mild and manageable :

  • Constipation: If present, it is usually a routine you have learned to manage with fiber, water, and magnesium.
  • Reduced alcohol tolerance: Many patients notice they can drink less before feeling the effects of alcohol.
  • Injection site reactions: Rare at this point, but rotating injection sites prevents any buildup of scar tissue.
  • Occasional mild nausea: May occur if you eat a particularly large or rich meal.

If you are considering stepping up to 2.0 mg, be prepared for a brief return of mild GI side effects similar to previous dose increases .
