What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1, a hormone your gut naturally produces after eating. It tells your brain you are full, slows stomach emptying, and helps regulate blood sugar. GLP-1 medications are synthetic versions of this hormone that last much longer than the natural version, providing steady appetite control throughout the week.

How GLP-1 Medications Affect Family Life

Safety Considerations for Parents

Common Side Effects

Nausea, constipation, diarrhea, and decreased appetite are the most frequently reported effects. They are most pronounced during dose increases and typically fade within a few weeks. For parents worried about being unable to function, the vast majority of people continue normal daily activities.

Pregnancy and Family Planning

GLP-1 medications are not safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding. If you are planning to conceive, discuss timing with your provider. Semaglutide should be stopped at least two months before conception; tirzepatide at least one month. Use reliable contraception during treatment.

Interactions With Other Medications

GLP-1 medications can affect how quickly your body absorbs oral medications, including birth control pills. Discuss all current medications with your provider, especially oral contraceptives.

Contraindications

GLP-1 medications are not appropriate for individuals with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN type 2 syndrome. They should be used cautiously in patients with a history of pancreatitis.
