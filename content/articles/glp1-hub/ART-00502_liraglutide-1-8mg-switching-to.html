Switching to Liraglutide 1.8mg: The Key Transition Point

Switching to liraglutide 1.8mg is the third dose increase in the titration, moving up from 1.2mg after at least one week at that level. The increase is made by dialing the same pen to 1.8mg. This 50 percent increase is the largest remaining proportional step in the titration, making it the most significant transition you will feel. Most patients handle it smoothly, but this is the dose where GI side effects are most likely to appear during the dose-escalation process. {{CITE:liraglutide titration 1.2mg to 1.8mg}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:liraglutide 1.8mg transition protocol}}

Standard Titration: 1.2mg to 1.8mg

The move from 1.2mg to 1.8mg follows the standard weekly titration schedule. After seven days at 1.2mg, dial your pen to the 1.8mg mark on day 15 of treatment. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide titration schedule}}

Readiness indicators before switching:

  • At least 7 days completed at 1.2mg
  • GI symptoms from the 1.2mg transition have resolved
  • You are eating regular meals without distress
  • No concerning symptoms that require evaluation
  • Adequate pen supply for the higher dose (pens run out faster at 1.8mg)

If these criteria are met, proceed with the increase. If GI symptoms from 1.2mg are still present, consider an extra week before moving up. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide 1.2mg how long to stay on}}

What the Switch Feels Like

Patient experiences during the transition from 1.2mg to 1.8mg:

  • Appetite shift: The clearest change most patients notice. Hunger between meals diminishes noticeably. The drive to eat outside of planned meals weakens. This is the dose where many patients first describe feeling "in control" of their eating.
  • GI adjustment: Approximately 20 to 30 percent of patients experience some nausea during the first 2 to 3 days. This is the most GI-eventful transition in the titration for most patients. It is usually manageable with diet adjustments. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:liraglutide 1.8mg GI transition rates}}
  • Satiety signals: Full feeling arrives earlier and lasts longer. Patients commonly report that they stop eating mid-meal because they genuinely feel satisfied, not because they are forcing themselves to stop.
  • Mental clarity about food: The constant background hum of food thoughts, cravings, and planning begins to quiet. For patients who have struggled with food preoccupation, this is often the most valued change.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I skip 1.8mg and go directly from 1.2mg to 2.4mg?
This is not recommended. The jump from 1.2mg to 2.4mg doubles the dose and significantly increases nausea and vomiting risk. The one-week stay at 1.8mg helps your body adapt to the intermediate level. Follow the prescribed titration. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:risks of skipping liraglutide dose levels}}
I felt nothing at 1.2mg. Will 1.8mg be different?
Very likely. The 50 percent dose increase brings most patients into the perceptible range for appetite suppression. The 1.2mg dose is at the low end of therapeutic activity. At 1.8mg, the effect crosses a threshold that most patients can feel.
Should I adjust my meal plan when switching to 1.8mg?
Minor adjustments help. Slightly smaller portions, protein at every meal, and avoiding heavy or greasy foods for the first 2 to 3 days smooth the transition. You do not need a complete diet overhaul at this stage.
What if 1.8mg gives me too many side effects?
Options include staying at 1.8mg for an additional week to allow further adaptation, stepping back to 1.2mg for a week and then trying 1.8mg again, or discussing anti-nausea medication with your prescriber. Do not discontinue the medication without consulting your prescriber. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide 1.8mg side effects}}
Is 1.8mg a good maintenance dose for weight loss?
For most patients, 1.8mg is sub-optimal for weight management. The full 3.0mg dose produces approximately twice the weight loss. However, some patients achieve acceptable results at 1.8mg and prefer it for tolerability reasons. Discuss with your prescriber whether 1.8mg meets your goals. {{INTERNAL_LINK:liraglutide 1.8mg results timeline}}
