Side Effects at 1.7 mg

The dose increase from 1.0 to 1.7 mg is the largest percentage jump in the Wegovy titration. Side effects to watch for include {{CITE:Wegovy 1.7 mg adverse event data}}:

  • Nausea: May recur briefly. Usually milder and shorter than earlier episodes.
  • Reduced appetite to the point of food aversion: Some patients develop temporary aversions to specific foods, particularly meats and rich dishes.
  • Constipation: Remains the most persistent GI issue. A consistent daily routine of fiber, magnesium, and water is essential.
  • Heartburn: May develop or worsen. Consider elevating the head of your bed and avoiding eating within 3 hours of bedtime.
  • Dizziness: Possible if eating too little. Ensure adequate calorie and fluid intake.

If side effects are unmanageable, your provider may recommend staying at 1.7 mg longer before advancing, or even stepping back to 1.0 mg temporarily {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Wegovy dose adjustment strategies}}.

Tips for the Final Stretch Before Maintenance

  • Maintain minimum caloric intake. Do not drop below 1200 calories daily for women or 1500 for men, even if you are not hungry.
  • Eat nutrient-dense foods. Every meal should include lean protein, vegetables, and healthy fats.
  • Increase exercise if able. Aim for 150 to 200 minutes of moderate activity per week.
  • Plan for 2.4 mg. Talk to your provider about what to expect when you reach the maintenance dose next month.
  • Celebrate your progress. Losing 15+ pounds in four months is a significant achievement that improves your health in countless ways.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when I reach the 2.4 mg maintenance dose?

At 2.4 mg, you are at the full therapeutic dose. Weight loss typically continues for several more months before stabilizing. Many patients reach peak weight loss between months 8 and 12 on the maintenance dose {{INTERNAL_LINK:Wegovy results after 6 months}}.

Can I stay at 1.7 mg if I am happy with my results?

Some patients choose to remain at 1.7 mg, especially if they are tolerating it well and seeing adequate progress. Clinical data suggests that 2.4 mg produces more weight loss, but the difference may not be worth it for everyone {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide dose-response at higher doses}}.

How do I manage social events and eating out?

Order smaller portions or share entrees. Focus on protein and vegetables. Do not feel pressured to clean your plate. Most patients find that reduced appetite makes restaurant dining easier because they are satisfied with less.

Is the weight loss sustainable if I stop Wegovy?

Research shows that most patients regain weight after discontinuing GLP-1 medications. Maintaining an active lifestyle and healthy eating habits can slow regain, but many patients benefit from continued treatment {{CITE:semaglutide discontinuation weight regain data}}.

When should I get new blood work?

Four months is an excellent time for a comprehensive metabolic panel, lipid profile, and A1C test. These results help your provider optimize your treatment and document your health improvements {{INTERNAL_LINK:Form Blends telehealth consultation}}.
