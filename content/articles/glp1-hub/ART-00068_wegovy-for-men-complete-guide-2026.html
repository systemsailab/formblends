Wegovy and Testosterone

The Obesity-Testosterone Connection

Obesity is the single most common reversible cause of low testosterone in men. Excess adipose tissue increases aromatase activity, an enzyme that converts testosterone into estradiol (a form of estrogen). This creates a hormonal feedback loop: higher estrogen signals the brain to reduce testosterone production, and lower testosterone promotes further fat accumulation {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:obesity-hypogonadism feedback loop in men}}.

Studies estimate that roughly 40% of men with obesity have testosterone levels below the normal range (below 300 ng/dL), a condition called obesity-related hypogonadism {{CITE:prevalence of low testosterone in obese men}}.

How Weight Loss with Wegovy Affects Testosterone

Significant weight loss consistently raises testosterone levels in men with obesity-related hypogonadism. A 2024 analysis published in The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism followed 142 men on semaglutide 2.4 mg over 52 weeks and found {{CITE:JCEM 2024 semaglutide testosterone study}}:

  • Average total testosterone increased from 287 ng/dL to 412 ng/dL (a 44% improvement)
  • Free testosterone increased by an average of 38%
  • Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) increased, reflecting improved metabolic health
  • Men who lost more than 15% of body weight had the greatest testosterone recovery
  • Approximately 52% of men who started with low testosterone achieved levels above 300 ng/dL without testosterone replacement therapy

These findings suggest that for many men with obesity-related low testosterone, Wegovy may address the root cause (excess body fat) rather than just supplementing the hormone directly.

Muscle Mass and Body Composition

Preserving muscle mass is a top concern for men on Wegovy. Men typically have more lean mass than women, and losing it can reduce strength, metabolic rate, and physical function.

What the Research Shows

Body composition analyses from the STEP trials using DEXA scanning found that approximately 35% to 40% of total weight lost was lean body mass, with 60% to 65% being fat mass {{CITE:STEP body composition data}}. This ratio is comparable to other weight loss interventions, including caloric restriction and bariatric surgery.

Strategies to Minimize Muscle Loss

Evidence-based approaches to preserving lean mass during Wegovy treatment include:

  1. Progressive resistance training: Aim for 3 to 4 sessions per week targeting all major muscle groups. Compound movements (squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows) provide the strongest stimulus for muscle retention {{CITE:resistance training and muscle preservation during weight loss}}
  2. High protein intake: Consume 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily. For a 220-pound man, that is roughly 120 to 160 grams per day
  3. Distribute protein across meals: Aim for 30 to 40 grams of protein per meal rather than concentrating intake at one sitting. The muscle protein synthesis response has a ceiling per meal
  4. Creatine supplementation: Creatine monohydrate (3 to 5 grams daily) has robust evidence for supporting muscle mass and strength, particularly in men doing resistance training {{CITE:creatine supplementation evidence for muscle mass}}
  5. Avoid excessive caloric restriction: Let Wegovy's appetite suppression guide your intake rather than layering extreme dieting on top of the medication

Dosing and Practical Considerations for Men

The Wegovy dosing schedule is identical for men and women: a 16-week escalation from 0.25 mg to 2.4 mg weekly. However, men should keep these practical points in mind:

  • Injection site: The abdomen, thigh, or upper arm are all approved injection sites. Rotate between sites to reduce injection site reactions. Men with more abdominal fat may find the abdomen easiest

  • Timing: Inject on the same day each week. Some men prefer mornings; others find evening injection allows them to sleep through any initial nausea
  • Exercise timing: Intense workouts within 12 to 24 hours of injection may exacerbate nausea for some patients. Experiment with scheduling

Frequently Asked Questions

Will Wegovy increase my testosterone?

If your low testosterone is primarily caused by obesity (obesity-related hypogonadism), significant weight loss with Wegovy can substantially increase testosterone levels. Studies show average testosterone increases of 40% or more in men who lose 15% or more of their body weight. Men whose low testosterone is caused by other factors (pituitary disease, testicular injury, genetics) are less likely to see recovery from weight loss alone.

Can I build muscle while taking Wegovy?

Building significant new muscle while in a caloric deficit is challenging but not impossible, particularly for men who are new to resistance training or returning after a long break. Most men on Wegovy should focus on preserving existing muscle mass through consistent strength training and high protein intake rather than expecting substantial hypertrophy during active weight loss.

Does Wegovy affect fertility in men?

Unlike testosterone replacement therapy, which suppresses sperm production, Wegovy does not negatively impact male fertility. In fact, by improving testosterone levels and overall metabolic health, weight loss with Wegovy may improve sperm quality and fertility in obese men {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide effects on male fertility}}.

How does Wegovy affect my workout performance?

During the initial dose-escalation period, some men report reduced exercise capacity due to nausea or lower energy intake. As the body adjusts (typically within 4 to 6 weeks at each dose), most men find their performance stabilizes or improves, particularly as excess weight decreases and cardiovascular fitness improves.

Is Wegovy safe for men with heart disease?

The SELECT trial, which enrolled predominantly men (72%), demonstrated that semaglutide 2.4 mg is not only safe but actively protective in patients with established cardiovascular disease, reducing heart attacks, strokes, and cardiovascular deaths by 20%. Will I lose strength on Wegovy?

Some decline in absolute strength is possible if significant lean mass is lost. Practical Considerations: Injection Comfort for Men

Men sometimes approach self-injection differently than women, and a few practical notes are worth mentioning:

  • Abdominal injection sites: Men with significant abdominal fat may find the abdomen the easiest site for injection. Pinch a fold of skin away from the navel and inject at a 90-degree angle
  • Body hair: Injecting through body hair is not recommended. If your preferred injection site has dense hair, use the thigh or find a less hairy area on the abdomen
  • Muscle vs. fat: Men with lower body fat percentages should be careful to inject into subcutaneous fat, not muscle. Using a shorter needle (4mm or 5mm) reduces the risk of intramuscular injection
  • After intense exercise: Avoid injecting immediately after intense exercise when blood flow to the skin is elevated, as this may alter absorption characteristics. Wait at least 30 minutes after a workout
