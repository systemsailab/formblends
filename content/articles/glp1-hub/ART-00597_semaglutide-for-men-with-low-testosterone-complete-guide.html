Why Men With Low Testosterone May Consider Semaglutide

The connection between obesity and low testosterone is one of the most important and underappreciated links in men's health.

The aromatase cycle. Fat tissue, especially visceral fat around the abdomen, is rich in the enzyme aromatase. Aromatase converts testosterone into estradiol (a form of estrogen). The more visceral fat you carry, the more testosterone gets converted, and the lower your free testosterone drops. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: low testosterone makes it harder to lose fat, and more fat further lowers testosterone.

Insulin resistance and testosterone. Obesity-driven insulin resistance independently suppresses testosterone production at the testicular level. Semaglutide improves insulin sensitivity, which may help restore natural testosterone production.

Inflammation. Chronic low-grade inflammation from excess fat tissue also suppresses the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis, the hormonal signaling chain that regulates testosterone production. Weight loss reduces this inflammatory burden.

Symptoms overlap. Low testosterone and obesity share many symptoms: fatigue, low libido, brain fog, poor sleep, reduced muscle mass, and depressed mood. Addressing the obesity component with semaglutide often improves these symptoms from both directions simultaneously.

How Semaglutide Works for Men With Low Testosterone

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves insulin sensitivity. For men with low testosterone, the weight loss it produces has direct hormonal consequences.

Impact on Testosterone Levels

Studies show that significant weight loss in obese men can increase total testosterone by 50 to 100 ng/dL or more. This is because losing visceral fat reduces aromatase activity, allowing more testosterone to remain in its active form rather than being converted to estrogen.

Semaglutide is particularly effective here because clinical data shows it preferentially reduces visceral fat, the exact type of fat most responsible for testosterone suppression.

Weight Loss Mechanism

  • Appetite reduction: Decreased hunger makes it easier to maintain a calorie deficit without constant effort.
  • Improved insulin sensitivity: Better glucose handling reduces fat storage and supports testosterone production.
  • Visceral fat targeting: The composition of weight loss on semaglutide favors visceral fat reduction, which directly benefits hormonal balance.

Clinical Results

In the STEP 1 trial, men on semaglutide 2.4 mg lost an average of 14.9% of their body weight over 68 weeks. While the trials did not specifically measure testosterone outcomes, the established relationship between visceral fat loss and testosterone improvement means that significant weight loss on semaglutide is expected to produce measurable hormonal changes in men with obesity-related low T.

Safety and Special Considerations

Semaglutide and Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

Some men with low testosterone are already on TRT. Semaglutide can be used alongside TRT. In fact, the combination may be complementary: TRT addresses the hormonal deficit directly, while semaglutide addresses the underlying obesity that contributes to it. Some men who lose significant weight on semaglutide may eventually be able to reduce or discontinue TRT as their natural testosterone production recovers.

Monitoring Hormonal Changes

We recommend checking testosterone, free testosterone, estradiol, and SHBG levels at baseline and periodically during treatment (typically every 3 to 6 months). This allows your physician to track hormonal improvements and adjust any concurrent hormone therapy.

Muscle Preservation

Men with low testosterone already tend to have less muscle mass. Losing weight without protecting muscle can worsen this. Resistance training and adequate protein intake are critical. Aim for at least 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily, with resistance training at least 3 times per week.

Common Side Effects

Nausea, diarrhea, and constipation are the most common, primarily during dose escalation. They typically improve within weeks. Eating smaller meals, staying hydrated, and avoiding heavy foods helps.

Contraindications

Do not use semaglutide if you have medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 family history, active pancreatitis, or severe gastrointestinal disease.
