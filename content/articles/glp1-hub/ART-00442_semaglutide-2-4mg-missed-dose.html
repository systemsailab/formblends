Semaglutide 2.4mg: What To Do If You Miss a Dose

If you miss a dose of semaglutide 2.4mg, the prescribing information provides a clear rule: take it within 5 days of the scheduled injection, or skip it and wait for your next regular dose day. At the 2.4mg maintenance dose, a single missed injection is unlikely to derail your progress, but you may notice a temporary return of appetite and cravings within 3 to 5 days. {{CITE:Wegovy prescribing information missed dose instructions}} {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide 2.4mg missed dose protocol}}

Step-by-Step: What To Do When You Miss Your Injection

If Fewer Than 5 Days Have Passed

Take your 2.4mg injection as soon as you remember. Then choose one of two approaches to resume your schedule:

  • Shift your injection day: Make the day you took the late dose your new regular injection day going forward.
  • Return to your original day: Take the late dose and then resume on your original day the following week, provided at least 2 days separate the late dose from the next scheduled one.

Either approach is acceptable. Choose whichever is more convenient for your schedule. {{CITE:semaglutide prescribing information injection day flexibility}}

If More Than 5 Days Have Passed

Skip the missed dose. Wait until your next scheduled injection day and take your normal 2.4mg dose. Do not take two injections to compensate. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide double dosing contraindicated}}

Why the Cutoff Is 5 Days

Semaglutide has an elimination half-life of approximately 7 days. After 5 days without a dose, the medication is still partially active in your system. Taking a late dose at day 5 or 6, followed by your next dose just 1 to 2 days later, would effectively create a period of elevated drug concentration. This increases the risk of nausea, vomiting, and other GI symptoms without providing extra therapeutic benefit. {{CITE:semaglutide pharmacokinetics half-life data}}

What to Expect After Missing a Dose at 2.4mg

Because 2.4mg is the highest dose, the contrast between medicated and unmedicated states is most noticeable at this level. Here is what patients commonly report:

  • Days 1 to 2 after missed dose: Minimal change. The medication is still active at near-full levels due to its long half-life.
  • Days 3 to 5: Appetite begins to return. You may notice increased food thoughts, stronger cravings, and the ability to eat larger portions. Some patients describe the return of "food noise," the constant background mental chatter about eating.
  • Days 5 to 7: Appetite is approaching pre-medication levels. Hunger signals intensify, and the self-regulating portion control that semaglutide provides weakens noticeably.
  • Blood sugar: Patients with type 2 diabetes may see glucose levels rise 15 to 30 mg/dL above their medicated baseline. Monitor more frequently during this period. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:blood glucose impact of missed semaglutide 2.4mg dose in T2D patients}}

A single missed dose will not cause significant weight gain. Any scale increase is likely from water retention and slightly higher food intake over a few days. This resolves quickly once you resume dosing.

Multiple Missed Doses: When to Call Your Prescriber

Missing two or more consecutive doses of semaglutide 2.4mg creates a longer gap that may require adjusted management:

Two Weeks Missed

Most prescribers will have you resume at 2.4mg. You may experience a brief return of GI symptoms similar to when you first reached this dose, but the readjustment is typically milder than the initial escalation.

Three to Four Weeks Missed

Your prescriber may recommend restarting at 1.7mg for one week before returning to 2.4mg. This reduces the risk of severe nausea from resuming the full dose after an extended gap. {{CITE:clinical guidance semaglutide resumption after treatment gap}}

More Than Four Weeks Missed

An extended gap may require partial or full re-titration starting at 0.5mg or 1.0mg. Your prescriber will determine the best approach based on how you handled the original titration and how long you were off the medication. {{INTERNAL_LINK:semaglutide dose titration guide}}

Strategies to Prevent Missed Doses

  • Anchor to a weekly routine: Choose an injection day that coincides with something you always do (Sunday meal prep, Monday morning coffee, Friday night routine).
  • Set multiple reminders: A phone alarm the morning of injection day plus a backup alarm in the evening covers most cases.
  • Visual placement: Keep your Wegovy pen in a dedicated, visible spot in the refrigerator. Some patients place a note on the fridge door on injection day.
  • Travel kit: When traveling, pack your pen with temperature-stable supplies (insulated case with ice packs). Set reminders adjusted for time zone changes.
  • Partner accountability: If you live with someone, ask them to remind you on injection day. Some couples inject on the same day to create mutual accountability.
  • Pharmacy auto-refill: Set up auto-refill so you never run out of medication, which is a common reason for unintentional gaps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will one missed dose of semaglutide 2.4mg cause weight gain?
Unlikely. A single missed dose may cause a small, temporary scale increase of 1 to 3 pounds from water and food weight. This is not fat gain and reverses within a week of resuming treatment.
Can I take a half dose if I remember late?
No. Semaglutide pens are pre-set to deliver specific doses. There is no clinical guidance supporting partial doses, and splitting a dose is not mechanically possible with the standard auto-injector. Take the full 2.4mg or skip it entirely. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:semaglutide partial dosing not supported}}
I missed my dose because my pharmacy was out of stock. What should I do?
Follow the 5-day rule once you obtain your medication. If the delay is expected to be longer, contact your prescriber to discuss alternatives, such as using a compounded semaglutide product temporarily or adjusting your plan. {{VERIFY_PRICE:Wegovy}} {{VERIFY_PRICE:semaglutide compounding}}
Does missing a dose reset my tolerance?
One missed dose does not reset tolerance. Your GI system remains adapted to the 2.4mg dose. Extended gaps of 3 or more weeks may partially reset tolerance, which is why re-titration may be recommended after longer interruptions.
Is it better to take a late dose or skip it?
If you are within the 5-day window, take it. A late dose maintains medication levels and prevents appetite from fully returning. Only skip if you are past the 5-day mark.
