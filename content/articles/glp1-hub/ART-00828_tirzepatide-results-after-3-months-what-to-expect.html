Addressing 3-Month Concerns

Weight Loss Plateau

Some patients hit a temporary plateau around month three as their body adjusts to a significantly lower weight. This is normal. Strategies include:

  • Requesting a dose increase to 10 mg if appropriate
  • Adjusting macronutrient ratios (often increasing protein)
  • Incorporating high-intensity interval training once or twice per week
  • Reducing stress and improving sleep quality
  • Checking for and addressing any nutritional deficiencies

Plateaus typically resolve within one to three weeks with appropriate adjustments {{CITE:managing tirzepatide weight loss plateaus}}.

Loose Skin

With 15 to 25 pounds lost, some patients begin noticing skin laxity, particularly around the abdomen and upper arms. Building muscle underneath the skin improves its appearance. Staying well hydrated and consuming collagen peptides, vitamin C, and zinc supports skin elasticity. For patients who eventually lose significant amounts of weight, skin tightening procedures may be discussed later in the journey.

Should I increase my dose beyond 7.5 mg?

If you are losing weight steadily at 7.5 mg, your provider may keep you there. If weight loss slows or appetite suppression decreases, stepping up to 10 mg is the next option. At the highest doses, tirzepatide produces weight loss approaching that of some bariatric procedures, without the surgical risks, recovery time, or permanent anatomical changes.
