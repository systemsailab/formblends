Wegovy Constipation Relief

Primary Keyword: wegovy constipation relief

Wegovy constipation relief typically involves increasing fiber intake, drinking more water, staying physically active, and using over-the-counter stool softeners when needed. Constipation is one of the most common side effects of Wegovy, affecting roughly 24% of users in clinical trials. {{CITE:Wegovy prescribing information lists constipation in approximately 24% of patients}}

Why Wegovy Causes Constipation

Wegovy (semaglutide) slows gastric emptying as part of its appetite-suppressing mechanism. While this delay helps you feel full longer, it also slows the entire digestive tract, including the colon. Food moves through your system more slowly, and the colon absorbs more water from stool along the way. The result is harder, drier stool that is more difficult to pass. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:mechanism of GLP-1 induced constipation}} Reduced food intake on Wegovy can compound the problem, since less bulk in your diet means less material to stimulate bowel movements.

Increase Your Fiber Gradually

Fiber adds bulk and moisture to stool, making it easier to pass. We recommend working up to 25 to 35 grams of fiber per day through foods like vegetables, fruits, beans, oats, and whole grains. If your appetite is too suppressed for whole foods, a fiber supplement like psyllium husk can help fill the gap. {{CITE:psyllium supplementation improves stool frequency and consistency}} Start slowly, adding 5 grams at a time over a week, because adding too much fiber too fast can make bloating and gas worse.

Drink Plenty of Water

Fiber only works well when paired with adequate hydration. Without enough fluid, extra fiber can actually worsen constipation. Aim for at least 64 to 80 ounces of water daily. Warm water or herbal tea in the morning can stimulate the gastrocolic reflex and encourage a bowel movement. Avoid relying on caffeinated drinks as your primary fluid source, since caffeine can have a mild dehydrating effect at high doses.

Move Your Body Daily

Physical activity stimulates the muscles in your intestines and helps move stool through the colon. A daily 20 to 30 minute walk is often enough to make a noticeable difference. Yoga, swimming, and light cycling are also effective. {{CITE:regular physical activity reduces constipation frequency}} If you are also strength training for muscle preservation on Wegovy, the combined benefits for gut motility are even greater.

Over-the-Counter Options That Work

When lifestyle changes are not enough, several OTC products can provide relief. Stool softeners like docusate sodium make stool easier to pass without stimulating the bowel. Osmotic laxatives like polyethylene glycol (MiraLAX) draw water into the colon and are safe for short-term daily use. Stimulant laxatives like bisacodyl work faster but should not be used regularly. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:OTC laxative safety during Wegovy therapy}} Always check with your provider before starting a new laxative routine.

When to Contact Your Doctor

If you have not had a bowel movement in four or more days, experience severe abdominal pain, notice blood in your stool, or develop vomiting along with constipation, reach out to your healthcare provider promptly. These symptoms could indicate a bowel obstruction or another serious condition that needs medical evaluation. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:warning signs of severe constipation on GLP-1 medications}} Your doctor may adjust your Wegovy dose or prescribe a prescription-strength laxative.

Frequently Asked Questions

How common is constipation on Wegovy?

Constipation affects about one in four Wegovy users based on clinical trial data. It is more common during dose increases and often improves as your body adjusts to each new dose level. Some people experience constipation throughout treatment, while others find it resolves within the first few months. {{CITE:Wegovy STEP trial constipation incidence data}}

Does Wegovy constipation go away on its own?

For many people, constipation improves over time as the body adjusts to the medication. However, some users experience it throughout their treatment. Maintaining good hydration and fiber habits gives you the best chance of managing it long-term without medication. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:natural resolution of GLP-1 constipation}}

Can I take MiraLAX every day while on Wegovy?

Polyethylene glycol (MiraLAX) is generally considered safe for daily use for up to two weeks without a doctor's supervision. For longer-term use while on Wegovy, consult your provider. It works by drawing water into the colon and does not cause dependence. Many GLP-1 patients use it regularly with their doctor's approval. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:long-term polyethylene glycol use during GLP-1 therapy}}

What foods help with Wegovy constipation?

Prunes, kiwifruit, flaxseeds, chia seeds, leafy greens, and beans are all excellent choices. Prunes contain sorbitol, a natural laxative, and have strong evidence supporting their use for constipation. {{CITE:prunes are effective for increasing stool frequency}} Kiwifruit has been shown to improve bowel regularity even in people with chronic constipation. Incorporating these foods daily can make a real difference.

Should I lower my Wegovy dose if constipation is severe?

Never adjust your Wegovy dose without talking to your prescriber first. If constipation is significantly impacting your quality of life despite trying dietary changes and OTC remedies, your provider may recommend staying at a lower dose longer or temporarily reducing the dose. They can also rule out other causes and prescribe stronger treatments if needed. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Wegovy dose adjustments for GI side effects}}
