Let us be direct about what the scale shows after one week on semaglutide: most patients see 0 to 2 pounds of weight loss, and some see no change at all. This is completely normal and expected.

Why Week 1 Weight Loss Is Minimal

The 0.25 mg dose is sub-therapeutic for weight loss. Its purpose is tolerability, not results. Your body needs time to adjust to GLP-1 receptor activation. Trying to judge semaglutide by week 1 is like judging a marathon by the first 100 meters. semaglutide weight loss timeline

Common Side Effects During Week 1

The STEP trials documented side effect rates across the full treatment period, but week 1 side effects at the starting dose are generally milder than what occurs at higher doses.

What Patients Report

  • Mild nausea (15-20% of patients): Usually peaks around day 2-4, then fades. Eating smaller meals and avoiding heavy, greasy foods helps
  • Decreased appetite (30-40% of patients): This is the medication working as intended, not a side effect. You may simply feel less hungry between meals
  • Mild constipation (5-10% of patients): Semaglutide slows digestion. Increase water intake and consider adding fiber to your diet
  • Fatigue (5-10% of patients): Some people feel slightly tired during the first few days. This usually resolves quickly
  • Injection site reaction (rare): Minor redness or soreness at the injection site that fades within a day

What You Should Focus on During Week 1

Week 1 is a foundation-building period. Here is what matters most:

Establish Your Injection Routine

Pick a consistent day and time for your weekly injection. Many patients choose a day when they have a lighter schedule in case of mild nausea. Morning or evening both work; there is no clinically superior time. semaglutide injection guide

Start Tracking Your Food

You do not need to calorie count obsessively, but noting what you eat and how you feel afterward builds awareness. This data becomes valuable as your appetite changes in the coming weeks.

Increase Protein Intake

Start building the habit of eating 25 to 30 grams of protein per meal now. As semaglutide's appetite effects strengthen at higher doses, you will eat less overall. Making sure that smaller intake is protein-rich protects your muscle mass.

Do Not Weigh Yourself Daily

Daily scale fluctuations from water, sodium, and digestion will mask any real changes. Weigh yourself once per week at the same time (morning, before eating) for the most meaningful data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to feel nothing after the first injection?

Yes. Many patients feel no different during the first week on 0.25 mg. The dose is intentionally low. If you feel nothing, the medication is still working at a subclinical level, building a foundation for the effects that come at higher doses.

Should I change my diet during week 1?

Start making gradual improvements. Focus on adding protein and vegetables rather than eliminating foods. Major dietary overhauls are easier to sustain once the appetite-suppressing effects kick in at higher doses (typically weeks 5-8 at 0.5 mg).

Can I exercise during week 1?

Absolutely. Continue your regular exercise routine. If you experience nausea, you may want to avoid intense workouts on injection day and the following day. Light walking and resistance training are safe and beneficial. exercise on semaglutide

When will I see real results?

Most patients see noticeable appetite suppression at the 0.5 mg dose (weeks 5-8) and meaningful scale movement by month 2-3 at the 1.0 mg dose. The biggest weight loss occurs between months 3 and 9. Patience during the early weeks pays off. semaglutide results timeline

What if I have bad nausea on day 1?

First-dose nausea is uncommon at 0.25 mg but can happen. Eat a bland, light meal before your injection. Avoid large, fatty meals for the first 48 hours. If nausea persists beyond 3-4 days, contact your provider. Some patients benefit from taking over-the-counter anti-nausea medication for the first few days.

The Bottom Line

Week 1 on semaglutide is about setting the stage, not seeing dramatic results. Your body is adjusting to a new medication, and the starting dose is deliberately conservative. The patients who succeed long-term are the ones who stay consistent through these early, uneventful weeks.
