CrossFit-Specific Dosing Strategy

The standard tirzepatide titration runs from 2.5 mg to 15 mg. CrossFit athletes should titrate based on training demands:

  • 2.5 mg (weeks 1 to 4): Assessment phase. Train normally. Note any GI changes during high-intensity metcons.
  • 5.0 mg (weeks 5 to 8): Appetite begins to shift. Post-WOD hunger becomes manageable. This is where many CrossFitters stay during competitive seasons.
  • 7.5 mg (weeks 9 to 12): Significant appetite reduction. Active fat loss phase. Monitor training quality closely.
  • 10.0 mg (weeks 13 to 16): Strong suppression. Best used during off-season or dedicated cutting blocks when training volume is intentionally reduced.
  • 12.5 to 15.0 mg: Maximum effect. Rarely needed for CrossFit athletes. Eating enough to support training at these doses is extremely difficult.

We recommend that most CrossFit athletes cap at 7.5 to 10.0 mg. The goal is body composition improvement, not maximum weight loss, and your performance depends on adequate fueling. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:tirzepatide dosing for strength-endurance athletes}}

Nutrition Protocol for CrossFitters on Tirzepatide

This is where discipline meets pharmacology. Tirzepatide makes you eat less, but CrossFit demands that you eat enough:

  • Protein: 1.0 to 1.2 grams per pound of body weight. Non-negotiable. Every meal anchored by 30 to 45 grams of protein. Chicken thighs, ground beef, salmon, eggs, and whey are staples.
  • Pre-WOD fuel: 300 to 500 calories, 90 minutes before training. Rice and chicken, oatmeal with protein, or a PB&J sandwich. This fuels the glycolytic demands of CrossFit workouts.
  • Intra-WOD: For workouts over 20 minutes, sip diluted sports drink throughout. The sugar supports sustained output without relying on stomach-heavy solid food.
  • Post-WOD recovery: 30 to 40 grams of protein plus 50 to 80 grams of carbs within 45 minutes. A shake with fruit and rice cereal is quick and effective.
  • Daily calories: Minimum 2,200 for women, 2,800 for men training 5 to 6 days per week. Track for at least the first 4 weeks on tirzepatide to make sure you are not inadvertently eating 1,500 calories because the medication killed your appetite.
  • Creatine: 5 grams daily. Supports power output, cell hydration, and lean mass retention. There is no reason to skip creatine while on tirzepatide. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:creatine supplementation during GLP-1 therapy}}

Competition Preparation on Tirzepatide

If you are training for a competition (local throwdown, Quarterfinals, Semifinals), consider these adjustments:

  • Maintain your current dose during training. Do not increase during the competitive season.
  • Consider dropping back one dose level (e.g., 7.5 mg to 5.0 mg) in the 3 weeks before competition to normalize appetite and maximize glycogen storage.
  • Practice eating competition-day nutrition during training at your current dose. Make sure you can consume enough between events.
  • Inject 4 to 5 days before competition day to position the lowest appetite-suppression point on the day you need to eat and perform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is tirzepatide banned in CrossFit competitions?

No. Tirzepatide is not on any banned substance list for CrossFit, including the CrossFit Games. It is a prescribed medication for weight management, not a performance-enhancing drug.

Will tirzepatide make me nauseous during WODs?

Nausea is possible, especially during the first 2 to 4 weeks at each new dose. High-intensity WODs can amplify GI discomfort. Inject on rest days and avoid max-effort training during dose transition weeks. Most athletes report that nausea resolves within 2 weeks at each dose level.

Can I do the Open on tirzepatide?

Absolutely. Plan your injection 4 to 5 days before the Open workout. Eat a proper pre-workout meal. Many athletes perform at their best during the Open while on tirzepatide because their body composition has improved significantly.

How do I know if I am losing too much muscle?

Track your lifts. If your back squat, deadlift, and clean drop by more than 10% without a corresponding training change, you may be in too aggressive a deficit. Increase protein and calories, or reduce your tirzepatide dose. A DEXA scan every 3 months provides objective body composition data. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:monitoring lean mass during tirzepatide use}}

Should my CrossFit coach know I am on tirzepatide?

That is your choice. If your coach programs your nutrition or adjusts your training load, sharing this information helps them make better decisions. If your coach simply writes WODs, there is no requirement to disclose.
