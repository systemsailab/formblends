The Current State of Wegovy Coverage

When Wegovy launched in 2021, fewer than 25% of commercial insurance plans covered anti-obesity medications . The landscape has shifted considerably. A 2025 analysis by the Obesity Action Coalition found that approximately 58% of large employer plans now include some coverage for FDA-approved anti-obesity medications, up from 42% in 2023 .

Two developments have accelerated this trend. First, the SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial demonstrated that semaglutide 2.4 mg reduces heart attacks, strokes, and cardiovascular deaths by 20%, making it harder for insurers to classify Wegovy as purely "cosmetic" . Second, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) expanded Medicare Part D coverage of Wegovy for beneficiaries with established cardiovascular disease, creating a ripple effect across the broader insurance market.

Understanding Prior Authorization

Prior authorization (PA) is the process by which your insurance company reviews clinical information to determine if a medication is medically necessary before agreeing to pay for it. For Wegovy, PA is nearly universal.

What Your Provider Needs to Submit

A strong prior authorization request typically includes:

  1. Current BMI documentation: Height and weight recorded during a clinical visit within the past 3 to 6 months
  2. Comorbidity documentation: Lab results or diagnosis codes confirming weight-related conditions (hypertension, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, sleep apnea)
  3. History of prior weight loss attempts: Documentation of supervised diet programs, behavioral counseling, or previous medications tried
  4. Clinical rationale: A letter from your provider explaining why Wegovy is medically necessary for your specific situation
  5. Relevant lab work: A1C, lipid panel, and other metabolic markers

PA Timeline

Insurance companies are generally required to respond to standard prior authorization requests within 5 to 15 business days, depending on state regulations. Expedited or urgent reviews may be processed within 24 to 72 hours if your provider documents medical urgency.

What to Do If Your Claim Is Denied

Denial is not the end of the road. According to a 2024 report from the American Medical Association, approximately 40% of initial prior authorization denials for GLP-1 medications are overturned on appeal . Here is how to navigate the process:

Step 1: Understand the Denial Reason

Common denial reasons include:

  • Not on formulary: Wegovy is not listed on your plan's drug list
  • Step therapy not completed: Your insurer requires you to try other medications first
  • Insufficient documentation: The PA submission lacked required clinical information
  • Does not meet medical necessity criteria: Your insurer's criteria differ from FDA labeling

Step 2: File a First-Level Appeal

Work with your provider to submit additional documentation. Include:

  • A detailed letter of medical necessity referencing the SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial
  • Published clinical guidelines supporting GLP-1 use for your specific conditions (e.g., Endocrine Society 2024 guidelines, AGA 2025 obesity management guidelines)
  • Any missing lab results or clinical notes from the initial submission

Step 3: Request a Peer-to-Peer Review

If the first appeal is denied, your provider can request a peer-to-peer review, which is a phone conversation between your prescriber and the insurance company's medical director. This gives your provider the chance to make a direct clinical argument. Peer-to-peer reviews overturn denials in a meaningful percentage of cases.

Step 4: File an External Appeal

If internal appeals fail, you have the right to an external review by an independent third party. This right is guaranteed under the Affordable Care Act for most health plans. The external reviewer's decision is binding on the insurance company.

Understanding Prior Authorization Requirements

Nearly every insurance plan that covers Wegovy requires prior authorization before approving the prescription. This process can feel bureaucratic, but understanding what insurers look for can speed up approval significantly.

What Insurers Typically Require

Most prior authorization requests for Wegovy must include: a documented BMI of 30 or higher (or 27+ with at least one weight-related comorbidity), evidence of previous weight loss attempts (usually diet and exercise programs lasting 3-6 months), documentation of comorbidities such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or sleep apnea, and a prescribing physician's statement of medical necessity.

Some plans also require a step therapy protocol, meaning you must try and fail on a less expensive medication (like oral semaglutide or phentermine) before Wegovy is approved. This can add weeks or months to the process, but your provider can sometimes request an exception if there's a clinical reason to skip the step therapy.

Timeline for Prior Authorization

Standard prior authorization decisions typically take 5-15 business days. Urgent requests can be processed in 24-72 hours if your provider marks the request as expedited. If your prior authorization is denied, you usually have 30-60 days to file an internal appeal, followed by the option for an external review by an independent organization.
