Wegovy And Hair Loss?

About 3 percent of Wegovy users in the STEP clinical trials reported hair loss, compared to roughly 1 percent in the placebo group. This hair shedding is typically telogen effluvium, a temporary condition driven by the rapid weight loss Wegovy produces rather than a direct effect of the semaglutide medication. It resolves on its own in most cases. {{CITE:STEP trial alopecia data for Wegovy}}

The Connection Between Wegovy and Shedding

Wegovy is the higher-dose formulation of semaglutide (2.4 mg), specifically approved for weight management. Because it produces more significant weight loss than the lower diabetes dose (Ozempic at up to 2.0 mg), the rate of telogen effluvium tends to be slightly higher. The faster and more dramatic the weight loss, the more likely your body will shift hair follicles into a resting phase as a stress response.

This is not a sign that something is wrong with your health. It is your body's natural reaction to a major change in caloric intake and body composition. The same pattern shows up after bariatric surgery, crash diets, and significant illness. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Telogen effluvium rates with high-dose semaglutide for weight management}}

What Telogen Effluvium Looks Like

Telogen effluvium causes diffuse thinning across the entire scalp, not bald patches in specific areas. You might notice more hair in your brush, on your pillow, or in the shower drain. The shedding typically begins three to six months after starting Wegovy, once significant weight loss has occurred. It may last two to six months before gradually slowing. If you see patchy bald spots instead of overall thinning, that suggests a different cause and warrants a medical evaluation.

How to Support Hair Health on Wegovy

We recommend these steps to minimize shedding and support regrowth:

  • Protect your protein intake. Wegovy reduces appetite, so you may eat less overall. Make protein the centerpiece of every meal and snack. Aim for at least 0.7 grams per pound of your target body weight daily.
  • Address nutrient gaps. Iron, ferritin, zinc, vitamin D, and biotin are all important for hair health. Ask your provider for lab work to check these levels and supplement as needed.
  • Do not over-restrict calories. Let Wegovy manage your appetite naturally. Intentionally eating far less than your body needs on top of the medication's appetite suppression will worsen nutritional deficiencies and hair loss.
  • Consider a collagen or biotin supplement. These are safe to take alongside semaglutide and may support keratin production. Look for quality brands with third-party testing.
  • Be patient. Regrowth takes time. Most people see improvement within six to twelve months after the shedding peaks. The follicles are not dead, just temporarily dormant.

When to Seek Medical Attention

Contact your healthcare provider if you experience any of the following: hair loss that continues for more than twelve months, visible bald patches, scalp irritation or tenderness, or shedding that began before you started Wegovy. These patterns may indicate thyroid disorders, autoimmune alopecia, iron deficiency anemia, or other conditions that require their own treatment. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Warning signs requiring workup beyond telogen effluvium}}

Related Questions

Is hair loss more common with Wegovy than Ozempic?

Slightly. Wegovy's higher dose (2.4 mg vs. Ozempic's maximum 2.0 mg) tends to produce faster weight loss, which increases the likelihood of telogen effluvium. However, the difference is modest. The biggest factor is how quickly you lose weight, not which semaglutide product you use.

Will reducing my Wegovy dose help with hair loss?

It could, if a lower dose slows your rate of weight loss. Talk to your prescriber about this trade-off. A slower dose escalation or holding at a moderate dose may reduce the metabolic stress that triggers shedding, though it may also mean a slower pace toward your weight goals.

Can I use minoxidil while taking Wegovy?

Yes. There are no known interactions between semaglutide and topical minoxidil. If you want to try minoxidil to stimulate regrowth, discuss it with your dermatologist or primary care provider. Minoxidil works best for androgenetic alopecia but may provide some benefit during telogen effluvium as well.

Does hair loss from Wegovy affect eyebrows or body hair?

Telogen effluvium primarily affects scalp hair. Eyebrow and body hair loss would be unusual and could signal a different condition like alopecia areata or a thyroid problem. If you notice non-scalp hair loss, bring it up with your doctor for further evaluation.
