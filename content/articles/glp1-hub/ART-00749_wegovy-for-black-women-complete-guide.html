Nutritional Priorities for Black Women on Wegovy

When appetite decreases, the quality of what you eat matters more than ever. Black women on Wegovy should pay particular attention to several nutrients.

  • Protein (60+ grams daily): Protects muscle mass and supports hair health. Chicken, fish, eggs, beans, and Greek yogurt should anchor every meal.
  • Vitamin D (1,000-2,000 IU daily): Most Black women are deficient due to melanin's effect on skin vitamin D synthesis. Supplementation is recommended even before starting Wegovy and becomes more important during weight loss.
  • Iron: Black women are at elevated risk for iron deficiency anemia. Reduced food intake can worsen this. Red meat, spinach, fortified cereals, and supplements as needed.
  • Calcium (1,000 mg daily): Essential for bone health, especially during weight loss when bone density can decrease. Dairy, fortified plant milk, or supplements.
  • Fiber: Helps manage constipation, one of Wegovy's common side effects. Beans, vegetables, oats, and fruit provide fiber along with other nutrients.

Hair Health During Weight Loss

This topic comes up frequently among Black women considering Wegovy, and it deserves a direct discussion.

Significant weight loss from any cause, not just medication, can trigger telogen effluvium, a temporary condition where hair follicles enter a resting phase and shedding increases. This is driven by caloric restriction and nutritional shifts rather than semaglutide itself.

How to protect your hair during treatment:

  • Maintain protein intake above 60 grams daily
  • Ensure adequate iron, zinc, biotin, and vitamin D levels (get labs checked)
  • Lose weight at a steady pace rather than crash-dieting; Wegovy's gradual escalation naturally supports this
  • Reduce heat styling and tight protective styles that add mechanical stress during periods of increased shedding
  • If shedding occurs, it typically resolves within three to six months as the body adjusts to its new weight

Current evidence supports long-term use for sustained benefit. Stopping typically leads to weight regain {{CITE:Wilding et al., JAMA, 2022}}. Your physician will discuss a long-term plan tailored to your situation, which may include dose adjustments over time.
