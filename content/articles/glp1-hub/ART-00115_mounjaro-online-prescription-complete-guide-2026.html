Eligibility: What Physicians Look For

A responsible physician evaluating you for a Mounjaro online prescription will assess:

BMI and Weight Criteria

  • BMI 30 or higher (obesity): Eligible for weight management treatment
  • BMI 27 or higher with at least one weight-related condition: Also eligible. Qualifying conditions include type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, obstructive sleep apnea, fatty liver disease, and cardiovascular disease.

Medical History

Your physician will review your complete medical history, including:

  • Current and past medical conditions
  • All current medications and supplements
  • Previous weight loss attempts (diet, exercise, other medications, surgery)
  • Family medical history, particularly thyroid cancer and pancreatic conditions
  • Mental health history
  • Pregnancy status or plans

Contraindications

You will not be prescribed tirzepatide if you have:

  • A personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • A known allergy to tirzepatide or any component of the medication
  • Active pregnancy or breastfeeding

Relative contraindications (conditions that require careful evaluation rather than automatic disqualification) include a history of pancreatitis, severe gastrointestinal disease, and active gallbladder disease. A thorough physician evaluation addresses these on a case-by-case basis .

Is it legal to get Mounjaro prescribed online?

Yes. Telehealth prescribing is legal in all 50 states, though specific regulations vary by state. Tirzepatide is not a controlled substance, which simplifies the prescribing process.

Do I need to have a video call with the physician?

Requirements vary by state and platform. Some states require synchronous interaction (video or phone call), while others allow asynchronous evaluation (physician reviews your written assessment). Is the online assessment really thorough enough?

A well-designed online assessment collects the same medical information as an in-person visit. The main limitation is the absence of a physical examination and in-office vital signs. Can I transfer my online prescription to a local pharmacy?

This depends on the program. Compounded medications are prepared by specific pharmacies and typically cannot be transferred. Brand prescriptions may be transferable, though insurance and cost considerations still apply.
