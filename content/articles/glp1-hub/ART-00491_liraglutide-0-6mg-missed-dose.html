Liraglutide 0.6mg: What To Do About a Missed Dose

If you miss your daily liraglutide 0.6mg injection, skip the missed dose and take your next injection at the regular scheduled time the following day. Do not take a double dose to compensate. Because liraglutide is a daily injection with a 13-hour half-life, missed doses are handled differently than weekly GLP-1 medications. At the 0.6mg starting dose, a single missed day has virtually no impact on your progress.

The Missed Dose Rule for Daily Liraglutide

Unlike weekly injectables (tirzepatide, semaglutide) that have a multi-day catch-up window, liraglutide's missed dose guidance is simpler because it is a daily medication:

  • Remembered same day (within 12 hours of usual time): You may take it. Then resume your regular schedule the next day.
  • More than 12 hours late or remembered the next day: Skip the missed dose entirely. Take your next injection at the normal time.
  • Never double up: Taking two doses in one day significantly increases nausea and vomiting risk.

This protocol differs from weekly GLP-1 medications because liraglutide clears the body much faster. The 13-hour half-life means that within about 24 to 36 hours of a missed dose, blood levels have dropped substantially.

Impact of a Missed Dose at 0.6mg

At the starting dose, missing a single day has almost no detectable impact:

  • Appetite: The appetite-suppressing effect at 0.6mg is already minimal. Missing one day may produce no noticeable change in hunger at all.
  • Side effects: If you were experiencing mild nausea at 0.6mg, missing a day may actually provide brief relief. When you resume, the nausea is unlikely to return with the same intensity.
  • Weight loss: At a sub-therapeutic dose, one missed day has no meaningful effect on weight trajectory.
  • Titration timeline: One missed day does not require restarting the 0.6mg phase. Continue with your titration schedule as planned.

The main concern with missed doses at 0.6mg is building the daily habit. If you are missing doses in the first week, address the habit formation issue now before it becomes a pattern at higher, more important doses.

Building a Reliable Daily Injection Habit

Daily injections require more discipline than weekly ones. Strategies that work:

  • Anchor to a daily ritual: Link your injection to something you already do every day without fail. Morning coffee, brushing teeth, or setting your alarm at night are common anchors.
  • Same time every day: Consistency helps the habit become automatic. Choose a time when you are reliably home and calm.
  • Phone alarm: Set a non-dismissible alarm that repeats daily. Some patients use medication reminder apps that track adherence and send follow-up alerts if you do not confirm.
  • Visible pen placement: Keep the pen where you will see it at injection time. A bathroom shelf for morning injectors, a nightstand for evening injectors.
  • Weekly pill organizer strategy: Some patients mark each day of the week on a small card and check it off after injecting. This simple visual tracker helps identify missed doses.

Multiple Missed Doses at 0.6mg

If you miss two or more consecutive days at 0.6mg during the first week:

  • Two missed days: Resume at 0.6mg. No need to restart the clock, but consider extending your 0.6mg phase by the number of days missed before moving to 1.2mg.
  • Three or more missed days: Contact your prescriber. They may recommend restarting the one-week 0.6mg phase from the beginning to ensure proper GI adaptation.

Frequently Asked Questions

I forgot my injection this morning. Can I take it tonight?
If it has been less than 12 hours since your usual injection time, yes. If more than 12 hours have passed, skip it and inject at your normal time tomorrow. Taking it very late in the day and then again the next morning creates too short an interval between doses.
Will missing a dose at 0.6mg delay my move to 1.2mg?
One missed dose does not require extending the 0.6mg phase. Your body still received six out of seven days of GI conditioning. Proceed to 1.2mg on schedule unless your prescriber advises otherwise.
I keep forgetting my injections. Should I switch to a weekly GLP-1 instead?
If daily injections prove consistently difficult to remember after trying multiple reminder strategies, a weekly medication may be a better fit. Discuss this with your prescriber. Does it matter if my injection times vary by a few hours each day?
Minor variation (1 to 2 hours) is fine. Liraglutide maintains relatively stable blood levels with daily dosing even if the exact time shifts slightly. Consistency is ideal, but perfection is not required.
