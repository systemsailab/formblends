Side Effects at the Three-Month Mark

Most patients are well-adapted to Mounjaro by month three. The dose increase to 7.5 mg is usually well tolerated :

  • Nausea: Usually very mild and short-lived with each dose increase at this stage.
  • Constipation: The most persistent complaint. Effective management includes psyllium husk, magnesium, and 80+ ounces of water daily.
  • Decreased appetite to the point of food aversion: Some patients develop strong aversions to certain foods, particularly meats and rich dishes. This is temporary and usually resolves as your body adjusts.
  • Injection site bruising: Occasional minor bruising at the injection site. Rotating between abdomen, thigh, and upper arm helps.

If you experience persistent issues, your provider can slow the titration pace or temporarily reduce your dose .

Tips for Month Three and Beyond

  • Do not skip meals. Even though your appetite is reduced, aim for three balanced meals daily to maintain adequate nutrition.
  • Eat protein first. Start every meal with your protein source to ensure you get enough before feeling full.
  • Add creatine to your routine. Creatine monohydrate (3 to 5 grams daily) supports muscle preservation during weight loss.
  • Track body composition, not just weight. A body fat scale or periodic DEXA scans help you see muscle vs. fat changes.
