What Happens If I Miss A Semaglutide Dose?

Missing a semaglutide dose may cause a temporary return of appetite, slight blood sugar fluctuations, and reduced weight loss momentum. If fewer than five days have passed since your missed dose, take it as soon as you remember. If five or more days have passed, skip the missed dose and take your next dose on schedule.

The Five-Day Rule for Missed Semaglutide Doses

Semaglutide is a once-weekly injection with a half-life of approximately seven days. The prescribing information provides a clear guideline: if you remember within five days of the missed dose, administer it right away and then resume your regular weekly schedule. If more than five days have elapsed, skip that dose entirely and wait for your next scheduled injection day.

This five-day window exists because taking two doses too close together increases the risk of gastrointestinal side effects like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. The goal is to maintain a roughly weekly interval between injections.

What You May Notice After a Missed Dose

Because semaglutide has a long half-life, a single missed dose typically does not cause dramatic changes. However, you may experience increased hunger or appetite as the medication's appetite-suppressing effects begin to fade, mild fluctuations in blood sugar levels if you are using semaglutide for type 2 diabetes, reduced feelings of fullness after meals, and cravings that were previously well controlled. These effects are temporary and will resolve once you resume your regular dosing schedule.

Impact on Weight Loss Progress

A single missed dose is unlikely to significantly derail your weight loss progress. Semaglutide builds up in your system over time due to its long half-life, so one missed injection does not mean the drug has completely left your body. However, consistently missing doses or extending gaps between injections can reduce the medication's effectiveness and slow your results. If you find yourself regularly forgetting doses, consider setting a recurring phone alarm or choosing a specific day and time that is easy to remember.

What Happens If You Miss Multiple Doses

If you miss two or more consecutive doses, contact your prescribing physician before resuming the medication. Depending on how long you have been off the drug and what dose you were taking, your provider may recommend restarting at a lower dose and re-titrating upward. This reduces the likelihood of severe gastrointestinal side effects that can occur when reintroducing the medication after a gap.

For patients on higher doses (1.0 mg, 1.7 mg, or 2.4 mg), jumping back to the full dose after a prolonged break can cause intense nausea and vomiting. A brief re-titration period is a small inconvenience compared to days of severe GI discomfort.

What to Consider

Missing a dose is not a reason to panic or double up on your next injection. Never take two doses at the same time or within a few days of each other to "make up" for a missed dose. If you are unsure what to do after missing a dose, call your prescribing physician or pharmacist before taking action. Consistency is important for optimal results, so build your injection routine into a regular habit.

Related Questions

Can I change my semaglutide injection day?

Yes. You can change your injection day as long as there are at least two days (48 hours) between your last dose and the new dose. After the switch, continue injecting on the new day each week.

Does missing a dose of semaglutide cause withdrawal symptoms?

Semaglutide does not cause physical withdrawal symptoms. You may notice increased appetite or blood sugar changes, but these are simply the return of symptoms the medication was managing, not withdrawal effects.

Should I eat differently after missing a semaglutide dose?

You may feel hungrier after missing a dose. Focus on high-protein, high-fiber meals that promote satiety and avoid high-calorie, low-nutrient foods that could lead to overeating during the gap in medication coverage.

Will missing one dose affect my A1C?

A single missed dose is unlikely to have a measurable impact on your A1C, which reflects average blood sugar over two to three months. However, repeated missed doses could lead to sustained blood sugar elevations that affect A1C over time.

What if I vomited after taking semaglutide and am unsure if I received the full dose?

Semaglutide is injected subcutaneously, not taken orally, so vomiting does not affect absorption. If you vomited shortly after injecting, you still received the full dose. Do not administer a second injection.
