Side Effects and Safety at Three Months

By the three-month mark, your body has adapted to tirzepatide and most initial side effects have resolved. The step to 7.5 mg may produce brief GI effects :

  • Nausea: Mild and brief with dose increases. Typically 1 to 2 days at most.
  • Decreased appetite to the point of undereating: At 7.5 mg, some patients struggle to eat enough. This can lead to fatigue and nutrient deficiencies if not addressed.
  • Constipation: Remains the most common ongoing GI complaint. Magnesium supplements and increased water intake help.
  • Sulfur-smelling burps: Some tirzepatide patients report this unique side effect, which is harmless but unpleasant.

Talk to your provider about any concerns. Adjusting the dose or adding supportive treatments can make a significant difference .

Strategies for Continued Progress

  • Prioritize nutrient density. With reduced appetite, every meal needs to count nutritionally. Focus on lean proteins, vegetables, and healthy fats.
  • Consider a multivitamin. Reduced food intake can lead to micronutrient gaps. A daily multivitamin with iron and vitamin D is a good safety net.
  • Build a consistent exercise routine. Aim for 30 minutes of activity at least five days per week, mixing cardio and resistance training.
  • Stay hydrated. Target 80 to 100 ounces of water daily at this dose level.
