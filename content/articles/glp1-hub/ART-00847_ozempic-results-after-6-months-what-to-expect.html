Six months on Ozempic is a significant achievement, and the results speak for themselves. Ozempic Dosing at Six Months

By month six, most patients are well-established at either 1.0 mg or 2.0 mg weekly :

  • At 1.0 mg: Patients who achieved satisfactory results without needing the maximum dose. Average weight loss of 15 to 20 pounds.
  • At 2.0 mg: Patients who needed additional efficacy beyond 1.0 mg. Average weight loss of 18 to 25 pounds.

The SUSTAIN FORTE trial showed that the 2.0 mg dose produced an additional 1.8 kg (about 4 pounds) of weight loss compared to 1.0 mg over 40 weeks . Whether to advance from 1.0 to 2.0 mg depends on your response and goals.

The Ozempic Advantage for Diabetes Patients

Six months of Ozempic therapy produces remarkable diabetes outcomes :

  • A1C below 7%: Achieved by 60 to 75% of patients on the 1.0 mg dose.
  • Fasting glucose normalization: Many patients see readings below 100 mg/dL for the first time in years.
  • Reduced insulin needs: Patients on supplemental insulin often reduce or eliminate their doses.
  • Lower hypoglycemia risk: Unlike sulfonylureas and insulin, Ozempic has minimal risk of causing dangerously low blood sugar.
  • Cardiovascular protection: The SUSTAIN-6 trial demonstrated a 26% reduction in major cardiovascular events .

Side Effects at Six Months

By six months, Ozempic is well-tolerated for the vast majority of patients :

  • GI effects: Resolved for most patients. Rarely an issue at stable doses.
  • Constipation: May persist but is manageable with established routines.
  • Gallbladder considerations: Rapid weight loss can increase gallstone risk. Report right-sided abdominal pain promptly.
  • Nutritional adequacy: Extended reduced appetite requires conscious effort to meet nutritional needs.
