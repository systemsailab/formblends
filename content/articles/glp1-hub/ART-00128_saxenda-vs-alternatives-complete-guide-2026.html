Saxenda vs. Wegovy (Semaglutide)

This is the most direct comparison because both are pure GLP-1 receptor agonists. The STEP 8 trial (published in JAMA, 2022) randomized 338 adults to either semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly or liraglutide 3.0 mg daily for 68 weeks. The results were definitive:

  • Semaglutide group: 15.8% mean body weight loss
  • Liraglutide group: 6.4% mean body weight loss
  • Proportion achieving 10%+ weight loss: 70.9% (semaglutide) vs. 25.6% (liraglutide)
  • Proportion achieving 20%+ weight loss: 38.5% (semaglutide) vs. 6.0% (liraglutide)

{{CITE:Rubino DM et al. STEP 8 trial, JAMA 2022}}

The superiority of semaglutide comes from its higher binding affinity to the GLP-1 receptor and its dramatically longer half-life (7 days vs. 13 hours for liraglutide). This means more sustained receptor activation and stronger appetite suppression around the clock.

When Saxenda Might Still Be the Better Choice

  • Insurance coverage. Some plans cover Saxenda (especially if liraglutide has been on formulary since 2014) but have not yet added Wegovy.
  • Supply availability. Wegovy has experienced intermittent supply shortages since its launch. Saxenda supply has been more stable.
  • Preference for daily dosing. Some patients prefer the control of a daily injection that wears off in hours rather than a weekly injection whose effects last for days.
  • Dose flexibility. If you experience side effects, Saxenda's daily dosing allows for more granular adjustments (skipping a day, briefly stepping down to a lower dose).
  • Prior response. Patients who responded well to Victoza (liraglutide 1.2 or 1.8 mg for diabetes) are likely good candidates for the weight management dose.

Saxenda vs. Zepbound (Tirzepatide)

Zepbound represents the newest class of weight loss medication: a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. By activating both incretin receptors simultaneously, tirzepatide produces the most dramatic weight loss of any approved medication.

In the SURMOUNT-1 trial, patients on the highest tirzepatide dose (15 mg weekly) lost an average of 22.5% of their body weight over 72 weeks. At the lowest dose (5 mg), they still lost 15% {{CITE:Jastreboff AM et al. SURMOUNT-1 trial, NEJM 2022}}. Even tirzepatide's minimum dose produces nearly double the weight loss of Saxenda's maximum dose.

The mechanism difference is significant. GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) acts on fat tissue to improve fat storage and breakdown, reduces inflammation, and may have direct effects on brain appetite centers that complement GLP-1 signaling {{CITE:Samms RJ et al. J Clin Invest 2022}}.

Practical Differences

  • Weight loss magnitude: Zepbound produces roughly 2.5 to 3 times the weight loss of Saxenda.
  • Injection frequency: Weekly (Zepbound) vs daily (Saxenda). Most patients strongly prefer weekly dosing.
  • Side effect profile: Both cause similar GI side effects (nausea, diarrhea, constipation). Zepbound may cause more nausea during escalation due to dual receptor activation.
  • Cost: Zepbound is slightly less expensive at retail (approximately $1,000 to $1,100/month) compared to Saxenda ($1,350 to $1,500/month) {{VERIFY_PRICE:Zepbound}} {{VERIFY_PRICE:Saxenda}}.
  • Track record: Saxenda has over 10 years of post-market safety data. Zepbound was approved in late 2023 and has roughly 2 years of real-world experience.

Saxenda vs. Rybelsus (Oral Semaglutide)

Rybelsus is an oral form of semaglutide approved for type 2 diabetes, increasingly used off-label for weight management. It appeals to patients who want the GLP-1 mechanism without injections.

However, oral semaglutide has significantly lower bioavailability than injectable semaglutide. Only about 1% of the oral dose is absorbed, which is why Rybelsus doses (7 to 14 mg) are much higher numerically than Wegovy doses (up to 2.4 mg) {{CITE:FDA Rybelsus prescribing information 2024}}. The weight loss efficacy of oral semaglutide 14 mg falls somewhere between Saxenda and Wegovy, with studies showing approximately 6 to 10% body weight reduction depending on the population studied.

Saxenda vs. Contrave (Naltrexone/Bupropion)

Contrave works through an entirely different mechanism. It combines naltrexone (an opioid receptor antagonist) with bupropion (an antidepressant) to reduce food cravings and the reward value of eating. It does not affect GLP-1 receptors or gastric emptying.

In the COR-I trial, Contrave produced an average weight loss of 5.4% over 56 weeks, compared to 1.3% for placebo {{CITE:Greenway FL et al. COR-I trial, Lancet 2010}}. This is roughly two-thirds the efficacy of Saxenda.

Where Contrave May Be Preferred

  • Cost. At $300 to $500 per month, Contrave is significantly cheaper than any GLP-1 medication {{VERIFY_PRICE:Contrave}}.
  • No injections. Contrave is a twice-daily pill, which many patients prefer.
  • Emotional eating. Because bupropion affects dopamine and norepinephrine, Contrave may be particularly effective for patients whose weight gain is driven by emotional or reward-based eating rather than physiological hunger.
  • Depression co-occurrence. Patients with concurrent depression may benefit from bupropion's mood-elevating effects alongside weight loss.

Where Saxenda Is Superior

  • Weight loss magnitude. Saxenda produces approximately 50% more weight loss than Contrave on average.
  • Blood sugar benefits. GLP-1 agonism provides meaningful improvements in glucose metabolism that Contrave does not.
  • Cardiovascular data. Liraglutide has cardiovascular outcome data (LEADER trial) showing reduced major cardiac events in type 2 diabetes patients {{CITE:Marso SP et al. LEADER trial, NEJM 2016}}.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Saxenda still worth taking with newer options available?

For some patients, yes. If your insurance covers Saxenda but not Wegovy or Zepbound, if you prefer daily dosing, or if you have responded well to liraglutide in the past, Saxenda remains a reasonable choice. It produces meaningful weight loss (8% average) that significantly exceeds diet and exercise alone (2 to 3%).

Can I switch from Saxenda to Wegovy?

Yes. Many patients start on Saxenda and later transition to Wegovy for greater weight loss. There is no required washout period. Your provider will typically start Wegovy's dose escalation from the beginning (0.25 mg weekly) even if you were on full-dose Saxenda, though some may start at a slightly higher dose based on your tolerance history.

Why does Saxenda cost more than Zepbound?

Pricing in the pharmaceutical market is complex and does not always reflect efficacy. Saxenda's pricing was set in 2014 when it was the only GLP-1 weight loss option. Eli Lilly priced Zepbound more aggressively to capture market share. Actual out-of-pocket costs depend heavily on your insurance plan and available savings programs {{VERIFY_PRICE:Saxenda}} {{VERIFY_PRICE:Zepbound}}.

Is Contrave safer than Saxenda?

Both medications have distinct safety profiles. Contrave carries a boxed warning for suicidal ideation (due to bupropion) and is contraindicated in patients with seizure disorders, eating disorders, or those taking opioids. Saxenda carries a boxed warning for thyroid C-cell tumors (observed in rodents). Neither medication is inherently "safer." The right choice depends on your specific health profile {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Saxenda vs Contrave safety comparison}}.

Can I combine Saxenda with other weight loss medications?

Saxenda should not be combined with other GLP-1 receptor agonists (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Victoza). Combining Saxenda with non-GLP-1 medications like phentermine or Contrave is sometimes done off-label by weight loss specialists, but this requires careful medical supervision and is not FDA-approved {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 combination therapy safety}}.
