Key Strategies for a 60-Pound Goal

1. Optimize Your Dose Progression

Work closely with your provider on dose titration. Some patients do best moving steadily to 15 mg, while others achieve excellent results at 10 mg with fewer side effects. The right dose is the one that produces consistent weight loss with tolerable side effects.

2. Resistance Train Like It Matters (Because It Does)

At 60 pounds of loss, the difference between patients who strength train and those who do not is enormous. Four sessions per week targeting all major muscle groups is the standard. Compound movements, progressive overload, and adequate recovery are essential. resistance training program for tirzepatide patients

3. Consider Creatine Supplementation

Creatine monohydrate (3 to 5 grams daily) supports muscle preservation and exercise performance during weight loss. It is well-studied, safe, and may help counteract the muscle-depleting effects of a prolonged calorie deficit. Discuss with your provider.

4. Schedule Quarterly Comprehensive Lab Panels

Full metabolic panels, thyroid function, vitamin D, B12, iron, and liver enzymes should be checked every three months. A 60-pound weight loss journey puts demands on your body, and early detection of any deficiency or imbalance allows for prompt correction.

5. Build a Post-Goal Maintenance Strategy

Start planning for life after 60 pounds by month 4 or 5. What dose will you maintain on? What exercise level is sustainable long-term? What eating patterns can you stick with permanently? Having this plan in place before you reach your goal improves long-term success. GLP-1 maintenance strategies
