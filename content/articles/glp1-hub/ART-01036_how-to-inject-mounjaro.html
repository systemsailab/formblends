How To Inject Mounjaro?

Mounjaro is injected subcutaneously (under the skin) once per week using a single-dose prefilled pen. Pull off the gray base cap, place the clear base flat against your abdomen, thigh, or upper arm, unlock the lock ring, press the purple button, and hold until you hear two clicks confirming the dose is complete.

Step-by-Step Mounjaro Injection Process

Start by washing your hands with soap and water. Remove the Mounjaro pen from the refrigerator and allow it to reach room temperature for approximately 30 minutes. Do not try to speed this up with external heat sources. Inspect the medication through the pen window. It should be clear and colorless to slightly yellow. Do not use the pen if you see cloudiness, discoloration, or floating particles.

Pull the gray base cap straight off the pen. This step unlocks the pen and prepares it for use. Do not replace the cap once removed. The needle is hidden inside the clear base and will not be visible at any point during the injection. Choose an injection site: the lower abdomen (at least two inches from the navel), the front of the thigh, or the back of the upper arm. Clean the area with an alcohol swab and allow it to dry completely.

Place the clear base of the pen flat against your skin at the injection site. Press firmly so the base sits flush with the skin. Turn the lock ring to unlock. Press and hold the purple injection button with your thumb. You will hear a first click when the injection starts. Keep the pen pressed firmly in place. When you hear the second click, the injection is complete. Confirm delivery by checking that the gray plunger is visible in the pen window. Remove the pen from your skin. The needle retracts automatically into the pen housing.

Mounjaro Pen Dose Strengths and Color Coding

Mounjaro pens are color-coded by dose to help prevent dosing errors. The 2.5 mg pen is purple, 5 mg is brown, 7.5 mg is green, 10 mg is dark blue, 12.5 mg is light blue, and 15 mg is orange. Always check the label on your pen before injecting to confirm the dose matches your prescription. Each pen contains a single dose of tirzepatide and is discarded after one use. You cannot adjust the dose within a pen.

Choosing and Rotating Injection Sites

Rotate your injection site each week. Using the same spot repeatedly can cause hardening of the tissue beneath the skin, which may reduce how effectively the medication is absorbed. Alternate between different body areas or move the injection location within the same area. Keep at least one inch of space between consecutive spots. Avoid areas with tattoos, moles, scars, or skin conditions. The abdomen tends to be the most comfortable and accessible site for most patients.

What to Consider

The Mounjaro pen is designed for single use. Once you have injected, the pen cannot be reused even if you believe the full dose was not delivered. If the gray plunger is not visible in the window after injection, contact your prescribing physician before taking any additional action. Dispose of the pen in a sharps container. Never throw it in household trash or recycling. Do not share your Mounjaro pen with anyone else, as this creates a risk of infection transmission. If you have difficulty pressing the button or manipulating the pen due to hand strength or dexterity issues, ask your provider about assistive options or have a trained caregiver administer the injection.

Related Questions

Is the Mounjaro pen the same as the Zepbound pen?

The pen design and injection mechanism are identical, as both deliver tirzepatide. The difference is the indication: Mounjaro is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes, while Zepbound is approved for chronic weight management. The dose strengths and pen operation are the same for both products.

What should I do if the Mounjaro pen feels stuck or the button will not press?

Make sure you have removed the gray base cap and turned the lock ring to unlock the pen. If the button still will not depress, the pen may be defective. Do not force it. Contact your pharmacy for a replacement pen and store the defective pen for return.

Can I inject Mounjaro through clothing?

No. Always inject into clean, bare skin. Injecting through clothing increases the risk of infection and can prevent the needle from reaching the correct depth for subcutaneous delivery. Take the time to expose and properly clean the injection site before each dose.

How long does it take for the Mounjaro injection to absorb?

Tirzepatide is absorbed gradually from the subcutaneous injection site over the hours following administration. Peak blood levels typically occur about 8 to 72 hours after injection. The medication continues working throughout the full week until your next dose.
