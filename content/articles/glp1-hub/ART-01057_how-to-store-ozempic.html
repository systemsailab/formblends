How To Store Ozempic?

Ozempic (semaglutide) pens should be stored in the refrigerator at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius) before first use. After the first injection, the pen can remain refrigerated or be kept at room temperature below 86 degrees Fahrenheit for up to 56 days. Never freeze Ozempic, and discard it if freezing occurs.

Refrigerator Storage Before First Use

Keep unopened Ozempic pens in their original carton inside the refrigerator. The carton protects the medication from light exposure, which can degrade the semaglutide over time. Position pens away from the back wall of the refrigerator, where temperatures tend to dip closer to freezing. A refrigerator thermometer can help you confirm that your unit stays within the recommended 36 to 46 degree range consistently.

Unopened pens remain usable until the expiration date printed on the carton, as long as proper refrigeration is maintained. If you receive Ozempic through a mail-order pharmacy, open the package immediately and check that the cold packs are still intact and the medication feels cool. Contact your pharmacy if the shipment arrives warm or without adequate cold packing.

Storage After First Use

Once you give your first injection from an Ozempic pen, you have 56 days to use the remaining doses. During this window, the pen can stay in the refrigerator or at room temperature up to 86 degrees Fahrenheit (30 degrees Celsius). After 56 days, throw the pen away even if medication remains inside. Write the date of first use on the pen label or set a phone reminder so you do not lose track.

Always replace the pen cap after each injection. The cap shields the medication from light and helps maintain stability. Store the pen with the cap on and the needle removed. Leaving a needle attached between injections can allow air to enter the cartridge or medication to leak, which affects dose accuracy.

What Temperatures Will Damage Ozempic?

Temperatures above 86 degrees Fahrenheit or below 36 degrees Fahrenheit can compromise the medication. Freezing is especially harmful because it disrupts the protein structure of semaglutide, making the medication ineffective or unpredictable. Heat exposure above the safe range can cause similar degradation. Traveling With Ozempic

Pack your Ozempic pen in an insulated medication travel pouch with a gel ice pack when traveling. Place a cloth or barrier between the ice pack and the pen to prevent accidental freezing. On flights, always carry Ozempic in your carry-on bag. Cargo holds experience extreme temperature swings that can freeze or overheat the medication. Bring your prescription label or a letter from your prescriber to help with airport security screening.

For road trips, keep the travel case in the passenger cabin rather than the trunk. Even in mild weather, trunk temperatures can climb rapidly. If your trip lasts longer than a day, make sure your hotel room has a refrigerator to store the pen overnight. Most hotel front desks can also refrigerate medication for you upon request.

How to Inspect Ozempic Before Use

Before every injection, look through the pen window. The solution should appear clear, colorless, and free of particles. Do not inject if the liquid looks cloudy, has floating specks, or has changed color. These visual changes suggest the medication may have been compromised by heat, light, or freezing. When in doubt, replace the pen with a new one rather than risk an ineffective dose. {{CITE:Ozempic prescribing information on visual inspection requirements}}

Related Questions

Can I use Ozempic that was left out of the fridge overnight?

Yes, in most cases. As long as the room temperature stayed below 86 degrees Fahrenheit, one night at room temperature will not harm the pen. Ozempic is stable at room temperature for up to 56 days after first use. Simply return the pen to the refrigerator and continue using it within the 56-day window.

What should I do if my Ozempic pen was accidentally frozen?

Discard it immediately and contact your pharmacy for a replacement. Freezing damages the semaglutide protein, and a pen that has been frozen should not be used even after it thaws completely. To prevent accidental freezing, keep pens away from the freezer compartment and the back wall of the fridge.

Can I store Ozempic in a mini fridge or wine cooler?

A mini fridge is fine as long as it holds a steady temperature between 36 and 46 degrees Fahrenheit. Many mini fridges have inconsistent cooling and may freeze items near the cooling plate. Use a thermometer to verify. Wine coolers are typically set to around 55 degrees, which falls within the acceptable room-temperature range for an in-use pen but is too warm for long-term storage of unopened pens.

How do I dispose of an expired Ozempic pen?

Place used or expired Ozempic pens in an FDA-cleared sharps disposal container. Do not throw pens in the regular trash or recycling without a sharps container. Many pharmacies, hospitals, and community health programs offer free sharps disposal. If a sharps container is not available, use a puncture-resistant household container like a heavy plastic laundry detergent bottle, seal it, and label it before placing it in the trash.
