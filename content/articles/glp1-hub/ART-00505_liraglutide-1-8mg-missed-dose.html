Missed Your Liraglutide 1.8mg Dose? Here Is What to Do

If you missed a liraglutide 1.8mg dose, take it as soon as you remember on the same day, then return to your regular schedule the next day. If an entire day has passed, skip the missed dose and inject your next dose at the normal time. Do not double up. We know it can feel stressful, but a single missed dose will not undo your progress. Here is the full breakdown.

The Daily Dosing Rule for Liraglutide

Unlike weekly GLP-1 injections such as semaglutide, liraglutide is a once-daily medication. This means the missed-dose rules are different and simpler. The prescribing information for both Victoza (1.8mg for diabetes) and Saxenda (up to 3.0mg for weight management) provides clear guidance.

Why You Should Never Double Up

It might seem logical to take two doses to "catch up," but doubling your liraglutide dose is dangerous. Taking 3.6mg instead of 1.8mg significantly increases the risk of severe nausea, vomiting, and potentially hypoglycemia, especially if you are also taking insulin or sulfonylureas.

If you accidentally injected twice in one day, monitor for symptoms and contact your physician or a poison control center for guidance.

What Missing a Dose Does to Your Body

Liraglutide has a half-life of approximately 13 hours, which is why it requires daily dosing. After missing one dose, drug levels in your system drop but do not disappear completely. Here is what that means practically:

  • Blood sugar: If you are using liraglutide for type 2 diabetes, a single missed dose may cause a temporary rise in blood glucose. Monitor your levels more frequently on days you miss a dose.
  • Appetite: You may notice your appetite returns to a more normal level for a day or two. This is temporary and will resolve once you resume dosing.
  • Weight trajectory: One missed dose has a negligible impact on your overall weight loss trend. Consistency over weeks and months is what matters, not perfection on any single day.
  • GI symptoms: Some patients actually feel better on the day they miss a dose, with less nausea or fullness. This is normal and expected as drug levels decline.

Handling Extended Gaps: Three or More Days

If you missed liraglutide for three or more consecutive days, contact your physician before restarting. Depending on how long the gap was, they may recommend a brief re-titration, such as taking 0.6mg or 1.2mg for a day or two before returning to 1.8mg. This reduces the chance of a resurgence of nausea and vomiting that can happen when GLP-1 levels spike after a break.

Practical Tips to Avoid Missing Doses

Daily injection adherence is one of the biggest challenges with liraglutide. Here are strategies that work for our patients:

  • Set a daily alarm. Pick a specific time and set a recurring phone alarm. Many patients find that tying the injection to another daily habit (like brushing teeth) helps it stick.
  • Keep your pen visible. Store it in the refrigerator door (before first use) or in a consistent spot at room temperature (after first use, up to 30 days). Out of sight often means out of mind.
  • Use a tracking app. Several free medication reminder apps let you log daily injections and send push notifications if you forget.
  • Travel kit. When traveling, pack your pen in an insulated pouch with a cold pack and set a time-zone-adjusted alarm so you do not lose your routine.
  • Flexible timing. Liraglutide can be injected at any time of day, with or without food. If mornings are chaotic, try an evening routine instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change the time of day I take liraglutide?

Yes. You can shift your injection time gradually without restarting the titration. For example, if you normally inject at 8 AM and want to switch to 8 PM, you can shift by a few hours each day until you reach the preferred time. Consistency matters more than the exact hour.

Will one missed dose cause my blood sugar to spike?

If you take liraglutide 1.8mg for type 2 diabetes, a single missed dose may cause a modest rise in blood glucose, but it is unlikely to cause a dangerous spike in most patients. Monitor your levels and resume your normal dose the next day.

What if I am not sure whether I injected today?

If you cannot remember whether you took your dose, it is generally safer to skip than to risk a double dose. Consider using a tracking method (app, written log, or marks on a calendar) to prevent this uncertainty in the future.

Does missing doses reduce the effectiveness of liraglutide over time?

Occasional missed doses will not significantly undermine your results. However, a pattern of missing two or more doses per week can reduce the medication's effectiveness for both blood sugar control and weight management. If adherence is a persistent struggle, talk to your physician about whether a weekly GLP-1 injection might be a better fit. liraglutide vs semaglutide

Should I eat differently on a day I missed my dose?

There is no specific dietary protocol for missed-dose days, but you may notice increased hunger. Be mindful of portion sizes and try to stick to your usual eating plan. Avoiding high-calorie comfort foods on days when your appetite is less suppressed can help protect your progress.
