What Is Semaglutide and Why Is It Prescribed?

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist, a class of medication originally developed to treat type 2 diabetes. It works by mimicking a natural hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1, which helps regulate blood sugar and appetite.

Today, semaglutide is prescribed for two primary purposes:

  • Type 2 diabetes management (marketed as Ozempic)
  • Chronic weight management (marketed as Wegovy)

Clinical trials have shown that semaglutide can help patients lose an average of 15 to 17 percent of their body weight when combined with diet and exercise changes. For people with type 2 diabetes, it also lowers HbA1c levels by 1.0 to 1.8 percentage points on average.

Who Qualifies for a Semaglutide Prescription?

For weight management, you generally need to meet one of these criteria:

  • A BMI of 30 or higher (classified as obesity)
  • A BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related health condition such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol

For type 2 diabetes, your prescribing physician will evaluate your blood sugar control, current medications, and overall health history to determine if semaglutide is right for you. semaglutide for type 2 diabetes

Is It Legal to Get Semaglutide Online?

Yes, getting a semaglutide prescription online is completely legal when it comes from a licensed physician practicing within their state of licensure. The Ryan Haight Act requires that controlled substances prescribed online involve at least one in-person evaluation, but semaglutide is not a controlled substance.

Telehealth prescribing expanded significantly during the COVID-19 pandemic, and many of those regulatory changes became permanent. As of 2026, telehealth prescribing of non-controlled medications like semaglutide is well-established legal practice across all 50 states.

State-by-State Considerations

While semaglutide can be prescribed via telehealth in every state, some states have specific requirements. A few states require a video consultation rather than an asynchronous review. Others require that the prescribing physician hold a license in the state where the patient resides. Reputable telehealth platforms ensure they meet all state-specific requirements.

Semaglutide Dosing: What to Expect

Semaglutide is administered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection. Your physician will start you on a low dose and gradually increase it over several months. This titration schedule helps minimize side effects, especially nausea.

Standard Dosing Schedule for Weight Management

  1. Weeks 1 through 4: 0.25 mg weekly
  2. Weeks 5 through 8: 0.5 mg weekly
  3. Weeks 9 through 12: 1.0 mg weekly
  4. Weeks 13 through 16: 1.7 mg weekly
  5. Week 17 and beyond: 2.4 mg weekly (maintenance dose)

Your physician may adjust this schedule based on how you respond and any side effects you experience. Some patients stay at a lower dose if they are seeing good results without needing the full 2.4 mg.

Safety Considerations for Online Prescriptions

While telehealth is a safe and convenient way to access semaglutide, there are important safety points to keep in mind.

Contraindications

Semaglutide should not be used by people with:

  • A personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma
  • Multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • A history of pancreatitis (use with caution)
  • Known hypersensitivity to semaglutide or any of its components

Drug Interactions

Semaglutide can interact with certain medications, particularly insulin and sulfonylureas, increasing the risk of hypoglycemia. It can also slow gastric emptying, which may affect the absorption of oral medications. Always provide a complete list of your medications during your consultation.

When to Seek In-Person Care

While most side effects can be managed through telehealth, seek immediate in-person medical attention if you experience:

  • Severe abdominal pain that does not go away
  • Signs of an allergic reaction (swelling, difficulty breathing, rash)
  • Vision changes
  • Signs of kidney problems (reduced urination, swelling in feet)
  • Symptoms of pancreatitis (severe upper abdominal pain radiating to the back)

Clinical Evidence Supporting Semaglutide for Weight Loss

The effectiveness of semaglutide is backed by one of the most robust clinical trial programs in weight management history.

STEP Trial Program

The STEP (Semaglutide Treatment Effect in People with Obesity) trials enrolled over 4,500 participants across multiple studies. Key findings include:

  • STEP 1: Participants lost an average of 14.9% of body weight over 68 weeks compared to 2.4% with placebo.
  • STEP 2: In patients with type 2 diabetes, average weight loss was 9.6% with the 2.4 mg dose.
  • STEP 3: Combined with intensive behavioral therapy, participants lost 16% of body weight.
  • STEP 5: Over two years, sustained weight loss of approximately 15.2% was maintained.

SELECT Trial

The SELECT trial demonstrated that semaglutide reduces the risk of major adverse cardiovascular events by 20% in adults with obesity and established cardiovascular disease, even without diabetes. This finding expanded the case for semaglutide beyond just weight loss to broader cardiovascular health.
