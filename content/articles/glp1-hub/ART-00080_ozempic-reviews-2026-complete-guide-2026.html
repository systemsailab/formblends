Common Complaints in 2026 Reviews

Gastrointestinal Side Effects

Nausea remains the most cited downside. While most reviewers note that it improves over time, the first few weeks (especially after dose increases) can be challenging. Typical comments include: "Weeks 5 and 6 were rough, but by week 8 the nausea was almost gone." Constipation is the second most common GI complaint and can persist longer than nausea for some patients.

Cost and Insurance Frustration

Even in 2026, cost is a major pain point. Patients without insurance coverage report paying $900 to $1,000+ monthly out of pocket {{VERIFY_PRICE:Ozempic}}. Weight Regain After Stopping

Patients who stopped Ozempic (whether by choice, insurance loss, or supply issues) frequently report rapid weight regain. This is consistent with clinical trial data showing two-thirds of lost weight returns within 12 months of discontinuation {{CITE:STEP 4 weight regain data}}. The reality that Ozempic is a long-term commitment, not a short course of treatment, is a recurring source of frustration in reviews.

"Ozempic Face" and Appearance Concerns

A subset of reviewers, particularly those over 40, mention facial volume loss. As body fat decreases, subcutaneous fat in the face can thin, creating a gaunt or aged appearance. This is not unique to Ozempic but occurs with significant weight loss from any cause. Some patients pursue dermal fillers or other cosmetic treatments to address it.

Social Stigma

Some reviewers describe feeling judged for using a medication for weight loss. Comments like "just eat less" or "that is the easy way out" from friends, family, or even healthcare providers are mentioned in numerous reviews. This stigma, rooted in outdated views of obesity as a willpower problem rather than a chronic disease, remains a real barrier for some patients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Ozempic reviews trustworthy?

Online reviews come with inherent bias. People with very positive or very negative experiences are more likely to post. Clinical trial data provides a more balanced picture. That said, pattern recognition across hundreds of reviews does reveal consistent themes that align with published data.

Why do some people say Ozempic did not work for them?

Approximately 10% to 15% of patients in clinical trials did not achieve clinically significant weight loss (defined as 5% or more). Reasons include genetic differences in GLP-1 receptor sensitivity, concurrent medications that promote weight gain, inadequate dose escalation, untreated hormonal conditions (hypothyroidism, Cushing's syndrome), and lifestyle factors that counteract the medication's effects {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:non-response to semaglutide possible causes}}.

Do doctors recommend Ozempic?

Physician attitudes toward Ozempic have shifted significantly since its launch. A 2025 survey by Medscape found that 82% of endocrinologists and 67% of primary care physicians view GLP-1 agonists as first-line options for patients with obesity and type 2 diabetes {{CITE:Medscape 2025 physician GLP-1 prescribing attitudes survey}}. The SELECT cardiovascular outcomes data has been particularly influential in changing prescribing patterns.

Is the weight loss from Ozempic "real" or will it all come back?

The weight loss is real in every physiological sense: reduced fat mass, improved metabolic markers, and better cardiovascular health. However, like all chronic disease treatments, the benefits require ongoing treatment. Stopping Ozempic is analogous to stopping blood pressure medication: the underlying condition returns. This does not make the treatment any less valid or the results any less "real" {{INTERNAL_LINK:Ozempic weight loss timeline}}.

What do reviews say about Ozempic for people without diabetes?

Non-diabetic weight loss users are generally positive, particularly about appetite control and steady weight loss. The most common frustration unique to this group is insurance coverage challenges, since Ozempic is not FDA-approved for weight loss. Many non-diabetic reviewers note that they wish they had started sooner.
