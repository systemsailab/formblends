Liraglutide 0.6mg Weight Loss: What the Starting Dose Actually Delivers

Average weight loss on liraglutide 0.6mg during the first week of treatment is 0 to 2 pounds. This is not clinically significant weight loss, and it is not expected to be. The 0.6mg dose is an introductory titration step, not a therapeutic dose for weight management. Any weight change at this level reflects reduced food volume and water fluctuations rather than meaningful fat loss. Why Weight Loss Is Minimal at 0.6mg

Liraglutide works by activating GLP-1 receptors in the brain and gut, reducing appetite and slowing digestion. At 0.6mg, the level of receptor activation is low enough that the weight loss effect is barely detectable:

  • The therapeutic dose for weight management (Saxenda) is 3.0mg daily, five times the starting dose
  • Even the diabetes dose (Victoza) uses 1.2mg to 1.8mg, which is two to three times the starting dose
  • At 0.6mg, the appetite-reducing signal is present but weak. Most patients still feel hungry at meal times.
  • The gastric-slowing effect is minimal, so food moves through your system at near-normal speed

Think of 0.6mg as the warm-up lap before the race. The effort is not wasted, but the results come later. {{CITE:liraglutide dose-response relationship for weight loss}}

Should You Track Weight During Week 1?

We recommend taking a baseline weight on the morning of your first injection and then not weighing yourself again until the end of week 1 or even week 2. Daily weighing during the 0.6mg phase can create unnecessary frustration:

  • Normal daily fluctuations of 1 to 3 pounds mask any medication effect
  • Seeing "no change" or slight gains can feel discouraging, even though they are completely normal
  • Early fixation on the scale can set an unhealthy pattern for the rest of treatment

Instead, focus on adapting to the injection routine, managing any side effects, and building the eating and hydration habits that will support your weight loss at higher doses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I be concerned if I do not lose any weight at 0.6mg?
Not at all. Zero weight loss at 0.6mg is the most common outcome and says nothing about how you will respond at therapeutic doses. The medication needs time and dose to reach its full effect.
I lost 4 pounds in my first week on 0.6mg. Is that normal?
Above-average first-week loss is possible, especially if you also made dietary changes or if you had significant water retention before starting. This early result does not necessarily predict faster-than-average long-term results, but it is a positive start.
How does liraglutide 0.6mg weight loss compare to tirzepatide 2.5mg?
Both are introductory doses, but tirzepatide 2.5mg (injected weekly) tends to produce slightly more noticeable appetite suppression than liraglutide 0.6mg (injected daily). At their respective therapeutic doses, tirzepatide produces significantly more weight loss than liraglutide. {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide vs liraglutide}}
Can I speed up the titration to get to a dose that actually works?
Some prescribers allow faster titration in patients who tolerate 0.6mg very well. However, the standard weekly increases exist for good reason. Rushing to 3.0mg increases the risk of severe nausea and vomiting that could cause you to stop the medication entirely. Patience during titration usually pays off with better long-term adherence. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:risks of accelerated liraglutide titration}}
