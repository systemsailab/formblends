Managing Side Effects

Most side effects are gastrointestinal and temporary:

  • Nausea: Common in the first 4 to 6 weeks. Eat smaller meals, avoid greasy food, and try ginger-based remedies.
  • Constipation: Increase water intake to at least 80 ounces daily. Add fiber gradually. A magnesium supplement can help.
  • Acid reflux: Avoid eating within 3 hours of bedtime. Keep meals moderate in size.
  • Fatigue: Usually limited to the first week at each new dose level.

{{CITE:Gastrointestinal adverse events are the most common side effects of semaglutide and are typically transient.}} Men over 50 should also be aware of gallstone risk with rapid weight loss. Report severe abdominal pain immediately.
