What Is Compounded Semaglutide?

Compounded semaglutide is the same active molecule as brand-name Ozempic and Wegovy, prepared by licensed 503A or 503B compounding pharmacies . These pharmacies create custom formulations, often at a lower cost than brand-name products. The FDA has allowed compounded versions of semaglutide during periods of drug shortage, making them an accessible option for patients who cannot afford or access brand-name medications.

Side Effects at the 2-Month Mark

By month two, most initial side effects from the starting dose have resolved. However, the dose increase to 0.5 mg may temporarily bring back mild symptoms :

  • Nausea: Brief return possible with dose increase, usually milder than the initial episode and resolving within 2 to 3 days.
  • Constipation: May persist or worsen at higher doses. Adequate water intake (64+ ounces daily) and fiber supplementation help.
  • Fatigue: Some patients feel temporarily tired as their body adjusts to lower calorie intake.
  • Hair thinning: A small percentage of patients notice increased hair shedding around month two, related to rapid weight loss rather than the medication itself .

Tips for Maximizing Results at Month Two

  • Increase your protein intake. As your appetite decreases further, make every bite count. Target 80 to 120 grams of protein daily.
  • Add resistance training. If you have not started yet, now is the time. Preserving muscle mass improves your metabolic rate and body composition.
  • Track measurements, not just weight. Waist, hip, and thigh measurements often show progress when the scale stalls.
  • Stay consistent with injections. Same day, same time each week provides the most stable medication levels.
  • Prepare for the dose increase. Have bland, easy-to-digest meals ready for the first day or two after moving to a higher dose.
