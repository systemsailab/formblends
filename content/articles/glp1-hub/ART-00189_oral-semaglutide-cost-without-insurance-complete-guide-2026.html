Ways to Save on Oral Semaglutide

Novo Nordisk Savings Programs

Novo Nordisk offers several programs that can reduce out-of-pocket costs:

  • Rybelsus Savings Card: Available to commercially insured patients. Can reduce copays to as low as $10 per month for eligible patients. Not available to uninsured, Medicare, or Medicaid patients. Contact provider for current pricing
  • Novo Nordisk Patient Assistance Program (PAP): For uninsured patients who meet income requirements (typically below 400% of the federal poverty level). Eligible patients may receive Rybelsus at no cost. Application requires income documentation and physician attestation.

Pharmacy Discount Programs

  • GoodRx and similar platforms: Can sometimes offer discounts of 10 to 20% on Rybelsus, bringing the monthly cost closer to $750 to $850. Discount amounts vary by pharmacy and location.
  • Costco pharmacy: Costco consistently offers among the lowest retail prices for brand medications. You do not need a Costco membership to use their pharmacy in most states.
  • Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drugs: As of early 2026, semaglutide tablets are not available through Cost Plus, but this may change. Worth checking periodically. Contact provider for current pricing

HSA and FSA Accounts

Oral semaglutide prescribed for a diagnosed medical condition (type 2 diabetes, obesity) is typically eligible for payment through HSA and FSA accounts. Using pre-tax dollars effectively reduces your cost by 20 to 35% depending on your marginal tax rate.

Tax Deductions

Medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of your adjusted gross income are deductible on your federal tax return. If you are paying $900 or more per month for oral semaglutide, this could provide meaningful tax relief. Keep all receipts and pharmacy statements.

Side Effects and Cost Relationship

One cost-related consideration with oral semaglutide: if you experience significant nausea or vomiting in the first 30 minutes after taking a dose, the medication may not be absorbed properly. In effect, you lose that day's dose and the money it represents. This is less of an issue with injectable semaglutide, where absorption is guaranteed once injected.

Common side effects include nausea (20 to 22%), diarrhea (15 to 17%), and decreased appetite (8 to 10%). These rates are based on trials of the 14 mg dose.

Timeline: When Costs May Change

  • Generic semaglutide tablets: Novo Nordisk's patents on semaglutide are expected to begin expiring in the late 2020s. Generic oral versions could reduce prices by 50 to 80% when they become available.
  • Higher-dose oral FDA approval: If 25 mg or 50 mg oral semaglutide receives FDA approval for weight loss, insurance coverage and pricing dynamics could shift. The approval timeline is uncertain.
  • Medicare coverage: If the Treat and Reduce Obesity Act passes, Medicare Part D coverage of anti-obesity medications would affect pricing across the market.
  • Market competition: As more oral GLP-1 medications enter development (oral tirzepatide trials are underway), competitive pressure could drive prices down.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a generic version of oral semaglutide?

Not yet. As of March 2026, Rybelsus remains patent-protected. Generic versions are expected in the late 2020s or early 2030s.

Can I get oral semaglutide from a Canadian pharmacy?

Some patients import medications from Canadian pharmacies, where prices may be lower. However, importing prescription medications is technically a legal gray area under FDA regulations. Compounded semaglutide from a domestic pharmacy is a legal and often cheaper alternative.

Is the 3 mg dose worth paying for?

The 3 mg dose is a tolerability step, not a therapeutic dose. It is medically necessary to start here and titrate up, but you will not see significant weight loss at this dose. Think of it as the on-ramp, not the highway.

Should I choose oral or injectable semaglutide if cost is my priority?

Injectable compounded semaglutide is the clear winner on cost. At $179 to $399 per month, it costs 60 to 80% less than brand oral semaglutide. Unless avoiding needles is your absolute top priority, the injectable route delivers the same results for far less money.

Does oral semaglutide have manufacturer coupons for uninsured patients?

The Rybelsus Savings Card is only for commercially insured patients. Uninsured patients may qualify for Novo Nordisk's Patient Assistance Program if they meet income requirements. Otherwise, retail or discount pricing applies.
