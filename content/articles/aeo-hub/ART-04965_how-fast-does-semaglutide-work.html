How Fast Does Semaglutide Work?

Semaglutide begins working within the first week of your initial injection. Most patients notice reduced appetite and smaller portion sizes within 3 to 7 days . Visible weight loss typically appears within 2 to 4 weeks, and significant results become apparent by 8 to 12 weeks as the dose increases through the standard escalation schedule. The fastest rate of weight loss generally occurs between months 3 and 12, with most patients reaching maximum benefit by 15 to 18 months .

Week-by-Week Timeline

Week 1 (0.25 mg dose)

The starting dose is low and primarily meant to help your body adjust to the medication. Even at this dose, many patients notice a decrease in appetite and fewer food cravings. Weight loss during the first week is typically modest, ranging from 1 to 3 pounds, and some of this may be water weight.

Weeks 2-4 (0.25 mg)

Appetite suppression becomes more noticeable. You may find it easier to eat smaller portions and feel satisfied sooner. Total weight loss by the end of week 4 is often 3 to 6 pounds .

Weeks 5-8 (0.5 mg dose)

The dose increases to 0.5 mg. The appetite-suppressing effect strengthens, and weight loss typically accelerates. Many patients lose an additional 3 to 5 pounds during this phase. Some GI side effects like nausea may temporarily increase with the dose change.

Weeks 9-16 (1.0 mg, then 1.7 mg)

Progressive dose escalation continues. By this point, most patients are seeing meaningful changes on the scale and in how their clothes fit. Cumulative weight loss of 5 to 10% of starting body weight is common by week 16 .

Week 17 and Beyond (2.4 mg maintenance dose)

At the full maintenance dose of 2.4 mg, weight loss continues at its fastest pace. The STEP 1 trial showed that most weight loss occurred between months 4 and 12, with the rate gradually slowing as patients approached their plateau around months 15 to 18 .

Why the Dose Escalation Takes Time

Semaglutide uses a gradual dose escalation over 16 to 20 weeks for good reason. Jumping straight to the full dose would cause severe nausea and GI distress for most patients. The slow increase allows your body to adjust, minimizing side effects while progressively increasing the medication's effectiveness .

The standard escalation schedule for Wegovy is:

  • Weeks 1-4: 0.25 mg
  • Weeks 5-8: 0.5 mg
  • Weeks 9-12: 1.0 mg
  • Weeks 13-16: 1.7 mg
  • Week 17+: 2.4 mg (maintenance)

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I notice anything after my first semaglutide injection?

Many patients notice reduced appetite and a feeling of fullness within 2 to 5 days after the first injection. Some patients also experience mild nausea, which is a common early side effect. Weight loss in the first week is usually modest, around 1 to 3 pounds.

How much weight should I expect to lose in the first month?

In the first month on the starting dose of 0.25 mg, most patients lose 3 to 6 pounds. This is normal and expected since the initial dose is designed for adjustment. Weight loss accelerates as the dose increases over the following months .

Can I increase my dose faster to speed up results?

It is not recommended to escalate doses faster than the standard schedule. Rapid dose increases significantly raise the risk of severe nausea, vomiting, and other GI side effects. The standard 16-20 week escalation is designed to balance effectiveness with tolerability . Always follow your provider's guidance.

When does semaglutide stop working?

Semaglutide does not stop working, but weight loss naturally plateaus after 12 to 18 months as your body reaches a new energy balance.
