How Long Can You Stay On Semaglutide?

You can stay on semaglutide for as long as your healthcare provider determines it is medically appropriate. Semaglutide (Wegovy) is FDA-approved for chronic weight management, meaning it is designed for long-term, ongoing use rather than a short course of treatment . Clinical trials have followed patients on semaglutide for up to 2 years with continued benefit and acceptable safety profiles, and real-world use now extends well beyond that timeframe .

Why Semaglutide Is Intended for Long-Term Use

Obesity is classified as a chronic disease by the American Medical Association, the World Health Organization, and other major medical bodies . Just as chronic conditions like hypertension or diabetes require ongoing treatment, obesity often requires sustained medical intervention.

Semaglutide works by mimicking the GLP-1 hormone, which suppresses appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves metabolic function. When you stop taking the medication, these effects diminish and weight regain is common. What the Research Shows About Duration

The STEP clinical trial program provides the most comprehensive data on semaglutide duration:

  • STEP 1: 68 weeks of treatment showed 14.9% average weight loss that was sustained throughout the treatment period .
  • STEP 5: Extended the treatment period to 104 weeks (2 years) and showed that weight loss was maintained and even slightly improved compared to the 68-week mark .
  • STEP 1 extension: Participants who stopped semaglutide after 68 weeks regained approximately two-thirds of lost weight within one year of discontinuation .

These findings strongly support the case for continued, long-term use .

When Your Provider Might Adjust or Stop Treatment

While semaglutide can be used indefinitely, there are situations where your provider might modify your treatment:

  • Persistent side effects: If nausea, vomiting, or other GI symptoms do not improve with dose adjustments, your provider may lower the dose or consider an alternative medication.
  • Pregnancy planning: Semaglutide should be stopped at least 2 months before a planned pregnancy .
  • Surgical procedures: Some providers recommend temporarily pausing semaglutide before surgery due to its effects on gastric emptying .
  • Achieving and maintaining goal weight: Some patients and providers choose to taper to a lower maintenance dose rather than stopping entirely.

Maintenance Dosing

Some patients who reach their weight loss goals may work with their provider to reduce to a lower maintenance dose. While there is no officially defined "maintenance dose" in the FDA labeling, clinical experience suggests that some patients can sustain results on lower doses . Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to take semaglutide for years?

Current evidence supports multi-year use. The STEP 5 trial followed patients for 2 years, and the SELECT cardiovascular trial followed patients for over 3 years, both showing acceptable safety profiles . Your provider should monitor you regularly with follow-up visits and periodic lab work.

Will semaglutide stop working over time?

Weight loss typically plateaus after 12 to 18 months on semaglutide, which is normal and expected. The medication continues to help maintain the weight you have lost rather than causing indefinite weight loss. This plateau does not mean the medication has stopped working .

Does long-term semaglutide use affect the thyroid?

Semaglutide carries a boxed warning about thyroid C-cell tumors based on animal studies. However, this effect has not been observed in human studies to date . Patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma should not use semaglutide. Regular thyroid monitoring is not routinely required for most patients.
