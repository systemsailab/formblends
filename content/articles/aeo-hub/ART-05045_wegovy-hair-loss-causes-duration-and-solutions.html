Wegovy Hair Loss: Causes, Duration, and Solutions

If you need to know what to do about Wegovy hair loss, here is the bottom line: this shedding is driven by your weight loss, not by Wegovy attacking your hair. At the 2.4 mg maintenance dose, Wegovy produces some of the most significant weight loss results of any medication, and that scale of physical change can temporarily affect hair growth cycles.

Why Hair Loss Happens on Wegovy

Wegovy is semaglutide at its highest approved dose for weight management (2.4 mg weekly). In the landmark STEP trials, patients lost an average of 15 percent of their body weight. That magnitude of weight loss is clinically impressive but physiologically stressful, and hair is one of the body's most expendable functions when resources are being conserved.

The weight loss connection. Telogen effluvium, the medical term for stress-related hair shedding, occurs whenever the body experiences a significant physical disruption. Rapid weight loss ranks among the most common triggers, alongside major surgery, childbirth, and severe illness. The STEP trials reported alopecia in approximately 3 percent of Wegovy-treated patients versus about 1 percent on placebo.

Nutritional depletion at higher doses. At 2.4 mg, appetite suppression is profound. Many patients struggle to eat more than 1,000 to 1,200 calories per day. This level of caloric restriction makes it challenging to consume adequate protein, iron, and other nutrients that hair follicles depend on.

The speed factor. The faster the weight loss, the more severe the hair shedding tends to be. Patients who lose weight very rapidly during the first few months of Wegovy treatment may notice more dramatic shedding than those whose weight loss is more gradual.

Individual variability. Genetics, pre-existing nutritional status, age, and hormonal factors all influence whether you will experience hair shedding and how severe it will be. Some patients lose significant weight on Wegovy with no noticeable hair changes.

How Long Wegovy Hair Loss Typically Lasts

Wegovy-related hair shedding follows the same timeline as telogen effluvium from any cause.

Delayed onset. You will not notice hair loss right away. It typically begins 2 to 4 months after the start of rapid weight loss, which for many Wegovy patients corresponds to roughly months 3 to 5 of treatment.

Peak shedding window. The heaviest shedding usually occurs over a period of 6 to 12 weeks. You may see clumps of hair in your brush or shower that feel alarming. During this time, you might shed 200 to 300 hairs per day instead of the normal 50 to 100.

Natural resolution. As your weight loss decelerates and your body reaches a new metabolic steady state, hair follicles re-enter the growth phase. Shedding rates typically normalize within 6 to 9 months from onset, and visible regrowth follows over the subsequent 6 to 12 months.

What You Can Do About Wegovy Hair Loss

You have real options for minimizing hair loss and supporting faster recovery.

  • Fight for your protein. Even when your appetite is almost nonexistent, aim for at least 70 to 90 grams of protein per day. Protein shakes, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, and collagen powder make it easier to hit this target without eating large meals. protein intake on GLP-1 medications
  • Get your ferritin checked. Ferritin (stored iron) is the single most important lab value for hair health. Optimal levels for hair growth are generally above 40 to 70 ng/mL, which is higher than the lower limit of the standard reference range. Many patients benefit from iron supplementation.
  • Supplement strategically. A comprehensive hair support plan might include vitamin D (2,000 to 5,000 IU daily), zinc (15 to 30 mg), biotin (2,500 to 5,000 mcg), and a B-complex vitamin. Always discuss doses with your provider.
  • Do not eliminate food groups. Avoid combining Wegovy with restrictive diets like keto or very-low-carb plans unless specifically directed by your provider. Diverse food intake supports a broader nutrient profile.
  • Be kind to your hair. Switch to a volumizing, sulfate-free shampoo. Avoid tight buns, ponytails, and braids. Let hair air-dry when possible and minimize coloring or chemical processing.
  • Discuss pacing with your provider. If hair loss is severe, your doctor might consider holding your dose at a lower level for a longer period before escalating, giving your body more time to adapt at each step.

When to See a Doctor

See your healthcare provider or a dermatologist if you notice any of the following.

  • Distinct bald patches rather than overall thinning
  • Hair shedding that persists or increases beyond 6 months
  • Scalp tenderness, redness, or flaking
  • Other unexplained symptoms like fatigue, joint pain, or weight gain despite treatment
  • A family history of pattern baldness with concern about accelerated thinning

Related Questions

Is hair loss a side effect of Wegovy?

Hair loss is listed as a reported side effect in Wegovy clinical trials, occurring in approximately 3 percent of patients versus 1 percent on placebo. However, it is attributed to the rapid weight loss rather than a direct effect of semaglutide on hair follicles.

How much hair loss is normal on Wegovy?

It is normal to lose 50 to 100 hairs per day. During telogen effluvium from Wegovy-related weight loss, you may lose 200 to 300 hairs per day during peak shedding. While this feels like a lot, it represents a temporary shift in the hair cycle and typically does not result in visible bald spots.

Will my hair grow back after stopping Wegovy?

Yes, hair regrowth occurs whether you continue or stop Wegovy. The key factor is that the rate of weight loss slows or stops, allowing the body to redirect resources back to hair production. Most patients see regrowth within 6 to 12 months.
