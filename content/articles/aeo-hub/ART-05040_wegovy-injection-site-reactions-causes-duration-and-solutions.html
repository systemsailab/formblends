Wegovy Injection Site Reactions: Causes, Duration, and Solutions

Why Wegovy Causes Injection Site Reactions

Wegovy uses a single-dose, pre-filled pen to deliver semaglutide subcutaneously once per week. Even though the pen system is designed for ease and comfort, local reactions can occur for several reasons.

Immune surveillance. Your subcutaneous tissue contains immune cells that monitor for foreign substances. When semaglutide solution enters the tissue, these cells respond with controlled inflammation. This is a normal protective response, not a sign that something is wrong. {{CITE:STEP clinical trial injection site reaction incidence}}

Pen design differences. Wegovy uses a disposable, single-use pen that differs slightly from the multi-dose Ozempic pen. The needle gauge and injection mechanism may feel different, and patients switching between products may notice a difference in comfort.

Dose-related volume. At higher doses (1.7 mg and 2.4 mg), the volume of solution injected increases. More fluid in the tissue can create a temporary bump and greater local pressure, leading to increased tenderness. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:injection volume and local reaction correlation}}

Injection depth. If the needle enters too shallowly (intradermal) or too deeply (intramuscular), the medication does not distribute properly through subcutaneous fat. Both scenarios increase the risk of pain, bruising, and localized swelling.

How Long Wegovy Injection Site Reactions Typically Last

Wegovy injection site reactions have a predictable and generally reassuring course.

  • Itching and redness: Onset is usually within minutes, with resolution in 1 to 6 hours.
  • Tenderness: Usually noticeable for 1 to 2 days, peaking in the first 12 hours.
  • Localized swelling: A small, firm bump may appear and resolve within 24 to 48 hours.
  • Bruising: Typically clears within 5 to 7 days.

Patients on Wegovy for 3 months or longer frequently report that injection site reactions have become minimal or have stopped altogether. {{CITE:long-term tolerability of weekly semaglutide injections}}

Our team has found that these strategies make the biggest difference for Wegovy patients.

  • Choose fatty tissue. Injection site reactions are less common in areas with more subcutaneous fat. The abdomen (away from the navel) and outer thighs are often the most comfortable sites.
  • Do not rush the injection. After pressing the pen button, wait until the window shows completion. Hold the pen in place for an additional 5 to 10 seconds to prevent medication from leaking back out.
  • Use the pen at room temperature. Wegovy pens can be stored at room temperature for up to 28 days. If yours is refrigerated, take it out 30 minutes before use.

  • Wear loose clothing. After injecting in the abdomen or thigh, avoid tight waistbands or form-fitting pants that could press on and irritate the site.

When to See a Doctor

Most injection site reactions do not need medical attention, but contact your provider if you experience any of the following.

  • Redness that grows larger over 24 to 48 hours rather than fading
  • Heat and pain that increase after the first day
  • Any sign of infection (pus, streaking redness, fever)
  • Nodules or hard lumps that persist beyond 10 days
  • Symptoms beyond the injection site (widespread rash, facial swelling, breathing difficulty)

Related Questions

Are injection site reactions normal with Wegovy?

Yes, injection site reactions are a normal and commonly reported side effect of Wegovy. In the STEP clinical trials, localized reactions including redness, pain, and itching were experienced by a percentage of participants. These reactions were predominantly mild and did not lead to treatment discontinuation.

How long do Wegovy injection site reactions last?

Wegovy injection site reactions usually resolve within 1 to 3 days. Minor redness and itching often clear within hours, while bruising can take up to a week. Most patients experience fewer reactions as they continue treatment.

Can I switch injection sites to reduce Wegovy reactions?

Absolutely. Rotating injection sites is one of the best ways to reduce reactions. Alternate between the abdomen, front of the thighs, and upper arms, and shift your exact injection point within each area to give tissue time to recover.
