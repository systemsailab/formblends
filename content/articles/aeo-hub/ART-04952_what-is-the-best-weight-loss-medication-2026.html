Top Weight Loss Medications Available in 2026

The prescription weight loss landscape has changed significantly over the past few years. Older medications like phentermine and orlistat are still available, but GLP-1 based therapies now dominate due to superior outcomes and growing long-term safety data {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 therapies superior to older weight loss drugs}}.

Semaglutide (Wegovy / Ozempic)

Semaglutide works by mimicking the GLP-1 hormone, which regulates appetite and blood sugar. In the STEP clinical trials, participants on semaglutide lost an average of 15% of their body weight over 68 weeks {{CITE:STEP trial semaglutide 15% weight loss}}. It is administered as a once-weekly injection, and an oral form is also available. Brand-name Wegovy is FDA-approved specifically for chronic weight management, while Ozempic carries a type 2 diabetes indication {{VERIFY_PRICE:Wegovy}}.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound)

Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, meaning it targets two incretin hormones instead of one. The SURMOUNT trials showed average weight loss of up to 22.5% of body weight {{CITE:SURMOUNT trial tirzepatide 22.5% weight loss}}. Zepbound is the weight management brand, and Mounjaro is approved for type 2 diabetes {{VERIFY_PRICE:Zepbound}}.

How to Choose the Right Medication

Selecting the best weight loss medication depends on several factors:

  • Your BMI and health history: Most GLP-1 medications require a BMI of 30 or higher, or 27 with a weight-related condition {{INTERNAL_LINK:BMI requirements for weight loss medication}}.
  • Budget and insurance: Brand-name GLP-1s can cost over $1,000 per month without insurance. Compounded options may cost significantly less {{VERIFY_PRICE:compounded semaglutide}}.
  • Side effect tolerance: Common side effects include nausea, constipation, and diarrhea. Tirzepatide may cause slightly more GI symptoms at higher doses {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:tirzepatide GI side effects at higher doses}}.
  • Convenience: Both semaglutide and tirzepatide are once-weekly injections. Oral semaglutide is available for those who prefer pills.

What About Newer Medications on the Horizon?

Several next-generation weight loss drugs are in late-stage clinical trials as of 2026. These include triple-agonist compounds targeting GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors simultaneously. Early data suggests potential weight loss exceeding 25% {{CITE:triple agonist weight loss trial data}}. However, these are not yet FDA-approved and remain unavailable for general prescribing.

For now, semaglutide and tirzepatide remain the gold standard, backed by years of real-world use and robust clinical evidence.
