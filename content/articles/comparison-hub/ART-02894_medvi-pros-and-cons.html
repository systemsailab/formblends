The Pros, Explained

1. Physician-Led Consultations

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians for patient consultations, not just nurse practitioners or physician assistants. For a standard GLP-1 prescription, the clinical difference between an MD and an NP is minimal. But for patients with complex medical histories, multiple medications, or conditions like thyroid disease, kidney issues, or a history of pancreatitis, having a physician review your case from the start adds a layer of clinical safety.

Patient reviews consistently note that MEDVi's initial consultations feel thorough. Physicians ask detailed questions and spend time reviewing medical history rather than rushing to prescribe.

2. Metabolic Lab Panels Included

MEDVi includes comprehensive metabolic labs as part of its program. This sets it apart from competitors that either skip lab work entirely or charge for it separately. The labs cover markers like fasting glucose, HbA1c, thyroid function, kidney function, and lipid panels. This data helps physicians make more informed prescribing decisions and can catch potential contraindications early.

For patients who already have a primary care doctor running regular labs, this may feel redundant. But for patients without easy access to lab work, it is a meaningful value-add that partially justifies the higher monthly cost.

3. No Contract Required

MEDVi operates on a month-to-month basis. You are not locked into a 3-month, 6-month, or annual commitment. This is a genuine advantage in a market where several major competitors (including Hims for some plan tiers) offer their lowest prices only with multi-month prepayment.

You can cancel at any time through your portal or by contacting customer support. The only catch is timing: cancel before your renewal date to avoid being charged for the next month.

4. Semaglutide and Tirzepatide Both Available

Having access to both compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide gives your physician more flexibility in building your treatment plan. Some patients respond better to one than the other, and having the option to switch without changing platforms is convenient.

5. Competitive First-Month Pricing

At $179 for the first month of compounded semaglutide, MEDVi's entry price is competitive. This lower barrier makes it easier to try the service before committing to the ongoing $299/month rate.

6. Free Shipping

All orders ship free with standard delivery. Medications typically arrive within a few business days, though delays can occur during periods of high demand.

7. Strong Trustpilot Ratings

A 4.5 out of 5 rating on Trustpilot from thousands of reviews is a meaningful signal. While no review platform is immune to manipulation, the volume and detail of MEDVi's positive reviews suggest genuine patient satisfaction with the clinical experience.

The Cons, Explained

1. Higher Ongoing Monthly Cost

At $299/month for ongoing semaglutide refills, MEDVi is more expensive than several competitors. Hims starts at $199/month, and some smaller platforms offer compounded semaglutide for under $250. Over a year, the difference between $199 and $299 per month adds up to $1,200. MEDVi partially offsets this with included lab work, but patients who already have labs done through their primary care doctor are paying a premium for services they may not need.

2. Documented Billing Issues

BBB complaints and negative reviews consistently cite billing problems. Reported issues include:

  • Being charged for refills that were not authorized
  • Double billing for the same monthly period
  • Being charged for a multi-month bundle without consent
  • Receiving incomplete medication vials while being charged full price

These are not one-off incidents. The pattern of billing complaints suggests a systemic issue with MEDVi's billing processes or subscription management system. If you enroll, monitor your credit card or bank statements closely.

3. Restrictive Refund Policy

MEDVi markets a "results-based guarantee," which sounds reassuring until you read the details. You must follow the program for at least five months before you can claim the guarantee. If approved, the refund is reduced by a 25% consultation fee. Once a prescription has been issued, standard refunds are not available.

4. Compounded Medications Only

MEDVi does not offer brand-name Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound. If your physician or insurer requires brand-name drugs, or if you have concerns about compounded formulations, MEDVi is not the right platform for you.

5. No Insurance Accepted

Everything is cash-pay. This is standard for compounded GLP-1 services, but it means you are paying 100% out of pocket with no reimbursement from your health plan.

6. Patient Portal Needs Work

MEDVi's digital platform handles the basics (messaging, subscription management, shipment tracking) but lacks the polish and feature depth of competitors like Hims. There is no dedicated mobile app with progress tracking, community features, or integrated health metrics.

7. Business-Hours Provider Communication

Provider messaging is available during business hours only. If you experience side effects or have urgent questions outside of those hours, you will need to wait or seek care elsewhere. Larger competitors offer 24/7 messaging, even if response times vary.
