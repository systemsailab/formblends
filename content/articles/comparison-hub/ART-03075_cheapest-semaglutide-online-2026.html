How We Evaluated

Price comparisons in this space are full of misleading claims. A provider might advertise "$99/month" but charge a separate consult fee, shipping fee, and dose-adjustment fee that brings the real cost well above $200.

We calculated the true all-in monthly cost for each provider at a standard maintenance dose of 1.0 mg to 1.7 mg compounded semaglutide (or equivalent brand-name dose). Our methodology:

  • All-in cost: Medication + consult fees + shipping + platform fees, calculated monthly
  • Price consistency: Whether the advertised price holds as you titrate to higher doses
  • Pharmacy sourcing: 503B compliance and transparency about where medication comes from
  • Medical oversight per dollar: How much clinical care you actually get for your money
  • Hidden fees: We penalized any provider that tacked on undisclosed charges during enrollment

FAQ

Why is compounded semaglutide so much cheaper than brand-name?

Compounded semaglutide is produced by specialized pharmacies rather than the original manufacturer (Novo Nordisk). These pharmacies can produce the active ingredient at a fraction of the brand-name cost. Compounded medications are legal when produced by FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities under current regulatory frameworks.

Is cheaper semaglutide less effective?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient as Ozempic and Wegovy. When sourced from reputable 503B pharmacies, it should perform identically. The risk comes from unregulated sources, which is why pharmacy sourcing matters more than price alone.

What is the cheapest way to get semaglutide if I have insurance?

Book a low-cost telehealth consult through Sesame or a similar marketplace, get a prescription for Wegovy, and fill it through your insurance. If your plan covers it, your out-of-pocket cost could be as low as $25 to $50/month.

Do any of these programs offer price-lock guarantees?

Henry Meds and Hims/Hers have historically maintained stable pricing. Form Blends locks your rate at enrollment. Ro's pricing varies by dose, so your cost will increase as you titrate up. Always confirm current pricing before enrolling, as rates in this space change frequently.
