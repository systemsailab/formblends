How Hims Keeps Costs Down

Hims operates at high volume. The company processes hundreds of thousands of prescriptions across its various product lines (hair loss, ED, mental health, weight loss), which gives it leverage with compounding pharmacies and keeps per-unit medication costs low. Hims also owns parts of its pharmacy and fulfillment infrastructure, cutting out intermediary markups.

The tradeoff for this efficiency is less personalized care. Hims providers manage large patient panels, and the care model is designed for standardized protocols rather than deep physician-patient relationships. For patients who are medically straightforward and respond well to standard titration, this works fine. For patients with complex medical histories or who need more hands-on management, a more physician-intensive program may be worth the extra cost.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

Hims does not accept health insurance for its GLP-1 weight loss program. This is a cash-pay service. Insurance plans do not cover compounded medications, so this limitation is industry-wide, not specific to Hims.

Hims payments are generally eligible for HSA and FSA reimbursement. The program involves a physician-prescribed medication for a medical condition, which meets IRS eligibility criteria for qualified medical expenses. To use your HSA or FSA:

  1. Pay your Hims subscription with your regular payment method or HSA/FSA debit card
  2. If needed, download your receipt from the Hims platform
  3. Submit the receipt to your HSA/FSA administrator for reimbursement

At a 30% combined tax rate, using HSA/FSA reduces your effective cost from $175/month to about $123/month.
