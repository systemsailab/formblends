Hims Customer Reviews: What Real Users Are Saying in 2026

Quick Verdict

Hims customer reviews are genuinely mixed, and that is not unusual for a company operating at this scale. The platform has millions of users, and experiences vary widely depending on the product category, the individual provider, and the patient's expectations. Hair-loss and ED customers tend to be the most satisfied. Weight-loss and GLP-1 users are more divided, with praise for affordability but criticism for limited provider access and clinical support. The pattern that emerges from thousands of reviews is clear: Hims delivers well on convenience and price but struggles with the human side of care.

What Customers Are Actually Saying

The Positive Reviews

Satisfied Hims customers tend to share a few common traits: they knew what they wanted, they had straightforward conditions, and they valued speed over hand-holding. The most consistently positive feedback clusters around these themes:

Convenience and speed. Hundreds of reviews praise how fast the process is. Users describe going from sign-up to prescription in under a day, sometimes within hours. For people who have put off seeing a doctor about hair loss or ED, the low barrier to entry is genuinely life-changing in a practical sense.

Discreet packaging. This comes up constantly, especially from ED and hair-loss customers. The unmarked boxes and the ability to avoid pharmacy interactions matter to people. Several reviewers specifically mention that Hims made them comfortable seeking treatment they had been avoiding.

Product effectiveness. For finasteride, minoxidil, and sildenafil, effectiveness reviews are generally positive. These are well-established medications with decades of clinical data, and they work for most people. Customers report results consistent with what you would expect from these drugs regardless of the source.

Pricing. Many reviewers compare Hims favorably to retail pharmacy prices, especially for uninsured patients. The ability to get a month of sildenafil for under $30 without insurance is a genuine value proposition that users appreciate.

The Negative Reviews

The critical reviews reveal patterns that potential customers should take seriously:

Billing and cancellation. This is the single most common complaint across every review platform. Users describe being charged after they thought they had canceled, difficulty finding the cancellation option in the app, and unclear communication about auto-renewal. Some report needing to contact their bank to stop charges. While Hims has improved its cancellation flow, the volume of these complaints suggests it remains a problem.

Impersonal provider interactions. Customers who expected a real doctor-patient relationship are often disappointed. The async model means most users never speak to their provider. When they message with questions or concerns, responses can be slow or generic. Several reviews describe feeling like they were "just getting a rubber stamp" rather than a genuine medical evaluation.

GLP-1 program concerns. The weight-loss program has attracted a newer cohort of customers, and their reviews are noticeably more mixed than those from hair-loss or ED users. Common complaints include: limited guidance on dose titration, difficulty reaching a provider when experiencing side effects (nausea, injection-site reactions), and a general sense that the program feels more like a medication delivery service than a treatment plan.

Customer support responsiveness. Users who need help with something beyond a simple refill often find customer support slow and unhelpful. The lack of a phone support option is a frequent point of frustration. Chat and email support can involve long wait times, and multiple users report needing to send the same message several times before getting a resolution.

Quality concerns with compounded products. A smaller but notable subset of reviews raises questions about the compounded medications. Some users report inconsistencies between batches, different packaging than expected, or products that seemed less effective than anticipated. It is worth noting that these are subjective reports and difficult to verify, but the pattern exists.

The Weight-Loss-Specific Reviews

Because GLP-1 therapy is a newer offering for Hims, the review landscape is still developing. What stands out so far:

  • Customers who primarily wanted affordable access to semaglutide and did not need much guidance tend to be satisfied.
  • Customers who expected a structured weight-loss program with regular check-ins and ongoing support tend to be disappointed.
  • Several reviewers mention switching to more specialized platforms after starting with Hims, citing the need for better clinical oversight.
  • Positive reviews often mention the price savings compared to brand-name options.
  • Negative reviews often mention the lack of lab monitoring and the difficulty of getting dose adjustments.

It is important to contextualize Hims' reviews against the telehealth industry as a whole. Billing complaints, cancellation issues, and impersonal care are common across many large-scale telehealth platforms. Hims is not uniquely bad in these areas; it is operating a high-volume model that inherently trades personalization for scale.

Where the gap becomes most apparent is in specialized care areas like GLP-1 therapy. Platforms built specifically for weight management and peptide therapy, such as Form Blends, consistently receive better feedback on provider engagement, clinical oversight, and treatment personalization. Frequently Asked Questions

Are Hims reviews trustworthy?

Reviews on independent platforms like Trustpilot, BBB, and Reddit are generally authentic. Reviews on the Hims website itself may be curated. As with any company, it is best to read across multiple platforms and look for recurring themes rather than focusing on individual reviews.

What do most people complain about with Hims?

Billing issues, subscription cancellation difficulty, and impersonal provider interactions are the three most common complaints across all review platforms.

Do people like Hims for weight loss?

Opinions are divided. Budget-conscious users who mainly want medication access tend to be satisfied. Users who expected comprehensive clinical support tend to be disappointed.

Has Hims improved based on customer feedback?

Yes. The company has made changes to its cancellation process and app interface based on customer complaints. However, many of the underlying issues (async-only care, limited provider continuity) are structural to the business model and have not fundamentally changed.
