Pricing Breakdown

BreezeMeds is one of the more affordable compounded GLP-1 options on the market. Plans start around $149/month for lower-dose compounded semaglutide, with tirzepatide options topping out around $299. MEDVi's $249-$399 range reflects its more comprehensive clinical offering.

The $100/month gap adds up. Over six months, choosing BreezeMeds over MEDVi could save $600 or more. Whether that savings matters depends on what you give up. With BreezeMeds, you forgo included lab work, proactive physician follow-ups, and the deeper clinical assessments that MEDVi builds into its program.

For patients who have a primary care doctor running their metabolic panels and monitoring their health independently, that trade-off makes perfect sense. For patients who want everything managed under one roof, MEDVi's higher price includes services that would cost hundreds of dollars to arrange separately.

Medications Available

The medication options are essentially the same. Both platforms offer compounded semaglutide and compounded tirzepatide from licensed compounding pharmacies. Neither offers brand-name GLP-1 products.

BreezeMeds has optimized its pharmacy relationships for speed. Most patients receive their first shipment within 3-5 business days of completing their consultation. MEDVi's process takes longer (7-10 days) because the lab work component introduces additional turnaround time before prescriptions are finalized.

For dose titration, MEDVi's physicians actively manage escalation based on lab results, side effect reports, and progress tracking. BreezeMeds provides standard titration schedules, with patients expected to reach out if adjustments are needed.

Patient Experience

BreezeMeds has built its platform around speed and minimal friction. The onboarding questionnaire is concise, consultations are focused, and the path from intake to medication is as short as possible. For patients who value efficiency, this is a genuine strength.

MEDVi takes the opposite approach, prioritizing thoroughness over speed. Longer intake questionnaires, detailed medical history reviews, lab orders before prescribing, and scheduled follow-ups create a more traditional medical experience. It takes longer to get started but creates a more complete clinical picture.

Both platforms use basic patient portals. Neither has invested in a standout app experience. The portals handle messaging, prescription management, and appointment scheduling without much polish.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians for all consultations. BreezeMeds uses a mix of licensed providers, including nurse practitioners and physician assistants. Both are qualified to prescribe GLP-1 medications.

The depth of engagement differs. MEDVi providers spend more time per interaction and proactively schedule follow-ups. BreezeMeds providers are responsive when contacted but do not typically initiate follow-up conversations. This works well for self-directed patients but can leave less engaged patients without adequate guidance.

Customer support at both platforms is business-hours messaging with generally responsive turnaround times.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I get medication from BreezeMeds?

BreezeMeds typically ships within 1-2 business days of an approved consultation, with most patients receiving medication within 3-5 business days total. This is faster than most competitors, including MEDVi.

Is MEDVi's lab work worth the extra cost?

It depends on your situation. If you do not have a doctor ordering metabolic panels for you, MEDVi's included labs provide valuable health data that can inform treatment decisions and catch potential issues. If you already get regular bloodwork, you are paying for redundancy.

Do either platform offer oral GLP-1 medications?

Neither platform currently offers oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) or other oral GLP-1 formulations as part of their standard programs. Both focus on injectable compounded formulations.

Can I cancel BreezeMeds at any time?

Yes. BreezeMeds operates on a month-to-month basis with no long-term contracts or cancellation fees. You can pause or cancel before your next billing cycle.

Which platform is better for patients over 50?

MEDVi's included lab work and more thorough medical assessments make it the safer choice for older patients who may have undiagnosed metabolic issues. The physician-led model also provides more clinical oversight for managing potential medication interactions.
