How We Evaluated

We calculated affordability as a combination of four factors:

  • 12-month total cost (35%): The real cost of a full treatment course, not just the first month. Programs with dose-based pricing increases were penalized for unpredictability.
  • Care quality per dollar (25%): How much medical oversight, personalization, and support you get relative to what you pay. The cheapest program is not the best value if the care is negligent.
  • Pharmacy sourcing (20%): 503B compliance and medication quality. Saving money on poorly compounded medication is not saving money.
  • Pricing transparency (10%): Clear, predictable pricing with no hidden fees or surprise charges
  • Financial flexibility (10%): Easy cancellation, no long-term contracts, and the ability to pause if needed

FAQ

What is the absolute cheapest way to get GLP-1 medication?

If you have insurance coverage for Wegovy or Zepbound, using a low-cost telehealth consult ($30 to $75) and filling through your plan is the cheapest. Without insurance, Henry Meds at $149/month for compounded semaglutide is the most affordable reliable option we found.

Are cheaper GLP-1 programs less effective?

The medication itself should work the same regardless of price, assuming the pharmacy sourcing is legitimate. What cheaper programs often lack is personalized dosing, lab monitoring, and responsive clinical support. These factors can affect how well your titration is managed and how effectively side effects are addressed.

How long do I need to budget for GLP-1 therapy?

Most patients see significant weight loss within 6 to 12 months. However, research shows that stopping GLP-1 therapy leads to weight regain for most people. Budget for at least 12 months, and plan for the possibility that you may want to continue at a maintenance dose long-term.

Can I switch between providers to save money?

Yes. There are no medical reasons you cannot switch from one compounded semaglutide provider to another. Make sure your new provider has your current dosing information so they can continue your protocol without disruption. Some patients start with a pricier, more supportive program and then move to a cheaper maintenance option once they are stable.
