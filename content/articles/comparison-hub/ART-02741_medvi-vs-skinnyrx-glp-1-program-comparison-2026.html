Pricing Breakdown

SkinnyRx positions itself as a value option in the compounded GLP-1 market. Monthly plans start around $149 for lower-dose compounded semaglutide, with tirzepatide options running up to about $299. MEDVi starts at $249 and can reach $399 for higher-tier plans.

The price gap is real, but it reflects different service levels. MEDVi's pricing includes comprehensive metabolic bloodwork that would cost $200-$400 if ordered independently through a lab. SkinnyRx typically does not include lab work, which means you either skip it (not ideal for health monitoring) or pay for it separately through your own doctor.

For a healthy patient who has a primary care doctor already managing routine labs, SkinnyRx's lower price makes sense. For a patient who needs or wants metabolic panels as part of their weight loss program, MEDVi's bundled approach may actually be the better value despite the higher sticker price.

Medications Available

Both platforms offer compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide. Neither provides brand-name GLP-1 medications. The medications themselves are functionally similar, sourced from licensed compounding pharmacies.

The difference is in how dosing and titration are managed. MEDVi follows a more structured titration protocol, with physician-guided dose escalation based on tolerance, side effects, and weight loss progress. SkinnyRx offers standard titration guidance, but the level of hands-on management tends to be lighter.

For patients new to GLP-1 therapy who want more guidance on navigating side effects (nausea, appetite changes, injection technique), MEDVi's more involved approach has value. For patients who have taken GLP-1 medications before and know what to expect, SkinnyRx's streamlined process is efficient and sufficient.

Patient Experience

Neither platform offers a standout digital experience. Both use functional patient portals that handle the essentials: messaging, prescription management, and appointment scheduling. Neither app is on par with larger platforms like Hims or Ro.

MEDVi's onboarding process is more thorough. Expect longer intake questionnaires, more detailed medical history review, and a more in-depth initial consultation. SkinnyRx's onboarding is faster and more streamlined, which some patients find refreshing and others find insufficient.

For ongoing check-ins, MEDVi offers scheduled follow-ups with more clinical depth. SkinnyRx follow-ups tend to be brief check-ins focused on refill coordination and basic progress monitoring.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians for consultations. SkinnyRx employs a mix of licensed providers including NPs and PAs. Both are qualified to prescribe and manage GLP-1 medications.

The practical difference is in the depth of engagement. MEDVi providers spend more time per consultation and are more likely to discuss lifestyle factors, metabolic biomarkers, and long-term strategy. SkinnyRx providers focus more narrowly on medication management, which keeps things efficient but can feel transactional.

Support responsiveness is comparable, with both platforms operating primarily during business hours via messaging.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SkinnyRx safe?

SkinnyRx works with licensed providers and licensed compounding pharmacies. The medications are legally prescribed and dispensed. However, the lighter clinical oversight compared to platforms like MEDVi means you should ideally have a primary care doctor monitoring your overall health while using a service like SkinnyRx.

Does MEDVi's lab work replace my regular doctor visits?

No. MEDVi's metabolic panels are focused on weight loss and metabolic health markers. They do not replace comprehensive annual physicals or screenings your primary care doctor would order. Think of them as an addition, not a replacement.

Can I switch from SkinnyRx to MEDVi mid-treatment?

Yes. You can switch platforms at any time. You will need to complete MEDVi's intake process, but your existing GLP-1 treatment history will inform their prescribing decisions. There is no need to restart from scratch medically.

MEDVi focuses on metabolic health optimization alongside GLP-1 therapy, including nutritional guidance. SkinnyRx is primarily a medication access platform. Neither offers the breadth of wellness services available through platforms like Form Blends.
