Pricing Breakdown

Pricing works fundamentally differently at these two platforms. MEDVi charges an all-in monthly fee ($249-$399) that covers the consultation, lab work, and compounded medication. It is straightforward but not cheap.

Ro Body uses a membership model. The monthly membership runs $99-$145, which covers provider access, the app platform, and ongoing support. Medication cost is separate. If Ro's team helps you get brand-name Wegovy or Zepbound covered by insurance, your out-of-pocket medication cost could be as low as your insurance copay. If insurance does not cover it, Ro also offers compounded alternatives, though the total cost can rival or exceed MEDVi's pricing when you add membership plus medication.

The bottom line on price: Ro Body can be significantly cheaper if your insurance covers brand-name GLP-1s. If you are paying cash for everything, MEDVi's all-in pricing may actually be more predictable.

Medications Available

This is the biggest differentiator between these two platforms. Ro Body has made brand-name GLP-1 access a core part of its offering. The platform actively works with your insurance to secure coverage for Wegovy (semaglutide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide). For patients who qualify and have cooperative insurance plans, this is a genuine advantage that most telehealth competitors cannot match.

MEDVi focuses primarily on compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide. The compounded route avoids insurance headaches entirely but means you are not getting the brand-name formulations. For most patients, compounded GLP-1s produce similar results, but some patients prefer the consistency and regulatory oversight of brand-name products.

Ro also offers compounded options for patients whose insurance will not cover brand-name medications, giving it more flexibility across patient situations.

Patient Experience

Ro Body offers a more integrated experience. The app includes provider messaging, medication tracking, educational content, and can sync with the Ro Body Scale for body composition monitoring. This data integration gives both you and your provider a clearer picture of your progress over time.

MEDVi's portal is more straightforward, handling consultations, messaging, and prescription management without the lifestyle tracking features. The initial intake at MEDVi tends to be more thorough from a clinical standpoint, with longer consultations and more detailed health history reviews.

For patients who want a data-rich experience with progress tracking, Ro Body delivers more. For patients who just want a solid medical consultation and their medication shipped on time, MEDVi's simpler approach works fine.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians for all consultations. Ro Body staffs with a mix of MDs, NPs, and PAs. Both approaches are appropriate for GLP-1 prescribing.

Where Ro Body adds significant value is its insurance navigation team. Having a dedicated team work prior authorizations, appeals, and pharmacy coordination on your behalf saves hours of frustrating phone calls. This is a real service that translates to real savings for patients with compatible insurance.

MEDVi's support is more clinically focused. You get deeper medical conversations but no help navigating the insurance landscape, since MEDVi operates entirely outside the insurance system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Ro Body really get my insurance to cover GLP-1 medication?

It depends on your insurance plan. Ro Body's navigation team will attempt prior authorizations and appeals on your behalf, but coverage ultimately depends on your specific plan and whether you meet its criteria (typically a BMI of 30+ or 27+ with comorbidities). Not every patient gets approved, but Ro has a higher success rate than most patients achieve on their own.

Is compounded semaglutide from MEDVi the same as brand-name Wegovy?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient but is formulated by a compounding pharmacy rather than Novo Nordisk. It is not FDA-approved as a finished product, though it is legally prescribed. Some patients report similar results; others prefer the consistency of brand-name formulations.

Does Ro Body require the Ro Body Scale?

No. The scale is an optional add-on that integrates with the app for body composition tracking. The GLP-1 program works without it.

Which platform has better cancellation terms?

Both allow cancellation without long-term commitments. Ro Body's membership can be canceled monthly. MEDVi also operates on a month-to-month basis. Neither charges cancellation fees.
