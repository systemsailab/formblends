Why People Ask "Is MEDVi Legit?"

The GLP-1 telehealth market has exploded over the past two years, and with that growth has come a flood of new providers, some reputable and some not. When a company you have never heard of offers to prescribe weight loss medications online for a monthly fee, skepticism is appropriate.

MEDVi also deals with a naming confusion problem. A separate company called MEDvidi (with a "di" at the end) provides online mental health services. The similar names lead to crossed reviews and mixed search results. These are two entirely different companies. This review covers MEDVi, the weight loss telehealth clinic.

Evidence That MEDVi Is Legitimate

Registered Business Entity

MEDVi operates as a registered company based in Newark, Delaware. The business has a physical address, published terms and conditions, and a privacy policy, all standard indicators of a legitimate operation.

Licensed Physicians

MEDVi's consultations are conducted by board-certified physicians, not unlicensed providers or AI chatbots. Patients interact with real doctors who evaluate medical history, order lab work, and write prescriptions. Physician licensing can be verified through state medical board databases.

Regulated Pharmacy Partnerships

MEDVi partners with compounding pharmacies that operate under FDA oversight (503A and 503B facilities). While compounded medications themselves are not FDA-approved, the pharmacies that produce them are subject to federal and state regulation. This is an important distinction: the medications are legal and regulated, even though they have not gone through the FDA approval process that brand-name drugs require.

Verified Patient Reviews

MEDVi holds a 4.5 out of 5 rating on Trustpilot based on thousands of reviews. The positive reviews consistently describe effective medications, responsive physicians, and straightforward onboarding. The volume and consistency of these reviews is difficult to fabricate, though it is worth noting that some telehealth companies incentivize reviews through discounts or other perks.

Red Flags and Concerns

BBB Complaints

MEDVi has complaints on file with the Better Business Bureau. The most common issues involve billing disputes, including unauthorized charges, double billing for the same period, and difficulty obtaining refunds. One documented case describes a patient being charged for a six-month bundle they never authorized.

BBB complaints do not necessarily indicate a scam. Even well-established companies accumulate BBB complaints, especially subscription-based businesses. However, the pattern of billing-related complaints is worth noting and suggests that MEDVi's billing systems or processes have gaps.

Refund Policy Concerns

MEDVi's refund policy has drawn criticism. The company advertises a "results-based guarantee," but the fine print requires five months of program adherence before you can qualify. The refund also deducts a 25% consultation fee. Some patients report that the guarantee terms changed from three months to five months after they enrolled.

Once a prescription has been issued, standard refunds are generally not available. This is common across telehealth, but MEDVi's marketing can create expectations that the actual policy does not match.

Compounded Medications Are Not FDA-Approved

This is not specific to MEDVi. It applies to every telehealth company prescribing compounded GLP-1s. These medications use the same active ingredients as brand-name drugs, but they have not undergone the FDA approval process. Compounded formulations may vary in concentration, purity, or effectiveness. Patients should understand this trade-off before choosing any compounded medication provider.

How MEDVi Compares to Other GLP-1 Telehealth Platforms

MEDVi is one of dozens of telehealth companies offering compounded GLP-1 medications. Its physician-led model and metabolic lab work distinguish it from more transactional competitors. Its pricing ($179 first month, $299 ongoing for semaglutide) is mid-range for the category. Larger platforms like Hims offer lower monthly pricing and more polished technology, while smaller clinics may charge more for similar services.

The key question is not whether MEDVi is legit (it is), but whether it is the right fit for your needs and budget. The clinical offering is solid. The operational execution, particularly around billing and refunds, is where problems arise.

Frequently Asked Questions

MEDVi itself is a telehealth service, not a drug. The medications it prescribes are compounded versions of semaglutide and tirzepatide. These compounded formulations are not FDA-approved, though the active ingredients are the same as FDA-approved brand-name drugs. The compounding pharmacies MEDVi partners with are regulated under FDA guidelines.

MEDVi's medications are sourced from compounding pharmacies that operate under FDA oversight.
