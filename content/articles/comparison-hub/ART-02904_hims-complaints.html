Hims Complaints: The Most Common Issues and Whether They Should Concern You

Quick Verdict

Every large company attracts complaints, and Hims is no exception. With millions of customers and a subscription-based model, the volume of complaints is partly a function of scale. But the types of complaints matter. The most persistent issues center on billing practices, subscription cancellation difficulty, and the lack of meaningful clinical engagement, especially in the weight-loss and GLP-1 program. Some of these are fixable process issues. Others are structural to how Hims operates. Understanding the difference will help you decide whether the platform is right for you.

Breaking Down the Complaints

1. Subscription Cancellation

This is the most reported issue across Trustpilot, BBB, Reddit, and app store reviews. The complaint follows a consistent pattern: a customer decides to cancel, has difficulty finding the cancellation option, successfully cancels (or believes they have), and then gets charged again the following month.

Hims has made improvements to its cancellation flow over the past year, adding clearer in-app options and confirmation emails. But the sheer volume of cancellation complaints suggests that the underlying experience still creates confusion. Some users report needing to contact customer support to finalize a cancellation, only to encounter long response times.

To be fair, difficult subscription cancellation is a widespread problem across the entire subscription commerce industry, not just telehealth. But for a health service, where customers may be making decisions about active medications, the stakes feel higher.

2. Unexpected Charges and Billing Disputes

Closely related to cancellation issues, billing complaints include: charges appearing after cancellation, confusion about auto-renewal dates, being billed for a higher-tier plan than expected, and charges for products that the customer thought were one-time purchases but were actually subscriptions.

Some of this comes down to how Hims displays its pricing. The most prominent price shown is often the per-month cost of a multi-month plan. If a customer does not realize they are committing to a 3- or 6-month plan, they may be surprised when they cannot cancel after the first month without incurring additional charges.

3. Impersonal Provider Interactions

Hims operates on an asynchronous model for most consultations. You fill out a form, a provider reviews it, and a prescription decision is made without a live conversation. For many use cases, this is perfectly fine. But customers who expect to actually speak with a doctor are often disappointed.

The complaint is not that the providers are unqualified. They are licensed and credentialed. The complaint is that the interaction feels transactional. Providers do not have time to build context about your health history, ask follow-up questions in real time, or adjust their recommendations based on a conversation. For routine refills, this is acceptable. For new GLP-1 prescriptions, it is less so.

4. Customer Support

Hims offers support through in-app chat and email. There is no phone support line for general inquiries. Customers report response times ranging from a few hours to several days, with some issues requiring multiple follow-ups to resolve. For billing disputes in particular, the process can be frustratingly slow.

The absence of phone support is a deliberate business decision that keeps operational costs down, but it creates a poor experience when someone needs an urgent issue resolved. Several reviewers describe the chat support as heavily scripted, with representatives offering generic responses that do not address the specific problem.

5. GLP-1 and Weight-Loss Program Complaints

This is the fastest-growing complaint category. As Hims has scaled its weight-loss offerings, more users are reporting issues specific to that program:

  • Insufficient onboarding: Customers receive their medication without a clear guide on injection technique, titration schedule, or what side effects to watch for.
  • Difficulty reaching providers for dose adjustments: When patients need to move up or down in dosage, the async system can introduce delays of several days.
  • No lab monitoring: Hims does not routinely order or review bloodwork as part of its GLP-1 program. Patients who want metabolic monitoring must arrange it independently.
  • Side-effect management: GLP-1 medications commonly cause nausea, constipation, and other GI issues. Without timely provider access, patients sometimes reduce or stop their medication without clinical guidance.

These complaints are particularly relevant because GLP-1 therapy is not like taking a daily pill. It involves subcutaneous injections, careful dose escalation, and monitoring for potentially serious side effects. The level of clinical oversight matters.

6. Product Quality Concerns

A smaller but notable set of complaints involves the quality or consistency of compounded medications. Some users report receiving products with different packaging, different consistency, or different coloring between orders. Others question whether the compounded semaglutide is as effective as brand-name versions.

It is important to note that compounded medications are legal and regulated, but they are inherently more variable than commercially manufactured drugs. This is not unique to Hims. Any platform offering compounded products faces similar challenges. However, Hims' scale means that more people encounter these inconsistencies.

7. Shipping Delays

Most Hims customers report smooth shipping experiences, but delays do occur, particularly for GLP-1 medications during periods of high demand. Some users report going a week or more past their expected delivery date, which can be problematic for injectable medications that need to be taken on a consistent schedule.

8. Marketing and Upselling

The checkout flow on Hims includes multiple opportunities to add supplements, anti-nausea medications, and other products to your order. While these are optional, the presentation can feel pushy. Email marketing after sign-up is also frequent. Some users describe feeling like they are being marketed to rather than cared for.

Are These Complaints Deal-Breakers?

It depends on what you are using Hims for. For a straightforward finasteride or sildenafil prescription, the billing and cancellation issues are an annoyance but unlikely to affect your health. For GLP-1 therapy, the clinical oversight complaints are more consequential. The difference between a responsive provider and an unresponsive one can affect your safety, your results, and your experience.

Who Is Hims Best For?

Hims works well for self-directed patients who want affordable access to routine medications and do not need or expect a deep clinical relationship. If you understand the subscription terms, know how to manage your account, and are comfortable with limited provider interaction, the platform delivers solid value for basic prescriptions.

It is less suitable for patients starting GLP-1 therapy, especially first-time users who need guidance on injections, dose titration, and side-effect management. For those patients, a physician-supervised platform like Form Blends eliminates many of the complaints that Hims users report.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hims a scam?

No. Hims is a legitimate, publicly traded telehealth company. The complaints are about service quality and business practices, not fraud.

How do I cancel Hims without getting charged again?

Cancel through the app or website before your next renewal date. Take a screenshot of the cancellation confirmation. If you are charged after canceling, contact support with your confirmation as evidence. Some users recommend canceling at least a week before the renewal date to be safe.

Are the complaints about Hims GLP-1 different from other products?

Yes. GLP-1-specific complaints focus more on clinical support (provider access, dosing guidance, side-effect management) than on billing or shipping. This reflects the higher clinical complexity of injectable weight-loss medications.

Has Hims responded to customer complaints?

Hims has made changes to its cancellation process and customer support based on feedback. The company responds to many BBB and Trustpilot complaints publicly. However, structural issues like the async care model have not changed significantly.
