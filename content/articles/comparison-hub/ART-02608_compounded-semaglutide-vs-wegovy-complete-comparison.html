How Wegovy Works

Wegovy is Novo Nordisk's brand-name semaglutide product specifically developed and approved for chronic weight management. It received FDA approval in June 2021 for adults with a BMI of 30 or greater, or 27 or greater with at least one weight-related comorbidity .

Wegovy works by activating GLP-1 receptors throughout the body, particularly in the brain's appetite centers and in the gastrointestinal tract. This activation reduces hunger, increases feelings of fullness, and slows how quickly food moves through your stomach. The net effect is a natural reduction in caloric intake that leads to sustained weight loss over time.

The Wegovy titration schedule runs through five dose levels (0.25, 0.5, 1.0, 1.7, and 2.4 mg) over 16 to 20 weeks. The gradual increase helps patients adjust to the medication and reduces the severity of GI side effects. The maintenance dose of 2.4 mg is the highest approved dose of semaglutide for any indication.

Wegovy also holds an FDA indication for cardiovascular risk reduction, making it the only GLP-1 weight loss medication with this additional benefit .

How Compounded Semaglutide Works

Compounded semaglutide uses the same active molecule as Wegovy. The semaglutide is sourced from FDA-registered bulk drug substance suppliers and then formulated into an injectable preparation by a licensed compounding pharmacy.

The pharmacological action is identical. The same GLP-1 receptors are activated, the same appetite suppression occurs, and the same metabolic improvements follow. The biological experience of taking compounded semaglutide is the same as taking Wegovy because the molecule doing the work is the same.

One advantage of compounded semaglutide is dosing flexibility. While Wegovy locks patients into five specific dose levels, compounded semaglutide can be prescribed at any dose the physician chooses. This allows for more gradual titration if a patient is particularly sensitive to side effects, or for fine-tuned adjustments that are not possible with the brand-name product.

Side Effects Comparison

Side effects are molecule-dependent, not brand-dependent. Both compounded semaglutide and Wegovy carry the same risk profile:

  • Nausea: Very common, especially during early treatment and after dose changes. Affects 20-44% of patients .
  • Diarrhea: Reported in 15-30% of patients.
  • Vomiting: More likely during titration.
  • Constipation: A common GI effect.
  • Headache and fatigue: Reported in a smaller percentage of users.

Serious risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, kidney injury, and the thyroid C-cell tumor warning. These warnings apply equally to both products .

The main practical difference in the side effect experience relates to injection delivery. Wegovy uses a prefilled pen with a built-in needle that patients click to deliver a pre-measured dose. Compounded semaglutide typically uses a vial-and-syringe approach, requiring patients to draw up the correct dose manually. While this is straightforward once learned, it does add a step and a small potential for dosing inaccuracy.

Is compounded semaglutide legal?

Yes. Compounding is a well-established and legal practice in the United States. The FDA allows compounding of medications, including semaglutide, under specific regulatory frameworks (503A and 503B). Compounded semaglutide must be prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider and prepared by a licensed pharmacy.
