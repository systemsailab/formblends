Detailed Pricing Breakdown

How MEDVi Structures Its Pricing

MEDVi uses a dose-based pricing model. Your monthly cost is tied to the amount of medication in your prescription. This means your price at month one (low starting dose) will be different from your price at month four or five (maintenance dose). This is common across compounded GLP-1 providers, but it matters for budgeting.

A patient starting semaglutide at 0.25 mg might pay $249/month, but by the time they reach a 1.0 mg or 1.7 mg maintenance dose, the monthly cost could be $349 to $399. Over a 12-month treatment period, the effective average monthly cost lands somewhere around $300 to $350 for semaglutide.

Enrollment and Consultation Fees

MEDVi includes the initial medical consultation in your first month's payment. There is no separate enrollment fee or sign-up charge. Follow-up consultations and check-ins are also included in the monthly subscription. This keeps the pricing straightforward compared to providers that layer separate consult fees on top of medication costs.

Shipping and Fulfillment

Shipping is included in all MEDVi plans. Medication is shipped directly from a compounding pharmacy via cold-chain packaging to maintain medication integrity. Most patients receive their medication within 5 to 7 business days of their prescription being written.

Lab Work

Lab work is not included in MEDVi's pricing. If your provider orders blood panels (which is standard before starting GLP-1 therapy), you will need to get those done through your own insurance or pay out of pocket through a service like Quest Diagnostics or Labcorp. Budget $50 to $200 for basic metabolic panels depending on your insurance situation.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

MEDVi is a cash-pay platform. Insurance is not accepted for the program or the compounded medication. This is typical for compounded GLP-1 providers, since insurance plans generally do not cover compounded medications.

That said, MEDVi charges are often eligible for HSA and FSA reimbursement. These are physician-prescribed medications for a diagnosed medical condition (obesity or overweight with comorbidities), which qualifies under most health savings account rules. Request an itemized receipt from MEDVi and submit it to your benefits administrator.

If you have insurance that covers brand-name GLP-1s like Wegovy or Zepbound, you may get better value through a provider that helps you navigate insurance coverage rather than paying cash for compounded medication.

FAQ

Does MEDVi offer a free consultation?

MEDVi includes the initial consultation in your first month's payment. It is not separately billed, but it is not free either. You pay your first monthly subscription fee, which covers the consult and your first shipment of medication.

Will my MEDVi price go up over time?

Your monthly cost will likely increase as your dose increases during titration. Once you reach your maintenance dose, the price should stabilize. MEDVi does not currently guarantee rate locks, so general price increases are possible.

Is MEDVi cheaper than brand-name Wegovy?

Without insurance, yes. Brand-name Wegovy lists at over $1,300/month. MEDVi's compounded semaglutide at $249 to $399/month is significantly cheaper. But if your insurance covers Wegovy, the out-of-pocket cost through insurance may be lower than MEDVi's cash price.

Can I get a refund if MEDVi does not work for me?

MEDVi's refund policy varies. Generally, once medication has been compounded and shipped, refunds are not available for that month. Check MEDVi's current refund terms before enrolling, and ask specifically about their policy for patients who experience adverse reactions.

Does MEDVi charge for switching medications?

If you need to switch from semaglutide to tirzepatide (or vice versa), there may be an additional consultation and your pricing tier will change to reflect the new medication. Confirm the process and any fees with MEDVi before requesting a switch.
