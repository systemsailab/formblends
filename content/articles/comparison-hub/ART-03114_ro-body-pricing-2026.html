What Ro Body's Price Includes

Ro Body bundles more into their subscription than many competitors, which partly explains their pricing. Here is what you get for your money:

  • Compounded GLP-1 medication: Either semaglutide or tirzepatide, depending on your provider's recommendation and clinical eligibility.
  • Board-certified physician oversight: Initial evaluation, ongoing check-ins, and dose adjustments are all handled by licensed physicians through the Ro platform.
  • At-home metabolic lab kit: A testing panel that measures key metabolic markers. Results are reviewed by your provider and used to guide treatment decisions.
  • Body composition tracking: Tools within the Ro app to monitor weight, measurements, and progress over time.
  • Injection supplies: Syringes, needles, alcohol swabs, and instructions are shipped with your medication.
  • Free shipping: Temperature-controlled packaging delivered to your home.

What Is NOT Included

  • In-person medical visits
  • Insurance billing or claims processing
  • Brand-name medications (Wegovy, Ozempic, Mounjaro)
  • Nutritional counseling or meal planning (though some guidance is provided via the app)

How Ro Body Sets Their Pricing

Understanding the cost structure helps you evaluate whether the pricing is fair.

Medication Sourcing

Ro Body sources compounded GLP-1 medications from licensed 503B compounding pharmacies. These pharmacies produce semaglutide and tirzepatide formulations at a fraction of the brand-name cost. This is the primary reason Ro Body can price so far below retail Wegovy or Mounjaro.

Clinical Operations

Ro employs a network of physicians and clinical staff who manage patient care. Their salaries, malpractice coverage, and continuing education represent a significant operating cost built into each subscription.

Technology and Lab Testing

The Ro app, secure messaging platform, and at-home lab kit logistics add to the per-patient cost. The lab testing component is a meaningful differentiator that most budget-priced competitors do not offer.

Scale Advantage

Ro is one of the larger telehealth companies in the U.S. Their patient volume allows them to negotiate better rates with compounding pharmacies and lab partners, which is reflected in their competitive pricing.

Is Ro Body's Pricing Worth It?

The answer depends on what kind of patient you are.

Ro Body is a strong choice if:

  • You want a data-driven approach to weight loss with lab testing
  • You value physician-level oversight (vs. NP-only platforms)
  • You want metabolic health tracking alongside weight management
  • You appreciate a well-built app and clinical reporting tools

You might find better value elsewhere if:

  • You primarily want medication access at the lowest price
  • You already get regular lab work through your primary care provider
  • You do not need the body composition features
  • You prefer a shorter commitment with no quarterly prepayment

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest Ro Body plan?

The quarterly plan at $145 per month (billed as $435 every 90 days) is the lowest-cost option Ro Body offers. Contact provider for current pricing

Does Ro Body charge separately for lab testing?

No. At-home metabolic lab testing is included in both the monthly and quarterly plans. There is no additional fee for the lab kit or results review.

Can I get a refund if I cancel Ro Body?

Refund policies vary. Monthly plans can be canceled before the next billing cycle. Quarterly plans are generally not refunded for unused months.

Does Ro Body price vary by medication type?

The base pricing covers the primary medication in your plan. If your provider switches you to a different compound, pricing may change. Confirm with Ro support before agreeing to a medication change.

Is Ro Body available in my state?

Ro Body operates in most U.S. states, but availability varies. Check the Ro website for current coverage.

Does Ro Body accept insurance?

No. Ro Body does not bill insurance for their GLP-1 weight loss program. HSA and FSA cards may be accepted.
