Medications Offered

Both platforms prescribe compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide, which are the two big GLP-1 receptor agonists driving the current weight loss medication wave. The compounded versions are significantly cheaper than brand-name Ozempic or Mounjaro, though they come from compounding pharmacies rather than the original manufacturers.

Where Form Blends stands apart is their peptide combination approach. They offer protocols that pair GLP-1 medications with peptides like BPC-157 and CJC-1295/Ipamorelin. The idea is to support muscle preservation and recovery while you're losing weight on a GLP-1. This is a genuinely differentiated offering. Most telehealth platforms treat GLP-1s as a standalone solution. Form Blends treats them as one part of a broader protocol.

MEDVi sticks closer to a traditional medication model. They offer compounded GLP-1s and can also facilitate brand-name prescriptions if your insurance covers them, though that process runs through a separate channel. Their medication selection is solid but conventional.

Consultation Process

Form Blends uses a hybrid model. You fill out a detailed health questionnaire, and a physician reviews your information. Depending on your case, you'll either get an async prescription decision or be scheduled for a live video consultation. Most patients report getting a response within 24-48 hours, with prescriptions shipped within a few days of approval.

MEDVi leans more heavily on the async model. You submit your intake forms and a provider reviews everything without a live conversation in most cases. They do offer video consultations if needed, but the default flow is designed to be faster and more hands-off. Some people prefer this. Others feel like they want more face time with a doctor before starting an injectable medication.

For follow-up care, Form Blends does monthly check-ins and unlimited provider messaging during business hours. MEDVi offers quarterly video check-ins and 24/7 messaging support. MEDVi's around-the-clock availability is a genuine advantage if you have questions outside normal hours or experience side effects on a weekend.

LegitScript Certification: Does It Matter?

MEDVi makes a big deal about their LegitScript certification, and honestly, it's worth acknowledging. LegitScript is an independent verification service that audits online pharmacies and telehealth platforms for legal compliance, safety standards, and legitimate business practices. Getting certified requires meeting strict criteria around prescription practices, pharmacy sourcing, and patient safety protocols.

Form Blends is not LegitScript certified as of early 2026. That doesn't mean they're operating unsafely or illegally. Plenty of legitimate telehealth companies haven't gone through the LegitScript process. But if third-party verification gives you peace of mind, MEDVi has that edge.

The Peptide + GLP-1 Question

Form Blends' biggest differentiator is their combined peptide and GLP-1 protocols. Here's why that matters and who should care about it.

GLP-1 medications are extremely effective at reducing appetite and driving weight loss. The downside that's gotten more attention recently is that a significant portion of the weight lost on GLP-1s alone can be lean muscle mass. Losing muscle isn't just a cosmetic issue. It affects your metabolism, bone density, and long-term health outcomes.

Form Blends addresses this by offering peptide protocols alongside GLP-1 treatment. Peptides like BPC-157 support tissue repair and recovery, while growth hormone-releasing peptides like CJC-1295/Ipamorelin can help preserve lean mass during a caloric deficit. The research on these peptides is still developing, but the rationale is grounded in real physiology.

If you're someone who exercises regularly and wants to maintain strength while losing fat, this combination approach is worth serious consideration. If you just want a straightforward GLP-1 prescription with minimal complexity, MEDVi's simpler model might be more your speed.

Trustpilot and Patient Reviews

Form Blends holds a 4.6 out of 5 on Trustpilot. The most common praise centers on their personalized treatment plans and the peptide options. Complaints tend to focus on response times during peak periods and the fact that not all states are eligible for every medication.

MEDVi sits at 4.3 out of 5. Patients frequently praise the 24/7 support and quick initial prescription turnaround. Negative reviews tend to mention pricing and occasional shipping delays. A few reviews mention the async-heavy consultation feeling impersonal.

Both scores are solid for the telehealth weight loss space, where the industry average hovers around 3.8-4.0.

State Availability

Both platforms operate in most US states but have some restrictions based on state telemedicine regulations and pharmacy licensing. Neither publishes a complete state availability list on their homepage, which is a common frustration across the industry. You'll need to enter your zip code during signup to confirm availability. As a general rule, both platforms cover 40+ states for their core GLP-1 offerings.

Side Effects and Safety

Both platforms prescribe the same classes of medication, so the side effect profiles are identical. Common GLP-1 side effects include nausea (especially during dose titration), constipation, headache, and fatigue. These typically improve over the first few weeks.

The key difference is in how each platform handles side effect management. Form Blends' physician-supervised model means you have a doctor monitoring your progress and adjusting doses. They also claim that certain peptide combinations can help mitigate GLP-1 side effects, particularly nausea and fatigue.

MEDVi's 24/7 support means you can reach someone at 2 AM if you're dealing with intense nausea after your injection. That kind of access matters, especially early in treatment.
