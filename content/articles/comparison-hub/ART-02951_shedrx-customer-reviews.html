Shipping and Supply Gaps

A notable number of reviewers report gaps between running out of medication and receiving their next shipment. Some describe waiting two to three weeks for a refill, during which they missed doses. For GLP-1 therapy, medication gaps can mean:

  • Loss of appetite suppression and return of cravings
  • Need to re-titrate from a lower dose, extending treatment time
  • Increased side effects when medication is restarted

Review Patterns Worth Noting

Looking at reviews chronologically reveals some interesting trends. Early reviews tend to be more positive, reflecting a period when ShedRx likely had fewer patients and could provide more attention to each one. More recent reviews show an uptick in service-related complaints, which often correlates with rapid growth.

This is not inherently damning. It is a growth pattern that many telehealth companies experience. But it means the experience described in a review from six months ago may not match what you encounter today. Weight recent reviews more heavily when making your decision. {{CITE:ShedRx review trend analysis over time}}

How to Use Reviews to Make Your Decision

Here is our framework for evaluating telehealth reviews:

  • Focus on recurring themes, not isolated incidents. One bad review is an anecdote. Dozens of similar complaints are a pattern.
  • Read the company's responses to negative reviews. Do they take responsibility and offer solutions, or do they deflect?
  • Check dates. A review from 2024 may not reflect the current experience.
  • Weight Reddit feedback highly. Users post anonymously with no incentive to inflate or deflate their assessment.
  • Remember that satisfied patients are less likely to leave reviews than dissatisfied ones. A skew toward negative feedback is normal.
