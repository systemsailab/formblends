What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications that mimic the naturally occurring hormone glucagon-like peptide-1. This hormone plays several roles in weight regulation: it signals fullness to the brain, slows stomach emptying so you feel satisfied longer, and improves how your body processes insulin.

Originally developed for type 2 diabetes management, GLP-1 medications gained FDA approval for chronic weight management after clinical trials demonstrated average weight loss of 15-22% of body weight. The two primary GLP-1 medications used for weight loss are:

  • Semaglutide: Available as brand-name Wegovy (weight loss) and Ozempic (diabetes), as well as compounded formulations from providers like Form Blends
  • Tirzepatide: Available as brand-name Zepbound (weight loss) and Mounjaro (diabetes), plus compounded versions. Tirzepatide is technically a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist

What Every GLP-1 Beginner Needs to Know

Side Effects to Expect

Nearly all GLP-1 beginners experience some gastrointestinal effects during the first 2-4 weeks. These typically include:

  • Nausea: The most common side effect, affecting 40-50% of new users. Usually mild and temporary.
  • Reduced appetite: This is actually the desired effect, but it can feel dramatic in the first weeks.
  • Constipation or diarrhea: GI transit changes are common as the body adjusts.
  • Fatigue: Some users report tiredness in the first 1-2 weeks.
  • Injection site reactions: Minor redness or swelling at the injection site.

These side effects almost always resolve within 4-6 weeks as your body adjusts. A physician-supervised program like Form Blends can adjust your dose timing, provide anti-nausea guidance, and ensure your titration schedule matches your tolerance level.

How to Give Yourself an Injection

Self-injection sounds intimidating, but the process is simpler than most beginners expect. GLP-1 injections use small subcutaneous needles (similar to insulin pens) and are typically administered in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. The injection itself takes about 5 seconds. The Titration Schedule

Titration means gradually increasing your dose over several weeks. This approach minimizes side effects and allows your prescribing physician to find the dose that balances efficacy with tolerability. A typical semaglutide titration looks like:

  1. Weeks 1-4: 0.25mg weekly
  2. Weeks 5-8: 0.5mg weekly
  3. Weeks 9-12: 1.0mg weekly
  4. Weeks 13-16: 1.7mg weekly
  5. Week 17+: 2.4mg weekly (maintenance)

Form Blends physicians may customize this schedule based on your response, which is one of the key advantages of personalized compounded dosing over fixed brand-name pen steps.

Diet and Lifestyle During Your First Month

GLP-1 medications work best when paired with basic dietary adjustments. Because these medications reduce appetite significantly, the quality of what you eat becomes more important than the quantity. Key recommendations include:

  • Prioritize protein (aim for 0.7-1.0g per pound of body weight to preserve muscle mass)
  • Eat slowly and stop at the first sign of fullness
  • Stay well hydrated (dehydration worsens nausea)
  • Avoid high-fat, greasy meals during the titration period
  • Include basic resistance training to prevent muscle loss during weight loss

Most people notice appetite suppression within the first week. Measurable weight loss typically begins within 4-6 weeks. Over 68 weeks, clinical trials show average weight loss of 15% (semaglutide) to 22% (tirzepatide) of total body weight.

Do I need a BMI of 30+ to qualify for GLP-1 medication?

FDA guidelines indicate GLP-1 medications for people with BMI 30+ or BMI 27+ with at least one weight-related comorbidity (high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol). Form Blends physicians evaluate each patient individually during the consultation.

Current research suggests that GLP-1 medications are most effective as ongoing therapy. Studies show significant weight regain within one year of stopping treatment. Your Form Blends physician can help you develop a long-term plan that may include dose reduction for maintenance.

What should I do if I experience severe nausea?
