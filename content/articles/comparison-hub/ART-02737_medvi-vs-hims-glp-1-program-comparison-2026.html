Pricing Breakdown

Hims generally undercuts MEDVi on price. The Hims GLP-1 program starts around $199/month for compounded semaglutide at lower doses, scaling up as doses increase. MEDVi's pricing begins closer to $249/month, and their higher-tier plans with tirzepatide can push past $399.

Both platforms include the provider consultation in their monthly pricing, which is standard practice across telehealth weight loss companies now. Neither typically requires a separate enrollment fee, though MEDVi has occasionally run programs with an initial assessment charge.

Where it gets interesting is what you get for that price. MEDVi bundles more comprehensive metabolic lab panels into its program, which adds clinical value but also explains the higher cost. Hims keeps its offering leaner: medication, provider access, and a strong digital platform. For patients who already have a primary care doctor managing their labs, the Hims approach avoids paying for services they don't need.

Medications Available

Both platforms focus on compounded semaglutide as their primary GLP-1 offering. Compounded tirzepatide availability fluctuates at both companies, depending on supply chain conditions and regulatory changes affecting 503A and 503B pharmacies.

Hims has the advantage of scale. As one of the largest telehealth providers in the country, it has established relationships with multiple compounding pharmacies, which can mean more consistent supply. MEDVi works with a smaller pharmacy network, which can occasionally lead to delays during periods of high demand.

Neither platform is a reliable source for brand-name Ozempic, Wegovy, or Mounjaro. If you specifically need brand-name medications, you will likely need to work through a traditional provider and your insurance company.

Patient Experience

This is where Hims clearly pulls ahead. The Hims app is one of the better telehealth experiences available. Messaging your provider, tracking shipments, requesting refills, and reviewing treatment plans all happen in a single interface that feels modern and responsive.

MEDVi's platform is functional but less refined. The patient portal handles the basics (messaging, prescription management, appointment scheduling) without the polish of the Hims app. MEDVi compensates somewhat with what patients describe as more thorough initial consultations and follow-up appointments that feel less rushed.

For ongoing support, Hims offers 24/7 messaging with a care team, though responses can sometimes feel templated. MEDVi's communication tends to be more personalized but operates on business-hours response times.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi emphasizes its physician-led model, with board-certified doctors conducting consultations. Hims uses a broader provider network that includes nurse practitioners and physician assistants alongside MDs. For a straightforward GLP-1 prescription, the provider type matters less than some patients assume. NPs and PAs are fully qualified to prescribe and manage these medications.

Where MEDVi's physician focus might matter is for patients with complex medical histories or multiple comorbidities. Having a physician review your case from the start can provide an extra layer of clinical oversight.

It is also a reasonable choice if you prefer the feel of working with a dedicated medical practice rather than a large consumer health platform.

Hims is the better fit for most healthy adults who want an affordable, convenient path to compounded GLP-1 medication. The Hims app experience is genuinely good, and the lower price point makes long-term treatment more sustainable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MEDVi cheaper than Hims for GLP-1 medication?

No. Hims is generally less expensive, with plans starting around $199/month compared to MEDVi's $249+ starting price. However, MEDVi includes more comprehensive lab work, which could save money if you would otherwise pay for those tests out of pocket.

Do both platforms offer tirzepatide?

Both offer compounded tirzepatide when available. Supply can be inconsistent at either platform due to ongoing regulatory and supply chain factors in the compounding pharmacy space.

Can I use insurance with MEDVi or Hims?

Neither platform typically accepts insurance for their GLP-1 programs. Both are cash-pay services. Some patients use HSA or FSA funds to cover the cost.

Which platform has better customer support?

Hims offers more hours of availability and faster initial responses. MEDVi tends to provide more personalized, detailed responses but with slower turnaround. It depends on whether you prioritize speed or depth.
