Detailed Reviews

#1. Calibrate

Calibrate runs the most comprehensive online weight loss clinic we evaluated. Their program pairs GLP-1 medication with a full year of one-on-one metabolic health coaching covering nutrition, exercise, sleep, and emotional health. The medical care is physician-led, and the coaching team includes registered dietitians and health coaches.

The $1,650 annual program fee is significant, but it includes more clinical touchpoints than any competitor. Calibrate works with insurance for brand-name GLP-1s and provides prior authorization support. For patients who want a full medical weight loss program rather than just a prescription, this is the gold standard in telehealth.

Pros:

  • Most comprehensive coaching and medical support
  • Insurance navigation for brand-name medications
  • Evidence-based metabolic health methodology
  • Year-long commitment builds lasting habits

Cons:

  • $1,650 program fee is a barrier for many patients
  • No compounded medication options
  • Requires a 12-month commitment

#2. Ro Body

Ro Body delivers excellent GLP-1 therapy in a clean, well-designed telehealth package. Board-certified physicians, lab monitoring, and an in-house pharmacy make for a smooth clinical experience. Pricing for compounded semaglutide starts at $149/month, with tirzepatide also available.

Ro does not offer the deep coaching that Calibrate provides, but their medical care is on par or better. If you are primarily looking for well-managed medication therapy without paying for lifestyle coaching you may not use, Ro is a strong choice.

Pros:

  • In-house pharmacy ensures quality and speed
  • Lab-based monitoring and dosing
  • Transparent pricing
  • Clean, easy-to-use platform

Cons:

  • Limited coaching and lifestyle support
  • Price scales with dose
  • Not available in all states

#3. Found

Found takes a broader approach than most clinics on this list. Rather than defaulting to GLP-1s, their physicians evaluate your metabolic profile and may prescribe semaglutide, bupropion-naltrexone, topiramate, metformin, or other medications based on what your body is likely to respond to best.

This makes Found particularly valuable for patients who have not responded well to GLP-1s, or who have contraindications. The app-based coaching is decent, and pricing varies by medication ($99 to $299/month).

Pros:

  • Widest medication options
  • Physicians who think beyond GLP-1s
  • Personalized medication matching
  • App-based coaching and tracking

Cons:

  • GLP-1 pricing is not the most competitive
  • App quality is inconsistent
  • Provider response times can be slow

#4. Form Blends

Form Blends operates as a physician-supervised telehealth clinic specializing in GLP-1 and peptide therapy. The clinical model emphasizes personalized care. Each patient gets a dedicated prescriber who actively manages their protocol rather than handing off a standard prescription.

Beyond semaglutide and tirzepatide, Form Blends also offers peptide therapy protocols, which makes them a good fit for patients interested in broader metabolic optimization. Pricing starts at $199/month for compounded semaglutide with medication included.

Pros:

  • Genuine physician oversight with individualized protocols
  • Peptide therapy options beyond standard GLP-1s
  • Responsive, engaged clinical team
  • 503B pharmacy sourcing

Cons:

  • Smaller clinic, limited scale
  • No brand-name medication prescribing
  • No dedicated coaching staff

#5. Noom Med

Noom built its reputation on behavioral psychology-based weight loss coaching. Noom Med adds physician-prescribed GLP-1 medications on top of that foundation. The result is a program that takes behavior change seriously while giving patients access to medical tools that accelerate results.

The coaching is app-based and group-oriented, which works for some people and not others. Medical care is competent, though less individualized than programs like Ro or Form Blends. Pricing varies, with GLP-1 medication plans starting around $199/month.

Pros:

  • Strong behavioral coaching methodology
  • Combines medication with habit formation
  • Established brand with millions of users

Cons:

  • Coaching is group-based, not one-on-one
  • Medical care is less personalized
  • App can feel pushy

#6. Sequence (now Ww Clinic)

Sequence was acquired by WeightWatchers and rebranded as Ww Clinic. The program focuses on helping patients get brand-name GLP-1 prescriptions covered by insurance. Their team handles prior authorizations and appeals, which is genuinely valuable given how difficult insurance coverage can be to obtain.

Medical care is solid, and the WeightWatchers coaching platform provides additional support. The $99/month program fee is reasonable if insurance covers your medication.

Pros:

  • Best insurance navigation in the space
  • Brand-name medication focus
  • WeightWatchers coaching included
  • Reasonable program fee

Cons:

  • Value depends heavily on insurance coverage
  • No compounded options
  • Program integration post-acquisition is still uneven

#7. Hims/Hers

Hims/Hers is the highest-volume compounded GLP-1 platform in the country. They have scaled aggressively, and the result is competitive pricing ($175/month for compounded semaglutide), fast fulfillment, and a polished digital experience.

The trade-off is that care can feel impersonal. Providers are competent but managing large patient panels, and you may not see the same provider twice. For medically straightforward patients who prioritize affordability and convenience, this works fine.

Pros:

  • Competitive pricing
  • Fast, reliable fulfillment
  • Well-built app and messaging system

Cons:

  • Care feels high-volume and impersonal
  • Provider continuity is weak
  • Limited support for complex cases

FAQ

Are online weight loss clinics legitimate?

The best ones are. Legitimate online clinics employ licensed physicians, prescribe FDA-approved or properly compounded medications, and provide ongoing medical supervision. The key is verifying that the clinic uses board-certified providers and sources medications from reputable pharmacies.

Do I need to see a doctor in person for weight loss medication?

In most states, no. Telehealth regulations allow physicians to prescribe weight loss medications after a virtual consultation. Some programs also order lab work, which you complete at a local lab, to ensure safe prescribing.

What is the difference between an online clinic and a prescription mill?

A legitimate clinic evaluates your medical history, monitors your progress, adjusts your treatment based on results, and is available when you have concerns. A prescription mill processes your intake form, ships medication, and provides minimal follow-up. The programs on this list were selected specifically because they operate as real clinics.

Can I use my insurance with an online weight loss clinic?

Some clinics, particularly Calibrate and Sequence/Ww Clinic, actively work with insurance for brand-name GLP-1 medications. Most compounded medication programs are cash-pay only. Check with each clinic about insurance compatibility before enrolling.

How long should I stay on a GLP-1 medication?

Current evidence suggests that long-term use produces the best sustained weight loss results. Most patients who discontinue GLP-1 therapy regain weight within 12 months. Your physician should help you develop a long-term plan that may include dose adjustments, medication transitions, or lifestyle strategies to maintain results.
