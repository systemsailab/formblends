What Is SkinnyRx?

SkinnyRx is a telehealth platform that prescribes compounded GLP-1 medications for weight loss. Patients consult with a licensed provider online and receive compounded semaglutide or tirzepatide shipped monthly. The platform focuses on affordability and positions itself as one of the lower-cost options in the market. SkinnyRx review 2026

How It Works

SkinnyRx bundles everything into one monthly payment. You complete a health questionnaire, get a provider evaluation, and receive medication by mail. Monthly check-ins for dose adjustments are included. The subscription renews automatically each month unless you cancel.

What the Price Does Not Include

While SkinnyRx's monthly fee covers the basics, there are costs and limitations to be aware of:

  • Lab work: SkinnyRx does not include metabolic lab panels. If your provider recommends blood work, you will need to arrange and pay for it separately.
  • Brand-name medications: If you need or prefer brand-name Ozempic, Wegovy, or Mounjaro, SkinnyRx cannot provide them.
  • Insurance reimbursement: SkinnyRx is cash-pay only. You cannot submit claims to your insurance company.
  • Nutritional or lifestyle coaching: There is no structured coaching or counseling component beyond basic provider check-ins.

User Experience

From a cost perspective, SkinnyRx patients generally find the pricing transparent and the monthly charges predictable. There are no surprise fees at signup or hidden charges during treatment. The issue is when patients need to cancel or dispute a charge. Customer support can be slow, and getting a refund after medication has shipped is difficult.

For patients who treat SkinnyRx as a straightforward monthly medication delivery service and manage their billing proactively, the cost experience is positive. The value equation gets more complicated for patients who need clinical support that the platform is not built to provide.

Who Is SkinnyRx Best For?

SkinnyRx is best for budget-conscious patients who prioritize low monthly costs above all else. It works well for generally healthy individuals who do not need extensive lab work, coaching, or deep clinical oversight alongside their GLP-1 prescription.

If you are looking for the best overall value rather than just the lowest price, consider platforms that bundle more clinical services into their monthly fee, even if the sticker price is slightly higher.

Frequently Asked Questions

No. The monthly subscription covers the provider visit, medication, supplies, and shipping. However, your monthly cost may increase with dose changes or medication switches.

Can I use insurance with SkinnyRx?

No. SkinnyRx is a cash-pay platform. HSA and FSA cards may be accepted depending on your specific plan.

Is SkinnyRx cheaper than brand-name Ozempic?

Significantly. Brand-name Ozempic costs $900 to $1,300 per month without insurance. SkinnyRx's compounded semaglutide is a fraction of that cost, though they are different products.

Will my SkinnyRx price go up over time?

It may. Dose increases and medication switches can raise your monthly cost. Check with SkinnyRx about the pricing at higher dose levels before you start.
