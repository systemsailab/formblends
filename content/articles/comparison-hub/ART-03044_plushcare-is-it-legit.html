Business Legitimacy

PlushCare operates as a registered telehealth company based in San Francisco, CA. The platform works with licensed healthcare providers who are credentialed in the states where they practice. Prescriptions are filled through licensed pharmacies, whether retail or compounding, depending on the medication type.

The company has been operating since 2014 and maintains a presence on major review platforms. This does not mean every patient has a perfect experience, but it does mean PlushCare is not a scam or fly-by-night operation. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:PlushCare business legitimacy}}

Medication Sourcing

PlushCare offers brand-name GLP-1 prescriptions through insurance. If the platform prescribes compounded medications, those are prepared by licensed 503A or 503B compounding pharmacies. Compounded medications use the same active ingredients as brand-name drugs but are not individually FDA-approved products. If PlushCare prescribes brand-name medications like Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound, those come directly from the manufacturer through standard pharmacy channels.

What Patient Reviews Say

PlushCare has a rating of approximately 4.0/5 on Trustpilot. Positive reviews commonly mention effective weight loss results, convenient telehealth experience, and straightforward onboarding. Negative reviews tend to focus on billing disputes, difficulty canceling subscriptions, and inconsistent provider communication.

A pattern we see across many telehealth weight loss platforms is that the medical side, meaning the medications and clinical protocols, generally works well. Where companies run into trouble is on the operational side: billing, customer service, and subscription management.

Red Flags to Watch For (With Any Provider)

  • No consultation with a licensed provider before prescribing medication
  • Pressure to commit to long-term contracts before trying the service
  • Unclear pricing with hidden fees that appear after enrollment
  • No option to cancel month-to-month
  • Medications shipped without proper cold-chain packaging

We recommend checking for these red flags with any telehealth provider, not just PlushCare.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PlushCare FDA approved?

PlushCare is a telehealth platform, not a medication. The medications it prescribes may be FDA-approved (brand-name) or compounded (not individually FDA-approved but prepared by licensed pharmacies). The platform itself operates under state telehealth regulations.

Can I trust PlushCare with my health information?

Licensed telehealth platforms are required to comply with HIPAA regulations for patient data protection. Verify that PlushCare explicitly states HIPAA compliance in its privacy policy before sharing personal health information.

What if PlushCare does not work for me?

If you do not see results or experience issues with PlushCare, you can typically cancel and explore other providers.
