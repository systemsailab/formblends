What Positive Reviews Say

Medication Effectiveness

The most consistent theme in positive MEDVi reviews is that the medication works. Patients report significant appetite reduction, decreased food cravings, and measurable weight loss within the first few months of treatment. This is not surprising given that semaglutide and tirzepatide are well-studied GLP-1 receptor agonists with strong clinical evidence behind them. The positive outcomes reported by MEDVi patients are consistent with published clinical trial data for these medications.

Physician Interactions

Many positive reviews specifically mention the quality of physician consultations. Patients describe initial evaluations that feel thorough and personalized rather than rushed or transactional. Several reviews note that their prescribing physician took time to explain dosing schedules, potential side effects, and what to expect during the titration process.

Easy Onboarding

The sign-up process receives positive marks. Patients report that the intake questionnaire is straightforward, the consultation is scheduled quickly, and medication ships within a reasonable timeframe after the prescription is issued.

No Contract Flexibility

Multiple reviewers appreciate that MEDVi does not require a long-term contract. The month-to-month structure gives patients confidence that they can stop if the medication is not working or if side effects become unmanageable.

What Negative Reviews Say

Billing Errors and Unauthorized Charges

This is the single most common complaint across all platforms. Patients report being charged for refills they did not authorize, being double-billed for the same period, and being charged for multi-month bundles they never agreed to. One BBB complaint documents a patient being charged for a six-month bundle (February through August 2026) for $299 that they never requested, resulting in overlapping charges for multiple months.

Another complaint describes receiving partially filled medication vials while still being billed the full price. These billing issues appear frequently enough to suggest a systemic problem rather than isolated incidents.

Refund Difficulties

Patients who request refunds report a difficult process. MEDVi's results-based guarantee requires five months of program adherence, and approved refunds are reduced by 25%. Multiple patients claim that MEDVi originally advertised a 3-month guarantee that was later changed to 5 months.

Some reviews describe patients being denied refunds because they showed minimal weight fluctuation (one BBB complaint cites a refund denial over a 1-pound fluctuation). The gap between MEDVi's marketed guarantee and the actual refund experience is a recurring source of frustration.

Customer Support Response Times

Negative reviews frequently mention slow or unhelpful customer support. Patients report waiting days for responses to billing inquiries or refund requests. Provider messaging is limited to business hours, which can be frustrating for patients experiencing side effects outside of those windows.

Medication Delivery Delays

Some patients report delays between physician approval and medication arrival. While free shipping is standard, occasional backups at compounding pharmacies or high-demand periods can push delivery times beyond the expected window. This is a common issue across the compounded GLP-1 space, not unique to MEDVi.

Provider Availability

A smaller number of complaints mention providers missing scheduled appointments or rescheduling at the last minute. While this appears to be less common than billing complaints, it is worth noting for patients who value punctual, reliable scheduling.

Review Trends Over Time

MEDVi's review profile has evolved as the company has scaled. Earlier reviews (2024 and early 2025) tend to be more uniformly positive, with fewer billing complaints. As the company has grown, billing-related complaints have increased, which is a pattern commonly seen in telehealth companies that scale their subscriber base faster than their billing and customer support infrastructure.

The clinical reviews, however, have remained consistently positive throughout. This suggests that the medical side of the business (physician quality, medication effectiveness, treatment protocols) is functioning well, while the operational side (billing, customer support, refund processing) is where problems concentrate.

How to Interpret MEDVi Reviews

A few things to keep in mind when reading MEDVi reviews:

  • Naming confusion: MEDVi (weight loss) and MEDvidi (mental health) are different companies. Some reviews on Trustpilot and elsewhere may be misfiled between the two.
  • Selection bias: People who have strong experiences (positive or negative) are more likely to leave reviews. The silent majority of average-experience patients are underrepresented.
  • Incentivized reviews: It is unknown whether MEDVi incentivizes positive reviews through discounts or other offers.
  • BBB complaint context: BBB complaints skew negative by nature. The BBB is a complaint-resolution platform, so its data reflects problems rather than overall satisfaction.
