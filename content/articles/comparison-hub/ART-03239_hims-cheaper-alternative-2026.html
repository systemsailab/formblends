Why People Look for Cheaper Alternatives to Hims

The cost concern with Hims typically evolves over the course of treatment:

  • Starting price vs. maintenance price. The monthly cost at your initial dose may be $50 to $100 less than what you pay once you reach your maintenance dose. That increase catches many patients off guard.
  • Ongoing cost burden. At $200 or more per month for higher doses, the annual cost of Hims can exceed $2,400. For a medication you may need indefinitely, that number gets attention.
  • Value perception at scale. When provider interactions are brief and infrequent, paying premium prices for what feels like a prescription mill creates frustration.
  • Better deals available. The GLP-1 telehealth market has become increasingly competitive, and several programs now offer comparable or better care at lower price points.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Hims charge more at higher doses?

Higher doses require more medication per injection, which increases the pharmacy cost. Some programs absorb that cost and offer flat-rate pricing; others pass it through to patients. Hims uses a tiered model where price increases with dose.

Is the cheapest GLP-1 program the best choice?

Not necessarily. The cheapest program is the best choice if it provides adequate medical oversight and uses quality compounding pharmacies. Below a certain price point, programs may cut corners on clinical care or pharmacy standards. Look for the best value, not just the lowest number.

Will I get the same medication at a cheaper program?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient regardless of which telehealth platform prescribes it. What varies is the compounding pharmacy, the formulation details, and the quality standards. Programs that work with FDA-registered 503B compounding facilities provide the most reliable medication quality.

Can I keep my current dose when I switch to a cheaper provider?

Yes, in most cases. Your new provider will review your treatment history and continue you at your current dose if clinically appropriate. There is no medical reason to restart at a lower dose simply because you changed providers.
