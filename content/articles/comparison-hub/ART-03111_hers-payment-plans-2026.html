There is no interest, no credit check, and no third-party lender involved. Below, we explain each option in detail so you can pick the plan that works best for your budget and commitment level.

Understanding How Hers Payment Plans Work

Hers uses a subscription model, not installment financing. When you sign up, you select a billing cycle and pay upfront for that period. Your payment covers medication, provider consultations, and shipping for the duration of the cycle. {{CITE:Hers payment structure}}

This approach has some advantages: no interest charges, no credit impact, and predictable costs. The downside is that longer plans require a larger upfront payment, and refunds for unused portions are generally not available.

Does Hers Offer Traditional Financing?

No. As of 2026, Hers does not partner with financing companies like Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay, or CareCredit. {{CITE:Hers financing options}} You cannot split your payment into interest-free installments through the Hers platform.

However, there are workarounds if you need to spread costs:

  • Credit card installment plans: Many credit card issuers offer the ability to convert purchases into fixed monthly payments. Check if your card offers this feature after making a Hers payment.
  • HSA/FSA cards: If your treatment qualifies, paying with pre-tax health funds effectively reduces your cost. See our {{INTERNAL_LINK:Hers HSA FSA 2026}} guide.
  • Start with monthly: If the quarterly or semi-annual upfront cost is too steep, begin with the monthly plan and switch to a longer cycle once you are confident in your treatment.

Cancellation and Refund Rules

Understanding the cancellation terms is critical before selecting a plan, especially for the longer billing cycles.

Cancellation Policy

You can cancel your Hers subscription at any time through the website or by contacting support. However, cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing period. If you are two months into a six-month plan, you will not receive a refund for the remaining four months. {{CITE:Hers cancellation policy}}

Refund Policy

Hers generally does not issue refunds once medication has been shipped. If you experience a medical complication, their clinical team will address it, but financial refunds are handled on a case-by-case basis and are not guaranteed.

Pausing Your Plan

Some Hers programs allow you to pause your subscription temporarily. This is not widely advertised, so you may need to contact support directly to ask about pause options for your specific plan.

Payment Methods Accepted

Hers accepts the following payment methods for weight loss subscriptions:

  • Major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover)
  • Debit cards
  • HSA debit cards
  • FSA debit cards

Hers does not accept PayPal, Venmo, Apple Pay, or cryptocurrency for subscription payments as of 2026. {{CITE:Hers payment methods}}

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from a monthly plan to a quarterly plan with Hers?

Yes. You can typically change your billing cycle at the start of your next billing period. Contact Hers support or check your account settings to make the switch.

Does Hers offer a payment plan for the initial consultation?

The initial consultation is included in your first subscription payment, so there is no separate fee to finance. {{CITE:Hers consultation inclusion}}

What happens if my payment to Hers fails?

Hers will reattempt the charge. If payment cannot be collected, your subscription may be suspended and medication shipments will stop until the balance is resolved.

Can I use multiple payment methods for one Hers plan?

Hers typically allows one payment method per subscription. You cannot split a single payment across multiple cards.

Is there a penalty for canceling a Hers plan early?

There is no cancellation penalty or early termination fee. However, you will not receive a refund for the unused portion of your current billing cycle. {{CITE:Hers early cancellation terms}}
