How to Switch from MEDVi in 2026: A Step-by-Step Guide

Switching GLP-1 providers feels bigger than it actually is. You have a medication routine that works, a dosing schedule you have figured out, and a provider relationship (however minimal) that you are used to. Walking away from that can feel risky, even when you know the current setup is not meeting your needs.

The good news: switching from MEDVi to another GLP-1 telehealth provider is straightforward in 2026. The market has matured, providers are experienced at onboarding patients mid-treatment, and with the right approach, you can transition without a gap in medication or a setback in progress.

Here is exactly how to do it.

Why People Switch from MEDVi

The decision to switch usually builds over time rather than happening overnight. The most common triggers include:

  • Stalled progress with no clinical response. Weight loss plateaus are normal, but when your provider does not adjust your protocol in response, it feels like you are paying for autopilot.
  • Escalating costs. Monthly pricing that seemed reasonable at your starting dose may have climbed significantly as you titrated up.
  • Insufficient provider access. Difficulty reaching your prescriber when you have side effects or questions creates real safety concerns, not just frustration.
  • Interest in broader treatment options. Patients who want tirzepatide or peptide therapies like BPC-157 may have outgrown what MEDVi offers.
  • Service quality issues. Shipping delays, billing errors, or poor customer support compound over time.

How to Switch: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose Your New Provider Before Canceling MEDVi

This is the most important step. Do not cancel MEDVi until you have enrolled with your new provider and confirmed that your medication is being processed. The goal is zero gap in treatment.

Research your options, complete the initial consultation with your new provider, and wait until your new prescription is confirmed before touching your MEDVi subscription.

Step 2: Gather Your Treatment History

Your new provider will need to know:

  • Your current medication and dose
  • Your titration history (what doses you have been on and for how long)
  • Any side effects you have experienced
  • Your starting weight and current weight
  • Any lab work you have had done
  • Other medications you take

Most of this information is available in your MEDVi account. Download or screenshot it before you cancel. Good providers like Form Blends and Ro will use this history to continue your treatment seamlessly rather than starting from scratch.

Step 3: Complete Your New Provider Consultation

Every telehealth GLP-1 program requires an initial medical evaluation, even for patients transferring from another provider. This is not a formality. It is your new physician's opportunity to review your history, assess your current status, and determine whether to continue your current protocol or make adjustments.

Be honest and thorough during this consultation. Share everything, including side effects you may have downplayed with your previous provider.

Step 4: Time Your Transition

The ideal timing is to start your new provider consultation about two weeks before your next MEDVi shipment would arrive. This gives your new provider time to evaluate you, write your prescription, and ship your medication before your current supply runs out.

If there is a brief overlap where you have medication from both providers, that is fine. You will not use both. The overlap is just insurance against a gap.

Step 5: Cancel Your MEDVi Subscription

Once your new medication is in hand (or confirmed shipped), cancel your MEDVi subscription. Review their cancellation policy beforehand so you know:

  • Whether there are cancellation fees
  • When your next billing date is
  • How to avoid being charged for another month

Cancel in writing (email or through the platform) and save confirmation of the cancellation for your records.

Who Should Switch from MEDVi

Switching makes sense when the cost of staying is higher than the cost of leaving. That cost is not just financial. Consider switching if:

  • Your results have stalled and nothing is changing. A new physician with a fresh perspective on your protocol can sometimes identify adjustments that restart progress.
  • You are paying more than comparable alternatives. The GLP-1 market is competitive in 2026. If you can get the same or better care for less, there is no reason to overpay.
  • You do not trust your provider. Trust is fundamental to any medical relationship. If you do not trust that your MEDVi provider is making decisions in your best interest, that alone justifies a change.
  • You need treatments MEDVi does not offer. If your health goals require peptide therapies or medications outside MEDVi's formulary, you have no choice but to look elsewhere.
  • The service issues are chronic. One shipping delay is understandable. Recurring billing problems, communication failures, or supply issues are systemic and unlikely to improve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I lose progress if I switch from MEDVi?

No, as long as you avoid a significant gap in medication. If your new provider continues you at your current dose (which most will, given your treatment history), your body does not know or care which telehealth platform prescribed the medication.

Do I need to taper off before switching?

No. GLP-1 medications do not require tapering. You simply continue your regular dosing schedule with medication from your new provider. The transition should be seamless.

Can my new provider see my MEDVi records?

Not automatically. Medical records are not shared between telehealth platforms unless you authorize a transfer. The easiest approach is to download your treatment history from MEDVi and share it with your new provider during your initial consultation.

What if I am mid-titration when I switch?

A qualified physician will review where you are in your titration and continue the schedule appropriately. Being mid-titration is not a barrier to switching. Just make sure your new provider knows your current dose and how long you have been on it.

How long does it take to get started with a new provider?

Most telehealth GLP-1 programs can complete your consultation within 24 to 48 hours and ship medication within a few days after that. Plan for about one week from enrollment to medication delivery, though some programs are faster.
