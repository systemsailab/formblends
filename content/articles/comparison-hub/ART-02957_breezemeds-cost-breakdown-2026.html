Costs Not Included in the Monthly Fee

Laboratory work

Some providers require baseline labs before initiating GLP-1 therapy. Common tests include a comprehensive metabolic panel, thyroid function panel, lipid panel, and hemoglobin A1C. If BreezeMeds requires labs, you may need to pay $75 to $250 out of pocket, depending on which tests are ordered and where you get them done.

Some patients already have recent lab work through their primary care provider. Ask BreezeMeds whether they accept outside lab results to avoid duplicating tests.

Injection supplies

Compounded GLP-1 medication typically comes in a vial, requiring you to draw the dose with a syringe. You will need insulin syringes, alcohol swabs, and a sharps container. If these are not included with your medication shipment, expect to spend $10 to $25 per month on supplies.

Anti-nausea medication

Some patients need anti-nausea support during dose titration. If BreezeMeds does not include this in their program, you may need a separate prescription or over-the-counter remedy, adding $10 to $30 per month.

Expedited shipping

Standard shipping is typically included. If you need your medication faster, overnight or two-day options may be available at additional cost.

Getting the Most Value for Your Money

Regardless of which platform you choose, these strategies can help you maximize your investment:

  • Ask about the full dose escalation pricing schedule before committing. Know what month six will cost, not just month one.
  • Check whether your HSA or FSA covers telehealth weight loss programs. Many do.
  • Ask about multi-month payment options that may offer a discount.
  • Focus on compliance. Missed doses extend treatment and waste money. Consistency is the most efficient path to results.
  • Combine medication with lifestyle changes. Better outcomes in less time means a lower total treatment cost.
