Wegovy and Zepbound are both FDA-approved specifically for chronic weight management, but Zepbound (tirzepatide) has shown greater average weight loss in clinical trials compared to Wegovy (semaglutide), making it the more potent option on paper.

Wegovy and Zepbound are the only two injectable GLP-1-class medications with full FDA approval for chronic weight management. Everything else in this class is either approved for diabetes and used off-label, or still in clinical trials. How Wegovy Works

Wegovy uses {{INTERNAL_LINK:semaglutide}} at doses up to 2.4 mg per week to activate GLP-1 receptors throughout the body. These receptors exist in your gut, pancreas, and brain, and activating them produces a cascade of metabolic changes that promote weight loss.

The primary mechanisms are appetite suppression and delayed gastric emptying. When you take Wegovy, you feel less hungry between meals, you get full more quickly when you do eat, and food stays in your stomach longer, which extends that feeling of satisfaction. Your brain receives stronger "I am full" signals, which makes it easier to eat less without constantly fighting cravings.

Wegovy has a five-step titration schedule: 0.25 mg, 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.7 mg, and finally the maintenance dose of 2.4 mg. This gradual increase takes 16 to 20 weeks and helps your body adjust to the medication while minimizing GI side effects.

Beyond weight loss, Wegovy became the first GLP-1 medication to earn an FDA indication for reducing cardiovascular events in overweight and obese adults, based on the landmark SELECT trial {{CITE:claim}}.

How Zepbound Works

Zepbound uses {{INTERNAL_LINK:tirzepatide}}, a dual-action molecule that activates both the GLP-1 and GIP receptors. While Wegovy targets one pathway, Zepbound targets two, which appears to produce a more powerful metabolic response.

The GIP receptor activation adds several effects beyond what GLP-1 alone provides. GIP signaling may improve how your body metabolizes fat, enhance insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, and work synergistically with GLP-1 to further suppress appetite {{CITE:claim}}. This combination is believed to be the reason Zepbound produces more weight loss than Wegovy in clinical data.

Zepbound's titration moves through six dose levels: 2.5 mg, 5 mg, 7.5 mg, 10 mg, 12.5 mg, and 15 mg. This gives doctors more flexibility to find the dose that balances effectiveness with tolerability for each patient. Not every patient needs to reach the maximum 15 mg dose to see excellent results.

Efficacy Comparison: Weight Loss Results

Both medications deliver transformative weight loss, but Zepbound holds a numerical edge.

In the STEP 1 trial, Wegovy patients lost an average of 14.9% of their body weight over 68 weeks {{CITE:claim}}. About one-third of participants lost 20% or more of their starting weight. The STEP program included several trials across different populations, and results consistently fell in the 12-17% range depending on the dose and patient group.

Zepbound's SURMOUNT-1 trial showed more dramatic results: an average of 22.5% body weight loss at the 15 mg dose and 19.5% at the 10 mg dose over 72 weeks {{CITE:claim}}. Nearly 40% of participants on the highest dose lost more than 25% of their starting weight.

While there has not been a large, published head-to-head randomized trial comparing Wegovy 2.4 mg directly to Zepbound 15 mg, cross-trial comparisons strongly suggest that tirzepatide produces roughly 5-7 percentage points more weight loss than semaglutide. For a 250-pound person, that difference could translate to an additional 12-18 pounds of weight loss.

Both drugs show continued weight loss over the full study period, with the most rapid loss occurring in the first 6-9 months and then gradually plateauing as patients approach their new equilibrium weight.

Side Effects Comparison

The side effect profiles are broadly similar, since both drugs work through related pathways:

  • Nausea: The number one reported side effect for both. In clinical trials, nausea affected roughly 24-44% of Wegovy users and 24-33% of Zepbound users {{CITE:claim}}.
  • Diarrhea: Reported in 15-30% of patients for both medications.
  • Vomiting: More frequent during dose escalation for both.
  • Constipation: Somewhat more commonly reported with tirzepatide {{CITE:claim}}.
  • Abdominal discomfort: Related to slower gastric emptying; common with both.

Serious risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder events (especially in patients losing weight rapidly), kidney injury, and the theoretical thyroid tumor risk from animal studies. Both medications carry the same boxed warning regarding thyroid C-cell tumors {{CITE:claim}}.

Discontinuation rates due to adverse events in the major clinical trials were roughly similar (6-7% for both), suggesting that neither drug has a clear tolerability advantage overall.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which produces more weight loss, Wegovy or Zepbound?

Zepbound (tirzepatide) produces greater average weight loss in clinical trials, roughly 18-22% versus 15-17% for Wegovy (semaglutide). Individual results vary, but the trend consistently favors tirzepatide across multiple studies and patient populations.

Can I switch from Wegovy to Zepbound?

Yes, under medical supervision. Because the two drugs contain different active ingredients, switching requires starting Zepbound at its initial 2.5 mg dose and titrating up. Your doctor can advise on timing and the best transition approach.

Do I need a BMI of 30 to qualify?

The FDA-approved criteria for both medications require a BMI of 30 or greater, or a BMI of 27 or greater with at least one weight-related health condition. However, prescribing decisions are made individually by your physician.

How do I decide between Wegovy and Zepbound?

Consider your weight loss goals, health conditions, insurance coverage, and prior medication experience. Patients seeking maximum weight loss may lean toward Zepbound, while those who value cardiovascular risk reduction or are already succeeding on semaglutide may prefer Wegovy. A physician can help you weigh these factors.

Both Wegovy and Zepbound represent the best that modern weight loss medicine has to offer.
