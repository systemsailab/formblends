Medication Options

This is where the two platforms diverge most. Hims focuses on compounded semaglutide, giving patients access to the medication at lower cost than brand-name alternatives. You get a consistent product shipped to your door on a regular schedule.

GoodRx takes a different approach. Their core product helps you find the best price on brand-name medications at local pharmacies. If your doctor prescribes Wegovy or Ozempic, GoodRx can show you which pharmacy near you has the lowest price and provide a coupon to reduce the cost further. Their telehealth arm may also prescribe compounded options, but this is not their primary strength.

If you want brand-name medication and already have a prescription, GoodRx's coupon tool can be genuinely useful. If you want a fully managed telehealth experience with compounded semaglutide, Hims is more streamlined.

Clinical Support

Hims provides ongoing access to your prescribing provider through the app. You can message with questions, request dose adjustments, and track your progress. The clinical model is designed for convenience and speed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a GoodRx coupon with a Hims prescription?

No. Hims uses its own pharmacy fulfillment system. GoodRx coupons are for use at retail pharmacies and cannot be applied to Hims prescriptions.

Is compounded semaglutide as effective as brand-name Wegovy?

Compounded semaglutide uses the same active ingredient. Clinical outcomes depend on proper dosing, patient adherence, and individual response. Many patients report similar results, but compounded formulations have not undergone the same clinical trials as brand-name products.

Does GoodRx offer compounded GLP-1 medications?

GoodRx Care's telehealth providers may prescribe compounded options, but availability varies. GoodRx's primary value for GLP-1s is helping patients find the best price on brand-name medications.

How long do I need to take GLP-1 medication?

Most providers recommend continuing GLP-1 medication for as long as you are benefiting from it. Stopping medication often leads to weight regain. Your provider will help you develop a long-term plan.

What if I cannot afford either option?

Manufacturer patient assistance programs, state Medicaid programs, and employer health benefits may help cover GLP-1 medication costs. Ask your provider about all available options.
