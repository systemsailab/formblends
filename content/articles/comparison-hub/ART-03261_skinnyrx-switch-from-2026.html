Why Patients Leave SkinnyRx

SkinnyRx makes it easy to start GLP-1 therapy. The problem is what happens after the start. Patients who contact us about switching typically share a few consistent frustrations:

Shallow Clinical Engagement

SkinnyRx's convenience model means consultations are often brief and follow a scripted format. Patients feel like they are being processed rather than treated. When they have specific questions about their response to medication, side effects, or plateau strategies, the depth of clinical engagement often falls short. {{CITE:SkinnyRx clinical engagement feedback}}

Limited Adaptability

Weight loss is not linear. It involves phases of rapid progress, plateaus, and sometimes setbacks. A good treatment plan adapts to each phase. SkinnyRx's standardized protocols may not evolve quickly enough to match your body's changing needs. When your dose needs adjusting or your medication type needs changing, the platform's response time can leave you stuck.

Wanting More From Your Provider

Many patients simply reach a point where they want a physician who knows their case, takes the time to explain clinical decisions, and proactively manages their treatment rather than reacting to problems after they arise. That level of care requires a platform built around clinical relationships, not transaction volume.

Your Step-by-Step Switching Guide

What Happens to Your Dose

Dose continuity is a top priority during the switch. In most cases, your Form Blends physician will maintain your current dose. This provides stability and avoids disrupting the progress you have made. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 dose stability during transitions}}

Your physician may recommend changes in specific situations:

  • If side effects at your current dose are persistent and undertreated, a brief step-down with slower re-escalation may be recommended
  • If you have been at a stable dose but your weight loss has plateaued, an increase may be appropriate
  • If you want to switch from semaglutide to tirzepatide (or vice versa), your physician will calculate the equivalent dose {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 medication cross-titration}}

Every dosing decision is made in partnership with you. Your physician explains the rationale, and you have the final say.
