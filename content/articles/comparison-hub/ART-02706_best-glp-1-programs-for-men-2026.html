Frequently Asked Questions

Do GLP-1 medications lower testosterone?

Rapid weight loss from any cause can temporarily lower testosterone levels. However, long-term weight loss often improves testosterone because excess body fat converts testosterone to estrogen through aromatization {{CITE:weight loss effect on male testosterone levels}}. Monitoring testosterone during treatment helps physicians catch and address any dips early.

Will I lose muscle on a GLP-1 medication?

Some muscle loss is common with any significant weight loss. Studies suggest that 25-40% of weight lost on GLP-1 medications may be lean mass {{CITE:lean mass loss during GLP-1 therapy}}. Resistance training and adequate protein intake are the best defenses. Some providers also offer {{INTERNAL_LINK:peptide therapies}} that support lean mass preservation.

Can I combine GLP-1 therapy with testosterone replacement?

Yes, many men safely use GLP-1 medications alongside testosterone replacement therapy (TRT). However, both should be managed by a physician who understands the interactions between weight loss, hormones, and metabolic health.

How much weight can men expect to lose on semaglutide or tirzepatide?

Men in clinical trials lost an average of 15-20% of body weight over 12-18 months {{CITE:GLP-1 weight loss outcomes in male patients}}. Individual results depend on starting weight, medication adherence, diet, exercise, and metabolic factors.
