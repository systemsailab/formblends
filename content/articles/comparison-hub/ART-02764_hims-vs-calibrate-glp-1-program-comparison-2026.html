Understanding the Two Approaches

Hims

Hims makes GLP-1 medication accessible with minimal barriers. You complete an online assessment, a provider reviews your case, and compounded semaglutide is shipped to your door. The Metabolic Health Difference

Calibrate's core argument is that weight gain is a metabolic problem, not just a willpower problem. Their program starts with metabolic lab work to assess markers like insulin resistance, thyroid function, and lipid levels. This data informs a personalized plan that addresses not just what you eat, but how you sleep, move, and manage stress.

There is solid science behind this approach. Poor sleep increases ghrelin (the hunger hormone) and decreases leptin (the satiety hormone). Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which promotes fat storage. Addressing these factors alongside medication can improve outcomes significantly.

Hims does not engage with these factors. The platform trusts that the medication will do its job and that patients will manage the rest on their own. For many patients, this is fine. GLP-1 medications are powerful tools that reduce appetite and improve blood sugar control regardless of coaching. But for patients with complex metabolic profiles, the extra support may matter.

Insurance and Medication Costs

One of Calibrate's key advantages is insurance navigation. Their team works with patients to get brand-name GLP-1 medications covered by insurance. If successful, the medication cost can drop to a copay rather than the full out-of-pocket price. This can make Calibrate's total program cost competitive with or even lower than Hims, depending on your insurance plan.

Hims does not work with insurance for compounded GLP-1 medications. The monthly price is what you pay. This simplicity is a strength for patients without insurance coverage, but it means you will never benefit from insurance-negotiated pricing.

The Commitment Factor

Calibrate requires a one-year commitment. This works in the program's favor from a clinical perspective, since meaningful metabolic changes take time. But it is a significant financial and time commitment. If your circumstances change, you are still locked in.

Hims operates month-to-month. You can start, pause, or cancel without penalty. For patients who are unsure about GLP-1 medication or who want to test the waters before committing, Hims offers much lower risk.

Who Should Choose Hims?

Hims makes sense if you want affordable, no-commitment access to GLP-1 medication. It is best for self-motivated patients who do not need coaching and who want to keep things simple. If cost is a primary concern and you do not have insurance coverage for brand-name GLP-1s, Hims' compounded option is hard to beat on price.

Who Should Choose Calibrate?

Calibrate is worth the investment if you want a comprehensive metabolic health program, have insurance that may cover brand-name medication, and are willing to commit to a year of structured coaching. If you have struggled with weight loss in the past and want to understand the metabolic factors behind it, Calibrate's approach is thorough and evidence-based.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Calibrate worth the higher cost?

It depends on your needs. If your insurance covers brand-name GLP-1 medication, Calibrate's total cost may be competitive with medication-only platforms. The coaching and metabolic testing add value for patients who want a comprehensive approach.

Can I get lab work on my own and use Hims for medication?

Yes. You can order metabolic lab panels through your primary care physician or direct-to-consumer lab services and use Hims separately for medication. You would need to coordinate interpretation of results on your own.

Does Calibrate guarantee weight loss?

No weight loss program can guarantee specific results. Calibrate reports that their patients lose an average of 15% of body weight over one year, but individual results vary.

What happens after Calibrate's one-year program ends?

Patients can continue medication independently or through another provider. Calibrate's goal is to establish habits during the year that help maintain results long-term. Some patients continue coaching through Calibrate's alumni program, if available.

Do I need to exercise on either program?

Neither program strictly requires exercise. Calibrate incorporates movement as one of its four health pillars. Hims leaves exercise entirely to the patient. Exercise enhances GLP-1 medication outcomes and supports long-term weight maintenance.
