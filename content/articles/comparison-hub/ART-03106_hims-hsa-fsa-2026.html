HSA and FSA Basics for Weight Loss Treatment

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) and Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) let you use pre-tax dollars for qualified medical expenses. This effectively gives you a discount equal to your marginal tax rate, which for most people means saving 20-35% on eligible purchases.

The key question is whether GLP-1 weight loss medication counts as a qualified medical expense. Under IRS rules, weight loss programs prescribed by a physician to treat a specific disease (such as obesity, type 2 diabetes, or cardiovascular risk) are generally eligible. Programs pursued purely for general health or appearance typically are not.

How to Use Your HSA or FSA Card on Hims

The process is fairly straightforward. When you check out on the Hims platform, you enter your HSA or FSA debit card as your payment method, just like you would a regular credit or debit card.

However, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Merchant category codes matter. Hims transactions may or may not be coded as a medical provider. If the merchant code does not match what your HSA/FSA administrator expects, the charge could be declined or flagged for review.
  • You may need a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN). Some plan administrators require your physician to provide documentation stating that the weight loss treatment is medically necessary. Your Hims provider can typically supply this.
  • Keep your receipts. Even if the transaction goes through, your plan administrator may request substantiation later. Save all receipts, Explanation of Benefits documents, and provider notes.

What If Your HSA/FSA Card Gets Declined?

It happens. Here are the most common reasons and what to do about each one.

  • Insufficient funds: Check your account balance. FSAs especially have annual limits and may be partially spent.
  • Merchant code mismatch: Contact your plan administrator and ask them to manually approve the charge. You may need to submit a claim for reimbursement instead.
  • Transaction flagged for review: Provide your Letter of Medical Necessity and prescription documentation. Most claims are approved within a few business days once documentation is submitted.
  • Telehealth exclusion: A small number of older FSA plans do not cover telehealth services. Confirm with your HR department or benefits administrator.

The Manual Reimbursement Option

If your HSA/FSA card will not work directly on the Hims website, you can pay with a personal credit or debit card and then submit a reimbursement claim to your HSA/FSA administrator. You will need an itemized receipt showing the medical service and medication, along with any supporting documentation your administrator requires.

If you are planning a multi-month GLP-1 treatment course, an HSA gives you more flexibility because unused funds carry over. FSAs work well if you know exactly how much you will spend this plan year and want to maximize your tax savings within that window.

Tax Documentation You Should Keep

Whether you use your HSA/FSA directly or submit for reimbursement, maintain these records:

  • Prescription or treatment plan from your Hims provider
  • Itemized receipts for each billing cycle
  • Letter of Medical Necessity (if your administrator requires one)
  • Any correspondence with your HSA/FSA administrator about the claim

The IRS can audit HSA/FSA expenses, and undocumented claims may be treated as taxable distributions with penalties. A few minutes of recordkeeping now can save you significant headaches later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Hims accept HSA cards directly?

Yes, Hims allows you to enter an HSA debit card as your payment method. However, whether the charge is approved depends on your HSA administrator's rules and the merchant category code.

Can I use my FSA for Hims compounded semaglutide?

Compounded medications prescribed for a diagnosed medical condition are generally FSA-eligible. You may need a Letter of Medical Necessity from your provider to confirm the medical purpose.

Will my HSA/FSA cover the full cost of a Hims subscription?

If you have sufficient funds in your account and the expense qualifies, yes. Keep in mind that some plan administrators may only cover the medication portion and not bundled services like app access.

What if my employer's FSA does not cover telehealth?

Some FSA plans have specific exclusions for telehealth. Check your Summary Plan Description or contact your benefits administrator. If telehealth is excluded, you may still be able to claim the medication cost separately.

Is weight loss medication considered preventive care for HSA purposes?

As of 2026, GLP-1 medications for weight loss are not classified as preventive care under most HSA guidelines. They are considered treatment for a diagnosed condition, which means they are eligible but count against your deductible.
