Medication Details

Both platforms prescribe compounded semaglutide, which is prepared by licensed compounding pharmacies. This is not brand-name Ozempic or Wegovy, but it contains the same active ingredient.

Hims has a well-documented titration process. Patients start at a low dose and gradually increase under provider guidance. BreezeMeds follows a similar protocol but may offer slightly different starting doses or titration timelines depending on the prescribing provider.

If you have a preference for tirzepatide (the active ingredient in Mounjaro), check with each platform. Hims has explored tirzepatide options, while BreezeMeds availability varies.

Provider Quality and Clinical Support

Hims employs a large network of licensed healthcare providers across the U.S. Consultations are typically asynchronous. You fill out a detailed health questionnaire, upload any required information, and a provider reviews your case. Follow-up messaging is available through the app, and some patients can request video calls.

BreezeMeds works with a smaller group of weight-loss-focused providers. The advantage is that these providers are well-versed in GLP-1 protocols. The downside is that during high-demand periods, response times can be slower. If responsive clinical support is a priority, ask about expected turnaround times before enrolling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are compounded GLP-1 medications safe?

When prescribed by a licensed provider and prepared by an accredited compounding pharmacy, compounded semaglutide is generally considered safe. However, compounded medications do not undergo the same FDA approval process as brand-name drugs.

How long does it take to get medication after signing up?

Hims typically ships within a few days of provider approval. BreezeMeds processing times vary, but most patients receive medication within one to two weeks of enrollment.

Can I use my HSA or FSA to pay?

Many patients successfully use HSA or FSA funds for telehealth GLP-1 programs. Check with your benefits administrator to confirm eligibility.

What happens if I experience side effects?

Both platforms provide access to your prescribing provider. If you experience side effects like nausea, constipation, or fatigue, contact your provider through the platform's messaging system. Dose adjustments are common and usually resolve most side effects.

Do I need a BMI of 30 or higher to qualify?

Most GLP-1 telehealth programs require a BMI of 27 or higher with a weight-related health condition, or a BMI of 30 or higher without one. Eligibility criteria can vary by provider.
