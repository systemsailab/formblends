The Regulatory Landscape for Compounded Tirzepatide in 2026

The FDA's stance on compounded tirzepatide has been evolving. During periods of brand-name shortage, compounding pharmacies were permitted to produce tirzepatide under federal shortage provisions {{CITE:FDA compounded tirzepatide shortage provisions}}. As supply normalizes, the regulatory pathway may shift. It is important to work with a provider that stays current on these changes and can pivot your care plan if needed.

Regardless of regulatory changes, patients should always verify that their provider sources from FDA-registered 503B facilities and can produce certificates of analysis for each batch. This is the minimum standard you should accept when choosing a compounded tirzepatide provider.

Is compounded tirzepatide as effective as Mounjaro or Zepbound?

When properly compounded at the correct concentration and purity, compounded tirzepatide contains the same active ingredient as the brand-name products. How do I verify that my compounded tirzepatide is safe?

Ask your provider for the name of their compounding pharmacy, confirm it is FDA-registered as a 503B outsourcing facility, and request a certificate of analysis (COA) for your specific batch. A reputable provider will provide this information without hesitation.
