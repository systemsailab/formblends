Complaint #1: Unauthorized Charges and Double Billing

This is the most frequently reported complaint about MEDVi. Patients document being charged for medication refills or subscription renewals they did not authorize. Specific examples from BBB complaints include:

  • A patient charged for a six-month medication bundle (February through August 2026) at $299 without their consent, creating overlapping charges across multiple months
  • Patients reporting double charges for the same monthly billing period
  • Automatic refill charges processed without confirmation from the patient

These are not minor billing glitches. Unauthorized charges of $299 or more have real financial impact, and patients report that reversing these charges through MEDVi's customer support can be slow and frustrating.

What to do: Use a credit card (not a debit card) when enrolling with MEDVi. Credit cards offer chargeback protections that debit cards do not. Monitor your statements monthly and flag any unexpected charges immediately, both with MEDVi and your card issuer.

Complaint #2: Refund Policy Disputes

MEDVi markets a "results-based guarantee" for patients who do not lose weight on the program. The actual policy has several restrictions that catch patients off guard:

  • 5-month minimum: You must follow the program for at least five months before you can claim the guarantee
  • 25% deduction: Approved refunds are reduced by a 25% consultation fee
  • No refunds after prescription: Once a prescription has been issued, standard (non-guarantee) refunds are generally not available
  • Narrow qualification criteria: One BBB complaint describes a refund denial based on a 1-pound weight fluctuation

The most concerning complaints allege that MEDVi changed its guarantee terms from 3 months to 5 months after patients had already enrolled under the original terms. If accurate, this represents a significant change to the terms patients agreed to at sign-up.

What to do: Screenshot MEDVi's refund policy and guarantee terms at the time you enroll. Save any emails or portal messages that reference guarantee terms. This documentation protects you if terms change later.

Complaint #3: Customer Support Delays

Patients who encounter billing issues or need to discuss their treatment report slow response times from MEDVi's support team. Common themes include:

  • Multi-day waits for responses to billing inquiries
  • Difficulty reaching a live person for urgent issues
  • Provider messaging limited to business hours only
  • Generic or unhelpful initial responses that require follow-up

Slow customer support is common across telehealth platforms, but it becomes especially frustrating when combined with billing errors that require urgent resolution.

What to do: When contacting MEDVi support about a billing issue, include your account details, the specific charge in question, and the date it appeared. Written communication (email or portal messaging) creates a paper trail that is helpful if you need to escalate to your credit card company.

Complaint #4: Medication Delivery Delays

Some patients report waiting longer than expected for their medication to arrive after the prescription is issued. Delays are typically a few days beyond the expected window, though some patients report waiting a week or more during periods of high demand.

This is not unique to MEDVi. Compounding pharmacies across the industry have faced capacity constraints as demand for GLP-1 medications has surged. However, a delay in medication delivery while being charged the full monthly price understandably frustrates patients.

What to do: Ask MEDVi's support team about expected delivery timelines before your renewal date. If a delay occurs, document the dates and communicate with support to ensure you are not charged for a period during which you did not have medication.

Complaint #5: Incomplete Medication Vials

A smaller number of complaints describe receiving medication vials that appear to be less than the full prescribed amount while still being billed the full price. This is a more serious concern because it directly affects treatment effectiveness and dosing accuracy.

What to do: If you receive a vial that appears underfilled, document it with photos and contact MEDVi support immediately. Report the issue to your prescribing physician as well, since dosing accuracy is a clinical concern, not just a billing one.

Context: How MEDVi Complaints Compare

It is important to put MEDVi's complaints in context. Most telehealth companies that operate on subscription models accumulate billing-related complaints as they scale. Hims, Ro, Noom, and other major platforms all have BBB complaints and negative reviews citing billing issues and cancellation difficulties.

What stands out about MEDVi's complaint profile is the refund policy disputes. The allegations of changing guarantee terms after enrollment and the restrictive qualification criteria are more specific to MEDVi and represent a higher-severity concern than typical billing errors.

The clinical complaints (medication not working, provider quality issues) are relatively rare in MEDVi's review profile. The vast majority of negative feedback is operational, not clinical.

MEDVi can work well for patients who are vigilant about monitoring billing statements and proactive about documenting their enrollment terms. The clinical product is strong, but you should approach the business relationship with the same diligence you would bring to any subscription service with documented billing issues.
