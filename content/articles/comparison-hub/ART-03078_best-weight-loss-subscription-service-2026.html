#1. Calibrate : $139/month (billed annually)

Calibrate is the most fully built-out weight loss subscription we tested. For $1,650/year, you get physician-led GLP-1 prescribing (brand-name, through insurance), one-on-one coaching with a registered dietitian, and a structured program covering food, exercise, sleep, and emotional health.

This is not a subscription where you just get a refill shipped every month. Calibrate actively manages your care, checks in regularly, and adjusts your approach over a full year. The clinical results back it up: Calibrate reports average weight loss of 15% of body weight in their patient population.

Pros:

  • Most comprehensive subscription on this list
  • One-on-one coaching with RDs
  • Insurance navigation for brand-name GLP-1s
  • Structured year-long program

Cons:

  • $1,650 annual commitment is steep
  • Medication cost is separate (depends on insurance)
  • No compounded medication option

#2. Ro Body : $149 to $249/month

Ro Body runs a medical-first subscription. You pay monthly for your medication (compounded semaglutide or tirzepatide), physician oversight, lab monitoring, and pharmacy fulfillment through Ro's in-house pharmacy. There is no coaching layer, but the medical care is thorough.

What makes Ro's subscription model work is simplicity. One monthly payment covers everything. No surprise fees, no separate consult charges, and the in-house pharmacy means your medication arrives on a predictable schedule.

Pros:

  • All-inclusive monthly pricing
  • In-house pharmacy with reliable fulfillment
  • Lab-based physician oversight
  • Easy to understand and manage

Cons:

  • Price increases with dose
  • No coaching or lifestyle programming
  • State restrictions apply

#4. Noom : $59 to $199/month

The coaching is Noom's strength. Their curriculum is rooted in cognitive behavioral therapy, and the daily lessons are genuinely educational. The medication component is newer and less mature than dedicated GLP-1 programs, but it is improving.

Pros:

  • Excellent behavioral coaching curriculum
  • Flexible tiers (coaching-only or coaching plus medication)
  • Established brand with a large user community

Cons:

  • Medical care is less robust than dedicated clinics
  • App can be notification-heavy

The unique value here is medication flexibility. If semaglutide does not work for you, Found's physicians can pivot to alternatives without you switching platforms.

Pros:

  • Lower-cost non-GLP-1 options available

Cons:

  • App quality is uneven
  • Provider responsiveness varies

For patients who know what they want and just need affordable, reliable access to compounded GLP-1s, this is the most economical subscription available.

The community aspect of WeightWatchers remains genuinely helpful for accountability. The clinical component is still maturing, but the combination of community support and medical access is unique.

FAQ

Can I cancel weight loss subscriptions anytime?

Most programs allow monthly cancellation, but some (particularly Calibrate) require an annual commitment. Always check the cancellation policy before signing up. Programs that require contracts should offer enough value to justify the commitment.

Do weight loss subscriptions cover the cost of medication?

It depends. Compounded medication subscriptions (Henry Meds, Form Blends, Ro Body) typically include medication in the monthly price. Programs like Calibrate and Ww Clinic charge a platform/program fee, and medication costs are separate (usually through insurance).

Is a subscription better than just seeing my doctor?

If your primary care physician is experienced with GLP-1 prescribing and your insurance covers the medication, seeing your own doctor may be the best and cheapest option. Subscriptions are most valuable when you lack insurance coverage, want specialized expertise in weight loss medicine, or need coaching and support that a busy PCP cannot provide.

What should I look for in a weight loss subscription?

Prioritize medical quality over price. The cheapest subscription is not the best one if the medical oversight is inadequate. Look for physician-led care (not just nurse practitioner sign-offs), transparent pricing, reputable pharmacy sourcing, and responsive support when you have questions.
