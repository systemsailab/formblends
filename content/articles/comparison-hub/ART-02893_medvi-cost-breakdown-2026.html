What the Monthly Price Includes

MEDVi bundles everything into one monthly payment. Here is what you get for that price:

  • Physician evaluation: Initial and ongoing consultations with a board-certified physician
  • Treatment plan: Personalized dosing protocol based on your labs and medical history
  • Metabolic health report: Comprehensive lab panels to track metabolic markers
  • Medication supply: One month of compounded semaglutide or tirzepatide
  • Injection supplies: Syringes, needles, and alcohol swabs
  • Shipping: Free standard shipping to your door
  • Unlimited messaging: Asynchronous provider messaging (business hours)

There is no separate consultation fee, enrollment fee, or platform fee. The all-in-one pricing simplifies budgeting, though the total monthly cost is higher than platforms that charge separately for some of these services.

The First-Month Discount

MEDVi leads its marketing with the $179 first-month price for semaglutide. This is a real price, not bait-and-switch, but it is an introductory rate. The standard ongoing price is $299/month. If you are evaluating MEDVi against competitors, compare using the $299 number, not $179, since that is what you will pay for the majority of your treatment.

The $120 jump from month one to month two is not unusual in telehealth. Many competitors offer discounted first months to reduce the barrier to trying the service. Just make sure you are budgeting for the long-term cost, not the promotional price.

Semaglutide vs. Tirzepatide: Cost Comparison

Tirzepatide is the more expensive option at MEDVi, which tracks with industry-wide pricing trends. Tirzepatide (the active ingredient in Mounjaro and Zepbound) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist that some clinical data suggests may produce greater weight loss than semaglutide alone.

At MEDVi, the price difference between semaglutide and tirzepatide is approximately $50 to $80 per month depending on your dose. Over a year, that adds up to $600 to $960 more for tirzepatide. Whether the additional cost is justified depends on your physician's recommendation and your response to treatment.

Insurance and Payment Options

MEDVi is entirely cash-pay. The platform does not accept health insurance for consultations or medications. This is standard for compounded GLP-1 telehealth services, since insurance typically covers only FDA-approved brand-name medications.

MEDVi does accept HSA and FSA cards, but coverage through these accounts depends on your specific plan. Some HSA/FSA plans cover telehealth consultations and compounded medications; others do not. Check with your plan administrator before assuming your HSA/FSA will cover the cost.

There is no financing or payment plan option published on MEDVi's site. You pay the full monthly amount at the time of billing.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

MEDVi's published pricing does not include hidden fees. However, there are costs that can catch patients off guard:

  • Dose increases: As your physician titrates your dose upward, the monthly cost may increase, particularly for tirzepatide
  • Refund deductions: If you qualify for a refund under the results-based guarantee, 25% is deducted as a consultation fee
  • Cancellation timing: If you cancel after your renewal date, you may be charged for the next month plus a telehealth fee
  • Unauthorized charges: BBB complaints document cases of patients being charged for refills or bundles they did not authorize. While this is a billing error rather than a published fee, it is a financial risk to be aware of
