How ShedRx Works

The ShedRx process follows the now-familiar telehealth weight loss model:

  • Complete an online intake form with your medical history, current medications, and weight loss goals
  • A licensed provider reviews your information and conducts a virtual consultation
  • If approved, a prescription is sent to a partnered compounding pharmacy
  • Medication is shipped to your home, usually within 7 to 14 days
  • Monthly check-ins and prescription renewals continue on a subscription basis

This model will feel familiar if you have looked at platforms like Ro, Found, Calibrate, or Form Blends. The differences are in the details: pricing, provider quality, medication sourcing, and the depth of support you receive. telehealth weight loss comparison

What Medications Does ShedRx Offer?

ShedRx offers compounded versions of the two most popular GLP-1 medications:

  • Compounded semaglutide, the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy
  • Compounded tirzepatide, the active ingredient in Mounjaro and Zepbound

Both are administered as subcutaneous injections, typically once per week. The compounded versions are made by licensed 503A or 503B pharmacies and contain the same active ingredients as their brand-name counterparts, though they are not FDA-approved as individual products.

Provider Quality and Medical Oversight

ShedRx states that all prescriptions are written by board-certified physicians or licensed nurse practitioners. The initial consultation involves reviewing your medical history, discussing your weight loss goals, and assessing whether GLP-1 therapy is appropriate for you.

Where ShedRx gets mixed marks is in the ongoing supervision department. Some patients report that follow-up visits are thorough and helpful. Others describe them as quick, formulaic check-ins that do not leave much room for personalized care. The quality of your experience may depend on which provider you are assigned.

Patient Results

Patients on ShedRx report weight loss consistent with what clinical trials show for GLP-1 medications generally. Those who stay consistent with weekly injections and make dietary changes tend to see the best results. Common outcomes reported by patients include:

  • 5 to 15 percent body weight loss in the first three to six months
  • Reduced appetite and fewer cravings, especially for high-calorie foods
  • Improved energy levels as weight decreases
  • Better blood sugar control for patients with prediabetes or type 2 diabetes

It is worth noting that these results reflect the medication's effectiveness, not the platform's specifically. You would likely see similar outcomes on any program that prescribes the same medications. The platform's value lies in how well it supports you throughout the process.
