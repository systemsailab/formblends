If you want a primary care relationship that happens to include GLP-1 therapy, Eden Health is the more complete medical home. Pricing Breakdown

Direct pricing comparison is difficult because these platforms use entirely different models. MEDVi charges a monthly cash-pay fee ($249-$399) that covers consultations, lab work, and compounded medication. The price is transparent and predictable.

Eden Health often operates through employer-sponsored health benefits, meaning many patients access it at little or no direct cost for primary care. For GLP-1 medications specifically, Eden Health providers typically prescribe brand-name drugs and work through the patient's insurance. Your out-of-pocket cost depends entirely on your insurance coverage for medications like Wegovy or Zepbound.

If your employer offers Eden Health and your insurance covers GLP-1 medications, Eden is likely the cheaper path. If you are paying out of pocket with no employer subsidy, Eden Health's individual pricing for primary care plus brand-name medication costs could easily exceed MEDVi's bundled pricing for compounded GLP-1s.

Medications Available

The medication approach is fundamentally different. MEDVi prescribes compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide, sourced from compounding pharmacies. This means cash-pay, no insurance needed, but you are getting compounded formulations rather than brand-name products.

Eden Health operates within the traditional healthcare system. Providers prescribe brand-name medications (Wegovy, Ozempic, Zepbound, Mounjaro) and route prescriptions through your insurance pharmacy benefits. When insurance covers these medications, you get FDA-approved brand-name products at your copay rate. When insurance does not cover them, you face the full retail price, which can run $1,000+ per month.

Eden Health does not typically prescribe compounded GLP-1s, which means it lacks the affordable cash-pay fallback that MEDVi and other telehealth-first platforms offer.

Patient Experience

Eden Health offers a broader healthcare experience. You can see your Eden provider for GLP-1 management, but also for annual physicals, acute illness, mental health referrals, and chronic condition management. In markets where Eden has physical clinics, you can see providers in person. This continuity of care is genuinely valuable and something standalone telehealth weight loss platforms cannot replicate.

MEDVi offers a more focused experience. The entire platform is built around weight loss and metabolic health. The intake process, follow-up cadence, and educational content are all geared toward GLP-1 therapy outcomes. You will not get primary care from MEDVi, but you will get a team that thinks about weight management all day.

For patients who already have a primary care doctor, MEDVi's specialization is an asset. For patients who want one provider relationship covering everything, Eden Health's integrated model is more convenient.

Provider Quality and Support

Both platforms use board-certified physicians. Eden Health also employs nurse practitioners within its care teams. The quality of clinical care is comparable, but the context differs significantly.

Eden Health providers see you for all your health needs, so they have a fuller picture of your medical history. This can be valuable when prescribing GLP-1 medications, especially for patients taking other medications or managing conditions like Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or thyroid disorders.

MEDVi providers specialize in metabolic health and weight management. They may have deeper expertise in dose titration schedules, managing GLP-1 side effects, and optimizing weight loss outcomes specifically, but they do not see the rest of your health picture unless you share it proactively.

Choose MEDVi if you want a dedicated weight loss program, prefer cash-pay simplicity, and do not need your GLP-1 provider to also handle your general medical care. MEDVi is the right fit for people who have a primary care doctor already and want a specialized addition for weight management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Eden Health for GLP-1 therapy without employer sponsorship?

Eden Health's availability outside employer partnerships is limited. In some markets you can enroll individually, but the platform is primarily designed for employer-sponsored access. Check Eden Health's website for current individual enrollment options in your area.

Does MEDVi accept insurance for anything?

No. MEDVi is entirely cash-pay. Consultations, labs, and medications are all included in the monthly fee without insurance involvement. Some patients use HSA or FSA funds.

Which platform is better for patients with Type 2 diabetes?

Eden Health has an advantage here because its primary care model allows providers to manage diabetes comprehensively, including GLP-1 therapy as part of a broader treatment plan. MEDVi can prescribe GLP-1s to diabetic patients but does not manage diabetes as a whole condition.

Can I get tirzepatide through Eden Health?

Eden Health providers can prescribe brand-name Zepbound (tirzepatide) if your insurance covers it. Without insurance coverage, the out-of-pocket cost for brand-name tirzepatide is prohibitively expensive for most patients. Eden does not typically offer compounded alternatives.
