The Medication Matching Advantage

Found's biggest selling point is its medication matching system. Rather than defaulting everyone to the same drug, Found's physicians consider your metabolic profile, health history, and treatment goals before recommending a specific medication.

This matters because GLP-1 medications are not the only effective option for weight loss. Some patients respond better to medications that target different pathways. For example, bupropion/naltrexone (Contrave) works on the reward center of the brain and may be better suited for patients who struggle with cravings. Metformin improves insulin sensitivity and can be effective for patients with insulin resistance.

Hims offers one medication, and it is a good one. Semaglutide is among the most effective weight loss drugs available. But if it causes intolerable side effects or does not produce the results you expect, you are somewhat stuck within the platform. Found can pivot to a different medication without you needing to switch providers.

Coaching and Support

Found includes a health coach who works with you on goal setting, habit formation, and overcoming barriers to weight loss. The coaching is delivered through the app and is designed to complement the medical treatment. Some patients find this support invaluable, especially during the early weeks when side effects are most common and motivation can waver.

Hims does not offer coaching. If you need support with nutrition, exercise, or behavioral change, you will need to source that independently. For patients who are already working with other health professionals or who prefer a self-directed approach, this is not a drawback.

Provider Quality

Found emphasizes that its medical team includes board-certified obesity medicine specialists. This is a meaningful credential. Obesity medicine is a subspecialty with specific training in the medical management of weight, including pharmacotherapy.

Hims uses licensed healthcare providers across a broad network. These providers are qualified to prescribe medication, but they may not have specialized training in obesity medicine. For straightforward cases, this difference may not matter much. For patients with complex medical histories or multiple failed weight loss attempts, the specialized expertise at Found could lead to better outcomes.

Who Should Choose Hims?

Hims is the right fit if you specifically want compounded semaglutide, prefer a simple and affordable program, and do not need coaching or medication matching. It is a reliable, no-frills option for patients who know what they want.

Who Should Choose Found?

Found is the better choice if you want a personalized medication plan, value coaching and community support, or have a complex weight loss history that might benefit from obesity medicine expertise. If you are open to medications beyond GLP-1s, Found's flexibility is a real advantage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if Found does not prescribe me a GLP-1?

Found's physicians prescribe the medication they believe is most appropriate for your biology and goals. If that is not a GLP-1, they will explain why and offer an alternative. You can discuss your preferences with your provider.

Can I request semaglutide specifically from Found?

You can express a preference, but the prescribing decision is ultimately up to the physician. Found's model is built around clinical judgment rather than patient self-selection.

Does Hims offer any medication besides semaglutide for weight loss?

Hims' weight loss program is primarily focused on compounded semaglutide. Their broader platform offers other medications for different health concerns, but the weight loss offering is less varied than Found's.

How does Found's community support work?

Found offers an online community where patients can share experiences, tips, and encouragement. Access is included with the program. Some patients find it helpful; others do not engage with it much.

Which program has better long-term outcomes?

There is no published head-to-head comparison. In general, programs that match medication to the individual and include behavioral support tend to produce more sustainable results.
