MEDVi vs GoodRx: GLP-1 Program Comparison 2026

Bottom line: MEDVi and GoodRx serve fundamentally different purposes. GoodRx is primarily a prescription savings platform that helps you find the lowest price on GLP-1 medications you already have a prescription for. MEDVi is a telehealth program that prescribes, manages, and ships compounded GLP-1 medications directly. If you already have a GLP-1 prescription from your own doctor and want to save on brand-name medication costs, GoodRx is the tool for that. If you need a provider to prescribe compounded GLP-1s and manage your treatment, MEDVi is the platform for that. They are not direct competitors, but patients frequently compare them.

Pricing Breakdown

These platforms have entirely different pricing structures because they do entirely different things.

MEDVi charges $249-$399/month for an all-in package: physician consultation, metabolic lab work, and compounded GLP-1 medication shipped to your door. The price is predictable and covers everything.

GoodRx's core service (prescription coupons and price comparison) is free. GoodRx Gold membership costs $9.99/month and provides deeper discounts at retail pharmacies. For GLP-1 medications specifically, GoodRx can show you prices across pharmacies and provide coupons that reduce the cost of brand-name semaglutide or tirzepatide. However, brand-name GLP-1 retail prices remain high (often $800-$1,300/month even with coupons) unless your insurance covers a significant portion.

GoodRx also offers GoodRx Care, a telehealth service where providers can prescribe medications. GoodRx Care consultations for weight management typically cost $49-$99, separate from any medication costs.

The math: If your insurance covers brand-name GLP-1s and you use GoodRx to find the best copay price, GoodRx is dramatically cheaper. If you are paying cash for everything, MEDVi's compounded medications at $249-$399/month are almost certainly cheaper than brand-name GLP-1s at retail prices, even with GoodRx coupons.

Medications Available

MEDVi prescribes compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide. These are formulations produced by compounding pharmacies, not the brand-name products from Novo Nordisk or Eli Lilly.

GoodRx helps you access brand-name medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound) at the best available price. GoodRx does not manufacture or compound medications. It simply aggregates pricing from retail pharmacies and provides coupons. Through GoodRx Care telehealth, providers can write prescriptions, but you fill them at a retail pharmacy at market prices (with GoodRx discount where applicable).

Some GoodRx partnerships also provide access to compounded GLP-1 options, blurring the line somewhat. But GoodRx's primary value proposition remains helping you save on brand-name drugs.

If brand-name medication matters to you (and your insurance or budget supports it), GoodRx is the path. If compounded medication at a lower cash price is acceptable, MEDVi is the path.

Patient Experience

GoodRx has a significantly more mature and user-friendly platform. The GoodRx app and website are used by millions of people. Price comparison, coupon generation, pharmacy search, and prescription tracking are all well-executed. GoodRx Care telehealth integrates into this ecosystem, though it is a lighter-weight clinical experience than dedicated weight loss platforms.

MEDVi offers a more focused clinical experience. The intake is thorough, follow-ups are scheduled, and the entire platform is designed around weight management. It is not as polished as GoodRx's consumer-facing tools, but it is more medically substantive.

The experiences are difficult to compare directly because they serve different needs. Using GoodRx is more like using a shopping tool. Using MEDVi is more like enrolling in a medical program.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians for dedicated weight management consultations. GoodRx Care uses licensed providers for telehealth visits across many specialties, including weight loss. The GoodRx Care model is more general-purpose, while MEDVi's providers specialize in metabolic health.

If you use GoodRx purely as a savings tool (bringing your own prescription from your own doctor), provider quality is not a factor because GoodRx is not providing clinical care. If you use GoodRx Care for a telehealth consultation, you are working with providers who handle many conditions, not just weight management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use GoodRx coupons with a MEDVi prescription?

No. MEDVi prescriptions are filled through MEDVi's compounding pharmacy partners and shipped directly to you. GoodRx coupons work at retail pharmacies for brand-name or generic medications. The two systems do not overlap.

Is GoodRx Care a real alternative to MEDVi for GLP-1 prescriptions?

GoodRx Care can provide a telehealth consultation and a GLP-1 prescription, but it is not a weight management program. You would still need to fill the prescription at a retail pharmacy at market price. For most patients, this means paying significantly more for brand-name medication than MEDVi charges for compounded versions.

How much do brand-name GLP-1s cost with GoodRx coupons?

Prices vary by pharmacy and location, but brand-name semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) typically runs $800-$1,300/month even with GoodRx coupons. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound) is in a similar range. These prices make compounded alternatives from platforms like MEDVi attractive for cash-pay patients.

Does GoodRx Gold help with GLP-1 medication costs?

GoodRx Gold can provide additional savings at participating pharmacies, but the discounts on brand-name GLP-1 medications are often modest given the high base price. GoodRx Gold is more impactful for generic medications where deeper percentage discounts make a bigger absolute difference.
