What to Know About Compounded Semaglutide Safety

The safety of compounded semaglutide depends almost entirely on the pharmacy that prepares it. FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities are inspected by the FDA and must follow current good manufacturing practices . These facilities perform sterility testing, potency verification, and endotoxin testing on their products.

By contrast, 503A pharmacies (traditional compounding pharmacies) face less regulatory oversight and typically prepare medications on a patient-by-patient basis. While many 503A pharmacies produce quality products, the 503B designation provides an additional layer of regulatory accountability that we recommend patients look for when choosing a provider.

Always ask your provider these questions about their compounded semaglutide:

  • Which pharmacy compounds the medication?
  • Is it a 503A or 503B facility?
  • What testing is performed on each batch?
  • Can they provide certificates of analysis?
  • How is the medication shipped and stored?
