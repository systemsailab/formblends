Form Blends vs Hims: Complete Comparison 2026

Updated March 2026 | Honest comparison, no affiliate bias

This is a David and Goliath comparison, and we should be upfront about that. Hims is a publicly traded company with massive brand recognition, a broad product line spanning hair loss, ED, skincare, mental health, and weight loss. Form Blends is a focused telehealth platform specializing in GLP-1 medications and peptide protocols. Comparing them requires acknowledging what each one is actually built to do.

The Fundamental Difference

Hims is a health and wellness superstore. Weight loss is one of many verticals they operate in. They sell shampoo, ED pills, anxiety medication, skincare products, and GLP-1s all under the same roof. This gives them scale, brand trust, and a polished consumer experience.

Form Blends is a specialist. They do weight loss and peptide therapy. That's it. Their entire clinical team, product offering, and business model is built around getting GLP-1 and peptide treatment right. This focus creates depth that a generalist platform can't easily match.

Neither approach is inherently better. But it matters which one serves your specific needs.

Medications and Treatment Approach

Hims offers compounded semaglutide as the core of their weight loss program. They've also introduced oral weight management options, including compounded oral semaglutide and some traditional appetite suppressants. What they don't offer is tirzepatide or peptide therapies. Their medication menu is relatively straightforward: you get semaglutide in injectable or oral form, potentially with some adjunct supplements.

Form Blends offers both compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide, plus their signature peptide combination protocols. Having tirzepatide available is a real advantage. Tirzepatide (the active ingredient in Mounjaro and Zepbound) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist that some patients respond to better than semaglutide alone. If you try semaglutide and don't get the results you want, Form Blends can switch you to tirzepatide. With Hims, you're more limited in that scenario.

The peptide protocols are Form Blends' most significant differentiation. Hims doesn't offer BPC-157, CJC-1295/Ipamorelin, or any other peptide therapies. If you're interested in a combined approach that addresses muscle preservation, recovery, and gut health alongside weight loss, Form Blends is your only option between the two.

The Hims Advantage: Scale and Convenience

Let's be fair about what Hims does well, because they do several things well.

Their app is excellent. It's polished, intuitive, and makes managing prescriptions, tracking deliveries, and messaging providers easy. Form Blends' digital experience is functional but doesn't match the Hims app for fit and finish.

Hims operates in all 50 states for most of their products, including weight loss. Form Blends covers 40+ states but has some gaps due to specific state compounding pharmacy regulations. If you live in a state where Form Blends isn't available, that makes the decision for you.

The brand recognition also carries a psychological benefit. Hims is a household name at this point. They're NYSE-listed, they sponsor major events, and millions of people use their products. For some patients, that familiarity and perceived stability provides comfort. That's not irrational. Trusting a lesser-known company with your healthcare is a real consideration.

Hims also offers the ability to consolidate multiple health needs on one platform. If you're already using Hims for hair loss or ED treatment, adding weight loss to your account is seamless. Having one provider for multiple health concerns simplifies your life.

Consultation and Provider Quality

This is where the specialist vs. generalist difference gets practical.

Form Blends' providers focus on weight management and peptide therapy. That's their daily work. When you message your Form Blends physician about adjusting your semaglutide dose or adding a peptide for muscle preservation, you're talking to someone who thinks about these protocols all day. They know the interactions, the dose-response curves, and the common patient presentations.

Hims uses a large network of providers who handle consultations across all their verticals. The provider who reviews your weight loss intake might also be reviewing hair loss cases and ED consultations the same day. This doesn't make them incompetent. But it does mean you're less likely to get deep, specialized expertise in GLP-1 and peptide treatment.

Hims' consultation process is also more heavily automated. The intake flow is optimized for speed and throughput. Most patients get a prescription decision within hours, often without a live conversation. Form Blends is typically 24-48 hours for initial review, but more likely to include physician interaction during the process.

The Compounding Question

Both platforms use compounding pharmacies for their GLP-1 medications. This is standard practice in telehealth weight loss. However, the quality and oversight of compounding pharmacies varies. Hims has invested heavily in their own pharmacy operations and vertically integrated much of their supply chain. This gives them more direct control over medication quality.

Form Blends works with vetted 503B compounding pharmacies (the FDA-registered, higher-oversight category). Both approaches produce legitimate medications, but it's worth understanding the difference in supply chain models.

What About Long-Term Treatment?

GLP-1 medications typically require ongoing use to maintain weight loss results. Most patients who stop taking them regain a significant portion of the weight. This makes the long-term relationship with your provider important.

Form Blends' approach to this is to build a comprehensive protocol that evolves over time. As you lose weight, they may adjust your GLP-1 dosing and modify your peptide protocol to focus more on maintenance and muscle optimization. The goal is a treatment plan that changes as your body changes.

Hims treats it more as a subscription. You stay on your medication, get regular refills, and continue with periodic check-ins. It's straightforward and predictable, but less adaptive than a protocol that's actively managed by a specialist physician.

The Bottom Line

Hims is the safe, familiar choice. They have massive scale, a great app, nationwide availability, and the backing of a public company. For patients who want a simple GLP-1 subscription and don't need peptides or a highly personalized protocol, Hims is a reasonable option. You're paying more, but you're getting convenience and brand trust.

Form Blends is the specialist's choice. If you're serious about optimizing your weight loss results (not just the number on the scale, but your body composition, energy, and long-term health), their peptide + GLP-1 combination approach offers something Hims simply doesn't have. You get physician-led care from providers who focus exclusively on this area, more medication options, and lower prices. The tradeoff is a smaller brand with slightly less geographic coverage and a less polished app.

For most people who are specifically seeking weight loss treatment, the specialist beats the generalist. But Hims' convenience and brand power are real advantages that shouldn't be dismissed.
