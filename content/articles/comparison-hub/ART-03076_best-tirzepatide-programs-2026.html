FAQ

Is tirzepatide better than semaglutide for weight loss?

Clinical trials show tirzepatide produces greater average weight loss than semaglutide. The SURMOUNT trials reported average weight loss of 20% to 26% of body weight with tirzepatide, compared to 15% to 17% with semaglutide in the STEP trials. Individual results vary, and some patients respond better to one medication than the other.

Why is compounded tirzepatide more expensive than compounded semaglutide?

Tirzepatide is a more complex molecule to compound, and the active pharmaceutical ingredient costs more. The price gap has narrowed through 2025 and 2026, but tirzepatide still typically costs $50 to $100 more per month than semaglutide in compounded form.

Can I switch from semaglutide to tirzepatide?

Yes, and many patients do. A physician will typically start you at a low tirzepatide dose regardless of your previous semaglutide dose, because the dual GLP-1/GIP mechanism produces a different side effect profile. Any reputable program should manage this transition carefully.

What are the most common side effects of tirzepatide?

Nausea, diarrhea, constipation, and reduced appetite are the most common, particularly during dose escalation. These side effects usually improve within 2 to 4 weeks at each dose level. Slower titration schedules reduce the severity of GI side effects.
