What Is Hims Weight Loss?

Hims (formally Hims & Hers Health) is a publicly traded telehealth company that started in men's health (hair loss, ED) and has expanded into weight loss, mental health, and other categories. The weight loss program offers GLP-1 receptor agonist medications through virtual consultations with licensed providers.

Hims provides access to both compounded GLP-1 injectables (semaglutide and tirzepatide) and oral medication kits that can include metformin, bupropion, topiramate, vitamin B12, and naltrexone. The platform also recently announced plans for a compounded oral semaglutide pill, though this has faced pushback from Novo Nordisk, the manufacturer of brand-name Ozempic and Wegovy.

The company's primary selling point is convenience: a clean sign-up process, transparent pricing pages, a well-built app, and medications shipped to your door.

Medications Available

Compounded Semaglutide (Injectable)

The primary GLP-1 offering. Compounded semaglutide uses the same active ingredient as Ozempic and Wegovy but is prepared by compounding pharmacies. It is not FDA-approved. Hims partners with FDA-regulated 503A compounding pharmacies for production.

Compounded Tirzepatide (Injectable)

Available when supply allows. Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist (same active ingredient as Mounjaro and Zepbound). Availability can fluctuate based on compounding pharmacy capacity and regulatory conditions.

Provider Quality and Consultations

Hims uses a broad provider network that includes MDs, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants. The initial consultation is an asynchronous health questionnaire rather than a live video call for many patients. A provider reviews your responses, medical history, and any relevant lab work before making a prescribing decision.

For straightforward cases, this works efficiently. You get a prescribing decision quickly without scheduling a video appointment. However, some patients report that the process can feel transactional, with limited opportunity to ask questions or discuss treatment options in depth.

Hims offers 24/7 messaging with a care team, which is a significant advantage over platforms like MEDVi that limit provider communication to business hours. Response quality can vary, with some patients noting that initial replies feel templated, though follow-up messages tend to be more personalized.

App and Digital Experience

This is where Hims genuinely excels. The Hims app is one of the best-designed telehealth interfaces available. It handles provider messaging, prescription tracking, shipment notifications, refill management, and treatment history in a single, clean interface.

The web experience is also well-built. Pricing pages are clear, the sign-up flow is streamlined, and information about medications and treatment plans is presented in accessible language. For patients who value a modern digital experience, Hims sets the standard in telehealth.

Subscription and Cancellation

This is the area that generates the most complaints about Hims. Key points:

  • Cancellation deadline: You must cancel at least 48 hours before your next order date
  • No refunds on processed orders: Once an order has been processed and shipped, Hims does not accept returns or offer refunds (medication cannot be returned)
  • Subscription auto-renewal: Plans renew automatically. Some patients report that refills are processed earlier than expected, sometimes a month before the stated refill date
  • Case-by-case refund exceptions: Hims may provide refunds at its sole discretion, but this is not guaranteed

BBB complaints about Hims frequently cite difficulties with cancellation and unauthorized subscription charges. This is a common pain point across subscription-based telehealth, but the volume of complaints suggests it is a persistent issue.

The Novo Nordisk Situation

Hims' move into compounded oral semaglutide has created a public conflict with Novo Nordisk, the pharmaceutical company that manufactures brand-name Ozempic and Wegovy. Novo Nordisk has criticized Hims for offering what it calls an "unapproved, inauthentic, and untested knockoff" and has signaled potential legal action.

For patients, this situation creates uncertainty. If legal or regulatory action limits Hims' ability to sell compounded semaglutide, it could affect product availability and pricing. Who Is Hims Best For?

Hims is best for patients who want a streamlined digital experience, competitive pricing (especially with multi-month plans), and the convenience of a large, well-known telehealth platform. It is a strong choice for tech-comfortable patients who value app quality and 24/7 messaging.

Hims is less ideal for patients who want in-depth physician consultations, prefer month-to-month billing without upfront commitments, or are concerned about the regulatory uncertainty around compounded GLP-1 products.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hims weight loss legitimate?

Yes. Hims & Hers Health is a publicly traded company (NYSE: HIMS) with licensed providers and regulated pharmacy partnerships. It is one of the largest telehealth platforms in the U.S.

Does Hims offer brand-name Ozempic or Wegovy?

Hims has limited availability for branded GLP-1 medications. The primary offerings are compounded versions. If you need brand-name medications with insurance coverage, a traditional provider is likely a better path.

What is the cheapest Hims weight loss option?

Oral weight loss kits start at $69/month with a 10-month prepaid plan. Injectable GLP-1s start at $199/month with a 6-month prepaid plan. Month-to-month rates are higher.

Can I cancel Hims at any time?

You can cancel, but must do so at least 48 hours before your next order date. No refunds are issued for processed orders. Cancelling a prepaid multi-month plan does not typically result in a refund for unused months.

How does Hims compare to MEDVi?

Hims offers lower pricing, a better app, and 24/7 messaging. MEDVi offers physician-led consultations and metabolic lab panels. Hims is better for price and convenience; MEDVi is better for clinical depth. Both have documented billing and subscription complaints.
