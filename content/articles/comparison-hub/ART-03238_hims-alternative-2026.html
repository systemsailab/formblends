Hims is one of the biggest names in telehealth, and their GLP-1 weight loss program benefits from that scale. Reliable shipping, a polished app, and competitive pricing make it an easy first choice for a lot of patients. The platform works.

Why People Look for Hims Alternatives

Hims patients explore other options for reasons that cluster around a few themes:

  • Impersonal clinical experience. The high-volume model means providers are moving through consultations quickly. Patients who need more time, more explanation, or more nuanced care feel underserved.
  • Limited customization. Hims follows standardized protocols that work for the average patient. But GLP-1 therapy is not one-size-fits-all, and patients whose response does not fit the standard model need a provider who will adapt.
  • No peptide therapy options. Patients interested in BPC-157, therapeutic peptides, or combination protocols cannot get them through Hims.
  • Dose-dependent pricing. Starting prices are competitive, but costs can increase as patients titrate to higher doses, making the long-term cost higher than initially expected.
  • Want a physician, not just a provider. There is a difference between a quick telehealth consultation and a physician who actually manages your care. Some patients want the latter and are willing to pay for it.

None of this makes Hims a bad program. It makes it a program built for a specific type of patient, and not every patient fits that mold.

Who Should Consider Switching from Hims

Hims works well for patients who want a straightforward, low-friction GLP-1 experience. But certain situations call for something different:

  • You want a physician who knows your case. If you feel like you are interacting with a different provider every time and nobody has continuity on your treatment, a program with dedicated physician oversight (like Form Blends) will feel very different.
  • Your results have plateaued. Standard protocols produce results for most patients, but when progress stalls, you need a provider who will think critically about your situation and adjust accordingly. High-volume platforms are not built for that.
  • You want peptide therapy. If your goals include recovery support, gut health, or inflammation management alongside weight loss, you need a provider with a broader formulary.
  • Pricing has increased beyond your budget. If Hims' dose-dependent pricing has pushed your monthly costs higher than expected, flat-rate alternatives may offer better long-term value.
  • You want more than a prescription. A prescription is the starting point of GLP-1 therapy, not the whole thing. Patients who want active medical management, not just a recurring prescription, should look at more clinically engaged programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hims a legitimate GLP-1 provider?

Yes. Hims is a publicly traded telehealth company that works with licensed providers and FDA-registered compounding pharmacies. It is a legitimate program. The question is not whether it is legitimate but whether its high-volume model provides the level of care you need.

Can I switch from Hims to another provider mid-treatment?

Yes. Enroll with your new provider before canceling Hims to avoid a gap in medication. Share your dosing history and any relevant records with your new physician so they can continue your treatment appropriately.

Are there any Hims alternatives that offer peptide therapy?

Form Blends is one of the few telehealth platforms that offers both GLP-1 medications and peptide therapies like BPC-157. Most major competitors, including Ro, Henry Meds, and MEDVi, focus exclusively on GLP-1s.

Will I get better results with a different program?

Results depend on medication, dosing, adherence, and lifestyle factors. What a different program can offer is more personalized dosing, closer monitoring, and adjustments when your standard protocol is not producing results. That clinical attention can make a meaningful difference for patients who have plateaued.

Is Hims cheaper than the alternatives?

Hims' starting prices are competitive, but costs can increase at higher doses. Flat-rate programs like Henry Meds ($149/month) and Form Blends ($199/month) may be cheaper at maintenance doses depending on your Hims pricing tier.
