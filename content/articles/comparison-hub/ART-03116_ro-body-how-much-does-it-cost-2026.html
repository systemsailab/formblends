Full Breakdown: Where Your Money Goes

When you pay Ro Body each month, your subscription covers several distinct services bundled into one charge.

Medication

The largest portion of your payment goes toward compounded GLP-1 medication. Ro Body works with licensed 503B compounding pharmacies to produce semaglutide and tirzepatide at a fraction of brand-name cost. This is why Ro Body can charge $145-$199 per month instead of the $1,000+ that brand-name Wegovy or Mounjaro costs at retail.

Clinical Care

Board-certified physicians evaluate your medical history, prescribe treatment, manage dose escalation, and monitor your progress. You communicate with your provider through the Ro platform via secure messaging.

Metabolic Lab Testing

Ro Body includes at-home lab kits that measure markers like blood glucose, HbA1c, and lipid panels. These results give your physician objective data to guide treatment decisions.

Logistics

Medication is shipped in temperature-appropriate packaging directly to your door. Injection supplies (syringes, needles, alcohol swabs) are included.

Costs You Might Not Expect

Ro Body's pricing is more transparent than many competitors, but there are still scenarios where your spending could deviate from the base rate:

  • Switching medications: If your provider moves you from semaglutide to tirzepatide (or the reverse), your plan pricing may adjust. Always ask before agreeing to a change.
  • Treatment duration: GLP-1 treatment often continues for 9-18 months. Budget for the long term, not just the introductory months.
  • Add-on products: Ro sells supplementary wellness products. These are entirely optional and are not included in the base subscription.
  • Re-enrollment after cancellation: If you cancel and later restart, you may be placed on current pricing, which could differ from your original rate.

Is Ro Body Worth the Price?

If you value data-driven care and want objective evidence that your treatment is working (beyond just the number on the scale), Ro Body delivers that.
