Frequently Asked Questions

Do telehealth weight loss programs really work?

Yes. Studies have shown that telehealth-delivered weight management programs produce outcomes comparable to in-person programs {{CITE:telehealth weight loss program efficacy data}}. The key factors are medication effectiveness, physician oversight quality, and patient adherence to the program.

Can I use my insurance with a telehealth weight loss program?

Some programs like Calibrate and Ro offer insurance coordination for brand-name GLP-1 medications. However, the program fee and compounded medications are typically paid out of pocket. Check with your specific insurance provider for current weight loss medication coverage.

How do I choose between these programs?

Start by defining your priorities. If you want robust coaching, look at Calibrate or Found. If price is your top concern, consider Hims & Hers or Henry Meds. If you want physician-led care with access to both GLP-1 and {{INTERNAL_LINK:peptide therapy}}, Form Blends is the best fit.

What happens if I stop taking the medication?

Research shows that weight regain is common when GLP-1 medications are discontinued without a plan {{CITE:weight regain after GLP-1 discontinuation}}. The best programs prepare you for an eventual transition by building sustainable habits alongside medication therapy.

Are these programs safe?

All of the programs on this list use licensed physicians to prescribe medications and supervise care. The medications themselves are FDA-approved (or FDA-regulated in the case of compounded formulations). As with any medical treatment, risks exist and should be discussed with your prescribing physician.
