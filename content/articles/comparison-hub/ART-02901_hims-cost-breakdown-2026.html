Detailed Cost Breakdown

Consultation Fees

Hims often advertises "free" consultations, and in many cases that is accurate. When you sign up for a subscription plan, the initial provider review is typically bundled into the subscription cost. However, if a provider determines that you need a different medication or a follow-up evaluation, additional consultation charges can apply. These are not always clearly communicated upfront.

For the weight-loss program specifically, the consultation is included in the monthly subscription, but the scope of that consultation is limited. You are primarily filling out a questionnaire and receiving an asynchronous review. If you want a live video visit, that may come at an additional cost or may not be available at all depending on your state and provider availability.

Subscription Structure

Hims uses an auto-renewing subscription model. Prices are often shown at the lowest rate, which requires committing to a longer plan (typically 3 to 12 months). Month-to-month pricing is notably higher. For example, finasteride might be advertised at $19/month, but that rate locks you in for a year. The month-to-month price could be closer to $30.

This is standard practice in the telehealth industry, but it is worth understanding before you sign up. Canceling mid-plan can be cumbersome, and some users report being charged for months after attempting to cancel.

GLP-1 Program Costs

The compounded semaglutide program is where the numbers get more interesting. Brand-name Wegovy can run $1,000 to $1,600 per month without insurance. Hims offers its compounded version at roughly $199 to $299 per month, which represents a significant savings.

However, there are important caveats. The compounded product is not identical to brand-name semaglutide. The dosing protocol, the inactive ingredients, and the overall formulation may differ. Additionally, the clinical support included in that $199 to $299 is relatively light compared to what you would get from a dedicated weight-management program.

When you factor in the level of physician oversight, ongoing monitoring, and personalized dose titration that platforms like Form Blends include in their pricing, the per-dollar value can shift. Paying slightly more for a program that includes regular provider check-ins, lab review, and customized protocols can actually save money in the long run by reducing the risk of adverse effects and improving outcomes.

Hidden and Indirect Costs

Several costs are easy to overlook when evaluating Hims:

  • Dose changes: If your provider adjusts your GLP-1 dose, your monthly cost may increase with the new dosage tier.
  • Add-on products: Hims frequently upsells supplements, anti-nausea meds, and other add-ons during the checkout process. These are optional but can add $20 to $50/month.
  • Lab work: Hims does not typically include lab work in its programs. If your provider recommends blood panels, you will need to arrange and pay for those separately.
  • Cancellation friction: While not a "fee" in the traditional sense, difficulty canceling can result in unwanted charges.

Insurance and HSA/FSA

Most Hims products are not covered by insurance. The company does not bill insurance directly. Some patients have had success submitting Hims receipts to their HSA or FSA for reimbursement, but this depends on your specific plan and the product in question. GLP-1 medications are more likely to qualify for HSA/FSA than cosmetic products like hair-loss treatments.

Other telehealth platforms offer compounded semaglutide at similar or lower price points, sometimes with more robust clinical support. The real question is not just "how much does the medication cost?" but "how much does effective, safe treatment cost?"

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Hims accept insurance?

No. Hims does not bill insurance directly. You pay out of pocket, though some products may be eligible for HSA or FSA reimbursement.

Why is Hims GLP-1 so much cheaper than Wegovy?

Hims uses compounded semaglutide, which is produced by compounding pharmacies rather than the brand-name manufacturer (Novo Nordisk). Compounded drugs are legal and regulated, but they are not the same FDA-approved product as Wegovy.

Are there cancellation fees?

Hims does not charge an explicit cancellation fee, but if you signed up for a multi-month plan, you may be charged for the remaining months. The cancellation process has been a common source of complaints.

Does Hims include lab work?

Generally, no. If your provider recommends blood work, you will need to arrange it through your primary care doctor or a lab service and cover the cost yourself.
