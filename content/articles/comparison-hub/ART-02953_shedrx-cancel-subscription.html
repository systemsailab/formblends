Canceling a telehealth subscription should be just as easy as signing up. Unfortunately, that is not always the case. Whether you are stopping treatment, switching providers, or simply taking a break, this guide helps you navigate the ShedRx cancellation process smoothly and protect yourself from unwanted charges.

Before You Cancel: Things to Consider

Before submitting your cancellation, think through a few important questions:

Why are you canceling?

Your reason matters because it affects your next steps:

  • If cost is the issue, ask ShedRx about lower-cost plans or dose adjustments before canceling
  • If side effects are the problem, speak with your provider about managing them or switching medications
  • If you are dissatisfied with service, consider switching to a different platform rather than stopping treatment
  • If you have reached your goals, discuss a maintenance plan with your provider before going off medication entirely

Do you have a transition plan?

If you are on GLP-1 medication and planning to continue treatment through another provider, coordinate the timing so there is no gap in your medication supply. Abrupt stops are medically safe but can lead to appetite rebound and potential weight regain.

What does your subscription agreement say?

Review the terms you agreed to when signing up. Look for:

  • Notice requirements (how far in advance you must cancel)
  • Commitment periods or minimum enrollment terms
  • Early cancellation fees, if any
  • Refund eligibility for unused services or medication

Step-by-Step Cancellation Process

Step 1: Log Into Your ShedRx Account

Navigate to your account settings or subscription management page. Some telehealth platforms have added self-service cancellation options in response to FTC regulations. If ShedRx offers this, it is the fastest route.

Step 2: Contact Support in Writing

If there is no self-service option, send an email to ShedRx's support address. Your message should include:

  • Your full legal name as it appears on the account
  • The email address associated with your account
  • A clear statement: "I am requesting cancellation of my ShedRx subscription effective immediately"
  • A request for written confirmation of the cancellation and the date it takes effect
  • A statement that you do not authorize any further charges to your payment method

Step 3: Save Your Confirmation

When ShedRx confirms your cancellation, save that email or screenshot. This is your proof in case any future charges appear. If you do not receive confirmation within 5 business days, send a follow-up email referencing your original request and include the date and time it was sent.

Step 4: Remove Your Payment Information

If ShedRx's platform allows it, delete your stored credit card or payment method after receiving cancellation confirmation. This prevents any possibility of unauthorized charges going forward.

Step 5: Watch Your Statements

Monitor your credit card or bank statements for at least two to three billing cycles after cancellation. If you see a charge from ShedRx after your confirmed cancellation date, dispute it with your payment provider immediately.

What If ShedRx Will Not Let You Cancel?

If you encounter obstacles, here is how to escalate:

  • Send a certified letter or registered email to create undeniable documentation
  • File a complaint with the Better Business Bureau, which often prompts companies to act
  • Contact your state Attorney General's consumer protection division
  • Dispute ongoing charges with your credit card company, providing your cancellation documentation
  • Report the company to the FTC if they are violating click-to-cancel rules

No legitimate company should make cancellation unreasonably difficult. If ShedRx creates persistent barriers, that tells you something important about their business practices. consumer rights for telehealth subscriptions

What Happens After You Cancel

Your medication supply

Use any remaining medication according to your prescribed schedule. Do not stockpile or share prescription medication. Once your supply runs out, dispose of unused medication properly at a pharmacy take-back location.

Your body's response

After stopping GLP-1 medication, expect the following changes over the next few weeks:

  • Appetite will gradually return to pre-medication levels
  • Cravings may increase, particularly for high-calorie foods
  • Weight regain is possible if lifestyle habits are not maintained
  • Digestion may speed up as the medication's gut-slowing effects wear off

There are no dangerous withdrawal effects from stopping GLP-1 medications. The primary risk is losing the progress you have made.

Your medical records

Under HIPAA, you have the right to request copies of your medical records from ShedRx. This includes consultation notes, lab results, prescription history, and dosing records. If you are switching to a new provider, having these records ensures continuity of care.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will ShedRx charge me a cancellation fee?

Check your specific subscription terms. Some plans may include a commitment period with early termination fees. If no commitment period is specified, you should be able to cancel without penalty at the end of your current billing cycle.

Can I pause my ShedRx subscription instead of canceling?

Some telehealth platforms offer a pause or hold option. Contact ShedRx to ask if this is available for your plan. Pausing can be a good option if you need a temporary break for financial, travel, or medical reasons.

How long does ShedRx take to process a cancellation?

This varies. Some patients report same-day processing, while others describe waiting several days for confirmation. Submit your request well in advance of your next billing date to avoid being charged for another month. A 7 to 10 day buffer is recommended.

Can I get a refund for medication I already paid for?

Refund eligibility depends on whether the medication has already been compounded and shipped. Once medication has been prepared, refunds are typically not available. If medication has not yet shipped, you may have a stronger case for a refund. Review ShedRx's refund policy for specifics.

Should I stop taking my medication before canceling?

Do not stop taking prescribed medication without consulting a healthcare provider. Use your remaining supply as directed. If you are switching to a new provider, coordinate the transition so you do not have a gap in treatment. If you are stopping entirely, your provider can advise on whether tapering is appropriate.
