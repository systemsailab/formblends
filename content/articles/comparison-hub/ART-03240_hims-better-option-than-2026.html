Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hims bad for GLP-1 therapy?

No. Hims is a legitimate, well-run platform that works for many patients. The question is whether it provides the level of care you specifically need. For patients who need minimal clinical interaction and prioritize convenience, Hims is fine. For patients who need personalized medical management, other programs are stronger.

What is the main weakness of Hims' GLP-1 program?

The high-volume clinical model. Hims processes a large number of patients, which means provider interactions tend to be brief and standardized. Patients who need individualized attention or complex dosing adjustments may find the experience insufficient.

Can I get peptide therapy through Hims?

No. As of 2026, Hims does not offer peptide therapies like BPC-157. Patients interested in peptides need to work with a provider that has a broader formulary, such as Form Blends.

Will switching from Hims to another program disrupt my treatment?

Not if you plan the transition properly. Enroll with your new provider and confirm your prescription before canceling Hims. Most patients can switch without any gap in medication.
