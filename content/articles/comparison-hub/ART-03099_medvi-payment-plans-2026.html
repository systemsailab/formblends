Detailed Breakdown of Each Payment Option

Month-to-Month Billing

The default MEDVi plan bills you monthly with no long-term commitment. You can cancel before your next billing cycle without penalty. This is the most flexible option and the right choice if you are unsure about staying on GLP-1 therapy long-term or want to test MEDVi's platform before committing.

The downside is price. Month-to-month billing is MEDVi's most expensive option per month. You also face the possibility of price increases between billing cycles, since there is no rate protection on a rolling monthly plan.

3-Month Prepaid Plan

MEDVi offers a 3-month commitment plan that reduces your per-month cost. The exact discount varies, but patients typically report savings of 5% to 10% compared to month-to-month pricing. You pay for three months upfront (or in three scheduled payments, depending on current terms), and your rate is fixed for that period.

This is a reasonable middle ground. Three months covers the initial titration phase, so you know what you are getting before you commit further. If you decide MEDVi is not right after three months, you have not locked yourself into a longer agreement.

6-Month Prepaid Plan

The 6-month plan offers MEDVi's deepest discount, typically 10% to 15% below month-to-month rates. For a semaglutide patient, this could mean saving $30 to $50 per month, adding up to $180 to $300 over the half-year commitment.

The risk is obvious: you are locked in for six months. If you experience side effects, want to switch providers, or your financial situation changes, exiting a 6-month commitment may involve forfeiting prepaid amounts. Read the cancellation terms before choosing this option.

Accepted Payment Methods

MEDVi accepts major credit cards, debit cards, and HSA/FSA cards. Some patients report success using services like CareCredit or other medical financing to cover MEDVi payments, though MEDVi does not offer in-house financing.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

MEDVi does not accept health insurance. All plans are cash-pay. However, your MEDVi payments may be reimbursable through your HSA or FSA, since the treatment involves a physician-prescribed medication for a medical condition.

Paying directly with an HSA or FSA debit card is the simplest approach. If MEDVi's payment system accepts your HSA/FSA card, the transaction processes like any other debit payment. If not, pay with a regular card and submit receipts for reimbursement.

The tax savings from using HSA/FSA are significant. At a combined federal and state tax rate of 30%, every $100 you pay through your HSA/FSA costs you only $70 in real terms. Over a 6-month plan, that could mean $600 to $1,000 in effective savings.

How to Save on MEDVi Payments

What to Watch Out For

Before selecting a MEDVi payment plan, clarify these points:

  • Cancellation terms: What happens if you need to leave a multi-month plan early? Is there a cancellation fee? Do you lose prepaid amounts?
  • Price escalation: Do multi-month plans lock your price at the starting dose, or does the monthly rate still increase as your dose goes up?
  • Refund policy: If you are medically unable to continue (adverse reaction, pregnancy, etc.), can you get a prorated refund?
  • Auto-renewal: Does the multi-month plan auto-renew at the end of the commitment period? At the same rate or at the current rate?

FAQ

Does MEDVi offer a free trial?

No. MEDVi does not offer a free trial period. Your first payment covers the initial consultation and first month of medication. If you are not approved for treatment, you should not be charged for medication, but verify this with MEDVi before enrolling.

Can I switch payment plans mid-treatment?

In most cases, you can switch from month-to-month to a multi-month plan. Switching from a multi-month plan to month-to-month mid-commitment may involve early termination terms. Ask MEDVi directly about their policy.

Does MEDVi accept CareCredit?

MEDVi does not currently offer CareCredit as a built-in payment option. However, you can use a CareCredit card like any credit card to pay your MEDVi bill, giving you access to CareCredit's promotional financing rates.

What is the cheapest way to pay for MEDVi?

The cheapest approach combines a 6-month prepaid plan with HSA/FSA funds. The multi-month discount lowers the sticker price, and the pre-tax payment reduces the real cost further. That said, if total cost is your priority, comparing other providers before committing to MEDVi may save you more than any payment plan discount.

Are MEDVi payment plans interest-free?

MEDVi's prepaid plans are not financing products, so there is no interest. You pay upfront (or in scheduled installments) and receive your treatment. If you use a credit card and carry a balance, you will pay your card's interest rate, not MEDVi's.
