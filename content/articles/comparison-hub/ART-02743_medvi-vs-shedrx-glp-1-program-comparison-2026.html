Pricing Breakdown

Pricing overlaps but with different ranges. MEDVi runs $249-$399/month. ShedRx has a wider range, starting as low as $199 for basic compounded semaglutide and going up to $449 for premium combination protocols that include additional metabolic compounds.

At the low end, ShedRx is cheaper for patients who want basic compounded semaglutide without extras. At the high end, ShedRx's combination protocols can cost more than MEDVi's most expensive plan. The question is whether the combination ingredients justify the premium.

MEDVi's pricing includes comprehensive metabolic lab work, which adds value that ShedRx's plans do not match. ShedRx invests that portion of its budget into the medication formulations themselves rather than diagnostics.

Medications Available

This is where ShedRx stands out. Beyond standard compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide, ShedRx offers combination protocols that blend GLP-1 peptides with supporting compounds. Common additions include vitamin B12 (for energy and metabolism), levocarnitine (for fat metabolism), and in some cases, other amino acids or peptides.

The rationale behind combination protocols is that supporting compounds can reduce side effects, improve energy levels during calorie restriction, and enhance fat oxidation. Some patients report better tolerability and results with combination formulations. Others find no meaningful difference compared to standalone GLP-1 medication.

ShedRx also offers sublingual (under-the-tongue) formulations in some plans, which appeals to patients who want to avoid injections. MEDVi sticks to injectable compounded GLP-1s, which generally have more consistent absorption.

MEDVi offers a cleaner, more traditional approach: compounded GLP-1 medication prescribed within a structured, lab-monitored protocol. There is value in simplicity, especially when the evidence base for many combination additives is still developing.

Patient Experience

Both platforms use basic patient portals without the polish of larger telehealth companies. The onboarding experience differs in focus. MEDVi's intake is clinically thorough, with detailed health history review and lab orders. ShedRx's intake is more product-focused, guiding patients through medication options and combination protocol choices.

ShedRx gives patients more agency in selecting their treatment plan, which some find empowering and others find overwhelming. MEDVi's approach is more physician-directed: the doctor reviews your health data and recommends a protocol, which feels more like traditional medical care.

For follow-up care, MEDVi schedules proactive check-ins. ShedRx operates more on a patient-initiated model, with messaging available for questions and concerns.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi uses board-certified physicians. ShedRx uses a mix of licensed providers. Both are appropriate for GLP-1 prescribing.

Where provider quality matters more than usual is with combination protocols. Mixing multiple compounds in a single injection or treatment plan requires informed clinical judgment. ShedRx's providers are trained on their specific protocols, but patients should ask questions about the evidence behind any combination they are offered. If a provider cannot explain why a specific additive is included or what evidence supports it, that is a red flag regardless of platform.

MEDVi providers may be less familiar with combination approaches but bring deeper metabolic health knowledge and the backing of lab data to support clinical decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are ShedRx combination protocols safe?

The individual ingredients (B12, levocarnitine, etc.) are well-established and generally safe. The combination with GLP-1 peptides is less studied in rigorous clinical trials. ShedRx's protocols are prescribed by licensed providers and dispensed by licensed pharmacies, but patients should understand that combination formulations do not have the same evidence base as standalone GLP-1 medications.

Do sublingual GLP-1 formulations work as well as injections?

The evidence suggests injectable GLP-1 formulations have more reliable bioavailability (absorption into the bloodstream). Sublingual formulations are convenient and avoid needles, but absorption can be more variable. If maximizing medication effectiveness is your priority, injections are the more proven route.

Does MEDVi offer any add-on treatments?

MEDVi focuses on compounded GLP-1 medication within a metabolic health framework. It does not offer combination protocols or supplementary compounds in the way ShedRx does. MEDVi providers may recommend supplements or lifestyle changes but do not compound them into the medication itself.

Can I start with ShedRx's basic plan and upgrade later?

Yes. ShedRx allows patients to start with a standard compounded semaglutide plan and upgrade to combination protocols during subsequent consultations. This lets you try the base medication first and add compounds only if you feel you need additional support.
