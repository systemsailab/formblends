What Is SkinnyRx?

SkinnyRx is a telehealth weight loss company that prescribes compounded semaglutide and tirzepatide. The entire process happens online: you answer a health questionnaire, a provider reviews it, and if approved, medication ships to your door. No brand-name GLP-1 drugs are available. How It Works

SkinnyRx keeps things minimal:

  1. Complete an online health intake (5 to 10 minutes).
  2. A provider reviews your information, usually within 24 hours.
  3. If approved, a compounded GLP-1 prescription is sent to a pharmacy.
  4. Medication ships to you within a few business days.
  5. Monthly check-ins are included, though they tend to be brief.

The speed is a selling point. Many patients go from signup to receiving medication within a week. But that speed comes at a cost: the provider evaluation is not as thorough as what you get from more clinically focused platforms. In many cases, the "consultation" is an asynchronous review of your questionnaire rather than a live conversation.

What They Do Well

Price. This is SkinnyRx's strongest card. For patients who are primarily cost-driven, the monthly rate is hard to beat. The all-inclusive pricing with no enrollment fee or hidden charges is straightforward.

Speed. Getting from signup to medication in hand happens faster at SkinnyRx than at many competitors. If you have already done your research on GLP-1 therapy and just need access to the medication, the streamlined process is convenient.

Flexibility. Month-to-month billing with no contract means you can try it without a major financial commitment.

What They Do Not Do Well

Clinical depth. The provider interactions feel transactional. Check-ins are brief and often feel automated. If you have questions about side effects, dose timing, or how GLP-1 therapy interacts with other medications, getting a thorough answer can be difficult.

Customer support. Response times are slow, especially for billing and cancellation issues. Patients who need help resolving problems describe a frustrating experience.

Transparency. SkinnyRx does not clearly disclose which compounding pharmacies fill its prescriptions. For a medication you are injecting into your body, knowing the source and its accreditation status matters.

Marketing honesty. SkinnyRx's marketing leans heavily on before-and-after imagery and fast results. The reality is that GLP-1 weight loss varies significantly by individual and takes months to produce meaningful results. The marketing sets expectations that the clinical experience may not match.

User Experience

Honestly, the SkinnyRx experience depends entirely on your expectations. If you approach it as a low-cost medication delivery service with light clinical oversight, it delivers on that promise. Patients who just want affordable compounded semaglutide with minimal friction tend to be satisfied.

If you expect a true medical partnership with your provider, or if you need help managing side effects and adjusting your treatment plan, SkinnyRx falls short. The platform is built for volume and efficiency, not personalized care.

Who Is SkinnyRx Best For?

SkinnyRx is best for generally healthy, cost-focused patients who understand GLP-1 therapy and want the cheapest path to a compounded prescription. It works for self-directed individuals who do not need hand-holding from their provider.

It is not a good fit for patients with complex health conditions, people who want thorough clinical oversight, or anyone who values responsive customer service.

Is SkinnyRx worth the money?

Does SkinnyRx use real semaglutide?

SkinnyRx prescribes compounded semaglutide, which contains the same active ingredient as Ozempic and Wegovy. It is produced by compounding pharmacies rather than the original manufacturer, and the finished product is not FDA-approved.

How do I cancel SkinnyRx?
