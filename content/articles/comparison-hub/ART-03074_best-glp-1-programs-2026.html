Sesame is a telehealth marketplace where you book consultations directly with physicians. It is not a structured weight loss program. You pay for a visit (often $30 to $75), get a prescription if appropriate, and fill it at your own pharmacy.

For patients who have insurance coverage for Wegovy or Zepbound, Sesame can be the cheapest route. You avoid monthly platform fees entirely. The trade-off is that ongoing management is on you.

Pros:

Cons:

How We Evaluated

We assessed each program across five categories:

  • Medical oversight (30%): Physician credentials, continuity of care, lab-based monitoring, and how actively the clinical team manages your protocol.
  • Pricing and transparency (25%): Total monthly cost including medication, consult fees, and any hidden charges. We penalized programs that advertise low prices but tack on surprise fees.
  • Medication sourcing (20%): Whether compounded medications come from 503B-compliant pharmacies, and whether brand-name prescriptions are handled properly.
  • Patient support (15%): Responsiveness of the care team, quality of coaching (if offered), and how well the program handles side effects or complications.
  • Real-world outcomes (10%): Published data, patient reviews, and reported weight loss results over 3 to 12 months.

We signed up for each platform, completed intake processes, and interviewed current or former patients where possible. No program paid for placement in this article.

FAQ

What is a GLP-1 receptor agonist?

GLP-1 receptor agonists are medications originally developed for type 2 diabetes that also produce significant weight loss. They work by mimicking a hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1, which reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying, and helps regulate blood sugar. Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound) are the most widely prescribed.

Do I need insurance to use these programs?

No. Most programs on this list offer cash-pay options, and several provide compounded medications that bypass insurance entirely. If you do have coverage for brand-name GLP-1s, programs like Sesame or Calibrate can help you use it.

Are compounded GLP-1 medications safe?

When sourced from FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities, compounded GLP-1s are produced under strict quality controls. The risk increases when medications come from unregulated sources. Every program on this list uses 503B pharmacies or prescribes brand-name medications directly.

How much weight can I expect to lose?

Clinical trials show average weight loss of 15% to 22% of body weight over 12 to 18 months with semaglutide or tirzepatide. Real-world results vary based on dosing, adherence, diet, and activity level. Programs with coaching and lifestyle support tend to produce better sustained outcomes.

What happens if I stop taking the medication?

Research consistently shows that most patients regain a significant portion of lost weight after discontinuing GLP-1 therapy. This is why programs that include habit-building coaching or metabolic health support can be valuable for long-term success.
