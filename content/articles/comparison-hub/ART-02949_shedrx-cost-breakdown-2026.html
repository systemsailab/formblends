What Is Included in the Monthly Fee

ShedRx's monthly subscription typically bundles several components:

  • Initial provider consultation and ongoing follow-up visits

  • Medication compounding and preparation at a licensed pharmacy

Additional Costs Not Included

Beyond the monthly subscription, you may encounter these extra expenses:

Lab work

Some providers require baseline lab work before prescribing GLP-1 medications, including metabolic panels, thyroid function tests, or A1C levels. If ShedRx does not include labs in your plan, you may need to pay $50 to $200 out of pocket or go through your primary care provider. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Recommended lab work before starting GLP-1 therapy}}

Injection supplies

While some plans include syringes and supplies, not all do. Budget $10 to $20 per month for supplies if they are not included.

Sharps disposal

You will need a proper sharps container for used needles. These cost $5 to $15 and can be purchased at most pharmacies. Some communities offer free sharps disposal programs.

Expedited shipping

Standard shipping is typically included. If you need faster delivery, expedited options may carry an additional fee.

The Dose Escalation Cost Problem

This is the single most important cost factor that patients overlook. GLP-1 therapy follows a standard dose escalation protocol:

  • You start at a low dose to minimize side effects
  • Every 4 weeks (approximately), your dose increases
  • It may take 3 to 5 months to reach a full therapeutic dose
  • Higher doses use more medication per injection, costing more to compound

A patient who signs up at $199 per month for a starting dose may be paying $400 or more by month four. Over a six-month initial treatment period, total costs could range from $1,800 to $2,700 or more. This is not unreasonable for medical treatment, but it is important to budget for the full trajectory rather than just the first month. {{CITE:ShedRx dose escalation pricing changes}}

Is ShedRx Good Value for the Money?

Value is not just about price. It is about what you receive for what you pay. Consider these questions when evaluating ShedRx's value:

  • How much time does your provider spend on each consultation?
  • How accessible is support between scheduled visits?
  • Does the program help with nutrition, exercise, or behavioral factors?
  • How reliable is medication delivery?
  • Is the pricing predictable, or does it change without clear notice?

A slightly cheaper program that leaves you without adequate support may actually cost you more in wasted months and lost progress. A slightly more expensive program that keeps you on track and optimally dosed can save you money in the long run by shortening your overall treatment time.

Is compounded semaglutide as effective as Ozempic?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient as Ozempic. When prepared by a properly licensed pharmacy with appropriate quality controls, it should produce equivalent clinical effects. Why does the price go up with higher doses?

Higher doses require more active pharmaceutical ingredient per injection. The compounding pharmacy uses more material, which increases the production cost. This cost is passed through to the patient. It is a legitimate expense, but one that deserves upfront disclosure. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 dose escalation protocols}}
