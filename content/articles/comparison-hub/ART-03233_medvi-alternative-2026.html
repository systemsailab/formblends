Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from MEDVi to another provider without a gap in treatment?

Yes, in most cases. The best approach is to enroll with your new provider before your current MEDVi prescription runs out. Most telehealth GLP-1 programs can get you started within a few days of your initial consultation, so there should be minimal or no gap in your medication.

Will I need to start over at a low dose if I switch providers?

Not necessarily. A good provider will review your treatment history and continue from your current dose if it is appropriate. Be prepared to share your dosing history and any relevant lab work with your new provider.

Are compounded GLP-1 medications the same across providers?

Compounded medications can vary by pharmacy. Different providers work with different 503B compounding pharmacies, and quality standards, formulations, and concentrations may differ. Programs that work with FDA-registered compounding facilities and maintain strong pharmacy relationships tend to deliver more consistent results.

Is it safe to switch GLP-1 providers?

Switching providers is safe as long as your new physician has a complete picture of your treatment history. The medication itself does not change; what changes is the clinical team managing your care. If anything, moving to a provider with better oversight can improve your safety profile.

Do I need to cancel MEDVi before signing up elsewhere?

You should cancel your MEDVi subscription to avoid being billed, but you do not need to cancel before enrolling with a new provider. Start the new program first, confirm your medication is on the way, and then cancel MEDVi to avoid a gap.
