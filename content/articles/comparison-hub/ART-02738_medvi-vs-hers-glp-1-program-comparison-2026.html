Pricing Breakdown

Hers comes in at a lower price point, with compounded semaglutide starting around $199/month. MEDVi starts closer to $249/month. At the higher end, both platforms charge in the $350-$400 range for tirzepatide options or higher-dose semaglutide protocols.

The price difference largely reflects what each platform bundles into the package. MEDVi includes comprehensive metabolic bloodwork, which adds clinical depth but drives up cost. Hers keeps the focus tighter on medication and provider access, which is reflected in the lower starting price.

For women who already have a gynecologist or primary care physician ordering regular labs, the Hers approach avoids duplicating services. For women who don't have an existing provider running metabolic panels, MEDVi's included lab work could actually be a bargain compared to ordering those tests independently.

Medications Available

Both platforms prescribe compounded semaglutide as their main GLP-1 option. Compounded tirzepatide is available at both when supply allows, though availability has been unpredictable across the entire telehealth industry.

Hers benefits from the same pharmacy infrastructure as Hims (they operate under the same parent company, Hims & Hers Health). This gives Hers access to a larger compounding pharmacy network, which generally translates to more reliable supply and faster fulfillment.

MEDVi works with a smaller set of compounding partners. The upside is potentially tighter quality oversight. The downside is that during supply crunches, MEDVi patients may experience longer wait times.

Neither platform is a consistent option for brand-name GLP-1 medications like Wegovy or Mounjaro.

Patient Experience

Hers wins on design and usability. The app is clean, intuitive, and built with women in mind. Everything from onboarding to refills to provider messaging is handled in a single interface. The content and educational materials within the app are also tailored to women's health contexts, which matters when discussing side effects, hormonal interactions, and weight loss expectations.

MEDVi's portal is more utilitarian. It does the job, but it lacks the design polish and women-specific content that Hers provides. What MEDVi does offer is a more involved intake process, with longer initial consultations and more detailed health history reviews.

Women who value a platform that speaks directly to their health concerns will find Hers more comfortable. Women who prioritize clinical thoroughness over design may appreciate MEDVi's approach.

Provider Quality and Support

MEDVi staffs its consultations with board-certified physicians. Hers uses a mix of MDs, NPs, and PAs, all licensed in the patient's state. Both models are medically sound for GLP-1 prescribing.

One area where Hers adds value is its integration of weight loss with other women's health services. If you are also dealing with hormonal acne, hair thinning, or mood changes, Hers can address multiple concerns under one roof. MEDVi is more narrowly focused on metabolic health and weight management.

For support responsiveness, Hers offers broader hours and faster initial reply times, consistent with the Hims & Hers infrastructure. MEDVi's responses tend to be more detailed but slower.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hers just Hims rebranded for women?

Not exactly. Hers is operated by the same parent company (Hims & Hers Health) and shares infrastructure, but its content, provider training, and product selection are tailored specifically for women. The GLP-1 medications are the same, but the surrounding experience is designed differently.

Does MEDVi offer anything specific for women's health?

MEDVi's metabolic health approach benefits anyone, but the platform is not specifically designed around women's health concerns. It does not offer the bundled services (skin, hair, mental health) that Hers provides.

Which platform ships faster?

Hers typically ships faster due to its larger pharmacy network. Most Hers orders arrive within 3-5 business days. MEDVi averages 5-7 business days, though this can vary with supply conditions.

Can I switch between platforms easily?

Yes. Neither platform locks you into a long-term contract. You can cancel and enroll elsewhere, though you will need to go through the new platform's intake and consultation process from scratch.
