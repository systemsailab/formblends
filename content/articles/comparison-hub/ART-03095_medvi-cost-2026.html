Detailed Cost Breakdown

Semaglutide Program

MEDVi's semaglutide program starts at the lower end of their pricing range for introductory doses and increases as you titrate up. The starting dose (typically 0.25 mg) costs less than maintenance doses (1.0 mg and above). Expect your monthly cost to increase as your treatment progresses through the standard titration schedule.

At maintenance doses, most patients land in the $299 to $399/month range for compounded semaglutide. This is mid-market pricing. It is not the cheapest available, but it is well below brand-name Wegovy without insurance.

Tirzepatide Program

Compounded tirzepatide through MEDVi runs higher than semaglutide, which tracks with the broader market. Starting doses begin around $399/month, with higher doses reaching $599/month. Tirzepatide is generally more expensive across all providers because the compound itself costs more to produce.

What Is Included

MEDVi bundles the following into the monthly price:

  • Compounded medication (semaglutide or tirzepatide)
  • Provider consultation and ongoing medical oversight
  • Dosing adjustments as you titrate
  • Shipping to your door
  • Messaging access to your care team

What Is Not Included

MEDVi does not cover lab work. If your provider orders bloodwork, you will pay for that through your insurance or an out-of-pocket lab service. Some patients also report that switching medications mid-cycle (for example, from semaglutide to tirzepatide) may require a new consult and reset your billing.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

MEDVi does not accept insurance directly. You pay out of pocket for the program, including medication. However, compounded GLP-1 medications prescribed through MEDVi may qualify as eligible expenses under your HSA or FSA, since they are prescribed by a licensed physician for a medical condition.

To use your HSA or FSA, request an itemized receipt from MEDVi showing the medical service and medication charge. Submit it to your benefits administrator for reimbursement. Most HSA/FSA plans cover prescription medications, but confirm with your plan before assuming coverage.

How to Save on MEDVi

  • Multi-month plans: MEDVi occasionally offers discounted rates for patients who commit to 3-month or 6-month plans. Ask about these during enrollment.
  • Start with semaglutide: If your provider recommends either GLP-1, semaglutide is the less expensive option and may be sufficient for your goals.
  • Use HSA/FSA funds: Paying with pre-tax dollars through your health savings account effectively reduces your cost by your marginal tax rate.
  • Watch for promotions: MEDVi runs periodic discounts on first-month pricing. Check their site before enrolling.

FAQ

Does MEDVi charge extra for dose adjustments?

No. Dose adjustments are included in your monthly subscription. However, your monthly price may increase as your dose increases, since higher doses cost more to compound.

Can I cancel MEDVi at any time?

MEDVi offers month-to-month plans that you can cancel before your next billing cycle. If you enrolled in a multi-month discounted plan, check the terms for early cancellation fees.

Does MEDVi accept insurance?

No. MEDVi is a cash-pay program. You may be able to submit receipts to your HSA or FSA for reimbursement, but the platform does not bill insurance directly.

What happens if MEDVi raises its prices?

MEDVi does not currently offer price-lock guarantees. If you are concerned about price increases, ask about multi-month plan options that may lock your rate for the plan duration.
