Why Women Need Different Weight Loss Approaches

Female physiology creates distinct weight loss challenges that generic programs often overlook. Estrogen and progesterone fluctuations throughout the menstrual cycle directly affect appetite, water retention, and fat storage patterns. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:hormonal impact on female weight loss}}

During perimenopause and menopause, declining estrogen levels shift fat distribution toward the midsection, reduce lean muscle mass, and alter insulin sensitivity. Programs that fail to account for these changes produce frustrating results and high dropout rates among women over 40.

GLP-1 receptor agonists like semaglutide and tirzepatide have shown strong efficacy across female populations in clinical trials, with some studies reporting average weight loss of 15-20% of body weight over 68 weeks. {{CITE:semaglutide clinical trial results in women}} Form Blends provides these medications under physician supervision, with dosing adjustments that reflect each patient's unique metabolic and hormonal profile.

GLP-1 Medications and Women: What the Research Shows

GLP-1 receptor agonists work by mimicking the incretin hormone GLP-1, which regulates appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves insulin sensitivity.
