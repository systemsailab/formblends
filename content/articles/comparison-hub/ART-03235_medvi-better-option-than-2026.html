Who Should Look for a Better Option

Not every MEDVi patient needs to switch. If your protocol is producing results and your experience is positive, staying put is a perfectly valid choice. But consider making a move if:

  • Your weight loss has stalled and your provider is not adapting. A plateau is normal, but how your provider responds to it matters. If the response is "keep doing what you are doing," a more engaged physician might be able to adjust your approach.
  • You want peptide therapies alongside your GLP-1. MEDVi does not offer the peptide options that programs like Form Blends provide. If your treatment goals extend beyond weight loss, you need a broader formulary.
  • You are paying more than you should. The GLP-1 telehealth market is competitive in 2026. If your MEDVi costs are higher than comparable programs with similar or better care, you are leaving money on the table.
  • You want to feel like you have a real doctor. The difference between a provider who follows a script and a physician who knows your case and thinks critically about your protocol is significant. If you are not getting the latter, you are settling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MEDVi a bad program?

No. MEDVi is a legitimate telehealth GLP-1 provider that works well for many patients. "Better" does not mean MEDVi is bad. It means different programs may be stronger in areas that matter specifically to you.

What makes one GLP-1 program better than another?

The quality of physician oversight, the transparency of pricing, the breadth of medication options, and the responsiveness of customer support are the biggest differentiators. The "best" program depends on which of these factors matters most to your situation.

Will switching providers affect my results?

A smooth transition should not negatively affect your results. In fact, moving to a provider with more personalized care can sometimes restart progress that had stalled. The key is ensuring your new provider has your complete treatment history.

How do I know if my current program is good enough?

Ask yourself: Is my provider adjusting my protocol based on how I am responding? Can I reach someone when I have a question? Do I understand exactly what I am paying and why? Am I making progress toward my goals? If the answer to any of these is no, it is worth exploring alternatives.
