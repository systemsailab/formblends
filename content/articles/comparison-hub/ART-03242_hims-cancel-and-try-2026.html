Why People Cancel Hims

Hims has millions of users, so there are millions of individual reasons. But the themes are consistent:

  • The "doctor" experience is not what they expected. Many patients sign up expecting a physician relationship and instead get brief, impersonal consultations with a rotating cast of providers. That gap between expectation and reality drives a lot of cancellations.
  • Results have plateaued. GLP-1 therapy produces significant early results for most patients. When progress slows and the provider response is to maintain the current protocol, patients look for someone who will actually troubleshoot.
  • Pricing has increased. Starting at one price and paying a higher price at maintenance doses feels like a bait and switch, even if it was technically disclosed in the fine print.
  • They want broader treatment options. Patients interested in tirzepatide, peptide therapies, or combination approaches have outgrown what Hims offers.
  • Service issues. Shipping delays, difficulty canceling subscriptions, unclear billing, or slow customer support push patients past their patience.

How to Cancel Hims

Before You Cancel

Take these steps before initiating cancellation:

  1. Enroll with your new provider. Start the consultation process with your replacement program before canceling Hims. This prevents any gap in your medication.
  2. Download your records. Save your dosing history, consultation notes, weight data, and any other treatment information from your Hims account. Your new provider will need this.
  3. Check your billing date. Know when your next charge is scheduled so you can cancel before it processes.
  4. Inventory your medication. Know how much medication you have left so you can time the transition to avoid running out.

Cancellation Steps

Hims subscriptions can be canceled through the app or website. Navigate to your subscription settings, select the GLP-1 program, and follow the cancellation flow. Hims may present retention offers or ask why you are leaving. You do not owe them an explanation, but if they offer a meaningful concession (lower price, different provider), consider whether it addresses your actual complaint.

After canceling:

  • Save a screenshot or email confirmation of the cancellation
  • Note the effective date of cancellation
  • Check your credit card statement on the next billing date to verify no charges
  • If you see unauthorized charges, dispute them with your card issuer and contact Hims support with your cancellation confirmation

Who Should Cancel Hims and Try Something New

Canceling Hims makes sense when the program is no longer serving your needs and better options are available. That applies if:

  • You have given Hims a fair shot. Several months on the program is enough time to evaluate whether the clinical model works for you. If it does not, that is unlikely to change.
  • Your needs have evolved. Maybe you started wanting basic semaglutide access and now want tirzepatide, peptide therapy, or a more comprehensive treatment approach. Your provider should grow with your needs.
  • The cost-to-value ratio is off. If you are paying more per month than you were when you started and the clinical experience has not improved proportionally, you are overpaying for what you receive.
  • You want a physician, not a provider. The difference matters. A provider approves your prescription. A physician manages your care. If you want the latter, Hims' high-volume model is not built to deliver it.
  • Your results need clinical intervention. When weight loss stalls, side effects persist, or your protocol needs adjustment, you need a clinician who will engage with the problem. If your current provider is not doing that, switching to one who will is not optional. It is necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it hard to cancel Hims?

Hims allows cancellation through their app and website. The process is straightforward, though you may encounter retention offers or surveys. Save confirmation of your cancellation for your records.

Will I have a gap in medication if I cancel Hims?

Not if you plan properly. Start your new provider consultation before canceling Hims. Most programs can get you medication within a week of enrollment. Time your cancellation so your new medication arrives before your current supply runs out.

Do I need a new prescription from my new provider?

Yes. Each telehealth GLP-1 program writes prescriptions through their own affiliated providers and pharmacies. Your new provider will evaluate you and write a new prescription based on your treatment history. You are not transferring a prescription; you are getting a new one.

What if I regret canceling Hims?

You can re-enroll with Hims at any time. There are no blackout periods or penalties for returning. You would go through the enrollment process again as a new patient.

How do I make sure my new provider continues my current dose?

Share your complete treatment history during your initial consultation. Include your current dose, how long you have been on it, your titration history, and any side effects. A responsible physician will continue your current dose if it is clinically appropriate.
