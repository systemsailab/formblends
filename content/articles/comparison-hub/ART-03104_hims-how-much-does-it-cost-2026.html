Detailed Breakdown: Every Dollar Explained

The Medication

The bulk of your $175/month goes toward the compounded semaglutide itself. Hims sources medication from 503B-registered compounding pharmacies and handles fulfillment through its in-house pharmacy network. The flat pricing means you pay the same whether you are on a 0.25 mg introductory dose or a 1.7 mg maintenance dose.

Compounded semaglutide is chemically the same active ingredient as Ozempic and Wegovy but produced by specialized pharmacies rather than Novo Nordisk. This is why it costs a fraction of brand-name pricing.

The Medical Consultation

Your initial medical evaluation is included in the first month's subscription. A licensed provider reviews your health history, assesses your eligibility for GLP-1 therapy, and writes your prescription. Follow-up consultations happen through in-app messaging and are included in every subsequent month's payment.

Hims' medical model is asynchronous. You submit health information, a provider reviews it, and communication happens through the app. There are no video calls or phone appointments by default, though some situations may warrant escalation.

Shipping and Handling

Free. Every shipment. Medication arrives in temperature-controlled packaging within 3 to 5 business days. Hims does not charge expedited shipping fees.

The Only Extra Cost: Lab Work

Hims does not include lab work in the subscription. Depending on your provider's requirements, you may need basic blood panels before starting treatment or periodically during treatment. Lab costs depend on your insurance:

  • With insurance: typically $0 to $50 (many plans cover preventive labs at no cost)
  • Without insurance: $50 to $200 through direct-to-consumer services like Quest or Labcorp

Not every Hims patient is asked to do lab work. If your health history and questionnaire responses indicate low risk, your provider may prescribe without labs.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

Hims does not accept health insurance. You pay cash for the entire program. Insurance does not cover compounded medications, so this is consistent across all compounded GLP-1 providers.

Your Hims payments should qualify for HSA and FSA reimbursement. The IRS considers physician-prescribed medications for medical conditions to be qualified expenses. Steps to claim:

  1. Keep your monthly Hims receipts (download from your account)
  2. Submit each receipt to your HSA/FSA administrator as a qualified medical expense
  3. Receive reimbursement from your pre-tax health savings balance

The tax savings are real. If your combined federal and state tax rate is 30%, paying $175/month through HSA/FSA effectively costs you $123/month. Over a year, that is $630 saved.

How to Save on Hims

  • Skip unnecessary labs: If your primary care doctor recently ran relevant blood work, share those results with your Hims provider to potentially avoid duplicate testing.
  • Consider a multi-month plan: When available, Hims' 3-month bundles offer a small per-month discount.

No. The only potential additional expense is lab work, and not every patient needs it. There are no hidden platform fees, sign-up charges, or cancellation penalties on month-to-month plans.

If your doctor prescribes brand-name Wegovy or Zepbound and your insurance covers it, you will likely pay less through insurance copays than through Hims. If your insurance does not cover GLP-1 medications, Hims' $175/month for compounded semaglutide is far cheaper than brand-name cash prices.
