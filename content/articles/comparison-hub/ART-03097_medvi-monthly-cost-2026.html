Detailed Month-by-Month Breakdown

Months 1 to 2: Starting Dose

Your first two months on MEDVi are typically the cheapest. You start at the lowest dose of your chosen GLP-1 (0.25 mg for semaglutide, 2.5 mg for tirzepatide), and the monthly cost reflects the smaller amount of medication being compounded. For semaglutide, expect around $249/month. For tirzepatide, around $399/month.

This first payment also covers your initial medical consultation, health history review, and prescription. There is no separate onboarding fee.

Months 3 to 4: Mid-Titration

As your provider increases your dose (typically to 0.5 mg semaglutide or 5.0 mg tirzepatide, then higher), the monthly cost steps up. Most patients see a $50 to $100 increase during this phase. The medication itself costs more to compound at higher concentrations, and that cost passes through to you.

This is the phase where your budget should be prepared for adjustment. If you started at $249, you are likely paying $299 to $349 by month three or four.

Month 5 Onward: Maintenance

Once you reach your effective maintenance dose, the monthly cost stabilizes. For compounded semaglutide, maintenance typically costs $349 to $399/month through MEDVi. For tirzepatide, $499 to $599/month. These are the numbers to use for long-term budgeting, since most patients stay on maintenance for 6 to 12 months or longer.

Insurance, HSA, and FSA

MEDVi does not bill insurance. The entire program is cash-pay, which is standard for compounded GLP-1 telehealth providers. Insurance companies generally do not cover compounded medications.

Your monthly MEDVi payments are likely eligible for HSA and FSA reimbursement. Since a licensed physician prescribes the medication for a medical condition, the expense qualifies under most health savings account guidelines. Save your receipts and submit them to your benefits administrator for reimbursement.

Using HSA or FSA funds effectively reduces your monthly cost by your marginal tax rate. If you are in the 24% federal bracket (plus state taxes), a $349/month payment really costs you around $250 in after-tax dollars.

FAQ

What is the cheapest month on MEDVi?

Your first month is typically the cheapest because you start at the lowest dose. For semaglutide, expect around $249. Some patients also benefit from first-month promotional pricing that brings this lower.

Does MEDVi lock in my monthly rate?

MEDVi does not currently offer rate-lock guarantees. Your monthly cost changes with your dose, and the company can adjust base pricing at any time. Multi-month prepaid plans may offer some protection.

How does MEDVi's monthly cost compare to Wegovy?

Brand-name Wegovy lists at roughly $1,350/month without insurance. MEDVi's compounded semaglutide at $249 to $399/month is significantly cheaper. But if your insurance covers Wegovy with a low copay ($25 to $50/month), the brand-name route through insurance beats MEDVi on price.

Can I pause my MEDVi subscription to save money?

MEDVi allows cancellation, but pausing mid-treatment is not ideal medically. Gaps in GLP-1 therapy can lead to weight regain and may require restarting the titration schedule. If cost is a concern, switching to a more affordable provider is typically better than pausing treatment.
