Why People Switch from Hims

The decision to leave Hims usually builds gradually. These are the patterns we see most often:

  • Clinical interactions are too brief. A five-minute consultation with a provider who does not remember your history is not medical management. It is a checkbox. Patients who need genuine clinical engagement seek it elsewhere.
  • Standard protocols are not producing results. Hims follows standardized titration schedules that work for many patients. When they do not work for you, the platform is not set up to provide the kind of individualized attention needed to get things back on track.
  • Costs increased with dosing. Competitive starting prices gave way to higher monthly bills as the dose went up. Patients who expected consistent pricing feel misled, even if the tiered model was technically disclosed.
  • Interest in broader treatment options. Peptide therapies, combination protocols, and medications beyond standard GLP-1s are not available through Hims.
  • Side effects are not being managed. Nausea, fatigue, and GI issues are common on GLP-1s. When your provider's approach is to wait it out rather than adjust your protocol, it signals a care gap.

How to Switch from Hims: Step by Step

Step 1: Research and Select Your New Provider

Before touching your Hims subscription, identify where you want to go. Consider what drove you to leave and make sure your new provider addresses those specific concerns. If clinical depth is the issue, look for physician-led programs like Form Blends. If cost is the issue, compare flat-rate options like Henry Meds.

Step 2: Collect Your Treatment Records

Download everything from your Hims account before you cancel:

  • Current medication and dose
  • Titration history and dates
  • Provider consultation notes
  • Side effects you have reported
  • Weight tracking data
  • Any lab work (if applicable)

Your new provider needs this information to continue your treatment without starting from scratch. Most good programs will ask for it during your initial consultation.

Step 3: Start Your New Provider Consultation

Complete the enrollment and initial medical evaluation with your new provider while you still have an active Hims subscription. This ensures continuous coverage. Most telehealth GLP-1 programs can complete a consultation within 24 to 48 hours.

Be thorough during this consultation. Share your full treatment history, be honest about side effects, and discuss what was and was not working with your previous protocol. A good physician will use this information to build a better plan.

Step 4: Time the Transition

Aim to receive your new medication before your current Hims supply runs out. Start the process about two weeks before your next Hims shipment date. This gives your new provider time to evaluate you, write your prescription, and ship medication.

Having a few days of overlap between old and new medication is fine. You are just creating a buffer to prevent a gap.

Step 5: Cancel Hims

Once your new medication is confirmed (shipped or received), cancel your Hims subscription. You can do this through the Hims app or website. Key things to keep in mind:

  • Check when your next billing date is and cancel before it
  • Save written confirmation of the cancellation
  • Monitor your credit card for any post-cancellation charges
  • Complete any remaining medication you have already paid for

Who Should Switch from Hims

Not every Hims patient needs to switch. If the platform is working for you, there is value in consistency. But certain situations make a move clearly worthwhile:

  • You have hit a plateau and nothing is changing. When weight loss stalls, you need a provider who will investigate why and adjust your protocol. If your Hims provider is not doing that, a physician-led program can make a real difference.
  • You feel like you do not have a real doctor. There is a meaningful difference between a telehealth consultation and a physician relationship. If you want the latter, programs like Form Blends provide it.
  • Your monthly cost has increased beyond your budget. Dose-dependent pricing at Hims can push costs higher than you planned. Flat-rate alternatives may save you $50 to $100 per month at higher doses.
  • You want treatments Hims does not offer. Peptide therapies, combination protocols, or medications outside Hims' formulary require a different provider.
  • Side effects are not being managed. GLP-1 side effects should be actively managed through dosing adjustments, timing changes, or supportive medications. If your provider is not doing this, your care is substandard.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I lose my progress if I switch from Hims?

No. Your progress is a result of the medication and your body's response to it, not the platform that prescribed it. As long as your new provider continues you at an appropriate dose with no significant medication gap, your results should continue uninterrupted.

Do I need to restart at a low dose with a new provider?

A responsible physician will review your treatment history and continue at your current dose if it is appropriate. You should not need to restart titration from the beginning. Be sure to share your complete dosing history during your initial consultation.

How long does it take to get started with a new program?

Most telehealth GLP-1 programs can complete a consultation within 48 hours and ship medication within a few days. Plan for about one week from enrollment to receiving medication, though some programs are faster.

Can I use my Hims medication while waiting for my new provider?

Yes. Continue taking your Hims medication as prescribed until your new medication arrives. There is no medical reason to stop early. The goal is a seamless handoff with no gap in treatment.

What if I want to come back to Hims later?

You can re-enroll with Hims at any time. There is no penalty for canceling and returning. You would go through the enrollment process again as a new patient.
