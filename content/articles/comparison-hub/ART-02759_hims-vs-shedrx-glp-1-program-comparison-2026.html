Medications and Dosing

Both Hims and ShedRx use compounded versions of GLP-1 receptor agonists. Compounded medications are mixed by licensed compounding pharmacies and are not the same as brand-name Ozempic or Wegovy, though they contain the same active ingredient (semaglutide).

Hims primarily offers compounded semaglutide, with providers adjusting dosage based on your response and tolerance. ShedRx also offers compounded semaglutide but adds tirzepatide (the active ingredient in Mounjaro and Zepbound) to its formulary, giving patients a second GLP-1 option if semaglutide is not a good fit.

Dosing protocols at both platforms generally follow a gradual titration schedule. You start at a lower dose and increase over several weeks to minimize side effects like nausea and GI discomfort.

Clinical Support and Provider Access

Hims provides access to licensed healthcare providers through its app. Most initial consultations happen asynchronously, meaning you fill out a questionnaire and a provider reviews it. Follow-up support is available through messaging, and some plans include video visits. The breadth of the provider network is a strength, but some patients feel that the asynchronous model lacks a personal touch.

ShedRx also connects patients with licensed providers, though their team is smaller and more specialized in weight management. This can be a double-edged sword. On one hand, the providers are deeply familiar with GLP-1 protocols. On the other, a smaller team can mean slower response times during busy periods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is compounded semaglutide the same as Ozempic or Wegovy?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient but is prepared by a compounding pharmacy rather than the brand-name manufacturer. It is not FDA-approved in compounded form, though the active ingredient itself is FDA-approved.

Can I switch from Hims to ShedRx (or vice versa) without restarting my dosage?

In most cases, yes. When you consult with a new provider, share your current dosage and treatment history. Your new provider can typically continue your existing protocol without requiring you to start over.

Do either Hims or ShedRx accept insurance?

Neither platform typically accepts insurance for their compounded GLP-1 programs. Some patients use HSA or FSA funds to cover costs.

What are the most common side effects of GLP-1 medications?

Nausea, constipation, diarrhea, and reduced appetite are the most commonly reported side effects. These tend to be mild and often improve as your body adjusts to the medication.

How quickly will I see results?

Most patients notice reduced appetite within the first week or two. Meaningful weight loss typically becomes visible within 4 to 8 weeks, though individual results vary based on starting weight, dosage, and lifestyle factors.

Are there long-term contracts with either platform?

Both Hims and ShedRx operate on a subscription model. You can typically cancel at any time, though it is worth reviewing each platform's cancellation policy before signing up.
