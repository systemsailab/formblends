The Insurance Factor

This is the most important distinction between Hims and Teladoc. Hims operates entirely outside the insurance system for its GLP-1 program. You pay a flat monthly fee, and that is it. There is no insurance paperwork, no prior authorization, and no coverage denials to worry about.

Teladoc, on the other hand, often works within the insurance framework. If your employer offers Teladoc as a benefit, your weight management visits may be covered at little or no cost. More importantly, if your insurance covers brand-name GLP-1 medications like Wegovy or Zepbound, Teladoc's providers can prescribe them, and your insurance handles the bulk of the medication cost. {{CITE:Teladoc insurance integration}}

For patients with good insurance coverage, Teladoc can be dramatically cheaper than Hims. For patients without insurance benefits that include GLP-1 coverage, Hims' compounded option is often the more affordable path. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:GLP-1 insurance coverage landscape}}

Clinical Model

Teladoc's weight management consultations typically happen via video, giving patients face-to-face time with a provider. This allows for more nuanced conversations about medical history, side effects, and treatment adjustments. For patients who value a more traditional doctor-patient interaction, Teladoc's video model is an advantage.

Hims primarily uses asynchronous messaging. You describe your situation, and a provider responds in writing. Video visits are available in some cases but are not the default. This model is faster for simple consultations but may not provide the depth that complex cases require.

Chronic Care Integration

Teladoc stands out for patients who manage multiple chronic conditions. If you are dealing with obesity alongside Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or cardiovascular disease, Teladoc can coordinate care across these conditions within a single platform. Your weight management provider can communicate with your diabetes care team, for example, ensuring medications do not conflict and treatment goals are aligned. {{CITE:Teladoc chronic care programs}}

Hims does not offer this kind of integrated chronic care. The weight loss program is standalone. If you need care for other conditions, you will need to manage those separately through other providers.

Hims is the right choice if you do not have insurance coverage for GLP-1 medications and want affordable, hassle-free access to compounded semaglutide. If you prefer a consumer-friendly app experience and do not need integrated chronic care, Hims keeps things simple.
