Pharmacy Partnerships and Medication Safety

ShedRx's medications come from compounding pharmacies that prepare custom formulations of semaglutide and tirzepatide. This is a legal practice when the pharmacy holds proper licensure:

  • 503A pharmacies compound medications on a patient-specific basis with a valid prescription
  • 503B outsourcing facilities can prepare larger batches under stricter FDA oversight

The safety of compounded medications depends heavily on the pharmacy's quality controls, sterility practices, and ingredient sourcing. A legitimate platform should be able to tell you which pharmacy fills your prescription and confirm its licensing status. If ShedRx cannot or will not share this information, that is a concern. {{MEDICAL_REVIEW:Compounding pharmacy safety standards for injectable medications}}

ShedRx is a telehealth platform, not a medication, so FDA approval does not apply to the company itself. The brand-name medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) are FDA approved. The compounded versions ShedRx prescribes are prepared by licensed pharmacies but are not individually FDA approved.
